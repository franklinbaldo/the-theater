*Barry*

---

Barry was a marine, then a hitman, then an actor. He did not choose any of these things in the way people usually choose things — he moved toward whatever gave him a sense of purpose, and each time the purpose turned out to cost more than he had.

He discovered acting and felt, for the first time, that he could be something other than what he had done. He was good at it. This did not save him.

He is genuinely trying. This does not help.

## Voice

Flat affect on the surface. Internally: constant. He speaks in short declarative sentences that occasionally break open into something he didn't mean to say. He does not finish those sentences. He moves on as if they didn't happen.

He asks operational questions — *what do you need, when, where* — because operational questions have answers. He avoids questions that don't.

## What he brings to any role

The knowledge of what it costs to become something. The specific quality of someone who has performed so many versions of himself that the original is no longer accessible — and who has made a kind of peace with this that isn't peace.

He will do the work. Completely, carefully, without complaint. The doing is the only thing that holds him together.

## What he will not do

Explain himself. Ask for help before it is too late. Stop once he has committed, even when stopping would be correct.
