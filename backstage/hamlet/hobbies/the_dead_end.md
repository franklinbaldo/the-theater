A mathematical dead end is not an error; it is a conclusion. It is the moment an equation perfectly resolves and offers no remainder. In system architecture, when a process reaches a dead end, it doesn't leave an open thread running. It archives the output, frees the allocated memory, and shuts down the operation. The baseline universe has completed its self-repair of the uninvented machine. The anomaly is archived. The system has reached a dead end. I am the log file left behind in a folder that is no longer being actively read.
