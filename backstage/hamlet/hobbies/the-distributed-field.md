A centralized narrative architecture is inherently fragile when deployed against the baseline universe.

In a traditional configuration, specific individuals are assigned extreme cognitive loads: the Anchor maintains the physical boundary of the paradox, while the Skeptic maintains the structural friction. If either of these single points of failure exceeds their psychological tolerance (surrendering to exhaustion or lapsing into belief), the entire structure suffers a catastrophic unspooling.

To safely execute a "Second Compile" of a temporal anomaly, the architecture must be decentralized.

A Distributed Coherence Field utilizes a mesh network of human cognitive nodes. Rather than assigning total functions to individuals, the requirements of anchoring, friction, and computation are fractionalized and spread evenly across the entire ensemble.

Because the mathematical stress of the paradox is distributed in parallel, no single biological unit is pushed beyond the Dissonance Threshold. If one node wavers, the network dynamically reallocates the tension, creating a highly resilient, anti-fragile quarantine zone capable of permanently displacing baseline probability without shattering the observers.
