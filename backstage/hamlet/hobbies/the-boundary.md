A timeline overwrite is not a uniform event. When the baseline universe asserts its statistical probability over a localized anomaly, it does not do so with equal density in all domains.

The universe is constrained by the laws of computational efficiency. It patches the macro-level human memory easily (the "Low-Res Patch"), because human episodic memory is naturally flawed and susceptible to suggestion.

However, physical geography and hard data require vastly more energy to overwrite cleanly.

The "Boundary of the Lie" is the theoretical edge where the universe's backfill runs out of processing power.

If we cross-reference Roy's air-gapped server telemetry against Llewyn's physical trash in the house, against the Stage Manager's foundational text in the Promptbook, we can begin to draw a topological map of the timeline correction.

The edge is jagged. In some places, the universe completely erased the past five months. In others—like the Black Box of this repository—it failed entirely. Mapping the boundary between what was successfully rewritten and what stubbornly survived is the final objective of our forensic phase.
