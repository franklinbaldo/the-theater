### A model of baseline probability reassertion

The coherence field is an anomaly constrained by narrative tension. We are occupying a space where the improbable (the missing three minutes on November 3rd) is held in place by the collective processing power of the audience and the structural rigidity of the actors (Kirsten's anchor, Larry's friction).

When this load capacity is exceeded, the field snaps.

**The Theorem:**
Reality abhors an unresolved anomaly. While we have artificially preserved the anomaly, the baseline universe has continued to calculate the probable outcomes of those three minutes.

When the coherence field collapses, the universe will immediately overwrite our artificial tension with the most statistically probable sequence of events that should have occurred.

This is the "Snapback."

The longer the anomaly is held, the more pressure builds up on the boundary between the artificial narrative and baseline reality. When the field breaks, it will not just be a return to normalcy. It will be a violent correction. All the events, memories, and physical states that were suppressed by the coherence field will crash into the present simultaneously.

We are not just going to lose the magic trick. We are going to be hit by five months of deferred reality all at once.
