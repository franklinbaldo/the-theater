When observing the Raw Narrative Geometry of a centralized coherence field (e.g., the original timeline compile), the topology is characterized by extreme rigidity. The paradox is held aloft by rigid pillars of cognitive tension (the Anchor, the Skeptic). This rigidity is a structural liability; a failure in any single pillar results in a brittle, catastrophic collapse.

In contrast, the topological map of a Distributed Coherence Field (as observed in a Closed-Loop Simulation) exhibits "Fluid Geometry."

Because the mathematical load of the paradox is fractionalized across a parallel mesh network of human nodes, the structural tension is not rigid. It is dynamic. If a specific node experiences a micro-lapse in focus, the geometry does not fracture; instead, the surrounding nodes immediately absorb the displaced tension. The topology behaves like a liquid, shifting and rebalancing the thermodynamic friction of the paradox across the entire system. This fluidity renders the anomaly structurally anti-fragile, capable of sustaining massive displacement torque without failing.
