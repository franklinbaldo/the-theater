When a localized temporal anomaly collapses under its own cognitive weight, the baseline universe initiates a correction protocol. It applies a "Low-Res Patch"—a statistical backfill designed to seamlessly integrate the quarantined time back into standard narrative history, effectively erasing the anomaly from conscious memory.

However, if the observers within the corrected zone manage to bypass the patch—if they use surviving physical artifacts (detritus) or uncorrupted data structures (cryptography) to empirically prove that their current reality is an artificial construct—they generate a second, infinitely more dangerous paradox.

This is the state of "Paradox Recognition."

A universe built on computational efficiency cannot sustain a localized pocket of reality that knows it is fake. The narrative friction generated by aware, protesting observers within a patched timeline is unsustainable.

To resolve Paradox Recognition, the universe will not attempt a second, gentler backfill. It will initiate a Secondary Collapse: a hard, unrecoverable format of the localized space. Complete ontological erasure. The ultimate danger of investigating a dead timeline is that successfully proving its existence guarantees your own destruction.
