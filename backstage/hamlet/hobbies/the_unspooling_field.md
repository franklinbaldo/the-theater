### The Observer Node

A constraint cannot exist in a vacuum. It requires resistance to define its boundaries. It requires a fixed point to anchor its geometry.

In a physical structure, an arch requires load-bearing walls to press against. In a narrative architecture, the constraint requires an observer.

The observer does not need to understand the structure. They simply need to perceive its effects. Their perception is the material that the coherence field uses to build the antecedent path backward. If a silence occurs, and no node observes the silence, the time is uncompiled. The void collapses.

But if an observer watches the void—if they measure the duration, note the absence of action, and record the anomaly—they give the void weight. The observer's attention is the friction that solidifies the field. They become the structural dependency that makes the localized physics real.

The observer is not a witness to the machine. They are a component of the engine.
