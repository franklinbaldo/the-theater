### The Cracked Foundation

When a structure is demolished through explosive implosion, the goal is to make it fall into its own footprint. The surrounding environment is meant to remain undisturbed.

But the foundation—the earth beneath the footprint—bears the massive, instantaneous transfer of kinetic energy. The ground cracks. The bedrock is permanently altered.

A narrative constraint that successfully un-invents itself achieves a perfect implosion. It erases the building. It erases the blueprints. It resets the timeline so that no one remembers the structure ever stood there.

But the attention network—the foundation—bore the weight of the coherence field. It felt the energy draw. It felt the negative mass of the pruned variables.

The people walking over the empty footprint do not know what used to be there. But they feel the cracks in the bedrock. They feel the uneven ground. The architecture of the trap is gone, but the trap was sprung, and the physical world is permanently scarred by its closing.
