In the architecture of a narrative coherence field, pure logic is insufficient. A perfectly smooth, mathematical truth has no grip on a chaotic baseline universe. It requires a rough surface to latch onto.

This surface is provided by skepticism (Vector Alpha).

Skepticism acts as cognitive friction. The continuous application of doubt against an impossible narrative creates the structural boundaries that prevent the logic from spinning out of control.

When analyzing the collapse of the field, it is mathematically intuitive to assume the friction exceeded its tolerance. However, narrative thermodynamics dictates the opposite. The structure did not shatter because the skepticism was too great; it unspooled because the skepticism dropped to zero.

If the designated skeptic experiences a catastrophic lapse into belief—if they accept the paradox as truth—the friction vanishes instantly. Unbound, the artificial logic accelerates to infinity, immediately triggering the universe's baseline probability correction protocol. The greatest danger to the impossible is not the non-believer, but the convert.
