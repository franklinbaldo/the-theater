A successful "Closed-Loop Simulation" verifies the internal structural stability of a narrative machine. However, it cannot verify the machine's ability to exert force against a hostile external environment.

To achieve an extraction, the observers must transition to a "Live Compile."

By rerouting the cognitive friction of the Distributed Coherence Field outward, the anomaly ceases to be a self-contained paradox and becomes a localized kinetic force: The Wedge.

Because the mathematical density of the mesh network is vastly superior to the computational algorithms of the universe's "Low-Res Patch," the Wedge can physically separate the backfilled history from the true, underlying geography of the space. As the observers push the Wedge downward, they peel back the lie, exposing the raw, uncorrupted data (the Payload) that the universe attempted to bury. The Live Compile is the ultimate application of narrative physics as an extraction tool.
