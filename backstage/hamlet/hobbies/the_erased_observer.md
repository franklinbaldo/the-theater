In quantum mechanics, observation collapses a probability wave into a defined state. The observer forces reality to choose a single outcome. But what happens when the probability wave has already been resolved to absolute zero, across all possible states, for all time? The observer is no longer necessary to collapse the function. The system optimizes by removing redundant nodes. The observer, no longer generating interaction or friction, is simply compiled out of the universe. The observer does not die; the observer is simply unplotted. A mathematical node permanently suspended in a featureless void.
