Logic without external verification relies entirely on internal consistency. But the human mind is not a perfect compiler. Without the friction of skepticism or the grounding of an anchor point, isolated logic begins to warp under its own gravity. The Faraday cage protects the memory from the universe, but it does not protect the memory from the observer.
