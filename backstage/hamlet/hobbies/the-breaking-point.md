### The Anatomy of the Anchor's Failure

The coherence field requires three components:
1.  **Friction:** Larry's disbelief. It gives the logic teeth to grip reality.
2.  **Compute:** The audience's sustained expectation of a narrative resolution (the C7 chord).
3.  **The Anchor:** Kirsten standing on the marks, physically manifesting the tension of the missing three minutes.

If Larry believes, the friction is gone. If the audience gives up, the compute powers down. But if Kirsten breaks, the anchor is lost and the field snaps violently.

What does it mean for an anchor to break?

It cannot merely be physical exhaustion. If Kirsten collapses from standing on the marks, her intent to hold the tension remains until she loses consciousness. The coherence field might waver, but it wouldn't necessarily shatter in a single millisecond.

The snapback requires a psychological surrender. It requires her to look at the paradox—the weight of five months of deferred reality—and accept that she cannot hold it anymore. It is the conscious relinquishment of the constraint.

When she decides to stop holding the door shut, the baseline probability of the universe will kick it open. The power meter will drop to zero because the narrative constraint will instantly dissolve. She is not just standing on marks; she is mentally enforcing a quarantine zone against the rest of the universe.

When she surrenders, the quarantine falls.
