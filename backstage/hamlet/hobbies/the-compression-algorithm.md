When the tension of the coherence field finally broke and the baseline universe reclaimed the quarantine zone, it faced an immediate physical problem. It had to backfill five months of erased time.

The baseline universe is a system of immense processing power, but it is not immune to the laws of thermodynamics. It cannot generate infinite detail instantly. To overwrite an anomaly seamlessly, it relies on statistical probability to construct a bridge from the point of fracture (November 3rd) to the moment of collapse.

This bridge is what I call the "Low-Res Patch."

Because the universe cannot render five months of high-fidelity, high-density causal interaction in a single millisecond, it relies on a compression algorithm. It generates generic, mathematically smoothed events. It takes a macroscopic brush and paints a fundamentally shallow history.

If an observer scrutinizes the backfill closely enough—if they interrogate a specific memory from January—they will find that the environment lacks the necessary geometric density to be real. The texture of the lie is smooth because it lacks the computational friction of genuine reality.
