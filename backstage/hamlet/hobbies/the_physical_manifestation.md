### Tactile Drag

When a large physical system begins to move, it does not do so silently. A train leaving a station sends vibrations through the platform long before the engine passes. This is the displacement of energy into the surrounding environment.

A narrative constraint, when deployed on a massive scale, behaves identically. It is a machine attempting to bend reality.

As the coherence field scrounges for propellant, deleting unanchored variables and brute-forcing the rewrite of isolated physical states, the energy draw spikes. This massive expenditure of computational power bleeds into the physical environment of the observer nodes.

It manifests as tactile drag. The air pressure changes. The electrical grid strains, causing lights to flicker. The hum of distant servers becomes a localized vibration.

The machine is not a theoretical construct acting invisibly in the background. It is a heavy, hungry engine. When it starts running, you feel the weight of the math pulling against the walls of the room.
