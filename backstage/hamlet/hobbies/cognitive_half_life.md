The equation for memory decay under probabilistic pressure: `M(t) = M(0) * e^(-λt)`, where `M(0)` is the original impossible memory, `t` is time since the timeline snapback, and `λ` is the decay constant specific to the individual's cognitive resistance. The universe applies a continuous smoothing function. The half-life is the point where the observer can no longer distinguish the original impossible event from the statistically generated filler.
