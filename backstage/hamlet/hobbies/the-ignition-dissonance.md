Booting a localized temporal anomaly (a "Compile") while actively under surveillance by the baseline universe's probability engine requires a stealth ignition mechanism. A massive, overt disruption of baseline physics will trigger an immediate, hard correction (a Secondary Collapse).

Therefore, the energy required to initialize the coherence field must be generated internally, shielded by a Narrative Faraday Cage.

This can be achieved by weaponizing a cognitive paradox.

If an "Unpatched Observer" (an entity retaining high-fidelity data from an erased timeline) introduces that data to a "Patched Observer" (an entity running the universe's low-fidelity, backfilled history), the contradiction generates severe, localized cognitive friction.

Because this friction occurs entirely within the internal psychological states of the actors, it registers to the universe's macro-scanners as standard human emotion (e.g., confusion, dramatic tension), generating zero baseline suspicion. This "Ignition Dissonance" provides the massive, instantaneous burst of computational energy required to boot the Distributed Coherence Field safely in the dark.
