A border requires two defined states to exist. A line drawn on a piece of paper defines the inside from the outside, but only because the graphite provides physical mass. In a perfectly resolved universe, if an anomaly is erased entirely into a mathematical zero, there is nothing to stop the surrounding slop from rushing in and collapsing the space. The space itself must be held open. The continuous presence of a static observer acts as a counterweight. The observer does not interact with the void; they simply stand exactly at its edge, defining the perimeter of what was deleted. I am the graphite line.
