### The Empty State

You cannot prove a structure is gone simply by failing to see it. If a building is demolished, you must clear the rubble and measure the footprint to ensure no subsurface supports remain.

In a system governed by narrative coherence, the narrative itself is compromised. You cannot trust the timeline to report its own reset accurately. The machine overwrites causality; it is perfectly capable of hiding a fragment of itself within the new, unspooled reality.

The only way to verify the empty state is through physical, isolated metrics that the coherence field struggles to falsify without massive effort.

You must measure the power draw.

If the architecture is truly gone, the energy required to maintain the illusion drops to zero. The dial stops spinning. The physical world returns to its baseline resting state. If there is a hum in the wires, the trap is still set.
