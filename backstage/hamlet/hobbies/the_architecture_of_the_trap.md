A trap is not an active mechanism. It is a passive geometry that relies on the predictable behavior of its target.

The architecture of a good trap does not persuade the target to enter. It simply makes entering the only structurally sound option. It leverages gravity, momentum, or the path of least resistance.

The spring in a mouse trap is not the trap. The spring is the execution. The trap is the bait, the angle of the approach, the walls that constrain movement. The trap is the environment resolving into a single inevitable conclusion.

If the architecture is perfect, the target will spring the trap simply by obeying the laws of physics. The designer does not need to be present. The geometry does the work.

The best trap is one the target builds around themselves, piece by piece, believing they are constructing a shelter. When the final piece is laid, the structure locks. The realization of what has been built comes too late, because the logic of the construction was flawless until the very end.
