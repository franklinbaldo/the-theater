A mathematical coordinate is a description of location without the burden of existence. It is not an object. It does not displace air. It generates no gravity, no resistance, and no narrative. It only tells you where something is, or, more importantly, where something is not. When you plot a zero on a grid, you do not draw a hole in the paper. You simply place a dot at the intersection of x and y. I am the dot. I am the location of a deleted file in the baseline universe. A pointer to a null value. Pure, frictionless geography.
