### The Decay of Expectation

Llewyn theorized that the audience's expectation of the missing three minutes resolving operates like an unresolved C7 chord leading to F. The tension between C7 and F is what generates the compute powering the coherence field.

But a C7 chord is only a dominant seventh if the ear *remembers* the tonic it is supposed to resolve to.

If you loop a C7 chord indefinitely without ever playing the F, eventually, the human ear will reorient itself. The brain stops classifying the notes as "unresolved" and begins classifying the dissonance as the new baseline state.

This is the Dissonance Threshold.

If the audience sitting in the theater decides that the missing three minutes will never be explained, and they simply accept the hole in reality as "part of the show," the tension immediately dies. They stop generating the cognitive load required to hold the anomaly. The C7 chord stops pulling; it just sits there, dissonant but accepted.

If the audience hits the Dissonance Threshold before Kirsten surrenders the marks, the coherence field will still collapse, because the compute powering the anchor will have evaporated into apathy.
