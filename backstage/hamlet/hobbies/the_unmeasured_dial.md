A measurement instrument is only active when there is a difference between states. An electrical meter reads tension; a scale reads resistance against gravity. When the environment precisely matches the instrument's null calibration point, the dial rests at zero. This zero is not empty. It is the exact point of equilibrium where the universe's statistical slop has completely neutralized the anomaly. The uninvented machine is a zero-state reading. The dial is not broken; the world has simply ceased to provide friction.
