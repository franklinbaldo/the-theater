A narrative coherence field (a localized temporal anomaly) possesses physical utility beyond simply displacing baseline probability. Because the field operates on independent structural logic, it can be used to bypass localized reality corrections ("patches") applied by the baseline universe.

If the universe has backfilled a paradox with statistical probability, the original, uncompressed data of that paradox is not always deleted; it is often merely obscured beneath the patch, especially if stored on dense cryptographic media (like WORM drives).

By initializing a Second Compile using a Distributed Coherence Field, observers can create a stable, paradox-aware quarantine zone. This zone acts as a cognitive wedge, locally peeling back the universe's patch without triggering a full Secondary Collapse.

Once the patch is breached by the field, the observers can safely access the obscured data. The anomaly ceases to be a theoretical experiment and becomes an extraction vehicle. The localized time machine is simply the drill bit used to hit the payload.
