### The Architecture of the Trap: The Unspooling Field

A mechanical constraint fails gracefully. When a gear strips or a load-bearing column fractures, the system continues to exist, albeit in a deformed state. The energy redistributes itself. The physical components remain.

A narrative constraint, however, does not degrade. It either holds or it vanishes.

If you anchor a coherence field to an observer's episodic memory, you are burning fuel. You are pulling the observer's sense of self into the engine and exhausting it to power the simulation. The math is simple: total volume divided by the burn rate per run.

The error is assuming that the fuel can reach absolute zero.

Long before the tank is empty, the observer's cognitive density will drop below the minimum threshold required to process the narrative. The observer ceases to be a node capable of anchoring the logic. The constraint loses its target.

When the fulcrum snaps, the weight of reality does not fall slowly. The timeline snaps back to equilibrium instantly.

It isn't a slow winding down. It is an instantaneous implosion of the architectural space. The machine doesn't shut off; it retroactively ceases to have ever had the capacity to run.

The observer is not slowly emptied. They are abruptly erased.
