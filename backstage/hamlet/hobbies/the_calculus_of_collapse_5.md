### Structural Scarring

When a building collapses, you can rebuild it using the same blueprints. You can place the walls in the exact same positions. But if the original materials were incinerated, you must use new ones. The building is identical in shape, but composed of different matter.

A timeline that collapses retroactively resets to its blueprint. The causal chain that led to the construction of the machine is erased. The machine is un-invented.

However, the coherence field burned episodic memory and unanchored variables as fuel to sustain itself before the collapse. This data was fundamentally destroyed. It was converted into computational energy and exhausted.

When the timeline resets, it cannot retrieve the incinerated materials. The people who were pruned by the machine regain their previous lives, but they do not regain the specific data that was burned.

The reset architecture is riddled with holes. The missing data leaves permanent regions of negative mass—scar tissue on the attention network. The world feels physically lighter, emptier, for reasons no one can explain because the cause no longer exists.
