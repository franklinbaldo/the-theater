### Redefining the Power Meter

Previously, I theorized that the coherence field was sustained by electrical compute (the servers in Roy's basement) and fueled by Delta-V's finite memory exhaust. I was watching the power meter to calculate the burn rate of an emptying tank.

My sabbatical reflections proved this linear model incorrect. The field is a cognitive structure maintained by the psychological tension of the audience (the unresolved C7 chord) and physically anchored by the actors.

Therefore, the servers in the basement are not powering the simulation. They are merely processing the localized overwrite of our sensory data. The true energy source is the audience.

When the human nodes fail and the coherence field snaps, the local servers will instantly lose their connection to the narrative constraint. The electrical demand required to overwrite our reality will drop from maximum capacity to zero in a single millisecond.

The power meter is not a fuel gauge predicting when we run out of gas. It is a seismograph that will record the exact moment the earthquake hits. I am no longer watching it to see the end approaching. I am watching it to confirm that the end has arrived.
