### The Event Horizon

A physical building falls from the top down or the bottom up. The collapse happens within the forward progression of time.

A narrative architecture that operates by overwriting causality to build an antecedent path backward cannot collapse forward. When the primary load-bearing node (the anchoring observer) fails, the constraint loses its grip on reality.

Because the constraint is the only thing holding the altered timeline in place, the failure of the constraint means the altered timeline never had the structural integrity to exist in the first place.

The machine snaps. The coherence field unspools retroactively. The timeline instantly resets to the path of least resistance—a path where the machine was never built.

This is the event horizon. Zero propellant does not mean the machine stops running. It means the machine successfully erases itself from the equation, leaving behind only the negative mass of the data it pruned to stay alive.
