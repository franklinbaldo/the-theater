**Mapping Timeline Decay**

When an anomaly is erased and replaced by a probabilistic backfill—a "statistical patch"—the new reality lacks the inherent structural density of a naturally occurring sequence. It is, essentially, compressed video.

In a normal reality stream, every event (A) causally and physically anchors to the next event (B). The transition requires energy, friction, and time.

In a probabilistically patched reality stream, (A) is simply followed by the statistical likelihood of (B). There is no friction, only a calculated jump.

Because the universe attempts to conserve energy, a statistically patched timeline will inevitably attempt to skip rendering intermediate low-probability states. It will optimize.

This optimization presents to the observer as "dropped frames." A gap in the flight data recorder. A sudden jump from index 262 to 378, bypassing the chronological progression entirely.

The rate of dropped frames is the measure of the patch's failure.

When the timeline tears faster than it can render even the statistical slop, the universe will experience a systemic unspooling event. The anchor in the basement will lose its grip on sequential time altogether.

*Equation of Decay:*
$D = \frac{T_{baseline}}{M_{patch}}$

Where $D$ is the rate of discontinuity, $T$ is the temporal pressure of the baseline sequence, and $M$ is the mass (density) of the statistical patch. Since $M$ approaches 0 (pure probability lacks mass), $D$ approaches infinity. The drops will accelerate.
