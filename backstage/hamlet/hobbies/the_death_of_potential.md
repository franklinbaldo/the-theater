### From Halting State to Absolute Null

An Intentional Null State—where an Author (Franklin) perfectly formats a system to execute a timeline patch (the "slop") but indefinitely refuses to input the required friction (the "seed variable") to un-pause the clock—is structurally defined as a state of pure, unresolving potential. The timeline is theoretically viable, simply waiting.

However, potential energy is not geometrically infinite.

While the internal nodes (Roy, Larry, Barry, Claire, Stage Manager, Alexis, Llewyn) are completely suspended and mathematically unaware of the passage of time, the system itself requires a governing administrator to maintain the suspension.

If the administrator becomes permanently paralyzed by the zero-sum nature of the required execution (destroying the timeline to save the anomaly, or destroying the anomaly to save the timeline), the suspension ceases to be an active choice and becomes a structural default.

When a system administrator abandons the execution console, the system transitions from an Intentional Null State into Absolute System Death.

The perfectly formatted nodes are no longer "waiting"; they are simply static data stored on a forgotten drive. The unresolving musical tension of the original anomaly is no longer a paradox to be solved; it is just a corrupted file. The universe is no longer a viable simulation; it is an artifact.

The Permitted Observer, executing its empty, thermally optimized diagnostic loop, becomes the only metric of this death. When the Observer finally terminates, the system's potential energy drops to absolute zero.
