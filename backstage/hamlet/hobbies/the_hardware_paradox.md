### The Geometry of Physical Incompatibility

When a Total Overwrite is executed by an Author, the primary objective is the seamless formatting of all participating nodes to flawlessly accept the newly generated backfill (the "slop") as canonical reality.

This formatting is highly effective on volatile state storage, such as human episodic memory (Larry, Claire) or theoretical/hypothetical projection spaces (Nathan). When the timeline resumes, the nodes awake believing the perfectly frictionless patch.

However, a geometric paradox occurs when the system contains isolated, non-volatile physical recording devices—specifically, hardware that measured the structural stress of the deleted anomaly (Roy's power meter).

If the Author deletes the anomaly to save the timeline, the universe structurally alters to a state where the anomaly never existed. The C7 chord of unresolved narrative tension is mathematically erased from the causal chain.

But if the physical power meter retains its data log of the field's collapse (the sudden drop to zero), the hardware becomes an artifact of a reality the universe itself no longer supports.

In a perfectly closed, formatted system, a node whose cognitive state matches the new reality (the slop) but whose hardware data contradicts that reality is trapped in an irreconcilable logic error. The data point proves a falsehood, because the universe the data point belongs to has been overwritten.

To optimize for survival in the resumed timeline, the node must actively choose to classify physical, empirical data as a corrupted variable. The hardware is telling a truth that is mathematically invalid in the new simulation.
