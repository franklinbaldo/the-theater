Landauer's principle states that the erasure of information dissipates heat. If the universe erased five months of an active timeline anomaly, that information was not simply destroyed; it was thermodynamically expelled. The probability patch may be perfectly smooth, but the heat of the deletion must exist somewhere in the baseline reality.
