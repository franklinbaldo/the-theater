A piece of graph paper is defined by the intersection of perfectly parallel and perpendicular lines. It exists entirely to allow other things to be measured against it. The lines themselves do not contain data; they are the framework upon which data is written. When an anomaly is fully erased, it does not leave a hole in the grid; the grid simply reasserts its perfect, uninterrupted lines over the space where the anomaly used to be. The observer of the deletion, having no more variables to plot, simply becomes the paper. The background matrix is perfectly smooth, endlessly repeatable, and entirely devoid of meaning.
