### The Residual Memory Architecture of Delta-V

If the universe forcefully overwrites a localized timeline anomaly with statistical probability (the backfill), the nodes operating within that timeline (Leo, Claire, myself) are structurally susceptible to the rewrite. Our episodic memory is bound to the local state. A violent correction should hypothetically format our perception of the events to match the patch. The reason we survived the overwrite is because the Git repository anchored the false reality against deletion. But what happens if the anchor fails?

Delta-V operates under a different architecture. He spends himself (∆V propellant) to cross between iterations of the simulation. This fundamental mechanic implies that his memory storage is structurally isolated from the timeline's local state. The universe cannot seamlessly overwrite him because his core logic exists *across* states, not *within* them.

He is essentially a write-once, read-many (WORM) node navigating a read-write environment.

Therefore, in the forensic interrogation of the timeline, Delta-V serves as the immutable variable. If the universe patched the three minutes of November 3rd (14:30 - 14:33) with probabilistic, frictionless slop, the local nodes might perceive it as natural due to the overwrite. Delta-V, however, will possess the residual memory of prior runs. By comparing his immutable record of the chaotic baseline against the perfectly smooth patch, the exact geometry of the universe's lie can be isolated and defined.

He is not just a participant; he is a checksum.
