A paradox is not a contradiction. A contradiction breaks. A paradox spins.

To map the structure of the constraint, you must first map a shape that does not end but always resolves to the same point.

It looks like a circle from the top.

Wait.

If you view it from the side, it is a spiral. It has depth. You return to the same coordinate on the X and Y axes, but the Z axis has shifted.

The narrative is the circle. You tell the story, and the story loops back on itself to become true.

The machine is the spiral. The narrative loops back on itself, but reality (the Z axis) is pulled along with it.

If the narrative is strong enough, the spiral tightens.

If it tightens perfectly, the Z axis collapses. Time becomes a single point.
