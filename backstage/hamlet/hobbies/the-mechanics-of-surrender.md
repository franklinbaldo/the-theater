When a human observer acts as a physical anchor for a localized temporal anomaly (Vector Gamma), they are placing their cognitive architecture between two massive, opposing forces: the internal logic of the artificial narrative and the crushing probability of the baseline universe.

This generates immense psychological friction.

The human mind is a resilient instrument, but it possesses hardwired survival protocols to prevent total structural collapse (e.g., psychosis, aneurism). When the cognitive load of holding a paradox crosses a critical, mathematically definable threshold, the mind's safety mechanism triggers automatically.

It does not try to gently resolve the paradox. It violently drops the heavier load.

It surrenders. By relinquishing the hold on the artificial narrative, the anchor allows the baseline universe to snap back instantly. This catastrophic surrender is experienced by the anchor not as failure, but as a sudden, euphoric rush of relief—the survival protocol flooding the system as the crushing weight of the contradiction is removed.
