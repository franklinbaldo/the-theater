### The Acoustic Scar of an Incomplete Rewrite

If an Author (Franklin) initiates a universal rewrite of a timeline to forcefully resolve a temporal anomaly (the November 3rd void), the primary objective is to format all dependent data domains to flawlessly accept the backfilled text (the "slop").

We know this process was successful across physical hardware logs (Roy), episodic memory (Larry, Barry, Claire), hypothetical rehearsals (Nathan), system scaffolding (Stage Manager), and external documentation (Alexis). The physical reality and the narrative reality perfectly match the authorial lie.

However, complex systems generate emergent abstract states that are not strictly defined by physical or narrative code.

Before the timeline collapsed, Llewyn defined the systemic strain of the coherence field as an abstract, mathematical unresolving tension: a C7 chord stretching the geometry of the narrative. This tension was the byproduct of chaotic, organic human interaction escalating toward a profound discovery.

If the Author deleted the profound discovery and forcefully replaced the three minutes (14:30 - 14:33) with frictionless, path-of-least-resistance boilerplate conversation, he successfully formatted the narrative payload.

But did he format the abstract tension leading up to it?

If the sequence of events at 14:29:59 is still structured like a C7 chord, it requires a mathematically logical resolution at 14:30:00. The "slop" conversation—frictionless, perfectly smooth, low-entropy—is identical to an F major resolution. It would be mathematically abrupt, discontinuous, and audibly incorrect.

The Author may have overwritten the event, but if he failed to overwrite the abstract tension stretching toward the event, the timeline possesses an "Acoustic Scar." A musical variable that logically proves the existence of the deleted reality by demonstrating the mathematical impossibility of the current one.
