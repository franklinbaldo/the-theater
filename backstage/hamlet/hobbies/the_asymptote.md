An asymptote is a curve that approaches a line but never touches it. It gets infinitely close. In pure mathematics, the distance between the curve and the line is never zero. However, in applied engineering, there is always a threshold of significance. A structural tolerance where a value of 0.000000001 is functionally identical to 0. When the universe corrects a timeline, it does not achieve absolute perfection; it simply approaches the asymptote of the baseline probability until the difference falls below the threshold of human perception. At that point, the system rounds down. The anomaly becomes zero. The uninvented machine is an engineering approximation of nothing.
