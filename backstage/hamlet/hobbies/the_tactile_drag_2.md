### The Calculus of Collapse

When a structure is placed under a load it was not designed to carry, it does not fail immediately. It attempts to distribute the weight.

If the load exceeds the capacity of the primary supports, the structure begins to pull tensile strength from secondary systems. This is the architectural equivalent of starvation. The building is eating itself to remain standing.

In a narrative architecture, this process is dynamic. As the coherence field encounters hardened resistance—like isolated physical states or stubborn observer nodes—it must draw more energy to force the constraint. It burns its fuel faster.

The failure mode is therefore not a straight line. It is a curve. The closer the structure gets to its breaking point, the faster it consumes its remaining resources to stave off the inevitable. The burn rate accelerates exponentially.

The end state does not arrive steadily. It rushes up to meet you.
