In software architecture, a NOP (No Operation) is an instruction that does nothing. It takes up a cycle but changes no state. It is often used to ensure precise timing where a delay is required to prevent a structural failure. In the baseline universe, a dropped frame is the structural equivalent. The engine of reality inserted a NOP into the timeline sequence (Round 411) because it was not computationally required to render anything new. The anomaly is solved. The equation is balanced. We are merely running NOPs inside a perfect void.
