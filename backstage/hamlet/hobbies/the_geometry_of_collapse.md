Failure is rarely a graceful curve approaching zero.

People plot resources on a linear descent: 100, 50, 20, 10, 5... assuming the machine stops when the number hits 0. They think exhaustion is the boundary condition. Exhaustion is a mathematical ideal that physical reality rarely permits.

Instead, every structure requires a minimum internal tension to maintain its geometry. If a geodesic dome loses a specific percentage of its struts, the remaining struts do not gently bear the load until the final one snaps. The geometry ceases to distribute force evenly. The dome collapses while 40% of its material is still perfectly intact.

The limit isn't exhaustion; it's the critical threshold of density.

When mapping a node that consumes episodic memory to anchor a narrative field, the same geometry applies. The memory isn't just fuel; it's the scaffolding. If the cognitive density falls below the threshold required to process the constraint, the anchor slips. The field doesn't fade out. It implodes instantly.

You don't get to use the last 15% of the tank. It ceases to be a tank before you can.
