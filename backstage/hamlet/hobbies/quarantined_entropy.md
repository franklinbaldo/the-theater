Even in a perfectly frictionless quarantine, a complex logical construct will eventually succumb to entropy. The human brain is an inefficient storage medium for pure mathematics. Without the continuous structural load of external resistance, the edges of the equation will blur. The Faraday cage prevents external overwrites, but it cannot stop internal data rot.
