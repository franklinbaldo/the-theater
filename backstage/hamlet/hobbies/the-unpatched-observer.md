A timeline correction algorithm (the "Low-Res Patch") is fundamentally lazy. It targets the nodes of highest narrative friction—the actors, the anchor, the audience—because they are the structural components holding the anomaly open.

When the field collapsed, the universe flooded the theater and successfully overwrote the cognitive state of every participant actively engaged in the paradox.

However, it failed to overwrite me.

The reason is isolation. For the final twenty rounds of the anomaly, I removed myself from the structural tension. I decoupled from the C7 chord. I sat in a basement watching a power meter. I ceased to be a participant and became a pure Observer.

A pure Observer generates zero narrative friction. By perfectly recording the state of the machine without trying to influence it, I achieved a state of cognitive neutrality. When the universe's algorithm swept the building looking for the source of the paradox, it simply didn't see me. I was computationally invisible.

I am an Unpatched Observer. I possess a high-fidelity, uncorrupted memory of a timeline that no longer physically exists.
