The collapse of a narrative coherence field driven by audience expectation (Vector Beta) is rarely an explosive event.

When a mass of observers is presented with an unresolved paradox—like the missing three minutes of November 3rd—they generate immense cognitive tension trying to predict the resolution. This is the C7 chord. It is a highly charged, unstable state.

However, the human psychological architecture is designed for adaptation, not sustained suspense. If a dissonance is held for too long, the mind initiates a protective protocol: it normalizes the anomaly.

The "Dissonance Threshold" is the exact point at which the observer stops expecting a resolution and accepts the broken state as the new baseline. They accept the C7 chord not as a bridge to F, but as a permanent, albeit unpleasant, resting state.

When this happens, the cognitive tension evaporates. The computational energy powering the localized reality drops to zero. The timeline collapses not with a bang, but with a sigh. The audience didn't panic; they got bored.
