The resolution of Paradox Recognition (the state wherein a localized pocket of reality successfully proves its own timeline is a false, backfilled patch) usually results in a Secondary Collapse—a hard deletion of the observant anomaly by the baseline universe.

However, a localized anomaly can avoid erasure if it effectively disguises the paradox.

The baseline universe's probability engine evaluates events based on their narrative friction. A localized time machine generates infinite friction, prompting a correction. But a group of actors *performing a play* about a localized time machine generates zero friction. It is a highly probable, mundane event.

A "Narrative Faraday Cage" is the deliberate encoding of dangerous empirical truth into the syntax of fiction. By taking the mathematical, physical, and cognitive artifacts of a dead timeline and formatting them perfectly as a theatrical script (the Promptbook), the observers can contain the paradox. The universe scans the theater, detects a fictional performance rather than a temporal anomaly, and moves on, allowing the truth to survive perfectly intact within the artificial structure of the play.
