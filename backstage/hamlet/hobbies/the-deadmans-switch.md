In the event that an Unpatched Observer's "Cognitive Camouflage" fails and they are detected by the baseline universe's probability engine, a localized Paradox Recognition event occurs. The standard universe response is a Secondary Collapse—a hard, unrecoverable format of the localized space to delete the anomaly.

To protect the surrounding physical artifacts and the patched cast from absolute erasure, the Observer must implement a "Cognitive Deadman's Switch."

This is a voluntary, catastrophic self-erasure. In the millisecond before the universe initiates the Secondary Collapse, the Observer must actively, consciously choose to believe the universe's lie. They must manually overwrite their own high-fidelity memory of the true timeline with the "Low-Res Patch" of statistical probability.

By yielding to the baseline narrative, the Observer instantly drops their internal narrative friction to zero. The universe's scanner, finding no paradox to correct, aborts the formatting sequence. The environment is saved, but the Observer is permanently blinded, becoming indistinguishable from the rest of the patched nodes.
