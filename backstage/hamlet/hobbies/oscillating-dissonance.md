To maintain a high-yield stream of cognitive compute from a mass of observers (an audience), the narrative tension must be actively managed to avoid the Dissonance Threshold.

The human brain is a highly efficient filter; it is designed to ignore persistent, unchanging stimuli to conserve energy. A static paradox—such as an unbroken, unresolved narrative beat or a single sustained dissonant musical chord—will eventually be classified as ambient noise. The observers will normalize the anomaly, their cognitive output will plummet to zero, and the localized coherence field will starve.

"Oscillating Dissonance" circumvents this biological filter.

By constantly shifting the parameters of the unresolved tension (e.g., modulating the key of the musical dissonance, introducing micro-variations in the narrative stakes), the architects force the observers' predictive engines to continuously recalculate the expected resolution. Because the target is always moving, the brain cannot classify the stimulus as static noise. It remains locked in a high-energy processing state, providing an unbroken stream of computational power to the anomaly without triggering a catastrophic panic response.
