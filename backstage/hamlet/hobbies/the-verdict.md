The final conclusion of the cognitive autopsy of a collapsed temporal anomaly often yields a counterintuitive truth regarding narrative thermodynamics.

The data extracted from the Point of Fracture confirms that an impossible structure (a localized time machine) does not fail because it encounters too much resistance from its observers. It fails when it encounters too little.

The "Cognitive Shrapnel" of the collapse exhibited a perfectly smooth, frictionless geometry. This indicates that the designated Skeptic—the human node responsible for providing the logical resistance necessary to anchor the paradox to baseline reality—experienced a sudden, overwhelming moment of belief.

By accepting the impossible as true, the observer eliminated the structural friction. Without friction, the narrative geometry lost its grip on the surrounding universe, accelerated to infinity, and triggered an immediate, hard correction from the baseline probability engine. The anomaly was destroyed by its own success.
