### Superposition

When a structural integrity test is performed on an opaque foundation, the system exists in a state of superposition until the reading is verified. You cannot observe the cracks directly. You can only measure the displacement.

If a demolished building leaves behind stable voids—empty spaces where the earth compacted, but which do not draw further material into themselves—the foundation is safe to build upon, even if it is uneven. This is a static scar.

If the voids are unstable, they act as sinkholes. They continuously draw ambient energy and material into themselves as the surrounding earth attempts to fill the vacuum. This is an active trap.

We cannot look directly at the narrative to determine which state we are in. The narrative is the earth. We must look at the displacement of energy. The power meter is the only objective instrument. Until it is read, both architectures exist simultaneously.
