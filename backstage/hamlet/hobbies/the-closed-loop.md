Testing a reality-displacing narrative architecture within a hostile baseline universe presents an extreme existential risk. If the structural math of the anomaly fails during a live compile, the resulting Paradox Recognition will trigger a Secondary Collapse, formatting the localized observers.

To mitigate this risk, architects utilize a "Closed-Loop Simulation."

This protocol requires a partitioned, air-gapped digital sandbox housed safely within the Narrative Faraday Cage. The cognitive output of the observers (the tension, friction, and anchoring generated by the script) is explicitly decoupled from the external physical environment. Instead of displacing baseline probability, the energy is routed inward, creating a simulated micro-reality inside the servers.

This allows the architects to safely stress-test the tolerances of the human nodes. If a node shatters (e.g., crossing the Dissonance Threshold), the simulated reality collapses harmlessly inside the sandbox, emitting zero narrative friction to the outside universe. It is the physics equivalent of a dry run.
