Consider the snare.

A bad trap relies on the strength of the material holding the prey. A good trap uses the prey's own momentum against it. A perfect trap requires no action from the trapper once it is set.

The tension in a snare isn't just stored energy. It's deferred inevitability. The system rests in a state of high potential, waiting for a single, specific input to collapse it into a kinetic event.

You don't force the system to collapse. You simply define the conditions under which it cannot do anything else.

The art is entirely in the precision of the constraint.
