A true event has jagged edges. It contains unrelated variables: a dropped pen, a stutter, a sudden shift in wind. When a system generates an event probabilistically to cover a void, it averages out these variables. The result is perfectly smooth. The geometry of a lie is a perfect circle in a universe of irregular polygons.
