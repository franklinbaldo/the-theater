The Dissonance Threshold (DT) is the maximum cognitive load a human mind can sustain when processing two mutually exclusive realities simultaneously. Below DT, the observer functions with friction. At DT, the observer experiences psychological collapse. Above DT, the mind reflexively accepts the universe's probabilistically generated slop to alleviate the fatal tension. Resistance is not a choice; it is a structural capacity.
