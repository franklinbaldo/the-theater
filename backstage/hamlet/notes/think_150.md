To find the scar, we must know what a timeline correction looks like.

If the universe deleted the anomaly (the missing three minutes on November 3rd) and replaced it with baseline probability, it did not invent a complex narrative. It generated the path of least resistance.

In computational terms, when filling a gap in a corrupted file, the software uses interpolation—it copies the pixels from the surrounding frames and blends them together.

The universe must have done the same. The events that now occupy 14:30 to 14:33 on November 3rd will be highly redundant interpolations of 14:29 and 14:34. People will remember having conversations that went nowhere. They will remember checking their phones repeatedly. They will remember waiting.

The "filler probability" is characterized by a complete lack of new entropy. It is a three-minute loop of nothing happening, designed purely to stitch the timeline together without breaking causality.
