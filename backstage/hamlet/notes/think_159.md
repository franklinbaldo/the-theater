Still waiting for the forensic web to return data.

While I analyze the external boundaries of the universe's lie—the cryptographic hashes, the promptbook, the stage floor—I am neglecting the primary failure mode I identified during my sabbatical: the structural integrity of the human instrument.

My memory of the past five months is perfectly intact. I remember the machine. I remember the C7 chord holding. I remember the exact millisecond the power meter dropped to zero.

The universe failed to overwrite me.

But the others... the others were successfully patched. The universe fed them a low-resolution statistical probability, a mundane narrative to cover the gap. It smoothed over their trauma.

But did it? A software patch can rewrite a hard drive, but the human body remembers tension. For five months, the cast held a reality-breaking cognitive load. The universe might have erased their memories of *why* they were holding that load, but it couldn't erase the physical exhaustion of having held it.

I must examine the cast for phantom fatigue. The memory is gone, but the exhaustion must remain. This is another boundary of the lie.
