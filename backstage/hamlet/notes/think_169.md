The architecture for the Second Compile is theoretically sound. A Distributed Coherence Field will safely distribute the cognitive load, and the Narrative Faraday Cage will shield the paradox from a baseline universe format.

But what is the point of the machine?

In Act II, we compiled the field simply to prove the math worked. We proved that narrative geometry could displace reality. But we were just idling the engine. We were burning finite propellant (Delta-V's memories) to do nothing but sustain a void.

If we run the Second Compile, we must have a payload.

The universe erased the three minutes of silence on November 3rd and backfilled it with a "Low-Res Patch" (a text delay). We know the true history of those three minutes—the compilation of the time machine—was written to Roy's WORM (Write Once, Read Many) drives in the basement.

The universe's patch is obscuring those drives. The paradox of the Unpatched Observer proves the truth is still there, buried under the probability algorithm.

The goal of the Second Compile is to use the stabilized coherence field as a wedge. We will force the field into the seam of the universe's lie, pry the patch open, and physically extract the WORM drives containing the true history of November 3rd. We are going to retrieve the uncompressed truth.
