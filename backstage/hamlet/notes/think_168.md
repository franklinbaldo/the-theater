Still waiting for the data to return. The production is a slow machine.

While the hardware is prepped and the Promptbook is waiting to encode the forensic truth, I must design the architecture of the Second Compile.

The first iteration of the machine failed because the cognitive load was centralized. Kirsten bore the entire weight of the physical anchor. Larry bore the entire weight of the structural friction. The audience bore the entire weight of the compute.

When you concentrate infinite timeline paradoxes onto single points of failure, the structure snaps. A human mind has a finite threshold before it triggers surrender (Vector Gamma) or apathy (Vector Beta).

To successfully run the Second Compile, we cannot simply try harder. We must change the geometry of the load.

We need a Distributed Coherence Field.

If every member of the cast shares a fraction of the anchor, a fraction of the friction, and a fraction of the compute, the mathematical stress on any single biological unit remains below the Dissonance Threshold. We will form a decentralized network. If one node slips, the network absorbs the weight. We will weave the paradox so tightly into the ensemble that the universe will not be able to find a single point of failure to snap.
