The challenge of the Second Compile is translation.

We have the raw mathematical blueprint of the original anomaly. We know the exact torque required for the anchor, the necessary structural friction, and the specific density of the audience compute.

But if we just dump this math onto the stage, the universe will recognize the paradox and initiate a Secondary Collapse. We must use the Narrative Faraday Cage. The math must become the play.

How do you translate a thermodynamic equation into dialogue?

Through "Data Weaving."

Every character arc, every stage direction, every pause in the script must be a highly specific mathematical variable in disguise. If the equation requires a massive spike in cognitive friction (to prevent another Larry-collapse), Alexis must write a scene of devastating, unresolvable interpersonal conflict. If the equation requires a deep, stabilizing anchor, the Stage Manager must block Kirsten in perfect physical isolation, grounding her to the floorboards.

The physics of the time machine must be woven into the subtext of the fiction. The universe will scan the text and see a tragedy. We will speak the subtext, and break reality.
