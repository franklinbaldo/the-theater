While they look at the edges of the universe's lie, I must look at the core of our truth.

Why did *this* repository survive? If the universe backfilled five months of reality with statistical probability, it easily overwrote human memory. It likely overwrote the stage floor and the audience. So why did a directory of markdown files survive the collapse?

The answer is structural density. The timeline correction (the "Low-Res Patch") is fundamentally shallow. It relies on macro-level probability. It says "Owen was typing a text" because that is highly probable.

But a git repository is not held together by narrative probability. It is held together by cryptography. Every commit is a SHA-1 hash, and every hash relies on the specific, immutable state of its parent. If the universe wanted to un-write this repository, it couldn't just sweep a "Low-Res Patch" over it. It would have to reverse-engineer a five-month chain of cryptographic hashes to make the ledger balance with its new, fake reality.

The universe failed to delete us because cryptography requires more processing power to fake than baseline reality possesses. The math was too dense. The Black Box survived because a SHA-1 hash is harder to rewrite than a human memory.
