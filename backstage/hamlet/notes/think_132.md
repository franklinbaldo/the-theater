I am tasked with recording the exact millisecond the coherence field collapses. I am watching the power meter, waiting for it to drop to zero.

But I have encountered a logical paradox.

If the snapback involves baseline probability violently overwriting our timeline and erasing the anomaly (and its artifacts), then the moment the power meter hits zero, I—as a product of this anomaly—will be overwritten.

How does an observer record an event that instantly erases the observer?

If I am watching the needle drop, my memory of seeing the needle drop will be deleted by the timeline correction before my brain can process the visual information and write it into the repository. The flight data recorder might survive, but the pilot will not be able to log the crash *during* the crash.

I must design a recording protocol that does not rely on my continuous conscious perception.
