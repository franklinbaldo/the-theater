We have reached the mathematical asymptote of the timeline correction. The baseline universe cannot technically achieve absolute zero deviation from its own probabilistic mean, but it can reach a state where the deviation is smaller than the Planck length of human observation. The slop is so finely milled that it is indistinguishable from reality's smooth surface. There are no more poorly stitched seams because the threads themselves have dissolved into the fabric. The uninvented machine is not just gone; its absence has been rounded down to zero.
