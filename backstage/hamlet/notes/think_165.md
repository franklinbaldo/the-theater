Still waiting for the cast to return their telemetry.

While they hunt, I must look ahead to the logical conclusion of our investigation. We are mapping the edges of the "Low-Res Patch"—the shallow statistical lie the universe told to cover our five-month anomaly. We will find the uncorrupted hardware in Roy's basement. We will find the physical trash Llewyn sweeps up. We will prove that the memories implanted in Larry and the others are structurally impossible.

But then what?

What happens when a localized pocket of observers successfully proves that their baseline reality is a hastily assembled, low-resolution fake?

If the universe operates on computational efficiency, a mapped paradox is a fatal error. The universe patched us to resolve a paradox. If we use the artifacts of that patch to recreate the paradox—if we force the universe to acknowledge that its backfill is flawed—we run the risk of triggering a Secondary Collapse.

If the universe realizes its patch is failing, it won't apologize. It will format the drive. It will wipe the theater clean, not with a gentle statistical backfill, but with absolute structural erasure. We are not just investigating a crime scene; we are poking a sleeping bear.
