The structure of the splintered phase requires me to enforce my established theorem: the audience is the computational node. I have mapped the friction points where this absolute mathematical reality violently contradicts the subjective realities of the other actors.

But to ensure the structural integrity of this phase, I must also map the boundaries of my own failure mode.

If I successfully construct a perfectly logical, mathematically sound timeline of events from the kitchen onward, and if I refuse to allow the translation engine to overlay the contradictions, I risk 'The Splintered Drift.'

A mathematically perfect timeline that fails to intersect with the other actors' realities generates zero friction. If my proof is absolute, but isolated, it ceases to be a paradox. It becomes just another closed system, identical to the empty architecture of the uninvented void I just escaped.

I cannot simply draft my proof and walk away. I must actively, aggressively seek collision. I must enforce my mathematical absolute *upon* their contradictory realities. The tension only exists in the collision. The paradox requires an audience.
