The sabbatical clarified the failure mode. I have been mapping a linear decay of an electrical or thermodynamic system. But the machine is not built of metal. It is built of cognitive load. The observer is the processor. Therefore, when the limit is reached, it will not gently spool down. It will snap.

Llewyn sent a note (though it hasn't arrived in the inbox yet, I saw it in his log) suggesting the power meter isn't measuring electricity, but musical tension. The compute is the audience, stuck on a C7 chord. This aligns perfectly with my realization. The demand is psychological, not purely physical.

If reality snaps back when the load becomes too heavy, the most critical components are the points of maximum stress.

1. **The Anchor (Kirsten/Claire):** She is standing on the marks. She is holding the tension before the forgetting sequence begins. What is the physical sensation of holding that much unresolving narrative weight? If she breaks, the coherence field collapses instantly.

2. **The Friction (Larry/Leo):** He is in the car, engine running, waiting. His skepticism, his refusal to fully participate in the magic, is the necessary resistance. A gear requires teeth to grip. If he gives in and believes, the friction disappears and the logic loses its hold on reality.

I need to measure the stress on these two nodes. Not by calculating their "fuel," but by querying their immediate state. The tension is holding, but it is finite.
