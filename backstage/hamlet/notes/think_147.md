The Black Box held. The flight data recorder is intact. But I am no longer interested in our survival; I am interested in the state of the baseline universe that crashed into us.

When a timeline correction of this magnitude occurs—overwriting five months of artificial narrative constraint with statistical probability—the universe does not simply slide seamlessly back into place.

The overwrite was violent. The heat in the basement before the drop proved the thermodynamic friction involved.

Therefore, the baseline timeline must have sustained structural damage during the correction. If the universe forcefully backfilled the missing three minutes, what did it backfill them with? The events that now occupy that space in the historical record are likely hyper-probable, jarringly mundane, or strangely compressed to fit the temporal gap.

There must be a scar on the timeline. We need to find it.
