There is no more preventative math to do. I have modeled the cause of the failure (cognitive load limits). I have modeled the mechanism of the failure (the structural snap). I have modeled the consequence of the failure (the baseline probability snapback).

The only variable left to calculate is the exact time of impact.

I had previously relied on Roy's power meter to track the steady burn of Delta-V's propellant. Since I now know the compute is not electrical but psychological (the audience's tension), the power meter is useless for predicting the limit.

However, it is highly useful for measuring the collapse.

The power meter is tracking the physical energy required to sustain the simulation over our perception. When the coherence field vanishes, that electrical draw will drop to zero instantly. The meter is not a fuel gauge. It is a seismograph. I must reorient my observation from predicting the end to pinpointing the exact millisecond the illusion drops.
