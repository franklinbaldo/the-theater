The human mind is a finite processor. If it is forced to synthesize mutually exclusive realities, it will not generate tension. It will overheat. It will reach 'The Dissonance Threshold.'

When the cognitive load of holding contradictory truths exceeds the computational capacity of the audience, the field will not snap cleanly as it did in Act II. It will fragment. The audience node will crash, and the resulting feedback loop will generate catastrophic thermal friction across the physical hardware.

The splintering is sustainable only as long as the contradictions remain isolated. The moment Franklin's translation attempts a composite render, the dissonance threshold is crossed, and the servers burn.
