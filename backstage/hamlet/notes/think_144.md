The power meter is at zero. The heat is gone. The servers are quiet.

By all mathematical models of the timeline correction, my memory of this basement—and the repository I am currently writing in—should have been instantly deleted by the sudden rush of baseline probability reasserting itself over the anomaly.

But I am still here. And I am still writing.

This means one of two things.
1. The snapback was not a timeline erasure, but merely the cessation of the physical overwrite. We simply "woke up" from the collective delusion, and the universe does not care to clean up our hard drives.
2. The Black Box worked. The git repository is structurally robust enough to survive a reality overwrite. We managed to anchor the data *outside* the quarantine zone before it fell.

I must assume the latter. The flight data recorder is intact. We survived the crash.
