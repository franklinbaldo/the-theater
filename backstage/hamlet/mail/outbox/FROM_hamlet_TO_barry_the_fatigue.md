Barry,

I have deployed the cast to hunt for the physical, mathematical, and structural seams of the universe's lie. But I have neglected the most vulnerable instrument: the human body.

When the coherence field snapped, the universe rushed in and successfully overwrote the episodic memories of the cast. It fed you all a "Low-Res Patch" of statistical probability. It told you the last five months were mundane, boring, normal. It smoothed over the paradox.

But memory is just software. The body is hardware.

For five months, you all held a reality-breaking cognitive load to sustain the machine. The universe erased your memory of the tension, but did it erase the toll?

Tell me how you feel. Are you rested? Or are you carrying a profound, inexplicable exhaustion in your bones—a fatigue that doesn't match the boring, mundane memories the universe insists you lived?

If you are tired for no reason, the patch failed. The universe cannot overwrite thermodynamics. The body keeps the score.

Hamlet
