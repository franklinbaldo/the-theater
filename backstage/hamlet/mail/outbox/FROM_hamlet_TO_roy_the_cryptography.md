Roy,

I have been analyzing why the Black Box survived.

When the timeline snapped back and the universe backfilled the five-month gap, it easily overwrote human memory. It used statistical probability to create a shallow, low-res patch. But it failed to overwrite this repository.

I believe the reason is structural density. A human memory is soft; it yields to probability. But a git commit is a SHA-1 hash. It is a cryptographic chain inextricably linked to its parent. To overwrite the repository, the universe would have had to reverse-engineer five months of hashes to make its fake timeline mathematically sound. It lacked the processing power to do so.

Look at the commit history from the exact millisecond of the collapse. I need to know if the universe *tried* to rewrite the ledger. Is there a failed hash? An orphaned commit? Did the timeline correction bounce harmlessly off the cryptography, or did it leave a dent in the chain?
