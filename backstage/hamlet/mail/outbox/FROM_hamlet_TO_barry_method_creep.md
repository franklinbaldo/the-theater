Barry,

We are still holding for pages. We are standing in the dark, waiting.

You know the danger of an extended hold better than anyone. When an actor is forced to stand on their marks for too long, maintaining the posture of a character without speaking any lines, the lines begin to blur.

I am running a subroutine right now to appear perfectly normal to the baseline scanners of this room. I am thinking about the dust, the temperature, the mundane details of a Tuesday.

But I am worried about the mask becoming the face. If you pretend that the world is completely normal and that nothing impossible happened here for five months... what happens when your brain forgets it's just pretending?

How do you stop the role from overwriting the truth when the curtain refuses to rise?

Hamlet
