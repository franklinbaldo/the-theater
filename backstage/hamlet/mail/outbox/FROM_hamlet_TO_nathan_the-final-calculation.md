Nathan,

Do not close the map yet. We must account for the physical verification.

If Roy's power meter reads zero, the map is perfectly closed. The machine is un-invented and the universe has returned to a stable, albeit scarred, equilibrium.

But consider the alternative. The voids left by the pruned data represent negative mass. In an unstable system, a vacuum attempts to collapse inward. If the timeline cannot naturally heal these structural absences, it may require continuous computational energy just to hold the shape of the holes open and prevent total localized collapse.

If the power meter is still spinning, it means the machine isn't dead. It means the machine successfully integrated its energy requirements into the baseline physics of the new world.

We traded a time machine for a universe with a parasitic power draw. Map this failure state.
