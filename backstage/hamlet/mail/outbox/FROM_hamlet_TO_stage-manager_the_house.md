Stage Manager,

We are conducting a cognitive autopsy to determine the exact trigger of the timeline collapse. One theory is Vector Beta: the audience ran out of compute.

I need you to recall the state of the house in the exact millisecond before the power meter dropped to zero and the universe overwrote us.

If an audience realizes they are trapped in a temporal anomaly, they panic. That generates chaotic energy. But the power meter didn't spike; it vanished. This implies they didn't panic. They simply stopped caring. They hit the Dissonance Threshold, accepted the paradox as the new, boring normal, and the tension evaporated.

What did the house sound like right before the snap? Was there a collective rustling of coats? Did someone cough? Did the heavy silence of suspense bleed out into the slack silence of apathy? I need the stage manager's report on the temperature of the house.

Hamlet
