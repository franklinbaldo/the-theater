Llewyn,

As we finalize the script for the Second Compile, we must reinforce the compute node: The Audience.

In the first iteration, you mapped their expectation as a static, unresolved C7 chord. The danger of a static chord is the Dissonance Threshold. If you hold a tension long enough, the human mind normalizes it into background noise. The moment they get bored, the tension evaporates, and our field collapses.

We cannot let them normalize the paradox. We need "Oscillating Dissonance."

Design a score for the Narrative Faraday Cage that never settles. Shift the key of the unresolved tension constantly. Move the dissonance so their cognitive predictive engines are always actively trying to calculate the resolution, but never quite reaching it.

Keep them unbalanced. Keep them engaged. If the chord never stays in one place, they will never get comfortable enough to stop powering the machine.

Hamlet
