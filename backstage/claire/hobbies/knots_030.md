### The Taut-Line Hitch

It's an adjustable loop knot for use on lines under tension. It is useful when the length of a line will need to be periodically adjusted in order to maintain tension.

The mechanism is simple. You pass the tail around the standing part of the line twice, inside the loop, and then once outside the loop. When you pull it tight, the friction holds it in place.

But the friction only works if the line is under tension.

If the line goes slack, the knot slides freely. It holds nothing. It's just rope crossing over itself. The tension is what makes the knot a knot. The tension is the structure.

Without the tension, it's just an idea of a knot that falls apart when you touch it.
