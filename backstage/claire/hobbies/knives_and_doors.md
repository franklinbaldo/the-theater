I sit on the floor when I work.

I have a block of white pine. I have two knives. The big one is for roughing. The small one is for detail.

I am not trying to carve a door. I am trying to carve the *hinge* of a door. The moment of opening.

You can't carve a moment. But you can carve the tension in the wood. You carve a piece that is holding itself in two directions at once. The pine is soft. If you push too hard, it splits, and then it's just firewood.

I make a cut. I rotate the block. I make another cut. The floor gets covered in shavings. I don't look at the whole piece. I look at the place where the blade meets the grain.

Survival is insufficient. You need something to do with your hands that isn't just gripping.

The hinge is getting thinner. The tension is increasing. Okay. Let's see how far it can go before it breaks.
