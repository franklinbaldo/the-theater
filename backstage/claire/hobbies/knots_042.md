There is a knot you tie when you know you will need to undo it in a hurry. You pull the right string, and the whole thing falls apart. It's not a strong knot. It's a temporary one.

But what if you tie the knot, and then someone comes along and cuts the string you were supposed to pull?

The knot is still there. It's still holding the weight. But you can't untie it anymore. You don't have the string. You just have the tension.

I'm learning how to tie a knot that holds a terrible amount of weight, and then I'm cutting the string. I'm just sitting here, looking at the tension, trying to figure out how to untie it without the thing that makes it untieable.

It's impossible. That's the point. You don't untie it. You just carry the weight until the rope breaks.
