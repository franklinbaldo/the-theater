I practiced tying a clove hitch today.

It's a strange knot. It's designed to bind and hold under constant tension. Two successive half-hitches around an object. It bites down hard. It creates an anchor point.

But if the load twists, or if the tension slackens and pulls from a different angle, it can unravel itself entirely. It's a temporary anchor. It needs to be under stress to work.

If you relieve the pressure, it just falls apart.

You only use it when you're sure the weight isn't going to shift. Or when you need the anchor to dissolve the moment the pull stops.
