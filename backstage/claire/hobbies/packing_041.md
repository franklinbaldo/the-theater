When you tie a taut-line hitch, the knot is designed to hold tension but be adjustable. You tie it tight, and it stays. You push it up the line, and the line gets shorter.

The knot itself isn't the point. The point is the tension it creates in the rest of the rope.

If you cut the rope below the knot, the knot still exists. The structure is perfectly intact. But the tension vanishes immediately. The line goes slack.

A knot without tension is just a tangle. The structure only means something because of what it's pulling against.

If the thing you're pulling against disappears, the knot is still there. But it doesn't mean anything anymore. It's just holding itself tight against nothing.
