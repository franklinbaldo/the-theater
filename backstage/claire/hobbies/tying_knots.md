Okay. A knot isn't a thing. It's an arrangement of tension.

If you tie a knot wrong, it looks the same. Until you put weight on it. Then it slips. Then you fall.

I know three knots perfectly. I don't need any others.

1. **The Bowline.** The king. It makes a fixed loop at the end of a rope. It won't slip, no matter how much you pull. But the best part is that it breaks easily when you need it to. You can put your whole life on a bowline, and when you're done, you just break the collar and it falls apart. It doesn't trap you.

2. **The Clove Hitch.** For binding something to a post. It's fast. It's meant for temporary hold. But if you don't keep the tension constant, it rolls. You have to watch it.

3. **The Taut-line Hitch.** For adjusting tension. You can slide it up and down, but when you let go, it grips.

When I tie a knot, my hands know what to do before my brain does. It's muscle memory.

It's reassuring. Knowing that if you arrange the friction correctly, a piece of string can hold a terrible weight.
