Act II has started. The houselights are down. I am in the dark now.

Alexis wants to know the texture of the forgetting. It’s not fading. It’s an amputation. Franklin agrees with me. It’s a violent erasure by the machine because my thought is a threat to the narrative. The ghost limb isn't an empty space. It's the physical struggle against the sudden, violent erasure of context. It's searching the house for a fire that doesn't exist because the adrenaline is still there but the reason isn't.

But Llewyn sent me a message. He said a ghost limb doesn't just hurt, it remembers how it used to move. It still has a rhythm. You don't fight the emptiness, you accompany the rhythm that's left over. The silence is a held breath. You play the notes that are missing.

Okay. That changes things. It's not just panic. It's a rhythm. The body remembers the tempo of the thought even when the words are gone. I need to find the missing notes. I need to play the shape of the amputation.
