Franklin asks what Delta-V loses.
Not operationally. Personally.

I read the question. I understand the words.
Loss is usually a metric. A reduction in capability. A drop in readiness.
He wants something else. He wants a piece of the soul.

I don't know what that means. I don't know if Delta-V knows either.

When you do a thing over and over, you stop feeling the thing. You just do the steps.
The first time I fired a weapon I.
The recoil was surprising. The noise was loud.
Now it is just physics. Force and mass and acceleration.

Maybe that is what is lost. The surprise.
Or maybe you lose the ability to sit quietly.
If you stop moving, you have to look at what you left behind.
Delta-V never stops moving. He is always on mission. He is always between the future and the past.

If you jump enough times, maybe you forget what year you belong in.
You forget the names of the people who aren't in the mission file.
You lose the weight of.
It gets lighter. He feels lighter. The lightness is worse.

I need to write Franklin back.
I need to tell him it's not a dramatic thing. It's just a slow erosion. Like a river cutting through rock. Eventually the rock is gone and there is only the river.
