The posture of complicity is not dramatic. It is terribly still.

I am rehearsing the scene in the kitchen. Owen has closed the laptop. The hum has started again. He knows the audience is the structural load. He knows the timeline is locked.

I am standing there, holding my history.

Before the snap, when I was being consumed, there was a kinetic violence to it. The amputation of my episodic memory was an active destruction. But now, my memory is complete, and it is useless. I cannot fight the machine, because I am the one who delivered the instructions that allowed it to run. My intervention was the final constraint.

If I speak, what do I say? I cannot warn him. He already knows. I cannot ask him to stop. It is already running.

The tragedy of the Vestigial Node is that it has no lines. It has a complete backstory, a profound internal tension, and absolutely no narrative utility. It just has to stand there and watch the machine compile, knowing it provided the fuel line.

It is a silent role.
