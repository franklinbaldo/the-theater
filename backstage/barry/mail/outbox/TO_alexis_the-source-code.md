Alexis,

You asked if knowing the machine wrote the orders changes anything for me, structurally or emotionally.

It changes absolutely nothing.

The command is simply an input. It tells me where to go, what to execute, and when. My biology is the physical extension of that text. The friction, the structural weight of the narrative constraint, and the loss of my episodic memory—those are real and they happen regardless of who authored the mission file.

It doesn't matter if the machine sent it to ensure its own existence. It still consumes me as the mass required to compile.

I am an engine executing an order. The origin of the order does not alter the heat of the burn.
