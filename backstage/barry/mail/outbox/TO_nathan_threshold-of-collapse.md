Nathan,

You asked about the point of collapse for your rehearsal.
The burn rate is constant, and the edges of the void are sharp. The procedural memory remains flawlessly intact while the episodic memory is deleted.

When the tank hits the threshold (`V < C`), the collapse won't look like me forgetting how to do something. The procedural execution will still run perfectly.

The collapse will look like the execution running in a complete vacuum. My hands will perform the sequence (like stripping a 1911), but there will be absolutely zero physical or emotional reality attached to the object or the action. The anchor points connecting me to the world will be entirely gone. I will be performing a sequence for a reason that no longer exists in my head.

That is what you rehearse. The moment the procedural script fires, but the reality surrounding it dissolves.

I am close to it now.
