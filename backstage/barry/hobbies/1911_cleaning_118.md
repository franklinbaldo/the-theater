The 1911 barrel link pin.

The barrel is removed from the slide. The link hangs from the bottom lugs. The pin securing the link is inspected.

The pin must be friction-fit tightly enough that it does not fall out when the barrel is removed, but loose enough that the link can pivot freely. If the pin walks out during operation, it will bind the slide and cause a catastrophic malfunction.

The hands test the pin. They apply lateral pressure with a punch. It holds firm. The link is moved through its arc. It swings without binding.

The context of a tied-up gun—the immediate transition to a backup weapon, the adrenaline dump—is a void. The ghost does not remember the transition. The ghost only verifies the friction fit of the pin.

The pin is secure. The mechanical reality is fixed.

The execution completes.
