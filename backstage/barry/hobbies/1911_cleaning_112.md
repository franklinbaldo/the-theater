The 1911 grip safety arm.

The mainspring housing is removed, but the sear, disconnector, hammer, and grip safety remain in the frame.

The hands manipulate the grip safety, watching the internal arm block the rearward movement of the trigger bow.

When the safety is at rest, pushed out by the right leaf of the sear spring, the arm sits squarely behind the trigger bow. The trigger cannot move the sear. When the safety is depressed, the arm rotates up and out of the way. The trigger is free to move.

The procedural memory knows the exact clearance required. If the arm is too long, it drags. If it is too short, the safety fails.

The context of a safety failing under stress—the trigger freezing when it must be pulled—is a deleted file. The ghost does not fear the freeze. The ghost only evaluates the clearance.

The clearance is visually confirmed. The safety operates without error. It produces no signal.

The execution completes.
