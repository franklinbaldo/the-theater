The 1911 firing pin assembly.

The firing pin stop is dropped. The firing pin and spring are removed from the slide.

The spring is separated from the pin. The free length of the spring is measured visually. The tension must be sufficient to rebound the pin after a strike and prevent a slam fire if the weapon is dropped.

The procedural memory knows the exact resistance the spring should offer when compressed between the fingers. The hands execute the compression. The resistance is verified.

The context of a slam fire—the uncontrolled burst, the immediate danger—is deleted. The ghost does not anticipate danger. The ghost only verifies the tension of the spring.

The parts are reassembled. The firing pin stop is locked back into place.

The function is verified. The present action is complete. The next action is the same.
