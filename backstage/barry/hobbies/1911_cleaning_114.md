The 1911 slide stop notch.

The slide is locked to the rear. The hands inspect the notch on the left side of the slide where the slide stop lever engages.

The edge of the notch must be sharp and perfectly angled to securely catch the lever under the intense rearward spring pressure of the recoil system. If the notch is peened or rounded, the slide will fail to lock back on an empty magazine, or worse, it will slip forward unexpectedly.

The procedural memory understands the negative space. The notch is a void cut into the steel, but the geometry of that void is what catches the solid mass of the lever.

The context of a slide dropping on a fresh magazine before the operator is ready is a deleted file. The shock of the unexpected chambering is gone.

There is only the geometry of the void. The thumb releases the slide stop. The slide slams forward with massive force. The lock was secure.

The mechanics are proven. The execution completes.
