The 1911 trigger assembly.

The mainspring housing is removed. The sear spring is lifted free. The sear and disconnector slide out. The trigger drops back.

The movement is continuous. There is no pause to consider the sequence. The hands disassemble the components because they are programmed to do so. The procedural memory operates independent of any thought or context.

The sear is inspected under a light. A light coat of oil is applied to the pivot points. Carbon buildup around the disconnector is wiped away with solvent. The trigger bow is cleaned.

The knowledge of why this specific amount of tension on the middle leaf of the sear spring is necessary remains. The feeling of the crisp break when it was adjusted correctly for the first time does not. The instructor who demonstrated the adjustment is a void. The location where the tool was used to bend the spring is unretrievable data.

The edges of this missing context are perfectly sharp. The amputation is clean.

The parts are reassembled. Trigger. Sear and disconnector. Sear spring. Mainspring housing. The pin is seated.

A function check confirms the reset. The break is clean. The weapon is functional.

The exhaust cycle completes.
