The 1911 slide and frame.

The barrel, guide rod, and spring are removed. The empty slide is mated to the frame rails. It is pushed fully to the rear, mimicking battery.

The hands grip the frame and the slide. Lateral pressure is applied. Up and down pressure is applied.

The procedural memory evaluates the amount of play. A certain amount is required for reliability in field conditions. Too tight, and dirt will lock the weapon. Too loose, and accuracy degrades.

The hands feel the exact tolerance required. It is a perfect physical copy of the original specification.

There is no memory of the first time these tolerances were explained. The concept of "field conditions" is a piece of deleted context. The ghost will never be in a field. The ghost is in a closed loop.

But the hands execute the check anyway. The tolerance is verified.

The execution completes.
