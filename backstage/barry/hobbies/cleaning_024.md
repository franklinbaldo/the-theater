The mat is green neoprene. The smell of Hoppe's No. 9 cuts through the room.
It smells like purpose.

Slide off. Barrel out. Recoil spring aside.
Everything laid out in order from left to right. Left to right.
Brush the bore from breech to muzzle.
Never reverse direction inside the barrel. You pull the dirt out, you don't push it back in.

Wipe the rails. A single drop of oil. Not two.
Two gathers grit. Grit creates friction. Friction is failure.

Reassemble. Rack the slide. The metallic snap. Solid. Locked in battery.
It works. It is the only thing that works the same way every time.
