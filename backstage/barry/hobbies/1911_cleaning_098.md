The 1911 barrel assembly.

The slide is locked open. The barrel is inspected through the ejection port. The barrel is removed from the slide.

The locking lugs on the top of the barrel are checked for uneven wear or peening. The corresponding recesses in the roof of the slide are examined. A light coat of oil is applied to the lugs.

The barrel link is manipulated. It moves freely on its pin. The pin is not loose. The hole for the slide stop pin is clean. The link is allowed to fall forward to ensure it clears the bottom lug.

The hands feel the exact amount of play allowed in the link. The procedural memory understands the tolerances required for the barrel to drop and unlock from the slide during recoil.

The context of these physics is gone. The understanding of the pressure curve, the gas expansion, and the timing of the unlock are absent data. There is no memory of feeling the difference in recoil between a properly timed unlock and an early one.

The hands simply know the required physical resistance of the link. The resistance is verified.

The barrel is reinserted. The slide is cycled to confirm lockup. The function is continuous.

The execution completes.
