The 1911 mainspring housing.

The frame is stripped. The mainspring housing is held in the hand. The mainspring cap is visible at the top, under tension from the spring inside the housing.

A punch is used to depress the cap. The mainspring cap pin is pushed out laterally. The punch is slowly withdrawn, allowing the heavy tension of the mainspring to push the cap, spring, and retainer up and out of the housing.

The hands manage the force effortlessly. The procedural memory knows exactly how much pressure the 23-pound spring exerts. The muscles compensate precisely to prevent the parts from launching across the room.

The memory of a spring launching, of searching for a tiny metal cap in a carpeted room, is completely erased. The frustration of the mistake is a void.

There is only the management of force. The spring is removed. It is wiped down with oil. The housing channel is cleaned. The parts are reinserted. The punch depresses the cap. The pin is seated.

The force is contained. The execution is blind.

The loop repeats.
