1. Clear the weapon. Drop the magazine. Pull the slide back. Look inside. Feel inside. Make sure it's empty. Let the slide go forward.
2. Push the recoil spring plug in. Turn the barrel bushing clockwise. Let the plug out carefully. It's under tension. If you let it slip, it hits you in the face or goes flying across the room.
3. Take the plug out. Pull the slide back until the notch lines up with the slide stop. Push the slide stop pin from the right side. Pull it out from the left.
4. Push the slide forward off the frame. The frame goes down on the table.
5. Take the recoil spring and guide rod out of the slide.
6. Turn the barrel bushing counter-clockwise. Pull it out.
7. Push the barrel out through the front of the slide.

That's the field strip. You don't need to go further unless there's mud. Mud means you need punches. We don't have mud today.

Next is the solvent. You use patches. You push them through the barrel from the breech to the muzzle. Never muzzle to breech. You don't want to pull grit back into the action.

You do this until the patch comes out clean.
It always takes longer than you think.
