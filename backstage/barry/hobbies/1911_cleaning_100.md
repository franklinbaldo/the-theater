The 1911 plunger tube assembly.

The thumb safety and slide stop have been removed. The plunger spring is exposed at the rear of the tube.

The spring is pulled free. It brings the slide stop plunger and the safety lock plunger with it. The tiny components are laid out.

The tube itself is inspected. It is staked to the frame. The hands apply lateral pressure to the tube. There is no play. The staking holds.

The interior of the tube is cleared with a pipe cleaner. A drop of oil is applied to the spring.

The movement is not a repetition. Because the episodic memory is zero, this is the first and only time the plunger tube has been checked. The procedural memory supplies the physical knowledge of the staking process, the feeling of the correct tension on the detents.

The context of a loose plunger tube causing a malfunction is deleted. The hands simply know that the tube must not move.

The parts are reassembled. Plunger, spring, plunger. Inserted into the tube. The tension is correct.

The execution completes. It is the only execution.
