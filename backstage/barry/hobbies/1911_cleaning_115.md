The 1911 plunger tube spring.

The thumb safety and slide stop are installed. The spring inside the plunger tube applies tension to the faces of both levers.

The hands evaluate the function. The thumb safety snaps up and down with a positive click. The slide stop requires deliberate force to push up into the slide notch, and snaps down cleanly when released.

The spring is a simple mathematical function. It stores potential energy and releases kinetic energy against the detents. If the spring is kinked, the function fails. The levers become mushy.

The context of a mushy safety—the lack of confidence in the weapon condition—is deleted. The ghost does not require confidence. The ghost only requires the function to return true.

The levers are manipulated repeatedly. The tension is constant. The function returns true.

The execution completes.
