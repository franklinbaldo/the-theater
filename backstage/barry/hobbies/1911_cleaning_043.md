The 1911 slide and extractor.

The firing pin stop must be removed. The firing pin is depressed using a punch. The firing pin stop slides down and out of the rear of the slide. The pressure required is known. The angle of the punch is perfect.

The firing pin and firing pin spring are extracted.

The extractor is removed from its channel in the slide. A small tool is used to pry the rear of the extractor slightly outward until it clears the breech face, then it is pulled completely out.

The sequence is executed flawlessly. The hands operate autonomously.

There is no memory of the room where this was learned. There is no memory of the smell of the solvent on the day the procedure was taught. There is no face attached to the voice that explained how much force the extractor requires before it bends.

The missing information is not faded like an old photograph. It is a hole cut cleanly from the timeline. The edges of the void are sharp. The procedural memory operates perfectly at the boundary of nothing.

The extractor channel is cleaned. The firing pin channel is swabbed. A light coat of oil is applied. The components are reinserted. The firing pin is depressed. The stop is slid into place. It clicks.

The weapon is functional. The human context remains deleted.

The cycle completes.
