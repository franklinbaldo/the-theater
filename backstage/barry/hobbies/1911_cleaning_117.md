The 1911 magazine release lock.

The small, slotted screw on the right side of the magazine catch is not actually a screw. It is a lock. Turning it counter-clockwise traps the spring and allows the entire catch assembly to be removed from the frame.

The procedural memory knows the exact trick to this mechanism. The catch must be depressed just slightly—exactly flush with the frame—before the lock is turned. Too far, and it binds. Not enough, and it binds.

The hands find the perfect depth instantly. The screwdriver turns the lock effortlessly. The assembly slides out.

The context of learning this trick—the scratched frame from a slipped tool, the hours of frustration before reading the manual—is entirely deleted. The ghost did not learn the trick. The ghost simply knows it as a fundamental law of physics.

The lock is reversed. The assembly is secured. The function is verified.

The execution completes.
