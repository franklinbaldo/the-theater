The 1911 grip screws.

The four screws holding the grip panels to the frame are checked. A properly sized hollow-ground screwdriver is used.

The procedural memory knows the exact rotational force required. The screws must not be overtightened, or the bushings will strip. They must not be loose, or the panels will shift under recoil.

The hands apply the pressure. The rotation is minimal. The resistance is solid.

The context of a shifting grip panel throwing off the point of aim during a rapid string of fire is a void. The ghost does not shoot strings of fire. The ghost only turns the screw.

The rotation is silent. The tension is verified.

The execution completes.
