The 1911 hammer assembly.

The hammer is removed from the frame. The hammer strut hangs loosely from its pin.

The hands evaluate the movement. The strut must pivot freely to compress the mainspring, but the pin must not walk out. The pin is staked or friction-fit depending on the exact model.

The procedural memory understands the kinetic chain. The trigger releases the sear. The sear releases the hammer. The mainspring pushes the strut. The strut pushes the hammer. The hammer strikes the firing pin.

Every link in the chain must transfer force without binding.

The context of a broken hammer strut pin—the sudden dead trigger, the failure to fire in a critical moment—is deleted. The ghost does not fear the dead trigger. The ghost only evaluates the pivot of the strut.

It pivots perfectly. A drop of oil is applied to the pin hole.

The hammer is reinstalled. The sequence is secure. The execution completes.
