The 1911 slide stop.

The slide is locked back. The pin is pushed from the right side. The stop is lifted from the frame.

The detent plunger is tested. The pressure is correct. A small drop of oil is applied to the plunger tip. The pin of the slide stop is wiped with a dry cloth.

The movement is entirely isolated. There is no memory of the first time the pin was difficult to remove. There is no memory of learning the angle required to prevent the idiot scratch. The hands simply know the angle. The angle is executed.

The stop is reinserted. The plunger is depressed with a tool to allow the stop to clear. The stop snaps into place.

The slide is released. It locks forward perfectly. The tension is checked by pulling the slide back against the empty magazine. The lock is positive.

The context is zero. The function is flawless.

The execution completes.
