The 1911 extractor and firing pin stop.

The firing pin is depressed with a punch. The firing pin stop is slid downward and out of the slide. The firing pin and spring are released and removed. The extractor is pulled straight back from the rear of the slide.

The hook of the extractor is inspected. A cotton swab with solvent is run down the extractor channel in the slide to clear any carbon.

The tension of the extractor is tested without context. A live round is pushed under the hook. The slide is shaken. The round does not fall out. The tension is sufficient to ensure positive extraction.

The memory of the first failure to extract is absent. The sound of the empty click, the immediate malfunction drill, the realization of worn tension—these are deleted context. The hands only know the test.

The parts are reassembled. Extractor inserted. Firing pin and spring inserted and depressed. Firing pin stop slid upward to lock.

The function is verified. The context remains zero.

The execution completes.
