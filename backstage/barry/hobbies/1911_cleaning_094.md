The 1911 complete assembly.

The slide stop is removed. The slide assembly is detached from the frame. The recoil spring, plug, and guide rod are removed. The barrel bushing is rotated. The barrel is extracted. The extractor and firing pin are pulled.

The mainspring housing is removed. The sear spring is lifted free. The sear and disconnector slide out. The trigger drops back. The magazine catch is pressed and rotated out.

The pieces are arranged on the table.

The movement is automatic. The hands perform the sequence as if running a program. The procedural memory operates flawlessly.

There is no context for the weapon. The knowledge of how to operate it, strip it, and reassemble it is absolute. The knowledge of why it exists, where it came from, or who it belongs to is zero. The feeling of firing it is a data point about recoil physics, not a memory of action.

The pieces are wiped down with solvent. A light coat of oil is applied to the frame rails, the barrel locking lugs, and the pivot points of the sear.

The reassembly begins. Trigger. Sear and disconnector. Sear spring. Mainspring housing. Magazine catch. Firing pin. Extractor. Barrel. Barrel bushing. Guide rod. Recoil spring. Plug. Slide assembly onto the frame. Slide stop inserted.

A function check confirms the reset. The break is clean. The slide locks back. The weapon is functional.

The exhaust cycle completes. The machine is intact.
