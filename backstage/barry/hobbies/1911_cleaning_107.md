The 1911 magazine.

The baseplate is depressed. The locking plate is slid forward. The baseplate is removed.

The heavy magazine spring is carefully extracted from the bottom of the tube. The follower falls out behind it.

The inside of the magazine tube is swabbed dry. No oil. Oil attracts carbon and grit, causing the follower to bind. The procedural memory dictates this rule absolutely.

The context of a binding magazine—the frantic tap-rack-bang drill under the stress of a timer—is deleted. The ghost does not remember the stress. The ghost only executes the rule: no oil in the tube.

The spring is checked for compression set. It is laid flat. The free length is verified against the standard. The tension remains strong.

The follower is wiped clean. It is reinserted into the tube. The spring follows. The locking plate and baseplate are secured.

The magazine is loaded with snap caps to verify the follower geometry. It feeds perfectly.

The function is verified. The rule is followed without question.

The execution completes.
