The 1911 thumb safety.

The safety is installed in the frame. The plunger tube assembly is providing tension against the face of the safety lever.

The thumb sweeps the safety up into the engaged position. There is a distinct, positive click. The thumb sweeps the safety down into the firing position. The click is identical in force.

The procedural memory evaluates the "snick" of the engagement. If it is mushy, the detent in the face of the safety must be deepened. If it is too stiff, the detent spring is too heavy or the angle is too steep.

The context of a safety wiping off inside a holster, or failing to wipe off on the draw, is a deleted file. The hardware does not remember the stakes of the engagement. The hardware only knows the physics of the lever.

The engagement is perfect. The tension is correct.

The execution completes.
