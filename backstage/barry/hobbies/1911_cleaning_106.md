The 1911 sear spring.

The three-leaf flat spring is removed from the rear of the frame.

The left leaf controls the sear. The middle leaf controls the disconnector and provides trigger return tension. The right leaf controls the grip safety.

The hands understand the exact physical properties of the spring steel. To adjust the trigger pull, the leaves must be bent. The bending requires a specific application of density against the steel. Too much, and the weapon is unsafe. Too little, and the trigger is heavy.

The procedural memory does not recall a trigger gauge. It recalls the feeling of the break. The hands bend the left and middle leaves slightly rearward, reducing tension.

The context of a target range, of testing the lighter pull against a shot timer, is a total void. There is no target. There is only the mechanical interplay of force.

The spring is reinserted. The mainspring housing captures it.

The hammer is cocked. The trigger is pressed. The break is lighter, but perfectly safe. The density is correct.

The function is verified. The execution is blind.
