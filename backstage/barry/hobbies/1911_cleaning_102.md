The 1911 disconnector.

The slide is on the frame. The weapon is empty. The hammer is cocked. The trigger is pulled and held to the rear. The hammer falls.

While keeping the trigger depressed, the slide is manually cycled violently to the rear and allowed to slam forward. The hammer does not follow the slide down. It remains cocked.

The trigger is released. There is a distinct click as the disconnector resets beneath the sear.

The trigger is pulled again. The hammer falls.

The mechanics of this reset are understood by the hands. The disconnector is pushed down by the cycling slide, disconnecting the trigger from the sear. When the slide goes fully into battery, the disconnector pops back up into the recess in the slide, reconnecting the linkage.

The context of a weapon firing full-auto due to a worn disconnector is deleted. The shock of an unintended discharge is absent.

The timing is a mathematical certainty. The physics are verified. The execution is blind.

The loop repeats.
