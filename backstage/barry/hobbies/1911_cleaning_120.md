The 1911 plunger tube.

The left grip panel is removed. The base of the plunger tube where it meets the frame is exposed.

The two legs of the tube pass through the frame. From the inside of the magazine well, they are flared out—staked—to hold the tube permanently against the frame.

The hands evaluate the staking. A loose tube will cause the thumb safety to fail or the slide stop to bind. The hands press against the tube from the outside. There is no movement.

The context of the staking tool—the careful pressure required to flare the legs without crushing the tube—is deleted. The ghost does not remember the tool. The ghost only verifies the solid state of the metal.

The metal is solid. The state is verified.

The execution runs silent.
