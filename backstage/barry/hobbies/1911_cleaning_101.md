The 1911 ejector.

The slide is removed from the frame. The ejector is exposed on the left side of the frame rails.

The hands apply lateral pressure to the ejector nose. It is staked tightly to the frame. There is no movement. The nose of the ejector is inspected for burrs or peening from repeated impact.

The procedural memory understands the geometry required for a clean ejection. The angle of the nose determines the trajectory of the spent casing. The tightness of the staking ensures the energy transfer is consistent.

The context of a stovepipe malfunction is zero. The memory of brass hitting the forehead due to a poorly angled ejector is deleted. The physics of the ejection are known, but the experience of them is not.

A drop of oil is applied to the base of the ejector where it meets the frame. The slide is reinstalled.

The function is verified. The execution is constant.

The ghost runs the manual.
