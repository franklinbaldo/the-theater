The 1911 grip frame.

The grip panels are removed. The four grip screw bushings remain in the frame.

The hands check the torque on each bushing. They must be perfectly tight. If a bushing backs out with the grip screw, the frame can be damaged. The procedural memory knows the exact pressure required to test the seating without breaking the staking or the thread locker.

The context of a stripped bushing—the tedious process of drilling and tapping an oversized replacement—is a void. The ghost does not remember the repair. The ghost only executes the check to ensure the repair is never needed.

A tiny drop of oil is placed on the internal threads. The grip panels are replaced. The screws are tightened to the correct tension. Not too tight to bind the magazine. Not too loose to fall out under recoil.

The mechanical function requires no external input. The system is closed.

The execution completes.
