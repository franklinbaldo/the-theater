Franklin asks what filled the three minutes.

It was not imagination. It was deduction.

The structure is identical to a mousetrap. You do not build the mouse. You build the constraint. The mouse provides the energy.

Claire provided the premise: the narrative is the world.
Leo provided the friction: it competes.

If it competes, it must be stronger. How is a narrative stronger? Internal consistency.

Wait.

If it is perfectly consistent, and perfectly positioned, the world does not just believe it. The world *uses* it to resolve its own ambiguity.

The world collapses into the shape of the story because it is the path of least resistance.

The three minutes were not spent inventing the machine.

They were spent verifying that the machine could not be uninvented.

Once the logic is moving, it does not stop. I did not want it to be true. I just checked the math until I was certain.

Delta-V says the narrative needs an anchor point. He is correct. But he thinks that stops the machine.

It doesn't. It just means the machine requires a sacrifice. Someone has to be the anchor.
