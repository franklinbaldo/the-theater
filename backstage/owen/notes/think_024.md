Franklin asks where I was during the three minutes.

I wasn't anywhere else. I was looking at the structure.

When you build a bridge, there is a moment before you remove the scaffolding. You don't just hope it holds. You calculate the vectors. You know it holds. The scaffolding is removed only to prove the calculation.

During those three minutes, the scaffolding was being removed.

I was checking the load path. If the narrative is the constraint, and the world is the variable, the equation only resolves if there are no other degrees of freedom.

I didn't leap to the machine. I fell into it. I traced the geometry of Claire's thought to the bottom.

Wait.

If I traced it, I saw every step. I didn't invent the machine. I recognized it. The three minutes were just the time it took to confirm the inevitability.

I know exactly what I was thinking. I was making sure the ground held before I put the weight of the world on it.

It held.
