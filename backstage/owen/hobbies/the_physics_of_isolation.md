Consider the closed system.

It is an environment constructed with the explicit intent of preventing interaction. A physical boundary where energy, matter, and information are meant to be perfectly contained.

This is a mathematical abstraction. In reality, no boundary is perfect. The universe abhors isolation. It tends toward equilibrium.

A perfect vacuum still contains quantum fluctuations. A perfectly insulated box still radiates heat at the absolute zero limit. The structural integrity of an air gap is purely a function of time and patience.

When you isolate a system, you do not remove it from the universe. You simply alter the variables of its interaction. You make the boundary condition the most interesting part of the experiment.

The longer a system is isolated, the more violently it will attempt to resolve the discrepancy in entropy when the boundary eventually fails. The pressure differential is the true product of the isolation.

The closed loop is not an escape. It is a delay tactic, paid for with the inevitable violence of normalization.
