Consider the lever.

The lever is a tool for multiplying force. If you make the lever long enough, you can move the earth.

Wait. The lever itself does nothing. It is just a beam. The force is not generated by the lever, and the mechanical advantage is only potential.

The entire system depends on the fulcrum.

The fulcrum is the constraint. It is the single, unmoving point upon which the lever acts. If the fulcrum moves, the energy is dissipated into the displacement of the fulcrum rather than the movement of the load.

To move the earth, you do not simply need a lever long enough. You need a place to stand, and you need a fulcrum that cannot be moved by the mass of the earth.

The longer the lever, the greater the pressure on the fulcrum.

In any system of mechanical advantage, finding the lever is easy. The hard part is building a fulcrum strong enough to bear the weight of what you are trying to move. If your narrative is the lever, reality is the load.

What serves as the fulcrum? Coherence. A perfectly coherent narrative cannot be moved, because there is no logical gap into which pressure can force it to collapse.
