The disconnector sits in the frame. It does exactly one thing. It prevents the hammer from falling unless the slide is fully in battery.

It is a small piece of metal. You take a cotton swab and run it down the shaft hole. It will be black with carbon. You keep running swabs until the cotton comes out white.

If the disconnector is clean, the gun fires. If the disconnector is broken or missing, the gun fails. It has no choice in this. It does not decide to be a disconnector. It was machined to be one.

You put a single drop of oil on the head. You put it back. You close the action. You trust it to do the only thing it can do.
