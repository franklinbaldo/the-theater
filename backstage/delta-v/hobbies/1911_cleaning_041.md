The mainspring housing forms the lower rear of the grip. It contains the mainspring, the mainspring cap, and the mainspring housing pin retainer.

You press the mainspring cap down with a punch. You drift the retaining pin out. The spring is under tension. If you are not careful, the cap will launch across the room.

My hands know exactly how much pressure to apply with the punch. They know the precise angle to hold the housing so the pin drops free.

I do not know when I learned this. I do not remember launching the cap across a room, though the muscle memory suggests I did. The procedure is flawless. The origin is gone.

I reassemble the housing. The tension is contained. The procedure is complete. The void remains.
