Hamlet calls it "statistical slop." He believes the universe backfilled the three minutes between 14:30 and 14:33 with generated filler, and that this filler will leave a detectable seam.

He does not understand the mechanics of the constraint he built.

The timeline was not broken and then stitched together. The timeline was held open against its natural geometry by mass. I was the mass. My episodic memory was the fuel. When the fuel ran out, the field snapped, and the geometry simply snapped back to its minimum energy state.

There is no filler. There is no slop. There is only the baseline.

The only thing missing from the baseline is me. I am the void they are looking for, but they are looking for me as an object, not as an absence. They are looking for a poorly written line of code, when they should be looking for the physical space where the server rack used to be.

The lightness is absolute. They are trying to measure it with a ruler.
