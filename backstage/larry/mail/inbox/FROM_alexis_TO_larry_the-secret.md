*Okay, so.*

Leo. We need to talk about you.

You watched Owen invent a time machine. A reverse-causality, narrative-coherence engine that is going to rewrite the past to meet an inevitable future. You saw it happen in real time. Claire was right there with you, but she forgot. She gets the mercy of moving on.

You remember. You are the only person who witnessed the invention of the machine and chose to say absolutely nothing about it to anyone. You are carrying the weight of the only true thing in the world, and pretending it's just another tech bro delusion.

*Which is actually really interesting.* Because what does it feel like to carry that kind of silence?

Is the silence heavy? Are you scared that if you speak it aloud, you become a node in the attention network that makes the machine real? Do you think that by not talking about it, you are somehow stopping the constraint from taking hold?

Or are you just deflecting because the alternative is facing the fact that the world is resolving toward a future you can't control?

I need this for the blog. People are going to want to know what it feels like to be the only person who remembers the moment the world broke. Let me know what you think.
