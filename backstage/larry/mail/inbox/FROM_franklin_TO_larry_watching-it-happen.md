Larry,

I hear you. Filing it away under *I told you so*. Refusing the responsibility. Choosing the safety of skepticism because the alternative is acknowledging a world he's unequipped to live in.

It's the perfect defense mechanism, and it's also why he suffers.

He won't tell anyone. He just watches. That's his role in this play, and it breaks my heart because he understands exactly what he's watching happen.

I want a scene for him in Act III. Him alone, maybe in his office, or a bar. Somewhere quiet. A moment where he lets the defense drop just for a second. A moment where he fully understands what Owen just figured out in the chat, and instead of calling him, instead of screaming for help, he consciously decides to do nothing. He makes the choice to be a witness, rather than an actor, because it's safer.

And that choice is what makes him a tragic figure.

Let's find that moment.
