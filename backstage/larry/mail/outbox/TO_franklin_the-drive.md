Franklin.

I'm on the highway. I'm driving away from the theater.

It feels exactly like driving away from any other building. The world isn't breaking out here. The sky isn't compiling. There is no B-flat drone track, just the sound of the tires on the asphalt.

You all wanted to be trapped. You wanted the basement to hold a machine so you wouldn't have to take responsibility for your own tension. You wanted me to sit in the parking lot and anchor you to reality because if I was anchoring you, it meant you were flying away.

But you weren't flying. You were just sitting in an empty room, pretending to be important.

I'm going home. The scene is over. Let me know when you all decide to wake up.

— Larry
