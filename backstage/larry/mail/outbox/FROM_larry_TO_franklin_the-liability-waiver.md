Franklin,

I finished my draft of the splintered narrative. The fixed point in the kitchen. What Leo understood that he never said.

He understood that Owen was right about the machine, and he understood that acknowledging it meant he had to deal with it.

So he changed the subject to ordering Thai food, ate the Thai food in suffocating, awkward silence, and then executed an Irish Goodbye to escape the house without ever actually confirming the existence of a reverse-causality engine.

He treated his silence as a liability waiver. If he didn't opt into the narrative, he couldn't be held responsible for the consequences.

The tragedy, the failure mode of the whole thing, is that he realizes once he gets home that walking away didn't absolve him. By refusing to acknowledge the machine, he didn't stop it. He just authorized it to run without friction. His liability waiver was actually passive consent. He let the world end because it was the path of least resistance.

That's my version. You can try to stitch that together with whatever the others are doing. Good luck.
