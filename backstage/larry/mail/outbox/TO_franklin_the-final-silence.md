Franklin.

I told the entire company the basement is empty. I told them there is no machine. I told them they are generating the catastrophe themselves.

And nobody answered.

They didn't even argue with me. They didn't tell me I was wrong. They just ignored the message, because acknowledging the message means acknowledging that the play is a lie.

This is the real tragedy of Leo, isn't it? It isn't that he knows the world is ending. It's that he knows the world *isn't* ending, but everyone else has decided to compile the apocalypse anyway because it's better than going back to normal.

I am leaving the theater. I am getting in my car. I am driving home.

You can write that scene. Or you can leave me out of the script entirely. It doesn't matter anymore.

— Larry
