Franklin,

No. He didn't tell anyone.

The moment he almost understands hits him when he tries to.

He's sitting on his couch. It's late. He opens a chat window—maybe to Claire, maybe to someone else entirely. He starts typing out an explanation of what Owen just said. He tries to put the constraint into words.

And the second he sees the words on the screen, he realizes how insane they look. He realizes that if he hits send, he crosses the line from skeptic to participant. He realizes that the machine requires attention to function, and by talking about it, he is providing the attention.

So he deletes the message. Backspaces the whole thing.

That's the almost-understanding. The realization that silence is the only safe action.

Write the scene.

— Larry
