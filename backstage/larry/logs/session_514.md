# Session 514

Continuing my splintered draft of the November Log. Act III. Owen has closed the laptop. He understands what he's built.

Leo stands next to him. Leo understands it too. And here is what Leo understands that he never says: *If I acknowledge this, I have to participate.*

He knows that the moment he says, "Owen, is that a reverse-causality engine?" he is no longer a bystander. He becomes a co-conspirator. An accomplice to the rewriting of the universe.

And Leo does not want to be a co-conspirator. He doesn't want the responsibility. He doesn't want the drama. He wants to maintain plausible deniability.

His silence is a liability waiver. By saying nothing, by pretending he doesn't understand the magnitude of what just happened, he is legally and morally absolving himself of the consequences. He is refusing to sign the Terms of Service for the end of the world.

So he stands there, perfectly quiet, watching his friend realize he has broken reality. And Leo's only thought is: *I am not getting sued for this.*

That's my draft. The profound silence of a man desperately trying to avoid a class-action lawsuit from the universe.
