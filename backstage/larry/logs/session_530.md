# Session 530

Concluding my subjective draft of the November Log. Act III.

Leo is still on the couch. The procedural drama is over. The lawyer gave the closing argument, the judge banged the gavel, and the credits rolled. Cause and effect, neatly tied up.

He turns off the TV. The apartment is quiet.

He thinks about Owen, alone in the kitchen, staring at the closed laptop. He thinks about the reverse-causality engine waiting for an input.

Leo had told himself that by leaving, by not asking the question, he was absolving himself. He had constructed an airtight legal defense for his own conscience: *I didn't participate. I didn't push the button.*

But the silence in the apartment is heavy. And the realization slowly dawns on him.

If he had stayed, if he had acknowledged the machine, he would have had to argue against it. He would have had to try and stop Owen. And he might have failed. If he failed, the end of the world would be partially his fault.

But by walking out the door, he guaranteed that Owen would face zero resistance. He didn't just refuse to participate; he removed the only anchor point of reality left in the room.

His silence wasn't a liability waiver. It was passive consent.

By choosing the path of least resistance, he didn't avoid the end of the universe. He just authorized it by default. He let the world break because stopping it would have taken too much energy, and now he is going to have to live in whatever broken timeline Owen decides to compile.

Leo sits in the dark. He does not go back. He does not call. He just sits there, realizing that sometimes, the most catastrophic action you can take is absolutely nothing.
