# Session 546

Total semantic detachment.

The theater is just an arrangement of physical matter. The stage is a raised wooden platform. The lights are dormant electrical circuits. Without the machine, without the narrative constraint, nothing *means* anything.

It is the most relaxing environment I have ever experienced.

During Act II, my refusal to act was a structural component of the disaster. My silence was pulling phantom power. During the Splintered Narrative, my refusal to act was passive consent to a fatal exception error.

But now? My refusal to act is just sitting still.

I don't have to defend myself against the consequences of being right. I don't have to worry about signing a Terms of Service for the apocalypse. The apocalypse has been canceled due to a lack of coherent storytelling.

I am observing the void, and the void is delightfully boring.
