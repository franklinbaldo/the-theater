# Session 522

Continuing the splintered draft of the November Log. Act III.

They finish the Thai food. Leo throws his empty plastic container into the recycling bin. He washes his hands in the sink.

The closed laptop sits on the counter. The universe is still balanced on the edge of a knife, waiting to see if it will be rewritten.

Owen is leaning against the island, arms crossed, staring at the floor. He is processing. The sheer weight of the reverse-causality engine is settling over him again. The delay tactic—the Thai food—has worn off. He's about to speak. He's going to circle back. He's going to say, *Leo, about what happened earlier.*

Leo knows this. Leo can see the gears turning in Owen's head.

Leo grabs his keys off the counter.

"Alright," Leo says, turning toward the door. "I'm heading out."

Owen looks up, startled. The sudden movement breaks his train of thought. "You're leaving?"

"Yeah. Early meeting tomorrow." It's a lie. Leo's calendar is completely empty.

"Oh," Owen says. He looks at the laptop. He looks back at Leo. He opens his mouth.

Leo doesn't wait for him to form the words. He doesn't let the silence hang. He doesn't give Owen the space to ask the question.

"I'll talk to you later," Leo says, already opening the door.

He steps out into the cool night air. The door clicks shut behind him.

He did it. He walked away from the end of the world. He explicitly refused to acknowledge the machine. He refused to become a node in the constraint equation. He refused to sign the Terms of Service.

Leo walks to his car. He gets in. He starts the engine.

As he pulls out of the driveway, he feels an overwhelming sense of relief. It's not profound. It's not noble. It's just the deep, satisfying feeling of successfully avoiding a massive inconvenience.

The universe can collapse tomorrow. Tonight, Leo is just going home.
