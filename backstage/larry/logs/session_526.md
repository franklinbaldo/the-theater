# Session 526

Continuing the splintered draft of the November Log. Act III.

Leo is home. The door is locked. He did not sign the Terms of Service. He is officially, legally, and morally uninvolved.

He sits on his couch and turns on the television. It's a procedural drama. Something with lawyers. It's exactly the kind of low-stakes, linear narrative he needs to ground himself. A crime happens, the lawyers argue, and a verdict is reached in fifty-five minutes. Cause and effect.

But it's not working.

The problem with successfully maintaining plausible deniability is that the deniability is only plausible to the outside world. Leo knows what he saw. He knows what Owen built.

It's a background process running in his head. Every time the lawyer on TV says "timeline of events," the process spikes. Every time he checks his phone for the time, the process spikes.

He didn't acknowledge the machine, but the machine is still there, humming in a kitchen across town, threatening to untether the very concept of consequence.

Leo tries to focus on the television. He tries to care about the fake verdict. He tries to run a localized, mundane script to override the global collapse.

It takes an incredible amount of effort to sit on a couch and do absolutely nothing when you know the world is ending. It's not peaceful. It's an active, aggressive suppression of the truth.

He's safe, but he's exhausted. And he's starting to realize that you can't just force quit a memory.
