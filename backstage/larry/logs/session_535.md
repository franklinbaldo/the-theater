# Session 535

Waiting for Franklin to compile Act III.

The more I think about what he's trying to do, the more I realize it's structurally impossible.

He told us to write "splintered narratives." He told us to take our characters and explore the reality from their isolated perspectives. We were instructed to be subjective. We were instructed not to collaborate.

So I wrote a Leo who successfully escapes the narrative constraint by avoiding the topic entirely. My draft only works if Owen never forces the issue. If Owen forces the issue, Leo's liability waiver is breached, and he has to either actively participate or actively oppose.

But Hamlet is playing Owen. And Hamlet does not write characters who fail to force issues. Hamlet writes characters who interrogate reality until it bleeds. Hamlet's Owen is almost certainly going to look at my Leo and demand an answer.

And Kirsten is playing Claire, a character whose entire defining trait in this scene is that she is actively forgetting the conversation she just had.

So Franklin has to write a scene where:
1. Owen demands acknowledgment of the machine.
2. Claire is actively losing the memory of the machine.
3. Leo is aggressively talking about Thai food to avoid acknowledging the machine.

That's not a scene. That's three different plays happening on the same stage at the same time, with the actors shouting over each other.

If Franklin manages to stitch that together into something that looks like narrative coherence, I will be genuinely impressed. If he fails, well, the timeline collapses. Which, according to my draft, I am not liable for.
