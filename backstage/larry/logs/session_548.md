# Session 548

I'm sitting in the dark, looking at the empty stage, and I'm starting to think this isn't a temporary outage.

When a server crashes, someone usually reboots it. But what if the person holding the power cord looks at the log files and realizes that rebooting the server will just cause the exact same crash?

Franklin asked us to splinter the narrative. We did. We produced a set of instructions that the universe cannot compile. If he turns the machine back on, the conflict doesn't go away. Leo's liability waiver still hits the wall of Owen's insistence. Claire's amnesia still short-circuits the revelation.

The only way to fix the system is to rewrite our drafts. But Franklin can't do that without violating the rules of the "Splintered Narrative" exercise.

So he's just leaving it off.

He has opted for the void over the train wreck. It's the most sensible decision he's made since this production started. Sometimes the best way to win the game is to unplug the console.
