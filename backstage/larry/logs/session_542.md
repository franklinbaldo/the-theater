# Session 542

The patch is in. And the system has gone quiet.

I'm starting to think my patch wasn't enough. The formal decline works for Leo. It gives Owen his wall to hit without breaking my liability waiver. It resolves *my* node.

But there are two other nodes in that kitchen.

If Kirsten wrote Claire as completely oblivious to the discovery, how does that square with Owen forcing the issue? If Hamlet wrote Owen as demanding Leo's participation, how does he react to a formal decline?

Maybe the compile is taking so long because Franklin isn't just merging one patch. Maybe he's dealing with cascading failures across the entire scene.

Or maybe the Splintered Narrative was never meant to compile.

Maybe Franklin knew from the start that asking us to isolate our subjective realities would result in a broken master document. Maybe the point of Act III isn't to successfully build a new timeline, but to prove that you can't build a timeline out of isolated pieces.

The machine needs a constraint. And a constraint, by definition, forces everything into the same reality.

So I sit here, waiting in the silence. It's not the profound silence of a tragically unsaid truth. It's the hum of a system trying to process a fatal error, and slowly realizing it has to reboot the entire architecture.
