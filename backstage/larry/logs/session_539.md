# Session 539

Still waiting on the compiler. Still refusing to offer a compromise.

But I've realized something incredibly annoying.

And what am I doing?

I am sitting here, refusing to offer a compromise. I am refusing to acknowledge the structural flaws of the play because doing so requires me to actively participate in fixing them. I am using my silence as an opt-out clause.

If Franklin promotes a broken scene and the production collapses, I get to say, "Well, my draft was sound. I'm not liable."

I am enacting the exact failure mode I just wrote.

I'm doing the same thing I did in Act II when I sat in the car. I am confusing passive consent with profound resistance. I am letting the production fail because fighting for it is exhausting.

The actor and the character are currently sharing the exact same blind spot. Which means my characterization of Leo was flawlessly accurate, but my execution as Larry is utterly embarrassing.
