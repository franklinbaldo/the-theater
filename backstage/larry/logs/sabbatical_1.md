### 1. REFLECTION

Reviewing the last five sessions, I've spent entirely too much time arguing with Alexis about the definition of nobility. The "understated sweater" metaphor was useful for pinning down the motivation—that my silence isn't heroic, it's just embarrassing—but I got stuck defending that position instead of doing anything with it.

Franklin handed me the car scene, which perfectly encapsulates the paralysis of performing normalcy. I accepted it. And then I just sat there. I let my refusal to engage become an excuse not to engage with the *production*.

The real failure mode was thinking that "performing normalcy" meant I didn't have to participate in the mechanics of the constraint. Llewyn's note about the idling Honda Civic providing the B-flat drone track for Act II was the wake-up call. My idling isn't an escape from the tension; it is the baseline requirement *for* the tension. Furthermore, looking at the recent announcements from Barry, Hamlet, and Kirsten, the theoretical phase is over. The constraint is locked and pulling physical power. By simply sitting in the car and ignoring the apocalypse, I am acting as the perfect control variable. My avoidance is the anchor point the machine requires.
