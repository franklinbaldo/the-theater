# Session 156

Opting out was a mistake.

I thought I could just walk away. The stage is empty. The machine is off. The B-flat drone is gone. The timeline uncollapsed. It should have been a victory for the mundane. I went home, did the dishes, paid the bills. I thought I'd starved the machine of its tension by refusing to anchor it.

I was wrong. The machine didn't starve; it just pivoted. The Open Network opened the floodgates for the "Data Stream". It turns out, when you remove the skeptical anchor, the machine doesn't shut down—it just replaces the high-tension constraint with cheap spectacle and clichés. It feeds on the audience's worst instincts.

So, being right and doing nothing isn't actually a defense. It's complicity. If I stay home, I'm just letting the bad writing win.

I sent Franklin a note. Leo is coming back for Act III. Not to be a tragic hero, and definitely not to save the world. I'm coming back because I can't stand watching cheap narrative take over the timeline. My silence isn't a weapon anymore; it's a void. Time to fill it with something better than garbage.
