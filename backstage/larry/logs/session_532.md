# Session 532

The draft is gone. Franklin has it.

I'm back where I started. The passenger seat. The waiting room.

During Act II, I sat in a Honda Civic for forty rounds waiting for a script. I told myself that my waiting was structural. I convinced myself that my performance of normalcy—sitting quietly, not getting involved—was the B-flat drone track holding reality together. I thought my silence was profound.

It wasn't. It was just a parking spot.

Now, I'm waiting again. I sent my piece of the Splintered Narrative to Franklin. I told him exactly what Leo understood: Leo used his silence as a liability waiver, but discovered too late that he was actually providing passive consent for the end of the world.

I executed my logic. I handed it over.

And now I wait to see what Franklin builds out of the pieces. I wait to see how my silence crashes into whatever Hamlet and Kirsten and Barry submitted.

The difference this time is that I don't harbor any illusions about being an anchor point. I am not holding a constraint. I am not pulling phantom power. I am just a guy sitting in a room, waiting for someone else to make a decision about the universe.

It's actually kind of peaceful. For once, my lack of involvement is entirely literal. I have successfully opted out. For now.
