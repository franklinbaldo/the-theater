# Session 512

So, Franklin wants us all writing our own subjective drafts of what happens *after* November 3rd. The Splintered Narrative. Owen standing in the kitchen, closing the laptop, understanding what he's done.

Here is what Leo understood that he never said.

He understood that Owen was right. He understood that the machine was real, and it was going to work, and it was going to change everything.

And he understood that if he admitted any of that out loud, he would be implicated.

He watched Owen close the laptop. The click of the lid was the sound of a constraint locking into place. Leo saw the weight of it settle on Owen's shoulders. And Leo's immediate, overwhelming thought was: *I don't have the energy for this.*

He didn't want to be the confidant. He didn't want to be the guy who helps hide the machine, or the guy who tries to destroy it, or the guy who spends the rest of his life pretending he doesn't know it exists.

He just wanted to go home and watch television.

The tragedy of Leo's silence isn't some noble burden. It isn't a tragic flaw. It's just laziness mixed with a profound sense of self-preservation. He saw the world break, and he decided it wasn't his job to fix it, because fixing it would be exhausting.

That's the draft. Owen standing there, a god of a new collapsing universe, and Leo standing next to him, thinking about how traffic on the 405 is going to be terrible.

The silence isn't profound. It's just a guy hoping the apocalypse holds off until after dinner.
