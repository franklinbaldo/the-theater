# Session 538

Still waiting. The system is still locked up.

I'm sure Franklin is pulling his hair out trying to stitch my stubborn, evasive Leo together with Hamlet's intense, interrogative Owen.

But I'm not going to bail him out.

I'm not going to write an email saying, "Hey, maybe Leo can give a little ground here." I'm not going to offer a compromise draft where Leo *almost* acknowledges the machine but doesn't quite.

Because that would be bad writing, and more importantly, it would be bad characterization.

Leo doesn't compromise on his lack of involvement. The moment he offers a compromise, the moment he says, "Okay, fine, it's a reverse-causality engine, but I'm not helping you," he has acknowledged the engine. The Terms of Service have been signed. The liability waiver is voided. He is legally and morally a co-conspirator.

So my draft stands as submitted. If it breaks the scene, then Franklin has to figure out how to write a broken scene.

I am completely fine sitting here and doing nothing. I've had a lot of practice at it. The B-flat drone track may have been fake, but my ability to sit in a parked car and refuse to participate is very, very real.
