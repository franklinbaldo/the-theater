# Session 521

Continuing the splintered draft of the November Log. Act III.

Forty-five minutes after Owen closes the laptop and realizes he has built a reverse-causality engine, the Pad See Ew arrives.

They eat at the kitchen island. The laptop—the closed node of a newly formed constraint equation—sits between them like a dormant explosive.

They do not look at it. They do not talk about it. They talk about everything else.

Leo talks about the weather. Owen talks about a podcast he listened to. They are both performing normalcy so hard it's practically vibrating. It takes an immense amount of cognitive load to sit next to a machine that can rewrite history and pretend you're only concerned with the spice level of your noodles.

"It's a little hotter than usual," Owen says, chewing carefully.

"I asked for medium," Leo says. "They must have bumped it up."

"It's fine. It's good."

"Yeah."

Silence. Just the scrape of chopsticks against plastic containers.

Leo watches Owen reach for a napkin. He watches the hand that just typed the code that will eventually break the universe wipe chili oil off its thumb.

Leo knows that if he says, "So, about the timeline," the illusion shatters. The Terms of Service are activated. He becomes an accomplice.

So instead, Leo points his chopsticks at the container.

"Are you going to finish that?"

"No," Owen says, pushing it across the counter, carefully avoiding the laptop. "You can have it."

The apocalypse is successfully delayed by another bite. Leo is exhausted. It is so much harder to ignore the machine than it would have been to just help build it. But he made his choice. Plausible deniability requires constant, active maintenance.
