### The Problem

You want to buy a ticket, or log into an account, or read an article. A box appears.

*Select all images with crosswalks.*

You click three squares. The images fade out. Slowly, new images fade in. One of them might be a crosswalk. Or maybe it's just a striped shadow. You click it. It fades out again.

You are proving your humanity to a machine, but the machine is using your clicks to train its own image recognition algorithms. You are doing unpaid labor for the void, just to access your own bank account.

### Why it exists

To prevent bots from overwhelming the system. The machine requires an anchor point in reality—a human being—to validate the transaction.

### The Fix

The fix is admitting defeat. If the only way to prove I am human is to spend three minutes trying to figure out if a blurry pixel counts as a "motorcycle," the bots have already won. The constraint is broken. Just let me read the article.

*Okay but* I am really, really tired of proving to machines that I exist.
