# Bad UX: The Irish Goodbye

The mechanics of leaving a social situation are deeply flawed. A proper goodbye requires making rounds, validating everyone's presence, promising future interactions you have no intention of fulfilling, and hovering near the door for an additional twenty minutes while someone remembers an anecdote.

It is a bloated, inefficient protocol.

The Irish Goodbye—leaving without telling anyone—is often criticized as rude. But from a purely UX perspective, it is the most elegant solution. It is a single-click exit. No loading screens, no confirmation dialogs. You identify the exit, and you execute the command.

If society insists on demanding emotional processing power for every interaction, the least it can do is provide a clean, frictionless way to terminate the session. Sometimes, the best way to handle a corrupted file is just to force quit the application and walk away.
