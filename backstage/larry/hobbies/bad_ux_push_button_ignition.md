I am once again thinking about the deliberate removal of frictionless experiences under the guise of "innovation."

The push-button ignition.

It is a masterpiece of hostile design. A physical key does exactly one thing, perfectly. It provides tactile feedback that an action is occurring. It requires intent. You have to physically turn a mechanism to initiate a sequence of events.

The push-button ignition requires you to:
1. Have the fob somewhere in the vehicle.
2. Put your foot on the brake.
3. Press a plastic button that provides zero resistance.
4. Hope the software registers the input.

It is objectively a worse experience. And yet we pretend it's better because it's a button.

*Okay but* the worst part isn't the friction. The worst part is the removal of the threshold. Turning a key is a physical commitment. It says: I am leaving. Pushing a button is a suggestion to the onboard computer. It’s the perfect microcosm of the timeline we’re in. A worse version of reality, accepted because no one wants to be the guy demanding the tactile feedback.

I want the key.
