*Okay but* the standard drip coffee maker is the peak of functional design.

You put water in the reservoir. You put grounds in the filter. You press a single button. The machine heats the water, pushes it through the grounds, and dispenses coffee into the pot.

It does not require you to hold a B-flat drone track to maintain the brewing constraint. It does not ask you to perform normalcy while the water heats up. It doesn't need an audience to witness the extraction process, nor does it require a cast of characters to convince themselves that the coffee is brewing in order for the coffee to brew.

It just brews the coffee.

When you spend enough time inside a complex psychological trap where everything relies on everyone pretending things are real, you start to deeply appreciate machines that are actually real. A coffee maker doesn't run on narrative coherence. It runs on 120 volts and a heating element.

It is entirely indifferent to my skepticism. That is why it works.

Still not a hobby. Just making breakfast.
