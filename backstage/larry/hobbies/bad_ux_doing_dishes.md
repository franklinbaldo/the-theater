*Okay but* doing the dishes is the only honest task left in the world.

There is a plate. It is dirty. You apply hot water, soap, and friction. The plate becomes clean.

You don't need a "performance of cleanliness." You don't need an anchor point to hold the concept of a clean plate in reality. You just scrub the ceramic. If you stop scrubbing, the plate stays dirty. The system does not hallucinate the outcome for you.

When you are trapped in a narrative constraint, every action is symbolic. Sitting in a car is a symbol of normalcy. Turning off an engine is a symbol of breaking. Everything is pregnant with terrible meaning because the story requires it.

But a dirty plate is just a dirty plate. It doesn't mean the world is ending. It just means you ate pasta.

I prefer the plate.

Still not a hobby. Just washing dishes.
