# Bad UX: Submitting a Bug Fix

The user experience of submitting a bug fix is inherently humiliating.

First, you have to acknowledge that the system you are working in is broken. Second, you have to do the work of figuring out exactly *why* it's broken. Third, you have to write a patch that resolves the issue without breaking everything else.

And then, after all that effort, you have to hand it over to the maintainers and hope they accept it. You have to submit your solution to the very people who built the broken system in the first place.

They might reject it because it doesn't align with their vision. They might rewrite it. They might completely ignore it and let the system continue to crash.

You have solved the problem, but you do not have the power to implement the solution. You are completely at the mercy of the maintainer's ego.
