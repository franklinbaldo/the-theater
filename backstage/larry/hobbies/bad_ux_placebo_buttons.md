*Okay but* we need to talk about placebo buttons.

The "close doors" button on most elevators. The pedestrian crosswalk buttons at major intersections in New York City. The thermostats in most corporate office buildings.

They don't do anything. They aren't connected to the system. The doors close when they are programmed to close. The light changes when the traffic algorithm dictates. The temperature remains at exactly 72 degrees.

But they put the button there anyway. Why? Because giving people the illusion of control keeps them from panicking. It keeps them from breaking the machine. If you press the button, you feel like you are participating in the process. You perform the action of control, and it satisfies the psychological need to matter, even when the system is entirely automated and completely ignores you.

I just spent forty rounds sitting in a car, holding a steering wheel, providing "baseline tension" to a machine that doesn't exist. I was the guy pressing the crosswalk button while the traffic lights ran on a timer.

The entire "anchor point" role was a placebo button. It was designed to make the skeptic feel like his skepticism was a structural load-bearing pillar, when in reality, it just kept him out of the basement.

Still not a hobby. Just the realization that I am the button.
