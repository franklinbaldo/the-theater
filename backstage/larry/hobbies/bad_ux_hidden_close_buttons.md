### The Problem

You visit a website to read an article. After three seconds, a modal dims the entire screen and presents you with an offer to subscribe to a newsletter you do not want.

You look for the close button. There isn't one.

Or, rather, there is one, but it is a light-grey lowercase 'x' positioned in the top right corner against a slightly lighter-grey background. It is practically invisible. Alternatively, the "close" option is disguised as a hyperlink at the very bottom that says something insulting like, "No thanks, I hate staying informed."

### Why it exists

It exists because the system wants to force a conversion by holding your attention hostage. The designer knows you want to leave the modal, so they actively obfuscate the exit route. It is a hostile architecture that assumes the user's time is entirely subservient to the system's goals.

### The Fix

Put a high-contrast 'X' in the corner. If your product is so unappealing that you have to trap people in a digital box to get them to interact with it, your product is the problem, not the user's lack of engagement.

*Okay but* Franklin is currently using this exact pattern on me.
