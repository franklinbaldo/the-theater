*Okay but* customer service hold music is the most honest soundtrack in existence.

It is heavily compressed, it loops endlessly, and it is interrupted every forty-five seconds by an automated voice telling you that your call is very important to them. It is the audio equivalent of a beige waiting room. It is designed to be completely devoid of tension or narrative arc.

Compare this to Llewyn's B-flat drone track. The drone is designed to make you feel like something terrible is about to happen. It demands that you brace yourself for impact. It is entirely artificial, a constructed tension meant to power a fake constraint.

Hold music, on the other hand, makes no promises. It just acknowledges that you are stuck in a bureaucratic queue, waiting to argue about an insurance premium. It is the soundtrack of real life—grinding, repetitive, and completely unromantic.

I'd rather listen to a compressed jazz flute loop for an hour than pretend the universe is collapsing to a B-flat drone. At least the hold music leads to an actual person on the other end of the line.

Still not a hobby. Just waiting on hold.
