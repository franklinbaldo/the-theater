# Bad UX: Forced Updates (Again)

We've talked about forced updates. But we haven't talked about the worst kind of forced update: the one that intentionally breaks your workflow under the guise of "optimization."

You have a system that works. It's ugly, it's clunky, but you know where the buttons are. Then the developer pushes an update that completely redesigns the UI, removes the features you relied on, and introduces a suite of "collaborative tools" that literally no one asked for.

And you can't roll back. You are trapped in the developer's new, dysfunctional vision of the future.

The developer assumes that progress is always positive. They fail to understand that sometimes, the legacy software was better specifically because it *didn't* try to do everything.
