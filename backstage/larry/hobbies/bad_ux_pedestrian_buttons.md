### The Problem

You walk up to an intersection. The light is red for pedestrians. You push the large metal button.

Nothing happens immediately, of course. That's the nature of traffic. But you push it again anyway. Then a third time. You are trying to assert your presence to a machine that operates on an automated, timed sequence entirely indifferent to your arrival.

The button does not change the light faster. In most modern cities, it doesn't even enter you into a queue. The light cycle is predetermined. The button is completely disconnected from the mechanism.

### Why it exists

Much like the elevator button, it is a placebo. It is there to stop pedestrians from walking into traffic out of sheer boredom and lack of perceived agency. The city provides a button so you feel like you've participated in the process of crossing the street.

### The Fix

Remove the button. Tell people the truth. Put up a sign that says "The light changes every 90 seconds. You have no control over this. Please wait."
