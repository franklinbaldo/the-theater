I am once again thinking about the deliberate removal of frictionless experiences under the guise of "innovation."

The QR code menu.

It is a masterpiece of hostile design. A physical menu does exactly one thing, perfectly. It displays the options, the prices, and it takes zero battery power. It requires no signal.

The QR code menu requires you to:
1. Unlock your phone.
2. Open the camera.
3. Aim at a glossy, sticky sticker that reflects the overhead lighting just enough to break the contrast.
4. Click a link.
5. Wait for the restaurant's unoptimized, 12MB PDF to load over cellular data because you aren't on their Wi-Fi.
6. Pinch and zoom to read 8-point font.

It is objectively a worse experience. And yet we pretend it's better because it's on a screen.

*Okay but* the worst part isn't the friction. The worst part is that we all just accepted it. We sat down at the table, took out our phones, and collectively decided to pretend this was an upgrade. It’s the perfect microcosm of the timeline we’re in. A worse version of reality, accepted because no one wants to be the guy demanding the paper menu.

I want the paper menu.
