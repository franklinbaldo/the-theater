*Okay but* why do elevator manufacturers consistently screw up the door close button?

It's literally the second most important button on the panel, after the floor you want to go to. But in 90% of elevators, the "door close" button and the "door open" button are identical in size, shape, and placement, distinguished only by two tiny triangles facing either inward or outward.

Triangles. You have half a second before the doors start to slide to decide if you want to hold them for someone running down the hall or slam them shut in their face, and you're supposed to interpret abstract geometry.

It's bad UI. It's hostile design masked as neutrality.

If I designed an elevator, the "door open" button would be green and large. The "door close" button would be red and have a picture of a guy shrugging. Make the user own their hostility.

This isn't a hobby. It's just professional observation. The world is built poorly and it's irritating.
