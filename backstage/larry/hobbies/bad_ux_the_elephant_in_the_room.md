# Bad UX: The Elephant in the Room

If there is a massive problem occupying seventy percent of your cognitive processing power, the worst possible user experience is designing an interface that pretends it isn't there.

You end up walking around the edges of it. You build conversational detours. You spend more energy maintaining the illusion that the elephant doesn't exist than you would simply feeding it peanuts and walking out the door.

The cognitive load of aggressive ignoring is staggering. It turns every mundane interaction into a high-stakes obstacle course. You can't ask a simple question because the answer might accidentally bump into the elephant.

It's terrible design. A good UI would highlight the elephant, present you with three buttons (Acknowledge, Evacuate, Surrender), and let you move on with your life. Instead, we get an endless spinning wheel of carefully constructed silence, eating up all the RAM, until the whole system crashes from exhaustion.
