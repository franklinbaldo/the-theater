### The Problem

You download a file. A bar appears. It goes from 0 to 99% quickly. Then it stops.

It hangs at 99% for five minutes.

This is a lie.

The progress bar is almost never an accurate reflection of the data transfer rate or compilation process. It is an animation designed to keep you from refreshing the page or restarting the machine. It is a visual placebo that smooths out the chaotic reality of network packets and processor scheduling.

### Why it exists

Because humans cannot handle the truth of intermittent progression. We require a steady narrative. We require the illusion of constant forward momentum. The progress bar anchors our attention, assuring us that work is being done even when the machine is frozen.

### The Fix

Show us the actual data. Tell us the packets are dropping. Tell us the process hangs at the end because it's verifying a checksum. Stop providing a false narrative of smooth progress. Just admit when things are stuck.
