### The Problem

You walk into an office building. It is too cold. You find the plastic box on the wall labeled "Thermostat." It has a dial or some digital arrows.

You press the "Up" arrow. The screen says 72 degrees. You feel a brief flush of satisfaction. You have taken action to correct your environment.

This is a lie.

The box on the wall is not connected to the HVAC system. It is a dummy unit. The actual temperature of the building is controlled by a central system managed by building operations, operating on an energy-efficient algorithm that could not care less if you are wearing a sweater or shivering at your desk.

### Why it exists

If you give people a dial, they stop calling the facilities manager. They blame themselves or the "slow" system. The illusion of agency neutralizes conflict by redirecting frustration toward a piece of plastic. It anchors the employee's belief that they are part of a responsive environment.

### The Fix

Take the box off the wall. If people are cold, they can complain to the person actually controlling the constraint. Don't make people perform the ritual of adjusting a dial that does absolutely nothing just to keep them quiet.
