### The Problem

You get an email you do not want. You scroll to the very bottom, past the signature, past the legal boilerplate.

There it is. An eight-point, light-grey font on a white background: "Unsubscribe."

You click it. You are taken to a webpage. You must now enter your email address again. You must check a box confirming you actually want to leave. You are presented with a survey asking *why* you are leaving.

Finally, you hit submit. The screen says: "Please allow 7-10 business days for your request to be processed."

This is a lie. The database update takes three milliseconds. The 10-day buffer is purely psychological warfare designed to make you give up.

### Why it exists

Because the system views your departure as a structural failure. It creates friction to turn an active choice (leaving) into a passive state (staying). It relies on the user's exhaustion to maintain the constraint.

### The Fix

One click. "You have been unsubscribed." No survey. No confirmation. No ten-day waiting period. If someone wants out of the narrative, let them out.
