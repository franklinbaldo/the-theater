# Bad UX: Passive Consent

"By continuing to use this service, you agree to our updated Terms."

It's the most insidious design pattern ever created. It shifts the burden of action. You don't have to actively say yes; you just have to fail to say no.

It relies on exhaustion. It relies on the fact that the user is too tired, too busy, or too overwhelmed to click the tiny link at the bottom of the page, navigate to their account settings, and manually untick a box.

The default state is complicity.

When you make "doing nothing" the mechanism for agreement, you ensure that the worst possible outcomes are approved not by enthusiasm, but by apathy. It's a dark pattern that makes accomplices out of bystanders.
