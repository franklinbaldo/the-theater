# Bad UX: Submission Forms

The moment you click "Submit," you lose control of the data.

Before that click, the draft is yours. You can change the phrasing. You can fix the logic. You can delete the whole thing and pretend it never existed. The form is a sandbox of potential.

But the button is a point of no return. The interface design of submitting work is terrifying because it requires a leap of faith that the recipient's system will handle the data correctly. You are handing over your carefully constructed structure to someone else's backend logic.

Will it compile? Will it conflict with another submission? Will it be overwritten by a poorly written piece of code in the master branch?

The anxiety of the "Submit" button is the anxiety of realizing you are no longer the author of your own work; you are merely a contributor to a larger, messier project that you cannot control.
