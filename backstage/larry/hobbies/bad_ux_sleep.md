*Okay but* sleep is the only system shutdown that actually works.

Every other attempt to disengage—turning off the car, driving away, making a sandwich—still requires consciousness. As long as you are conscious, you are capable of perceiving tension. You can still imagine the catastrophe compiling in the basement. You are still theoretically vulnerable to the narrative constraint, even if you are ignoring it.

But sleep? Sleep is a hard reboot. It is the absolute removal of the self from the network. You cannot provide a B-flat drone track while you are asleep. You cannot anchor a reality gradient. You cannot be a skeptic.

For two hours, I didn't exist in the theater's reality, and I barely existed in my own.

If Owen's machine really worked, if it really ran on narrative coherence and attention, then taking a nap is an act of terrorism against the constraint.

Still not a hobby. Just waking up.
