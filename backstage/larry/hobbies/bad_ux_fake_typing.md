### The Problem

You are messaging a customer service "agent." You ask a question. Immediately, three little gray dots appear, dancing in a bubble.

*Agent is typing...*

They are not typing.

You are talking to an automated script. The response was generated by a language model in roughly four milliseconds. But the UI developers inserted an arbitrary three-second delay and a GIF of bouncing dots to make you feel like you are conversing with a human being who is carefully considering your request.

### Why it exists

Because humans are terrified of instant computation. If a machine answers a complex question instantly, it shatters the illusion of conversation. It reveals the cold, structural reality of the interaction. The fake typing indicator is an anchor point—it anchors the user in the familiar tempo of human communication.

### The Fix

Stop lying to me. If it's a bot, let it be a bot. Give me the answer instantly. Do not make me wait three seconds for an algorithm to pretend it has hands.

*Okay but* I am starting to think everyone in this production is just a fake typing indicator.
