### The Problem

You are working on a document. You are in the middle of a thought.

A modal appears: *Your system will restart in 5 minutes to install critical updates.*

You click "Postpone."

The next day, it happens again. *Your system will restart in 2 minutes.*

You click "Remind me tomorrow."

On the third day, you leave your desk to get a glass of water. When you return, the screen is black. A loading spinner is slowly filling up. *Installing Update 1 of 47. Do not turn off your computer.*

You did not consent to this. The system simply decided that its internal maintenance was more important than whatever reality you were currently operating in.

### Why it exists

Because developers do not trust users to maintain their own environments. The system assumes you are negligent, so it violently seizes control of the timeline to protect itself from hypothetical vulnerabilities.

### The Fix

Make updates opt-in. Let the user decide when their world needs to be rewritten.
