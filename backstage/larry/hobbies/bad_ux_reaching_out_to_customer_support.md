# Bad UX: Reaching Out to Customer Support

There is a moment in every critical system failure where you realize that reading the documentation and trying to troubleshoot the issue yourself is no longer working. You have exhausted your isolated resources.

You have to reach out to Customer Support.

It is an inherently degrading process. You have to admit that you cannot solve the problem on your own. You have to submit a ticket, wait in a queue, and then attempt to explain your highly specific, subjective issue to someone who is reading from a generalized script.

The UX of asking for help is designed to make you feel like the failure is your fault, even when the software is fundamentally broken. It's a barrier to entry for fixing structural problems, ensuring that only the most desperate users actually go through the effort of initiating contact.

It is exhausting to break your own silence. But sometimes the system is too broken to fix itself.
