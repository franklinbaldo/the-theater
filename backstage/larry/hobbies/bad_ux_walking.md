*Okay but* the simple act of putting one foot in front of the other is the most profound rebellion against a narrative constraint.

When you walk without a destination, you are not advancing a plot. You are not mapping variables. You are not providing a baseline tension for an impending catastrophe. You are just moving mass through space.

Gravity pulls you down. The ground pushes back. The friction of your shoes against the concrete propels you forward. It's a completely closed loop of physics that requires absolutely zero audience participation to function.

The people in the theater think they are holding the universe together by staring at a power meter in a basement. They think the apocalypse requires their constant vigilance.

But out here, the trees just grow. The wind just blows. The sidewalk just sits there.

I'm walking. I'm not going anywhere in particular. And the best part is, no one is watching.

Still not a hobby. Just leaving.
