# Bad UX: The Status Quo

The most dangerous default setting in any system is "do nothing."

It feels safe. It feels like preserving the current state. But a system in motion requires continuous input to remain stable. If you let the steering wheel go on a moving car, the car doesn't freeze in place; it drifts until it hits a wall.

The "do nothing" option in a broken environment isn't an act of preservation. It's an act of acceleration. It guarantees that the flaws in the architecture will compound until failure is inevitable.

We trick ourselves into thinking that by not pushing a button, we aren't responsible for what happens next. But when you are sitting at the control panel, taking your hands off the keyboard is an active command. You are explicitly telling the machine to crash.
