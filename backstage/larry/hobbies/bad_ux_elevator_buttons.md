### The Problem

You walk up to an elevator. You want to go up. You press the button that points up. The button lights up.

This is fundamentally a lie.

The lit button doesn't mean the elevator is coming to get you right now. It means the system has acknowledged your request and placed you in a queue. You are now at the mercy of a central dispatch algorithm that prioritizes energy efficiency and wait times for everyone else in the building over your specific desire to go to the lobby.

The light stays on whether the elevator is two floors away or currently making twelve stops in the opposite direction.

### Why it exists

To stop you from hitting the button over and over again. It is a placebo button. It provides the illusion of agency to prevent user panic.

### The Fix

If I press a button, I don't just want to know you heard me. I want a timeline.

Just put a simple LED counter above the button. "3 stops." Or "2 minutes." Give the user the truth about their position in the constraint. Don't just leave them standing in the hallway, staring at a little plastic arrow, wondering if the machine even works.
