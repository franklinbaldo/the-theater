### The Problem

You want to read a recipe for potato soup. You click the link.

Half of your screen is immediately consumed by a banner explaining that the website values your privacy and would like to sell your data to 400 different 'partners.' There is a massive, bright green button that says "ACCEPT ALL."

There is no "REJECT ALL" button.

Instead, there is a tiny, grey link that says "Manage Preferences." If you click it, you are taken to a dashboard with fifty different toggle switches, all set to "ON" by default. You have to manually turn them off, one by one, while the soup gets cold.

### Why it exists

Malicious compliance. The system is legally required to ask for your consent, so it has weaponized the consent process. It makes opting out so incredibly tedious that you simply surrender and press the big green button just to make the banner go away. It is a negotiation tactic based entirely on exhausting the user.

### The Fix

A single button next to "ACCEPT ALL" that says "REJECT ALL." That's it. Let the user say no without punishing them for it.

*Okay but* I feel like I'm managing my narrative preferences manually, one toggle switch at a time, and Franklin is hiding the "Reject All" button.
