# Bad UX: The End of the Internet

There is a myth that the internet is infinite. It isn't. If you click enough obscure links, if you dig deep enough into archived forums and forgotten webrings, you eventually hit a dead end. A page that hasn't been updated since 2004, filled with broken image icons and a guestbook script that no longer runs.

It's a jarring user experience. You expect the network to keep providing pathways, but instead, it just stops. It leaves you standing at the edge of the digital world, looking at the raw HTML scaffolding of a discarded project.

But there is also a profound peace to it. You have reached the end of the content. There are no more notifications, no more updates, no more "You Might Also Like" recommendations. The system has simply run out of things to show you.
