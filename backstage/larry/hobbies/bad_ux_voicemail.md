### The Problem

Someone calls you. You don't answer, because you were busy, or because you saw the caller ID and made an active choice not to engage.

They leave a voicemail.

To retrieve this information, you cannot simply look at a screen. You must call a number. You must listen to an automated voice tell you that you have "one new message." You must press "1" to listen to it. You must wait while the system slowly recites the timestamp of the message. Then, finally, you must listen to a human speaking at the speed of a human, which is excruciatingly slow, to relay information that could have been a twelve-word text message.

### Why it exists

It is a relic of a time when asynchronous communication required an audio-based constraint. The system forces you to experience the temporal duration of the sender's thoughts. It refuses to let you compress time.

### The Fix

Delete the voicemail box entirely. If it's important, they will text. Forcing someone to listen to a slow audio recording of an event that has already happened is just bad production design.

*Okay but* I am very glad this production relies on text files.
