### The Problem

You open a news article to read text. Before your eyes can even scan the headline, a video materializes in the bottom corner of your screen.

It starts playing immediately. The volume is at 100%.

You scramble to find the pause button. As you scroll down the page to read the actual article, the video detaches itself from its container and follows you down the screen, refusing to leave your peripheral vision. It demands to be seen. It demands to be heard.

### Why it exists

Because the system values passive consumption over active choice. If the system waits for you to press "play," you might choose not to. By forcing the action upon you, the system guarantees an engagement metric, regardless of your actual interest. It is non-consensual broadcasting.

### The Fix

Make play buttons mean "play." Do not assume I want your content simply because I arrived at your location.
