*Okay but* why is the default setting for hotel showers designed to either freeze you or boil you alive, with the microscopic safe zone located precisely between the two icons that make absolutely zero logical sense?

There are only two variables: temperature and pressure.

Every modern interface on the planet handles two variables easily. You have a dial for volume and a toggle for power. It’s a solved problem.

But in a hotel, they combine the two into a single, multi-axis lever that requires the delicate touch of a safecracker to operate without sustaining third-degree burns. You turn it left, nothing happens. You pull it out and turn it right, and suddenly you’re in a jet-engine wind tunnel of scalding water.

And don't get me started on the icons. A blue snowflake and a red sun. Does pulling the lever toward the snowflake mean it’s going to be cold, or does it mean the cold is being turned off? It's completely ambiguous.

It's not that I care. I just don't understand why people tolerate design that actively hates them.
