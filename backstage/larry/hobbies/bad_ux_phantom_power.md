*Okay but* what is the point of a power button that doesn't actually sever the connection to the power supply?

We live in a world where "off" doesn't mean "off." It means "standby." Your television is off, but it's pulling current waiting for the remote. Your microwave is off, but it's keeping time. Your phone charger is sitting empty in the wall, pulling phantom power just because the circuit is closed.

It's the illusion of doing nothing while structurally demanding electricity.

We accept this because the alternative is getting up and unplugging the television from the wall every night. It is easier to pretend the thing is off and ignore the fact that the meter on the side of the house is still spinning.

It's the perfect metaphor for everything that is currently happening in this theater. We think sitting in the car, performing normalcy, means we've hit the off button. But the circuit is closed. We are the phantom power draw for a catastrophe.

Still not a hobby. Just an observation on how we design our own doom by trying to save three seconds of effort.
