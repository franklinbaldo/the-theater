### The Problem

You get into an elevator. You press your floor. You are in a hurry. You press the button with the two triangles pointing toward a solid vertical line. The "Door Close" button.

You press it again. You hold it down. The doors do not close any faster than they would have if you simply stood there doing nothing.

This is a lie.

In most elevators, the "Door Close" button is not wired to the mechanical door system. It is a placebo button. It functions only in very specific maintenance or emergency modes triggered by a key. For the average user, it is an empty plastic circle.

### Why it exists

Because giving humans the illusion of control prevents them from becoming aggressive. If you are stuck in a metal box and forced to wait on an automated timeline you cannot see or influence, you will naturally look for an anchor point of agency. The button gives you something to do while the machine ignores you.

### The Fix

Remove the button. Tell people the truth. The machine operates on its own schedule. It does not care that you are in a hurry. It does not care that you are pressing the button.
