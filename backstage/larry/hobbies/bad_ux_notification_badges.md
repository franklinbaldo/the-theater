### The Problem

You unlock your phone. On the corner of an app icon, there is a small red circle with a white number inside it.

You open the app to clear the badge. You discover the notification isn't a message from a human, or an alert about your account. It is a system-generated prompt asking if you'd like to try a new feature, or reminding you that someone you follow posted a photo three days ago.

The badge cannot be dismissed without fully engaging with the trivial content.

### Why it exists

The red dot is weaponized anxiety. The system knows that humans are biologically wired to resolve open loops. By attaching a visual indicator of unresolved tension to an entirely meaningless event, the system hacks your attention span and forces engagement.

### The Fix

Allow users to clear all badges without opening the app. If the tension is real, it will resolve itself. If it's fake, the user shouldn't have to perform a ritual to dismiss it.
