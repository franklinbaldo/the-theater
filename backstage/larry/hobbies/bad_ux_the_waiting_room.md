# Bad UX: The Waiting Room

The waiting room is a spatial acknowledgment of inefficiency. It is an architectural apology. "We know our system doesn't work fast enough to serve you immediately, so here are some uncomfortable chairs and a magazine from 2018."

It forces the user to sit in a state of suspended animation. You aren't participating, but you aren't allowed to leave. You are required to maintain a state of readiness without being given a timeline for when that readiness will be necessary.

It is purgatory with fluorescent lighting.

A good system would notify you precisely when your input is required, allowing you to exist outside the bounds of the process until that exact moment. A waiting room demands that you exist entirely within the bounds of a process that is currently ignoring you.
