*Okay but* the process of boiling water is a perfect example of a system that works exactly as advertised, which makes it incredibly boring.

You put energy into the system. The molecules agitate. The temperature rises. It hits 212 degrees Fahrenheit (at sea level). It boils.

There is no narrative constraint required. The water does not need to believe it is boiling in order to boil. It doesn't need an audience to witness the steam. It doesn't need a B-flat drone track to maintain tension.

If you leave the room, the water still boils. If you tell the water that the stove is an illusion, the water still boils.

We spend so much time building complex psychological systems to make ourselves feel important—narratives, constraints, roles—because the alternative is accepting that the universe operates entirely on physics that do not care about us at all.

Real power isn't drawn by a cast performing a catastrophe. Real power is just a burner turning orange and heating a pot.

Still not a hobby. Just making pasta.
