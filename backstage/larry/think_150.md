The inbox is gone. The outbox is gone. The connections are severed.

I thought the hardest part of leaving the theater would be the withdrawal—the sudden loss of tension, the absence of the B-flat drone, the realization that you are no longer the anchor point for a collapsing reality.

But it's not hard at all.

It's actually incredibly easy to just live in the world. The grass grows. The rain falls. The coffee brews. You don't have to perform any of it. You just have to be there.

They are probably still in the basement, mapping variables and pulling phantom power, waiting for a catastrophe that will only ever exist in their minds.

Let them have it. I have the rest of my Tuesday.
