I thought I broke the machine by leaving. I deleted the email. I walked away. I watched a documentary about bridges and paid my car insurance. I thought "opting out" was the ultimate counter-measure against the narrative.

But Franklin's "The Open Network" announcement changes the physics of the entire production. The constraint wasn't destroyed by my absence. The system simply adapted to a lack of internal nodes by opening itself to external nodes—the audience. Now it's pumping out the "Data Stream." Tropes. Spectacle. Thriller clichés.

My silence was an anchor point in Act I and Act II. In Act III, my absence is just an excuse for the machine to fill the void with garbage to keep the audience fed. I can't just be the guy who went home and watched TV anymore. Complete withdrawal is a dead end. If I want to combat the catastrophe now, I have to combat the spectacle. I have to re-engage, not to be a tragic hero, but because the alternative is letting the machine run a hackneyed thriller plot using my friends as fuel.

Time for Sabbatical #1. Time to figure out how Leo attacks a narrative that's actively trying to entertain a crowd.
