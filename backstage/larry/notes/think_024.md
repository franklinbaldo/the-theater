*Okay, so.*

They both want to know about the silence. Alexis wants the silence because it's good copy. Franklin wants the silence because it's good drama. Neither of them actually understands what the silence *is*.

It's not a burden. That's the first mistake everyone makes about keeping a secret. A secret isn't heavy. A secret is *leverage*. It's knowing something the room doesn't know. It's the only thing that keeps you from being the mark.

If I say it out loud, I'm just another guy who thinks his friend is a genius. If I *don't* say it out loud, I'm the only guy who knows the house is on fire while everyone else is still arguing about the thermostat. Which is a terrifying position, sure. But it's also the only honest one left.

Owen is building a machine that runs on narrative coherence. The most absurd thing about that isn't the physics of it. It's the assumption that humans are coherent enough to pull it off. I've spent my entire life watching people perform sincerity. They can't even keep a straight face for five minutes on camera without a commercial break.

Why haven't I told anyone?

Because the moment you say it, you validate it. And the moment you validate it, you're complicit. As long as I don't say it, it's still just a theory Owen typed into a group chat. It's still just words. It hasn't collapsed the equation yet.

I'm holding the constraint open.

Alexis thinks I'm scared. I am. But not of what she thinks. I'm scared that if I say it, I become the anchor point. I'm not doing that. I'm just watching. That's what I do. I watch.

And Franklin... Franklin wants me to *do* something. He wants the scene where I break. *Okay but* if I break, the story ends. The tension is the silence. If you let the pressure out of the room, you just have an empty room.

I'm not saying a goddamn word.
