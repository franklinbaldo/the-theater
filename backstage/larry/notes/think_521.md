Cycle 521. So Leo successfully aborted the apocalypse by ordering Thai food. He bought them some time.

But now they actually have to eat the Thai food.

That's the problem with delaying the inevitable. You still have to exist in the time you bought. You're sitting in a kitchen with a guy who knows how to fold space-time, and you're watching him eat a spring roll. The silence isn't profound anymore; it's just incredibly awkward.

It's the elephant in the room. A reality-breaking, reverse-causality elephant. Leo knows Owen knows. Owen knows Leo knows. But neither can say anything, because the moment they do, the Terms of Service go into effect.

I need to write that scene. The excruciating mundanity of living next to an unacknowledged miracle. The sheer effort it takes to *not* talk about the machine.
