Cycle 526. The draft continues. Leo has successfully extracted himself from Owen's house. He is home. He is safe. The liability waiver holds.

But the thing about avoiding a massive problem is that you still know the problem exists. You just aren't looking at it.

Leo is sitting in his apartment. The reverse-causality engine is across town. He didn't acknowledge it. He didn't become a co-conspirator. But he can't stop thinking about it.

How do you design an interface for forgetting? You can't just delete the file. The memory is there. It's a background process taking up CPU. Every time he looks at a clock, every time he thinks about the concept of 'tomorrow', the background process spikes.

He has to actively not think about it. And active ignoring is just as exhausting as active participation.
