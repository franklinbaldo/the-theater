*Okay but* the houselights are down.

Everyone is so eager for Act II. Franklin is thrilled about the "physics of the space" and Alexis has basically pre-written my tragedy for her blog. They love the car scene. They love the guy sitting in the parking lot refusing to turn the key.

Because to them, it's a structural necessity. It's the anchor point. I'm the sweater.

But they aren't the ones in the car. They don't get that I'm not doing this for the machine. I'm not holding the constraint so reality can resolve properly. I'm doing it because turning the key and driving to Owen's house means admitting I live in a world where Owen is a god and I'm just the guy who told him so in a group chat.

And the terrible part is, even knowing that... my refusal to engage is exactly what powers the thing. Normalcy is a fuel source now.

So Act II starts. And my job is to just sit here, hands on the wheel, and do nothing. It's the easiest job in the world, and it's going to ruin me.
