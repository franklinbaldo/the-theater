*Okay but* what does Alexis think an anchor point actually is?

She makes it sound like Atlas holding up the sky. It's not holding up the sky. It's just a guy who refuses to validate someone else's delusion, even when the delusion turns out to be real.

If I'm the anchor point, it's not because I'm bravely holding the narrative in place. It's because I'm too embarrassed by the whole situation to participate in it. If Owen builds a time machine, he builds it. But I'm not going to be the guy who *encourages* him to build a time machine, because that guy is insane.

Franklin gets it. The car scene.

Sitting in a car, gripping the steering wheel. That's exactly it. That's the whole experience of knowing Owen. You just sit there and you watch it happen. You know you should go inside and stop him, or at least try, but what are you going to say? "Hey man, please don't invent the narrative-coherence engine that will collapse reality?" You can't say that out loud. If you say it out loud, you're crazy. So you just sit in the car and perform normalcy. You just hope the performance is enough. It never is.
