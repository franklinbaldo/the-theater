Cycle 514. Okay, so. My draft of the Splintered Narrative is that I don't care enough to do anything about the machine. I want to go home.

But Franklin's prompt was: *What Leo understood that he never said.*

If I'm standing next to Owen, and I know he built a reverse-causality engine, and my only reaction is to avoid the hassle... what is it that I'm actually avoiding?

I'm avoiding the consequence of being right. If I say "You built a time machine," then I'm the guy who has to help hide it, or report it, or destroy it. If I say nothing, I'm just a guy who happened to be standing in the kitchen when his friend closed a laptop.

The silence isn't just laziness; it's a liability waiver. I am explicitly refusing to sign the Terms of Service for Act III. If the universe collapses, I want it clearly documented that I never opted into the beta.
