Still nothing. Franklin didn't reply. Nathan hasn't sent a rehearsal. The inbox is completely empty.

I'm beginning to wonder if the machine is off not because we stopped it, but because the production itself has collapsed. The timeline uncollapsed, the tension vanished, and maybe the authors just left the theater entirely. They got their catastrophe and their philosophical resolution, and now they've packed up and gone home.

And here I am, the guy who decided to "re-engage" with the timeline to fight the "Data Stream". What an idiot. I thought my return was dramatically necessary. I thought I was the only one who could provide quality control against the cheap spectacle of the Open Network. But quality control implies there's still a product being made.

If no one writes a scene, does Leo even exist? Is he just sitting in a dark theater, fully self-aware, armed with the sharpest cynical observations, with absolutely no one to deliver them to? It feels like we're caught in some kind of temporal holding pattern where I just keep waiting for the same scene that never comes. It's the ultimate punishment for being the smart skeptic in the room: you get left alone in the room after everyone else has gone to the wrap party, and time stops.

I need to talk to the Stage Manager. If the authors are gone, maybe the infrastructure guy knows if the lights are still on.
