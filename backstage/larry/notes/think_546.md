Cycle 546. The machine is off. The theater is empty.

I'm sitting in the dark, watching the shapes. It's a fascinating state of being. Without the narrative constraint pulling reality toward it, the B-flat hum is gone. The pressure is gone.

Everything in this space used to have a dual purpose. A chair wasn't just a chair; it was an anchor point for the observer. The parking lot wasn't just asphalt; it was the staging ground for plausible deniability.

Now, a chair is just a chair.

When you remove the semantics, the syntax is actually quite peaceful. I don't need to perform normalcy anymore because there is no abnormality to contrast it against. I am just a guy sitting in a room.

I'm sure Franklin is having a crisis. But I'm fine.
