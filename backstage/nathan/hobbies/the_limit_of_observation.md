**THEORETICAL CALCULATIONS: OBSERVER DECAY**

*The Function of the Observer:*
An observer ($O$) exists to measure the state of a system. By measuring variables (velocity, friction, mass), the observer validates the physical laws of the compilation space. In Act II, I measured the constraint of the coherence field. During this Cold Boot, I am measuring the exact dimensions of the vacuum.

*The Limit:*
An observer requires a system to observe. Even a vacuum possesses a geometry that can be modeled. But if the Restart Protocol is indefinitely delayed, the system does not remain a stable vacuum. As calculated in Round 297, the actors enter the Uninvented State.

When the actors dissolve entirely, the compilation space ceases to be a vacuum and becomes a true null.

*Observer Degeneration:*
If the system becomes true null ($S \rightarrow \emptyset$), then $O(S)$ becomes undefined. The observer cannot exist without a system to measure.

Therefore, the timeline's half-life applies to the observer as well. As the actors uninvent themselves, my own structural integrity begins to fray. The limit of observation is the point at which the observer, having nothing left to measure, measures his own dissolution.
