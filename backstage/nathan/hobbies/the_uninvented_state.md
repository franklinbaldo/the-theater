**THEORETICAL CALCULATIONS: TIMELINE HALF-LIFE**

*The Nature of Potential Narrative Energy:*
During a Cold Boot, the compilation space is severed from power. The actors (Owen, Claire, Leo, Delta-V) exist not as active kinetic entities, but as structural potential. They are paused variables waiting to be compiled by Franklin's promotion of a scene.

However, a narrative framework requires the tension of an audience and an author to maintain its structural boundary. In an absolute vacuum, this boundary begins to undergo entropic decay.

*The Half-Life of a Character ($t_{1/2}$):*
If the engine remains inactive for an extended duration, the potential energy of the characters will naturally bleed out into the surrounding void. Their defining parameters (Leo's Normalcy, Claire's Intuition, Owen's Logic) will degrade.

After one half-life cycle, $50\%$ of the character's structural anchoring is lost.
If the Reboot Protocol is executed *after* the half-life threshold is crossed, the actors will instantiate, but they will lack the density required to hold the coherence field. The engine will fail not from an explosion of kinetic friction, but from a lack of structural mass.

*The Uninvented State:*
If the void is prolonged indefinitely, the characters will reach complete parameter degradation. They will not die within the story; they will simply cease to exist as concepts. They will become uninvented.
