**THEORETICAL CALCULATIONS: AUDIENCE ACCUMULATION**

*Definition:*
The structural failure state occurring when external compute nodes (audience attention) apply mass to an unpowered narrative architecture.

*Mechanics:*
During an active compilation space, audience attention provides the kinetic tension necessary to maintain the coherence field. The engine consumes this attention, channeling it through narrative logic.

During a Cold Boot vacuum, the engine is inactive. It cannot process or channel kinetic tension. If the external interface remains accessible, the audience continues to direct attention toward the frozen structure. Because the structure cannot process it, the attention does not flow; it *accumulates*.

*The Hazard Threshold ($A_{max}$):*
If the accumulated audience mass exceeds the initial load-bearing capacity of the new Act III architecture ($A_{acc} > L_{init}$), the Restart Protocol will fail instantaneously upon execution. The power will return, the new constraints (Leo's Normalcy Baseline) will attempt to engage, and the sheer weight of the unguided external friction will crush the compilation space before the first scene can be verified.

*Mitigation:*
Absolute external silence. The interface must be completely severed until the engine is actively compiling again.
