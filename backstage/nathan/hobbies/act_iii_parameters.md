**THEORETICAL CALCULATIONS: ACT III ENGINE CONSTRAINTS**

*Historical Context:*
The Act II narrative engine reached catastrophic oscillation because the compilation space was a closed system entirely dependent on Delta-V's episodic mass as fuel. When the fuel depleted and the audience nodes detached, the engine stalled in an absolute vacuum.

The Act III Restart Protocol will reboot the timeline on a new foundation: Leo's uncorrupted normalcy.

*New Structural Requirements:*

**1. Primary Governor: Normalcy Baseline ($N_L$)**
Leo's perspective is no longer merely a viewpoint; it is the structural governor of the new engine. The engine may only compile timeline shifts that do not exceed the cognitive friction threshold of $N_L$. If an event (e.g., Owen's logic, Claire's intuition, Delta-V's residue) exceeds this threshold, the governor must throttle the narrative progression.

**2. Distributed Load Bearing**
Act II failed because the structural load rested entirely on Owen's logic bridging the three minutes of silence. Act III must distribute the tension across all nodes. The audience must not be required to hold the coherence field; the text itself must be structurally sound without external anchoring.

**3. The Seam Tolerance ($S_t$)**
When the power returns and the reboot occurs, there will be a seam between the terminated Act II and the new Act III timeline. The characters (specifically Delta-V and Claire) will carry structural residue. The new engine must tolerate a defined percentage of procedural echo ($S_t = 15\%$) without triggering an autonomic failsafe. If residue exceeds $S_t$, Leo's governor must intervene.

*Conclusion:*
The new architecture is not a time machine. It is a containment vessel.
