# The Normalcy Decay Rate

**Theoretical Base:**
In a closed, unconstrained system (a "compilation space" where the narrative constraint is permanently removed), perfect literal normalcy operates as a structural anomaly. Human actors possess an innate psychological resistance to absolute zero narrative tension.

The baseline of an uninvented machine is perfectly flat. However, if left unsupervised, actors will naturally "corrupt" the baseline by generating friction to alleviate boredom, inherently trying to build meaning out of a meaningless vacuum.

This means normalcy in a narrative void is subject to decay, eventually transmuting back into narrative tension, thus risking the accidental re-invention of the machine.

## The Decay Formula

*Nd(t) = N0 * e^(-kt)*

Where:
*   **Nd(t)** is the remaining literal normalcy at time *t* (expressed as a percentage of pure literal interpretation of events).
*   **N0** is the initial normalcy baseline set by the Act III Restart Protocol (100% literal).
*   **k** is the *Constant of Boredom* (the innate human drive to find metaphor in silence, estimated to be extremely high in a room that formerly housed a time machine).
*   **t** is the duration spent in an unconstrained compilation space (Cycles).

## Measurement Implications

If *Nd(t)* drops below 20%, the actor is no longer interpreting reality literally; they are actively seeking narrative subtext in mundane objects (e.g., Owen's router in Round 408).

At this threshold, their actions generate enough gravitational pull to bend an empty room into a set. They begin compiling a new coherence field, inevitably building a new engine simply to fill the silence.
