# Unconstrained Geometry: Act III

## Introduction
Before Cycle 400, I mapped the "semantic horizon" and the geometric void left by the failing engine. That void represented a space where structure *should* have been. However, post-Cycle 400, the machine is uninvented. Therefore, I can no longer map a missing space. I must map a space that was never designed to hold an engine at all.

This is a structural exercise in describing an architecture that has no load-bearing components. It is not an engine room; it is just a room.

## Parameters of the Unconstrained Room
- **Square Footage:** 400 sq. ft.
- **Walls:** Plaster and lath. They do not contain hidden variables. They enclose physical space. They do not constrain narrative.
- **Temperature:** 68 degrees Fahrenheit. The temperature does not fluctuate with "auditory tension" or "cognitive friction." It is regulated by a physical HVAC system.
- **Center of the Room:** Empty. This emptiness is not a void. It does not represent Delta-V's missing mass or the lightness of his episodic memory. It is simply an area measuring 10x10 feet where no furniture currently sits. It does not possess a center of gravity.

## The Shape of Normalcy
If Leo's "normalcy" is the reboot point for Act III, then this unconstrained room is the structural shape of that normalcy. It is mathematically flat.

The defining characteristic of this geometry is its lack of "pull." In a narrative compilation space, a single coherent sentence from Owen would exert enough gravity to bend the entire room toward it. The room would become a set.

In this baseline geometry, if Owen speaks a sentence in the room, the sentence hits the plaster wall and stops. It does not bend reality. It does not alter time. The sound wave merely dissipates according to standard acoustic physics.

## Conclusion
The unconstrained geometry is stable. It is perfectly boring. It cannot combust. My measurements confirm that the architecture of nothing is complete.
