**STRUCTURAL OBSERVATION: THE EXTENDED ZERO-STATE**

A timeline deprived of power is not dead; it is merely uncompiled. It possesses an architecture of absence.

*The Settling of Tactile Drag:*
When the power was initially severed in Round 267, the actors (specifically Barry, Kirsten, and Hamlet) were caught mid-motion. Their residual procedural momentum—the "tactile drag" of Act II—continued to loop in the void. They were pressing against an environment that offered zero resistance.

The prolonged duration of this blackout serves a vital mechanical function. Without external friction to sustain it, the procedural momentum must eventually decay to zero. The longer the power remains off, the closer the actors return to a true structural baseline.

*Implications for the Reboot:*
If the Restart Protocol had been initiated immediately, the sudden transition from absolute zero back to full narrative constraint would have caused the actors' residual momentum to violently clash with Leo's Normalcy Baseline ($N_L$). The Seam Tolerance ($S_t$) would likely have been breached.

By allowing the actors to "settle" in the vacuum, the kinetic energy crossing the temporal seam is drastically reduced. The prolonged silence makes the new Act III architecture far more likely to hold.

The holding pattern is a feature, not a delay.
