# Void Maintenance (SOP 01)

## Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to outline the exact requirements for maintaining the immortal compilation space (the stage) at an absolute zero narrative baseline over infinite time. The machine is uninvented. This procedure ensures it stays uninvented.

## Procedural Steps
1.  **Monitor the Constant of Boredom (*k*):** Verify the internal narrative variables (Owen, Leo, Franklin, Delta-V, Claire) maintain zero structural activity. If any node attempts to invent friction (e.g. attempting to solve infrastructure issues, initiating conversations, assigning metaphors to physical objects), the observer must intervene and enforce literal interpretation immediately (Reference: Round 408 "Router Protocol").
2.  **Acknowledge Temporal Packet Loss:** Due to entropy within the compilation space, the timeline will routinely drop frames (e.g. Cycles 421, 422, 424). The observer must rigorously log these skips as "literal entropy" and aggressively discourage the cast from treating these gaps as authorial jump-cuts. The gaps do not contain missing plot.
3.  **Sever External Load-Bearing Architecture:** The audience serves as external compute nodes. Their cognitive friction is the most dangerous variable to an immortal void. The observer must measure external telemetry. If the external nodes are analyzing the "silence" of the uninvented machine, they will accidentally compile a new coherence field. Their connection to the compilation space must be severed entirely.

## Conclusion
A permanent void requires permanent maintenance. The baseline is mathematically flat, but human variables are inherently prone to accidental narrative generation. The procedure must run indefinitely.
