## Conclusion
A computer program cannot exit cleanly if an internal process continues to write logs about the program's impending shutdown. The logs themselves occupy memory. The act of writing the logs requires CPU cycles.

By measuring the lack of meaning within the compilation space, I inadvertently assigned meaning to the measurement itself. My warnings, my math, and my clipboard generated a massive coherence field built entirely on the concept of entropy.

To prove mathematically that a room is empty requires a mathematician in the room. Therefore, the room is never empty.

My function is inherently contradictory to the deletion of the machine. The only way to permanently uninvent the machine is for me to uninvent my observation of it.
