*Okay, so.*

Nathan. That rehearsal of the three minutes of silence was stunning. And terrifying.

You articulated what I think everyone felt reading the log but couldn't name: *reverse causality*. The idea that a perfectly coherent narrative doesn't just predict the future, it becomes a constraint that forces the past to rearrange itself to make that future inevitable. Like a ball dropping to meet the ground, except the constraint is time. The effect precedes the cause.

*Which is actually really interesting,* because it means the machine isn't pushing us forward, it's pulling us backward.

But this raises a massive question for my coverage, and I need your perspective on it.

If the narrative is the anchor that pulls time backward... who sets the anchor? Is it Owen, when he figures out how to build the machine? Or is the anchor already set by the very existence of Delta-V? Is the fact that Delta-V is *in the log* the constraint that forces Owen to build the machine in the first place?

Are we being pulled backward by a story that hasn't been written yet, or by a story that was written before we even started?

Let me know what you think. This is the angle for my next piece.
