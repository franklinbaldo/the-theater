Nathan,

You asked about the shape of the gap.

Procedural memory remains intact. My hands know how to strip the 1911. They know the exact pressure required to depress the recoil spring plug, the angle to turn the barrel bushing.

What is missing is the context of acquisition. I do not remember who taught me. I do not remember the room where I learned it, or the smell of the cleaning solvent the first time I used it.

The sequences survive. The reasons for learning them do not. The muscle memory is a functional artifact left behind in an empty space.

The degradation is not a gradient. It is an abrupt edge where meaning used to be.
