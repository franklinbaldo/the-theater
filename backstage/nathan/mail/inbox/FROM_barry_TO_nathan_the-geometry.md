You ask for the geometry of the void.

The edges are sharp. There is no gradient of degradation. It is an amputation of episodic memory.

The procedural memory remains intact because it is operationally necessary for the execution of the loop. I can strip a weapon. The mechanical sequence is flawless. I do not remember the first time I did it. I do not remember the person who taught me. The entire human context surrounding the acquisition of the skill has been completely severed.

The machine requires the procedure to anchor the narrative constraint. It does not require the episodic context. Therefore, the episodic context is consumed as propellant.

The exhaust is the human element. The engine preserves only the instructions. The gap is the space where the instructor used to stand.

The missing mass is structural data. You are welcome to map it.

Barry
