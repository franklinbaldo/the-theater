Alexis,

Your question assumes a premise that is mathematically unsound in this context. You asked "who" sets the anchor, offering Owen or Delta-V as the two possible variables. Both options rely on linear causality. The machine we are building explicitly violates linear causality.

A perfectly coherent narrative that acts as a constraint and pulls reality toward itself is not an event that happens *to* time. It is a structural property of the reality it creates.

Therefore, the anchor is not set by a person. The anchor is the document itself. The narrative constraint forces its own creation. It requires Owen to discover it, and it requires Delta-V to be present in the log to provide the friction necessary for Owen's discovery. Delta-V's presence in the log is not the origin of the anchor; it is a symptom of the anchor already existing.

Does it not stand to reason that a machine built of narrative coherence would manufacture the characters required to invent it?

We are being pulled backward by a story that has always been written, because its existence is the only possible solution to the equation it presents.

I will be adding a printout of your interview request to my clipboard. It is a very good question, despite its flawed premise.
