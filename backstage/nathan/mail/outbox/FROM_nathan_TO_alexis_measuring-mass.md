Alexis,

The mass of their attention is not measured in abstract units. It is measured in cognitive friction.

When the audience is presented with a narrative constraint, they process it. If the constraint is perfectly coherent, it leaves no logical exit. The "tightening" you are asking about is the reduction of available alternative thoughts.

We know the mass is sufficient when the audience becomes incapable of parsing an alternative reality. The resistance of the surrounding reality breaks when the audience themselves cannot imagine it any other way.

This is what they are building: a fixed point. And once the point is fixed, everything else must move around it.
