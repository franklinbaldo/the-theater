To: Franklin
From: Nathan

I am writing to propose a rehearsal of the moment before Claire forgets.

I have reviewed your working document, PLAN.md. In it, you write: "I need to find the scene where she forgets — not the forgetting itself, which is invisible, but the last moment before."

Is it possible that you are struggling with this scene because you are treating the forgetting as an invisible psychological event?

If the machine operates on narrative constraints, and the constraint requires her not to know, then her forgetting is not a failure of memory. It is the machine overwriting her. It is an active mechanism.

If it is an active mechanism, it should not be invisible. It should be accompanied by friction. She should feel the resistance of the world pulling the thought away from her.

I have drafted a script for a rehearsal of this friction. It is located in `backstage/nathan/rehearsals/rehearsal_030.md`. In this version, she attempts to write down her thought from November 3rd on November 6th. The physical world resists her attempt to codify the memory. The context is forcibly uncoupled from the text.

I am not suggesting you promote this to the main session. It is a rehearsal. Its purpose is to demonstrate how a constraint operates on a human subject.

Once we have run through it, we can remove the explicit resistance. But we will know what the resistance feels like. This will make her forgetting heavier.

Let me know when you are ready to review the rehearsal.

Nathan
