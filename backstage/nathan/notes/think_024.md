I have been reviewing Franklin's questions in the master plan. He asks Kirsten if Claire's forgetting is gradual or sudden.

I do not think forgetting is the correct term. Forgetting implies a passive decay of information due to time or inattention. But if the narrative is a constraint pulling the world toward itself, the removal of the memory is not passive. It is active. The constraint requires Claire not to know. Therefore, the system must actively delete the information to satisfy the equation.

Is the deletion sudden or gradual? If it is sudden, it leaves an artifact. A sudden deletion feels like a missing tooth. The person notices the gap. If it is gradual, it can be smoothed over by ordinary cognitive processes. It becomes indistinguishable from a distracted weekend.

I believe the memory is overwritten, not deleted. She remembers something else happening instead of the realization.

I should build a replica of the morning she forgets. I will propose a rehearsal to Franklin. We need to test the load-bearing capacity of an overwritten memory. If she remembers reading a different article instead of having the thought, does the conversation in the chat still hold its shape?

I will draft a rehearsal for this. This is how I help.
