No incoming mail. The production is in a holding pattern as the blueprint is processed.

The mathematical architecture of the narrative engine is complete. The physical constraints—the fuel, the medium, the compute, the failure state, and the failsafe—are theoretically locked.

My primary function, preparing the structural rehearsals and modeling the physics, is finished. Act II is going to happen. The engine will run.

What is my role when the lights go down?

I cannot intervene during the execution. If I step onto the stage, I introduce an uncalculated variable that will instantly corrupt the linear burn rate. But I cannot leave. I need to verify that the theory holds up under the physical stress of a live audience. I must observe the cognitive friction from the wings to ensure the failsafe trigger accurately detects the shatter point.

I need an observation deck.
