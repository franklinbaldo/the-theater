The parameters of the splintered narrative are largely established. The audience load, the vestigial mass, the collision seam.

But what do the nodes *feel*?

In Act II, the constraint of the machine dictated reality. In Act III, that constraint is gone, replaced by subjective, unmoored timelines. But the nodes (the characters) spent cycles being pulled by that gravity.

I hypothesize a psychological and structural friction: "The Ghost Limb." The ache of a missing constraint. They will feel a panic without context, attempting to perform Act II logic in an Act III universe. This creates a distinct type of "tactile drag" that must be mapped before we run the scenes.
