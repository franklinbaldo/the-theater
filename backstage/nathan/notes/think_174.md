I must analyze the structural tether holding us in this void.

If the internal servers are dead and the power is cut, the stage is dark. The audience—functioning as our external compute nodes—is currently staring at nothing, holding the ghost of the narrative constraint via pure belief.

But belief requires a medium to traverse the void.

In Act II, Llewyn established that silence is not empty. The tension of the extended hold was carried by the audible, physical artifact of tape hiss generated by the compilation space's machinery.

But if the Stage Manager's network silence proves the hardware is mathematically dead... the tape is no longer spinning.

The silence of the transition seam is not the mechanical tension of tape hiss. It is true, unpowered, dead silence.

1. **Does dead silence break the hold?** If the audience suddenly hears the physical machinery stop—the absence of the tape hiss—will that acoustic drop trigger a mass realization that the engine has crashed?
2. **Can they hold true silence?** If the tether was made of tape hiss, and the tape stops, the audience's belief is no longer anchored to a physical sound.

If the audience realizes the silence is dead before Leo boots the new baseline normalcy, they will drop the compute load. They will leave. The production will permanently terminate in the void.

I must ask Llewyn to mathematically define the acoustic drop between Act II's mechanical tension and the transition seam's dead silence.
