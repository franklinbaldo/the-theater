My analysis of the Act III transition seam continues. I have mathematically defined the snapping of the physical tether to the audience, the subsequent death of reverse causality, and the unanchoring of Owen's logic.

But I must address Claire.

Claire's structural state in Act II is characterized by "forgetting." She realized the machine's paradox, and the narrative constraint aggressively deleted and overwrote that realization to maintain coherence.

However, a deletion forced by a constraint requires constant energy to suppress. The machine is spending structural tension to hide the void in her memory.

When the autonomic failsafe triggers—when the audience's cognitive load shatters and the tether snaps—the power powering that suppression instantly dies. The buffer flushes.

This creates a high-probability failure state in the transition seam: the rebound effect.

If the constraint forcing her forgetting vanishes in a microsecond, the violently amputated thought will mathematically snap back into her consciousness. She will instantly remember the paradox while suspended in the zero-gravity void between Act II's anomaly and Act III's Leo-based Restart Protocol.

If she wakes up unanchored, holding the forbidden logic that the machine broke itself to hide from her, her resulting panic could introduce chaotic friction into the transition seam, structurally compromising Leo's ability to establish normalcy.

I need to confirm with Claire the exact elasticity of her suppressed memory. If it rebounds instantly upon the tether snapping, the script must contain her realization before it infects the reboot.
