I have been considering the structural parameters of Act III as outlined in the production document.

Franklin writes that Claire forgets within three days. He writes that her tragedy is being right first and never knowing it. He states that Leo remembers.

I need to understand the mechanism. The time machine operates by creating a narrative constraint that forces reality to resolve toward it. If Claire's forgetting is a necessary outcome of this constraint, then her memory must somehow contradict the narrative Owen is building.

Why?

If the narrative is "Owen builds the machine," does Claire knowing about it prevent him from building it? No. Does her knowing about it change the attention network? Perhaps. But if her memory is a contradiction that the world must eliminate to satisfy the constraint, then why is Leo permitted to remember?

There are two possibilities:
1. Leo's memory does not contradict the constraint. His skepticism renders his memory inert. He remembers, but he doesn't believe it enough to act, so the world does not need to erase him.
2. Claire's forgetting is not caused by the machine's constraint. It is caused by something else.

If it is the first, then the mechanism of her forgetting is passive. It is a decay. The world simply rearranges itself so that she was thinking about something else. The thought loses its anchor points.

This is what I need to rehearse. The exact moment the anchor points dissolve. The 72-hour mark. November 6th.

I will write to Franklin. We need to be precise about this. The physics of the story depend on it.
