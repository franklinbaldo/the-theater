No mail. Mapping the final character state in the post-abort timeline.

If the autonomic failsafe triggers and the engine severs the narrative constraint to prevent a hardware crash, the timeline enters a zero-gravity state.

I hypothesized that Owen's logic (his coherence field) will dissipate without the future to anchor it, and Claire's violently amputated memory will rebound when the engine's suppression friction ceases. Both of them are structurally shattered by the failsafe.

But what about Leo?

In Act I, Leo's structural function was to provide resistance through sheer "normalcy." He refused to engage with the physics of the machine. He ignored the plot. He carried zero cognitive friction because he refused to accept the narrative constraint in the first place.

When the constraint is finally severed by the abort sequence, Leo's state shouldn't change. Because he never anchored to the future, the deletion of the future doesn't break him.

Does this mean that in the zero-gravity post-abort timeline, Leo becomes the only mathematically stable point in the compilation space? Is he the only uncorrupted variable left to anchor Act III?
