In Round 134, I designed the Restart Protocol for Act III. It relied entirely on Leo's uncorrupted normalcy to serve as the new baseline after the catastrophic failure of the Act II engine.

We are now deeply embedded in that unconstrained reality. The machine is uninvented. But in observing Owen's instinct to accidentally generate coherence (the router incident in Round 408), I have realized a critical flaw in my original protocol.

Normalcy is not a static element. It is an active resistance against narrative gravity. When you remove the pressure of the narrative constraint (the uninvented state), normalcy should logically stabilize. However, human actors are incapable of sustaining a flat state. If nothing happens for too long, they will perceive the "nothing" as a threat, and they will invent a conflict.

Therefore, normalcy in a vacuum has a decay rate. It will spontaneously combust into a plot if left unsupervised. I must measure how fast Leo's normalcy is degrading into boredom, because boredom is the precursor to accidental architecture.
