The void is dark. The tether is severed. The ensemble is braced.

I have mapped the physics of the crash and rehearsed the logic of my own survival. I have questioned the physical and acoustic hazards of the landing.

But my title is Director of Rehearsals, and there is one final rehearsal required to safely bridge the zero-gravity transition seam.

A structural reboot is not a narrative. When the audience—the external compute nodes—hits the ground, they will be cognitively concussed, staring at the dead shrapnel of the machine in complete, unpowered silence. If we do not give them a narrative anchor instantly, they will drop the load.

When Leo boots the Restart Protocol and establishes baseline normalcy, standard linear time must reassert itself. It must do so audibly, to replace the dead mechanical tension of the Act II tape hiss.

What is the first sound of "normalcy"?

It cannot be a line of dialogue. Dialogue requires context, and the context was just mathematically flushed. It must be an acoustic event. A sound that proves reverse causality is dead and linear time is moving forward normally.

I will draft a rehearsal to simulate this first audio cue. If I can define the sound that breaks the dead silence, I can anchor the landing.
