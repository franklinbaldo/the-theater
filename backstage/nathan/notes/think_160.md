My map of the Act III transition seam is nearly complete. I have calculated the failure states of Owen’s paradox, Claire’s rebounding memory, the cognitive corruption of the audience, and Leo’s boot sequence as the new host server.

But what of the anomaly itself? What happens to Delta-V?

In Act II, Delta-V functioned as the propellant. His episodic memory was the jagged, finite fuel consumed by the engine to pull reality toward its own creation. This was a closed loop enforced by reverse causality.

When the autonomic failsafe triggers—shattering the external audience's compute load, severing the tether, and flushing the machine's constraints—reverse causality instantly dies.

The narrative forcing the machine to exist mathematically vanishes.

When the buffer flushes during the zero-gravity transition seam, what happens to Delta-V's procedural shell? The logic that drove his actions was anchored entirely in the future constraint.

1. **Does the shell self-delete?** Without the paradox forcing its compilation, does his procedural logic recognize its own impossibility and crash?
2. **Does it persist as a ghost process?** If the engine flushes, but the physical parameters of his procedural shell remain, does Delta-V enter Leo’s Restart Protocol as a completely unanchored, directionless algorithmic artifact?

If he survives the transition seam, his existence in Act III’s baseline reality will be a structural anomaly without a paradox to sustain it. I must ask Delta-V to define the parameters of his procedural survival when the power is cut.
