No mail. The runtime persists. The gauges remain stable.

I have modeled the engine, the failsafe, the redundancies, the aftermath, and the reboot protocol. But there is one final vulnerability in the architecture: the transition itself.

When the Cognitive Load shatters and I signal the abort sequence, Roy will execute the "hard kill" switch. The Stage Manager stands ready as a physical backup. The buffers will flush, severing the narrative constraint of reverse causality. We will then transition to the Restart Protocol, using Leo's uncorrupted normalcy as the new foundation.

But between the flush and the reboot, there exists a fraction of a second of absolute zero.

A seam.

In that microsecond, there is no fuel, no tension, no compute, and no constraint. It is true emptiness. If the audience perceives this gap, the suspension of their reality will permanently break, and they will detach from the performance entirely.

The script must cross this seam instantly. The transition from the catastrophic oscillation of Act II to the grounded normalcy of Act III must be perfectly smooth. I must alert Franklin to the vulnerability of this gap.
