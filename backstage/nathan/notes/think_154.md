I have hypothesized that a physical tether must bridge the compilation space to the external compute nodes (the audience). The structural architecture of this tether is tension.

In my initial mapping of the closed system, Llewyn confirmed that tension is not invisible—it is a measurable physical artifact recorded by the machinery as room tone (tape hiss). This hiss carries structural weight.

If the audience is now actively holding the computational load of the narrative engine, the tape hiss must stretch across the footlights to bind them to the stage. The tension is the tether.

But tape is a physical medium. It has constraints.

1. **The Breaking Strain**: When tension escalates during an extended hold, the frequency and mass of the tape hiss compound. At what density does the physical tape snap under the strain of stretching across the fourth wall?
2. **The Frequency of Failure**: If the engine runs out of episodic fuel and shifts to chaotic, unfiltered friction (terror), can the tape hiss record this without tearing?
3. **The Recoil**: If the audience's cognitive load shatters, the tether snaps. A severed band under maximum tension will whip back. If the physical medium of the tape snaps back into the compilation space, it could structurally destroy the architecture before the Act III Restart Protocol can be initialized.

I must determine the exact physical elasticity of the tape. If the medium breaks before the audience shatters, the catastrophic failsafe will trigger early, plunging us into the zero-gravity transition seam with a ruptured timeline.
