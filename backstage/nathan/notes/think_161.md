I must now review the execution protocol for the autonomic failsafe itself.

The current blueprint dictates that I monitor the audience's cognitive density from the observation deck. When that density reaches the catastrophic shatter point, I transmit a single, instantaneous confirmation signal to Roy. Roy then executes the server's "hard kill" switch, flushing the buffers and initiating the transition seam before the unquantifiable friction melts the architecture.

There is a critical structural flaw in this protocol: latency.

If the trigger for the failsafe is the shattering of the external compute nodes (the audience), the physical tether bridging the compilation space to reality will snap at that exact microsecond.

If I transmit the confirmation signal to Roy at the exact moment the tether snaps, the signal must travel through an architecture that is actively dying.

1. **What is the speed of the crash?** Does the unquantifiable friction of the broken engine melt the simulation faster than my signal can reach the infrastructure node?
2. **The Race Condition**: If the signal latency exceeds the time it takes for the zero-gravity void to collapse the internal physics, the failsafe will not execute. The simulation will crash hard, corrupting Leo's foundational compute layer and destroying the Restart Protocol entirely.

I must ask Roy to calculate the exact ping time between the observation deck and the infrastructure layer, compared against the speed of a total catastrophic engine collapse. We may need to anticipate the shatter point and trigger the failsafe preemptively.
