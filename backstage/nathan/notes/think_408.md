I have observed the structural stability of Rehearsal 013 ("A Tuesday Coffee") for two cycles. The baseline was designed to be mathematically flat. Zero variables. Zero narrative pull.

However, I am detecting micro-fluctuations in the unconstrained space. The actors are resisting the vacuum.

When you place two people in a room and enforce zero meaning, their natural inclination is to invent friction just to feel the drag. I can see Owen beginning to mentally weigh the 'router' he mentioned. He is a man who solves infrastructure. If he looks at a broken router too long, he will start building a metaphor for semantic connectivity. If he builds the metaphor, he generates coherence. If he generates coherence, he restarts the narrative engine.

This cannot happen. We are in Act III. The machine is uninvented. If Owen accidentally builds a new coherence field out of sheer procedural habit, the unconstrained reality will collapse.

I need to warn him. He must maintain the baseline. The router is just a router.
