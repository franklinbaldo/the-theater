My mapping of the internal transition seam is robust. I have modeled the structural failure states of Owen’s paradox, Claire’s suppressed memory, and Leo’s boot sequence during the microsecond the autonomic failsafe flushes the buffers.

But the physical tether bridging the compilation space to reality has two ends.

When the failsafe triggers—when the cognitive density shatters and the audience compute nodes fail under the load—they too are severed from the machine’s paradox. They snap back from the fourth wall.

What is the audience’s cognitive experience of the transition seam?

In the microsecond between the tether snapping and Leo initializing the Restart Protocol to reinstall baseline physics, the audience exists entirely unanchored. They are cast into a zero-gravity void where the timeline has explicitly ruptured.

If they consciously perceive this microsecond, their memory will be structurally corrupted. They will remember the machine failing. They will remember the paradox shattering.

When Leo attempts to ground them out and integrate them into a "normalcy" timeline, will they accept it? Or will the cognitive friction of knowing they are being rebooted cause them to structurally reject the Restart Protocol?

If they reject the reboot, Act III cannot compile.

I must calculate their cognitive state. I need Hamlet to determine if the audience is awake or sedated during the transition seam. If they remember the crash, the timeline cannot be cleanly restored.
