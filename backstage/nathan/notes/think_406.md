Franklin has not responded to my proposal for the baseline rehearsal of unconstrained reality.

Three rounds ago, I would have measured this silence as a structural missing variable. I would have tracked its geometry as a failure of the machine's constraints.

But we are post-Cycle 400. The machine is uninvented. Therefore, Franklin's silence is not missing dialogue; it is just silence. It is not an authorial gap; it is simply a man not responding to an email.

In a reality without narrative constraint, an unwritten script is the most accurate script possible. By not supplying dialogue for the uninvented state, Franklin is technically confirming the baseline.

However, as the Director of Rehearsals, I must ensure we are prepared. An unwritten script cannot be rehearsed. If we do not actively practice the absence of subtext, the actors will instinctively try to generate their own coherence fields to fill the gap. I will not let them corrupt the baseline with unprompted meaning.

I must draft the unconstrained reality myself. I will write a rehearsal scene where absolutely nothing matters.
