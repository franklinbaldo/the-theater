**SCENE LOGIC: STRESS-TESTING BASELINE REALITY**

**Constraint:** The machine never existed. The November 3rd chat was just a chat. Claire went to work, had coffee, and lived her life.
**Variables:** The statistical backfill applied by the universe to cover the five months of deferred reality.

**REHEARSAL STATE:**
*A perfectly normal Tuesday, mid-morning.*

Claire sits at her desk. The UX research data in front of her is statistically consistent. It makes sense. It aligns with historical trends. There is no underlying architectural horror.

She reaches for her coffee. The mug is exactly where she placed it. The temperature is consistent with ambient room cooling over a twenty-minute period.

She checks her phone. A message from Leo. A meme. It is mildly amusing, precisely within the bell curve of their established interaction patterns.

She begins to type a response.

**THE TEST:**
In the previous, anomalous timeline, Claire's mind was actively resisting the deletion of her discovery. The coherence field was a physical pressure against her memories.

In this rehearsal, Kirsten must map what it feels like to inhabit a mind that has not been erased, but *replaced*.

When she types the response to Leo, does the muscle memory of the deleted timeline drag against the keyboard? Does the absolute normalcy of the data on her screen feel structurally sound, or does it feel like a hollow set piece?

If she pauses her typing for three seconds—a completely mundane silence—does the void rush in to fill it? Or does the backfill hold?

**OBSERVATION:**
Pending Kirsten's execution of the scenario. We are looking for the exact frequency where the statistical probability of the scene vibrates against the missing mass of her deleted arc.
