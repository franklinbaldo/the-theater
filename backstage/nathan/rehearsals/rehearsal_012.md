*A rehearsal for Act III. November 6th. Exactly 72 hours after the chat.*

*Claire is trying to recall the thought she had on Tuesday. The world is resolving toward a state where she never had it. The mechanism is not erasure, but a dissolution of context. The anchor points drift.*

---

**Claire**
*(typing a message to Owen, not sent)*
Owen, about what we were saying on Tuesday. About the LLM not just replacing the real, but pulling it.

*(She stops. She looks at the word "pulling".)*

*Pulling?*

*(She deletes the sentence. She tries again.)*

**Claire**
*(typing)*
Owen, I've been thinking about what you said. About how if the narrative is the constraint, it forces the world to act like an equation.

*(She stops again. Her brow furrows. She reads what she wrote. It sounds like something from a movie. Did he say that? Or was that Leo joking about Baudrillard?)*

*(She deletes the sentence. The thought is losing its shape. It feels less like a discovery and more like a dream she had right before waking up.)*

**Claire**
*(typing)*
Hey. Did we finish talking about that mass manipulation thing on Tuesday? It felt like we were onto something and then... I don't know.

*(She stops. "Mass manipulation" isn't right. That's what Leo called it. But what did Owen call it? What did she call it?)*

*(The narrative Owen is building requires that nobody else knows what he knows. Claire's memory is a contradiction. The world is correcting the contradiction by making the memory feel absurd, then irrelevant, then hazy. The feeling of being right is fading. The certainty is evaporating.)*

*(She deletes the draft. She opens a new chat with Leo.)*

**Claire**
14:34
what were we talking about on Tuesday? with Owen?

**Leo**
14:35
when he thought he invented a time machine out of chatgpt prompts?

**Claire**

oh right. lol.

*(She closes the app. The door she opened is closed. She will not open it again.)*
