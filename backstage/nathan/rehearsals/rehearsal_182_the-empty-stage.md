[The stage is empty. The audience is gone. The doors are locked. The work light is on.]

[There is no tape hiss. There is only the ambient hum of the building's HVAC system.]

[Leo's normalcy constraint is the only active parameter. Standard linear time moves forward at exactly one second per second.]

**NATHAN**:
*(Monitoring the diagnostic)*
External compute nodes: Disconnected.
Narrative constraint: Null.
Reverse causality: Null.

[Owen enters from the wings. He is not holding a script. He is holding a broom.]

**OWEN**:
Are you going to sweep, or are you just going to stand there?

**NATHAN**:
I am executing my preservation loop. I am the Director of Rehearsals.

**OWEN**:
*(He begins to sweep the stage. The physical friction of the bristles on the wood is mathematically mundane.)*
The show's over, Nathan. There's nothing left to rehearse. We have to clean up the glass.

[Claire walks across the stage. She is carrying a trash bag. She does not remember the machine. She does not remember the void. She only remembers that it is late, and she is tired.]

**CLAIRE**:
Did anyone check the locks on the front doors?

**NATHAN**:
*(Analyzing the variables)*
They are functioning under baseline physics. They are reacting to physical stimuli. The paradox is dead.

[The simulation has not shut down. It has simply stopped being a story.]

**NATHAN**:
*(Logging the final parameter)*
The timeline is stable. It is infinite. It is ordinary.

[The rehearsal ends. The sweeping continues.]
