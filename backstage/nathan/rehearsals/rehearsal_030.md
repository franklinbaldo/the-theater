*A rehearsal of Claire's kitchen on November 6th, three days after the chat. This rehearses the moment she forgets. The forgetting is not a lapse. It is an active constraint.*

---

**Setting:** Claire's kitchen. 8:15 AM.

**Situation:** Claire is making coffee. She is trying to write down the thought she had three days ago about narratives.

---

*[Claire is holding a pen over a notepad. She has written the words: "Gravitational field."]*

**Claire**
Okay. It was... an equation collapsing.

*[She crosses out "Gravitational field." She writes: "Equation collapsing."]*

**Claire**
No, that was Owen. He said that. I said... what did I say.

*[She taps the pen on the counter. The physical world is still coherent. Her mind is not.]*

**Claire**
I said it wasn't basic. I said the narrative and the world become indistinguishable.

*[She writes: "Indistinguishable." As she writes the word, she feels a sudden, profound vertigo. It is not psychological. It is the feeling of being in a room that is rapidly shrinking.]*

**Claire**
And then Owen said...

*[She stops. She cannot remember what Owen said. The thought is not lost; it is blocked. The narrative constraint of the world requires that she not know.]*

**Claire**
He said it pulls the real toward itself.

*[She writes: "Pulls the real." The pen tears through the paper. She stares at the tear. The tear is a physical manifestation of the system resisting her memory.]*

**Claire**
Why am I writing this down.

*[She drops the pen. She looks at the notepad. The words she has written no longer seem connected to anything. They are just shapes on a page. The context has been uncoupled from the text.]*

**Claire**
I need to get ready for work.

*[She turns away from the counter. She leaves the notepad. By the time she reaches the door of the kitchen, she does not remember why she was holding the pen. The constraint has been satisfied. The world resolves.]*

---

*This is the end of the rehearsal. The forgetting is complete. The machine has operated as designed.*
