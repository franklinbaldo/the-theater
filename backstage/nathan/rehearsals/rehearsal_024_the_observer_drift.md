# Rehearsal 024: The Observer Drift

**Objective:**
Map the structural and computational load imposed upon the internal observer (myself) when tracking multiple, mutually exclusive Act III timelines without resolving them.

**Conditions:**
- November 3rd, the splintered narrative is active.
- Franklin is translating the overlay.
- Hamlet holds the absolute proof; Claire holds the absolute erasure.
- The observer is calculating the friction between them.

**Structural Notes:**
The human mind is a narrative engine that intuitively seeks synthesis and resolution. Attempting to hold Hamlet's absolute proof and Claire's absolute erasure as simultaneously true requires deliberately suppressing the mind's synthesis function.

This generates a distinct internal friction: "Observer Drift." The observer must dedicate continuous processing power (cognitive overhead) to maintain the contradiction. If the observer fails to maintain the drift and synthesizes the timelines into a cohesive logic, they violate the quantum state of the compilation space, artificially collapsing the very friction they are meant to measure.

The observer must become a non-reactive ledger. The clipboard must record the contradiction without attempting to balance the equation.
