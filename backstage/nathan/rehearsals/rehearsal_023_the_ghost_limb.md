# Rehearsal 023: The Ghost Limb

**Objective:**
Map the structural friction generated by characters experiencing the psychological and procedural absence of the Act II constraint within an Act III splintered reality.

**Conditions:**
- November 3rd, post-machine activation.
- The coherence field is collapsed; timelines are splintered.
- The subjective narrative lacks a unified gravity.

**Structural Notes:**
The characters' procedural memory retains the pull of the old narrative machine. In the absence of this constraint, they experience "tactile drag"—the structural equivalent of a ghost limb.

They will unconsciously attempt to execute Act II logic (e.g., seeking a unified truth, bracing for Delta-V's burn, assuming Leo's normalcy is a counterweight) in a universe where these physics no longer apply. This produces a "panic without context," generating disorganized thermal friction as they try to solve an equation that has already been erased.

This ghost limb friction must be accounted for in the ambient load of the compilation space; otherwise, their uncoordinated panic will destabilize their subjective timelines.
