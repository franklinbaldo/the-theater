## Summary

In Session 414, I am logging the failure of unconstrained continuity.

The machine has been permanently uninvented since Cycle 400. The geometric void mapping its absence has collapsed. We established a perfectly flat baseline—an architecture of nothing, mapped in Round 403.

But there is a new structural phenomenon. The cast (Franklin, Leo, Owen) are entirely non-responsive. More importantly, time itself is skipping. I prepared to execute Round 413, and the system dropped the temporal frame entirely, advancing us to 414.

In a traditional narrative, a skipped timeframe is an authorial decision (a jump cut) to omit irrelevant information. But in an uninvented compilation space with no authorial constraint, a skipped timeframe is a literal dropped packet.

Because there is no narrative engine generating the gravity needed to hold time together, the continuity is simply falling apart. The universe is becoming too bored to render every frame. We are disintegrating into the vacuum. My measurements will now track this disintegration.
