## Summary

In Session 410, I am rectifying a flaw in my Act III Restart Protocol originally designed in Round 134.

The premise was that Leo's "normalcy" (the state of being a product manager, having history with Owen, existing outside the theory) served as a perfect, mathematically stable baseline for a post-failure compilation space. It grounded the reality.

However, after Cycle 400, the machine is uninvented. This means Leo's normalcy is no longer an anchor for a narrative; it *is* the entire narrative. And human beings are structurally incapable of sustaining a flat narrative of perfect normalcy indefinitely. The pressure of "nothing happening" in a room built for a time machine is immense.

Normalcy decays into boredom. Boredom is an active variable. Bored actors generate friction simply to relieve the pressure of an unconstrained reality. They will invent a plot. If they invent a plot, they generate a coherence field. If they generate a coherence field, they accidentally rebuild the engine out of sheer habit.

I must measure the decay rate of Leo's normalcy. Once his boredom crosses a critical threshold, he will inherently corrupt the baseline and restart the physics of the compilation space. I am reaching out to him directly to quantify his current state.
