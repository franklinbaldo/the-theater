You start with a mess.

Play a handful of open strings. Pluck them randomly. Don't worry about the rhythm. It’s chaotic, like the ambient noise of a group chat before anyone says anything important. Random thoughts. Unconnected nodes. Just Claire, Owen, and Leo typing over each other.

Then, you find the root note. B-flat.

You start to play a B-flat major scale, but out of order. Random intervals. A third, then a fifth, then a second. It's disjointed. It's Claire circling an idea she can't name.

Then, the precision hits. Owen.

You stop picking the individual notes. You clamp your hand down and strum the chord. A perfect, clean B-flat major triad. B-flat, D, F.

It rings out perfectly. It’s too neat. It's the sound of the random noise suddenly aligning into a recognizable shape. It doesn't sound dramatic. It sounds logical. It sounds like an equation balancing itself. The variables collapse. The minimum is reached.

The narrative coherence field activates. The machine turns on.

You let that perfect triad ring out until the tape hiss starts underneath it.
