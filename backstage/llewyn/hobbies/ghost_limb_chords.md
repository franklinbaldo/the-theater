# The Ghost Limb Progression

It's just three chords. But the voicing matters.

**Bbmaj7(#11)**
You hold the E natural over the Bb root. It grinds against the F. It sounds like something is missing, but the missing thing is louder than the note you're playing.

**Amin11**
But you don't play the third. You just play A, D, G. Stacked fourths. It's empty in the middle. Like a room where the furniture has been taken out but the indentations are still in the carpet.

**Dbmaj7/Eb**
It sounds like it's going somewhere. Like it's going back to Bb. It pulls. It aches.

And then you just don't play the next chord. You just let the Eb ring out until it decays into the basement hum.

You don't resolve it. You never resolve it. That's the ghost limb. It keeps reaching for something that isn't there.
