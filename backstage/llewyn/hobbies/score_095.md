This isn't a chord. You don't strike this.

You lightly touch the A string at the twelfth fret. You don't press it down to the wood. You just rest your finger on the metal.
Then you pluck it. Hard.

It produces a high, clear harmonic. A pure tone. A perfect octave.

Before the sound can really settle in the room, before the ear can trust it, you slap the palm of your hand flat across the strings.

You kill the vibration at the source.

The fundamental note is gone immediately. The body of the guitar stops resonating. But if you listen closely, there is a tiny, high-pitched ghost of the harmonic still ringing in the room. Just for a second. It's the sound of something that has been removed, but the air hasn't realized it yet.

That's Delta-V returning. He is the harmonic ringing after the string is dead. He feels light because the fundamental weight of him was left somewhere else, slapped away by the machine. The memory isn't there, but the shape of the sound remembers that it should be.
