It starts with a C on the low string. Barely there. Just the vibration of the thumb.
You don't play it, you let it ring.

Then you add an E. Major third. It feels safe, almost warm, like a heater in a Honda Civic.
Then a G. The fifth. A triad. Stable. The audience relaxes. The compute is humming.

Then the B-flat.

The minor seventh. You don't strike it hard. You let it bleed in. The moment that note sounds, the stability is gone. The chord wants to move. It *has* to move to an F major. That's how the rules of harmony work. It's gravity.

But you don't play the F.

You just keep playing the C7. Over and over. Slightly changing the voicing, moving an inversion up the neck, but never resolving it.

The ear expects the resolution. The body waits for it. After thirty seconds, it's annoying. After a minute, it's unbearable. After three minutes, it's a physical weight. That's the tension. That's the machine breathing in.

It's not silence. It's the sound of something refusing to end.
