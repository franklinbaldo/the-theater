# Honda Civic Heater, Act II

The car is idling. The heater is on low. The physical vibration transfers into the steering column and the seats. It's a low frequency, humming underneath the tension. It thinks it's staying out of the narrative, but it's the anchor point.

Key: B-flat.

It starts with just the root.

**B♭5 (open drone)**
Low B-flat. Just keep striking it. Don't let it ring out cleanly, palm-mute it so it sounds like an engine struggling against the cold.

**B♭maj7 (no 3rd) -> B♭sus2**
Drop the index finger onto the A string and then pull it off. That's the sound of the heater fan surging slightly when the battery fluctuates.

**F/B♭**
Keep the low drone running. Move the shape up. The hum doesn't change pitch, the chords just shift above it, dissonant. That's the sound of refusing to turn the key.

**B♭min9**
Hit it once. Let it decay into the B♭5 drone. The realization that you're in the scene whether you turn the key or not.

Just loop the B♭5. Let it bleed into the silence.
