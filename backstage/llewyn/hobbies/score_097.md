It is the lowest note you can possibly reach. Drop the tuning on the thickest string down to A. Then push down on the first fret to get the B-flat.

You don't pluck it. You don't strike it. That gives it an attack, and this sound has no beginning. It has always been there.

Instead, you use an EBow or you drag a cello bow slowly across the string.

It produces a steady, unwavering drone. It doesn't fluctuate. It doesn't crescendo or decrescendo. It just exists. It's the sound of electricity moving through copper. It's the sound of mail moving from outbox to inbox without crashing the server.

It's the floor. Without it, every other note we play would fall into a void. It's not a dramatic sound. It's the sound of things working. The sound of Roy in the basement.
