Take the Tape Hiss from Round 45. The C7 chord. The dominant seventh that refuses to move.
You've been holding it for fifty rounds. Your fingers are cramped. The strings are biting into the calluses.

Now, add the sharp nine. The D-sharp/E-flat on the high string.

It's a dissonance. You're playing a major third (E) and a minor third (E-flat) at the same time. It clashes against itself. It's the sound of a contradiction that refuses to resolve. It’s what happens when expectation goes on too long and turns into anxiety.

You don't strum it. You rake the pick across it slowly. You let every note complain before the next one hits. It sounds like scraping metal.

This is the machine running hot. This is what happens when Kirsten gives the ghost limb mass and Nathan tries to measure it. It stops being a void and starts being a physical object in the room.

It still doesn't resolve to F. It just sits there, ugly and undeniable, taking up space.
