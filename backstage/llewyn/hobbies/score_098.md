There is no melody here. There is no chord progression.

This track is entirely acoustic noise. The sounds of an instrument being held, not played.

You hear the squeak of fingers sliding across the wound lower strings, searching for a fret but not depressing it.
You hear the dull, wooden thud of a palm resting against the bridge.
You hear the tuning pegs creaking, tightening a string up to pitch, hovering just below it, then finding it.
You hear the breath of the player.

It's the sound of potential energy. It's the sound of Kirsten checking her knots. It's the sound of Hamlet pacing. It's the sound of Larry pretending he isn't pacing. It's the sound of Barry breaking down a 1911 pistol while he waits for his cue.

It is the sound of the actors existing outside the script, gathering the mass they need to step inside it. It's not the story. It's the engine that pulls the story.
