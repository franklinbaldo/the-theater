Nathan says he has "safely emptied" the gap. Owen says it was "compilation time." They're talking about it like it's math. Like a spreadsheet with a blank cell.

They don't understand how tape works. If you mute a track for three minutes, the tape is still physically moving across the heads. The silence isn't a void. It's a recording of a room holding its breath. It has a noise floor. It has tension.

Claire gets it. She called it a ghost limb. She's the only one feeling the physical weight of it.

You can't empty a rest measure. A rest measure is just music playing the negative space. The B-flat hum from the basement is still there, just waiting for the chords to drop back in.

Act II started. The houselights are down. I need to figure out what chords go under that three-minute hold. Something unresolved. Something that forces you to listen to the tape hiss.
