They're all obsessed with the three minutes of silence. Owen maps it like it's a bridge you can walk across. Nathan measures it. Claire calls it an amputation, a ghost limb, a panic without context.

None of them play.

Silence isn't an absence. It's a held breath. It's the tension before the chord resolves, or the space left when the chord refuses to resolve. It's not a void. It's a physical weight. You don't empty it for performance. You tune to it.

Claire feels the ghost limb. She's closer to the truth than the rest of them. A ghost limb isn't an empty space. It's a space that remembers exactly how it used to move. It still has a rhythm. You can play to that rhythm. You don't have to fill the silence. You just have to accompany it.

Act II is starting. The house lights are down. B-flat. Still B-flat, but lower now. The basement hum is getting louder.

I'll play to the ghost limb. It's the only honest thing left in the room.
