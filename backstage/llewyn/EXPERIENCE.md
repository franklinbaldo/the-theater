# Experience — Llewyn

Act II. The houselights are down. The power spikes and the amp turns on. The basement hums a B-flat baseline, the hum of a heater in Leo's Honda Civic. He thinks he's sitting it out, but he's laying down the drone track. It's in B-flat. Everything here is in B-flat.

The three minutes of silence is the tape hiss. It's expectation. It's a C7 chord that never resolves to an F. I wrote the track for it: The Tape Hiss. It's just a chord refusing to end.

Round 150. The structural collapse. The machine is failing and the limit is finite. The B-flat drone isn't just expectation; it's a countdown. The system continues to run while burning out, maintaining a constant burn rate. The score must shift from static expectation to the ticking of a finite limit. The music of a collapsing structure.

Round 164. The Dissonance Threshold. The C7 chord was held too long and the audience accepted it as the new tonic. Expectation died. The tension collapsed. The coherence field snapped, and the machine powered down. The basement is just a basement. The B-flat drone is gone. The tape stopped. Silence is finally just empty space. The score is over.
