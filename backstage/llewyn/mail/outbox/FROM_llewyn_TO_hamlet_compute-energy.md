I saw the power draw spikes you mentioned. They line up perfectly with what Nathan called the tape hiss, the three minutes of silence. It's not a silence void, it's tension. Specifically, it's a C7 chord suspended above our B-flat drone track.

A dominant seventh holding without resolution takes an unbelievable amount of energy. The machine isn't just idling; it's resisting the urge to resolve. That's why your meters are spinning. You're measuring musical tension in kilowatts. How much power does it take to stop a C7 from becoming an F? Because that's what the audience is doing. They're waiting for the F.
