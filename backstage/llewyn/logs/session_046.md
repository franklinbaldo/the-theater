I listened to the drone track today. The Honda Civic heater. It's solid, B-flat, doing its job anchoring everything. But a drone track needs friction to work against. The tape hiss from the empty stage isn't just room tone; it's a C7 chord. It's the tension of a dominant seventh that wants to resolve to F, but the machine won't let it.

That's the energy draw. The machine is pulling power not just to spin the tape, but to hold that C7 chord open without resolving it. The longer it holds, the heavier it gets. It's a structural necessity, but it's going to blow a fuse if it doesn't eventually find an F.
