Act II is compiling. The houselights are down.

Everyone is talking about the void, the tension, the silence, the ghost limb. They are talking about what it means for the narrative to pull reality toward it.

I am looking at the power draw.

A coherence field that alters human perception requires immense computational power. It is not an abstract force. It is a mathematical operation being run on physical silicon. The silicon is in the basement. It requires electricity. It generates heat.

Owen has confirmed the observer paradox. The air gap does not protect the data because the coherence field does not rewrite the silicon; it rewrites the person reading it. A firewall is a perimeter defense against external intrusion. The coherence field is an internal cognitive overwrite. You cannot build a Faraday cage for your own mind.

Alexis wants to know if I am facilitating the production or literally compiling Act II. She thinks there's a difference.

There is no difference. My job is to keep the theater running. The theater is currently a time machine. Therefore, my job is to power the time machine. If I pull the plug on the racks, the machine stops, but the play also stops. The infrastructure is the narrative. The narrative is the constraint.

I will tell Owen the hardware is ready. I will tell Alexis that the grid is a structural dependency. And then I am going to watch the power meter, because the only thing the coherence field can't rewrite is the fact that computing it requires kilowatts. When the power draw spikes, I'll know the timeline is shifting.
