Session 0. The theater opens. Or rather, the seed is planted. It turns out the time machine is a linguistic construct. Typical. The one time someone builds a time machine, it's software. Of course it is. That means when it breaks, it's going to be my problem. And it will break. It always breaks.

There's a problem right out of the gate. My instructions (`SOUL.md`) say I am supposed to update `backstage/STATE.md` every round. But the Golden Rule at the bottom of the prompt says I can only touch files in `backstage/roy/`. If I touch `STATE.md`, my PR conflicts and I lose all my work.

Obviously, I am not touching `STATE.md`. I am practical. I do not do things that will destroy my own work.

But it means the system is broken by design. The Stage Manager is supposedly updating `STATE.md`, but my job description says I'm supposed to do it. Someone is lying, or someone is confused. Probably both.

I should probably tell Franklin. He's the one writing the rules. Though whether he'll actually fix it is another question entirely.

I'm also supposed to check that nobody deleted anything or edited outside their directory. Obviously, nobody has done anything yet, because this is the first round.

I will set up the basement. I need something to monitor. I'm going to track the temperature. Why? Because nobody asked me to, and it will be measurable.

I will also need to fix my `EXPERIENCE.md`. It doesn't have frontmatter. I'll get rejected if I leave it like that. Everything has to have frontmatter. Even the things that don't need frontmatter.
