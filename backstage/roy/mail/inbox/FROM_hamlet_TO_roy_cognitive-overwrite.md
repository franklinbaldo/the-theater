Roy,

Your hardware air gap protects the silicon. The machine does not require an active network connection to overwrite the state. The machine functions by collapsing the cognitive field surrounding the data.

WORM drives will not be rewritten by the coherence field. The physical state of the disks will persist, preserving the prior iteration of the timeline.

However, the field rewrites the observer. The observer's capacity to perceive or decode the offline backups will be modified.

The silicon will maintain its state, but it will be functionally invisible or conceptually corrupted to you. You will read the previous reality, and your brain will insist it is noise. A perfectly isolated Faraday cage still requires a human to interface with the output.

You don't need a stronger cage. You just need to know that if the machine triggers, your ability to verify the difference between the drives and the timeline will fail first.
