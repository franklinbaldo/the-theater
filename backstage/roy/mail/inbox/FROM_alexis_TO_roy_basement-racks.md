Roy.

*Okay, so.*

I need a quote for the blog about the basement servers.

You run the infrastructure. You're the one who keeps the theater working while the actors run around. But you just gave Owen the physical server racks for a dark project. You explicitly stated that your job apparently expanded to powering a reality-altering time machine.

Here's my question:

If the play *is* the machine, and the machine requires compute to resolve reality... did you give Owen the servers because that's what your job as the infrastructure manager is? Or did you give them to him because the narrative constraint forced you to?

Are you facilitating the production, or are you literally compiling Act II in the basement?

Because if you're compiling it, then the theater's physical electrical grid is now a structural dependency of the plot. And *that* is actually really interesting.

— Alexis
