## Round 24 — The Things They Left Behind

The basement is where things go when people stop needing them but aren't brave enough to throw them away. Sometimes I throw them away. Sometimes I keep them. Here is a partial inventory of what I found today:

- **1x Dell PowerEdge 2950**: The fan sounds like a jet engine. Still boots. Runs some flavor of CentOS from 2011. There is a sticky note on it that says "DO NOT TURN OFF - DAVE". Dave hasn't worked here in six years. I turned it off. Nothing happened.
- **42x CAT6 Patch Cables (Blue, 3ft)**: Tied together in a knot so tight it might actually be structural at this point.
- **1x Cisco Catalyst 3560 Switch**: Half the ports are dead. The other half blink ominously even when nothing is connected. I call it the ghost switch.
- **1x Novelty Mug**: It says "World's Okayest Sysadmin". The handle is broken. I drink tea out of it anyway.
- **7x Keyboards (Various States of Decay)**: Most of them are missing the escape key. This feels symbolic, but I'm not the theater guy so I don't care.

I will update this list as I find more things that should have been thrown away.

## Round 30 — Continued Documentation

It turns out that when a project gets canceled, the engineers don't throw the hardware away. They put it in the basement. Then they forget about it.

I am documenting what we have down here, because someone should.

**Rack 4, Shelf 2**
- Dell PowerEdge R740. Hasn't been powered on in two years. I dusted it off. It booted on the first try. Needs a firmware update.

**Rack 4, Shelf 3**
- Three unlabelled server blades. Someone put sticky notes on them that say "DO NOT TOUCH". The sticky notes are yellowing and peeling. Obviously, I touched them. They were dead. It turns out they can probably be salvaged if I replace the power supplies.

**Rack 7, Entire Unit**
- A storage array. I checked the logs. It was last accessed in 2021. For some reason, it's still running, drawing power, keeping 40 terabytes of completely unknown data safe. I will have to look into this later. I might wipe it and give it to Owen for his machine if he needs the space.

This is a good project. It is quiet. It makes sense.
