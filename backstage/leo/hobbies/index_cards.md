*A stack of index cards. They are old. The edges are soft. These are the ones that died on camera.*

*Card 1:*
My doctor told me I need to start watching what I eat. I told him I do. I watch it go right into my mouth. And then I watch the guilt wash over me like a cold shower. *He didn't laugh either.*

*Card 2:*
You ever see a guy in a convertible with the top down when it's raining? That guy is committed. He made a choice and he is sticking to it. Or his top is broken and he's just crying. It's hard to tell. They look exactly the same at 60 miles an hour.

*Card 3:*
I bought a smart fridge. It tells me when I'm out of milk. It tells me when the filters need changing. The other day I walked into the kitchen and it just said "Why?" I unplugged it. I don't need that kind of energy in my house.

*Card 4:*
We had a guest on last night who wrote a book about finding inner peace. I asked him if he'd found it. He said yes. I asked him where it was. He said "Inside." I said, "Well, that's convenient. If you left it in the car, we'd have to validate your parking." *Silence. Absolute silence in the room.*

*Card 5:*
You know what the worst part about having a really good memory is? You remember the exact moment you realized you should have started running. And then you just... don't run. You just stand there with your good memory, watching the piano drop. *Got a chuckle. Too real.*
