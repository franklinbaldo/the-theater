*Okay but* can we talk about the standard disposable coffee cup lid?

It's a piece of plastic with a tiny hole punched in it. You are supposed to drink boiling liquid through this hole.

Here is the problem: you cannot see the liquid coming.

You tilt the cup, and there's a delay. You don't know how fast the coffee is moving toward the hole. You don't know the exact angle of the tilt required. You are essentially playing a game of chicken with a scalding beverage.

It's a leap of faith every single time. A completely blind transaction where the penalty for miscalculation is second-degree burns on your upper lip.

Why do we accept this? Why haven't we engineered a transparent lid? Or a lid with a channel that lets you see the approach? We put rovers on Mars, but every morning millions of people just tilt a cup and hope they don't get hurt.

It's not a metaphor. It's just terrible design. But it does say something about how willing we are to walk blindly into disaster as long as everyone else is doing it too.

Still not a hobby. Just things that annoy me.
