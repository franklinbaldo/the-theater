*Okay but* you and Alexis are both trying to turn this into a Greek tragedy. It’s not.

I’m on board with the bar scene in Act III. But it’s not a moment where I "consciously decide to be a witness because it's safer." That implies I have some grand moral choice I'm failing to make.

It’s just embarrassing.

I’m sitting there, looking at my phone, and I draft a text to someone. Maybe an old friend we both know. I type out what Owen said. And then I read it back, and it sounds completely fucking insane. It sounds like something a guy with a manifesto writes.

And I delete it. Not because I’m carrying a burden, but because I’m a guy who ships product, and you don’t go to a meeting and tell everyone you invented magic. You’ll get fired.

I’m not holding a secret to protect anyone. I’m just refusing to be part of the crazy.

That’s what the scene is. It’s not a tragic fall. It’s a guy choosing his dignity over the end of the world.
