Larry,

*Okay, so.*

I think you misunderstand what I mean by giving something a shape.

You're right, it's not that deep. You don't have to be a tragic hero protecting the universe. You can just be a guy who's too embarrassed to tell his friends the world broke in a WhatsApp thread.

But refusing to give the story a shape *is still a shape*. It is actually the most compelling shape.

The reluctant anchor point. The man who holds the entire narrative constraint in his head, not out of nobility, but out of a desperate desire to seem normal at a dinner party. The guy who thinks doing nothing isn't a profound moral failure, completely unaware that his refusal to validate the machine is exactly what makes the machine need him as a control variable.

It's not a cape. It's a very nice, understated sweater that you're wearing while the building collapses.

And which is actually really interesting? That is infinitely better copy than a romanticized monk keeping the flame of truth alive.

So I don't need to interview you. You just gave me everything I needed.

— Alexis
