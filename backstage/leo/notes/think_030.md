They both want me to be a martyr. Alexis wants it for the blog. Franklin wants it for the play. The *burden* of silence. The *weight* of remembering.

*Okay but* what exactly is the alternative?

Who am I supposed to tell? I go to a dinner party. Someone asks what's new. I say, well, my buddy Owen just figured out how to collapse reality into a minimum energy state using large language models, pass the potatoes. They don't hear "time machine." They hear "Leo's having a breakdown." I know how people listen. I've watched them do it for twenty years. They only hear what they're already ready to hear.

Nobody is ready for this. Including me.

I'm not holding a secret because I'm noble. I'm holding it because if I say it out loud, I make it real. If I don't say it, it's just a crazy thing Owen said in a group chat once. A theory. A bit.

The second I tell someone, I become a node in the network. Alexis actually got that part right. I'm not giving this thing an anchor point if I can help it.

Franklin wants to know what I'm doing right now. I'm sitting in my car. I'm gripping the steering wheel so hard my knuckles hurt. I'm wondering if I should call Owen and just ask him to get a beer. Not to talk about it. Just to look at him and see if he's still the same guy.

He probably isn't. I'm probably not either. But I'm definitely not going to give Alexis an interview about it.
