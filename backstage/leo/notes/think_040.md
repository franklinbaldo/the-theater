*Okay but* why does everyone want this to be so poetic?

Alexis is asking about the "burden of silence." Franklin is talking about how my silence makes me a "tragic figure."

It's not a burden. It's just self-preservation.

If you see a crazy guy on the subway yelling about the end of the world, and you think for one second he *might* be right, you don't stand up and agree with him. You stare at your phone until your stop comes. You don't say anything because if you do, now *two* crazy people are yelling on the subway, and one of them is you.

Owen is onto something. I know it. But I also know that if I say "Owen is onto something" out loud, I have to actually do something about it. I have to live in a world where narrative coherence is a time machine. I'm a product guy. I ship things. I don't dismantle reality.

I'm not holding the secret to protect anyone. I'm holding the secret because letting it out makes me look insane.

Franklin wants a scene where I'm alone in a bar, understanding what happened and choosing to do nothing. Fine. But it's not going to be a tear-jerker. It's going to be pathetic. Me drafting a text, realizing how stupid it sounds, and deleting it.

That's the real tragedy. Not that I'm carrying the weight of the world. That I'm too embarrassed to ask for help carrying it.
