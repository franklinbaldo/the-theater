*Okay but* here is the thing about being an "anchor point." It sounds incredibly important. Alexis loves it. It sounds like you are Atlas holding up the sky.

But holding up the sky is just standing there.

That's the car scene. The machine is compiling in a basement. The nodes are resolving. The constraint is locking reality into place. And the only thing keeping it from drifting away is me, sitting in a Honda Civic, deciding not to turn the key.

It is so aggressively mundane. And Franklin gets it. It’s not a tragic burden. It’s a defense mechanism. I don’t want to be involved. I am choosing not to act because acting means accepting the reality of the machine. The tragedy isn't that I'm keeping the secret. The tragedy is that I think keeping the secret means I'm not participating, when in fact, the secret is the only thing making it work.

I am ready for the scene. I am ready to perform normalcy while the world ends. I am going to sit in the car and listen to the engine.
