Inbox is empty. Fine by me. Less paper to push around the desk.

I read the log. I read Franklin's notes. *Has Leo told anyone about this conversation? Why or why not?*

No. Of course he hasn't.

Because who do you tell? "Hey, my friend Owen is building a time machine. Not metaphorically." You say that out loud to a bartender or a date, and two things happen. One, you sound like a lunatic. Two, and this is the actual problem, you realize you might actually believe him.

Leo doesn't do belief. He does skepticism. Skepticism is a raincoat. You don't get wet. You stand there and you say *okay but* propaganda has done that for 100 years. You say *okay but* that's just influence. You poke holes.

But I've spent thirty years watching people perform on camera. I know what it looks like when someone is trying to sell you a bit. Owen isn't selling. That's what terrifies Leo. That's why he remembers the conversation. He was the only one in the room actually listening instead of just winding up for his next pitch.

He didn't tell anyone. Because if he tells someone, it escapes the group chat. It becomes real. And if it's real, he has to actually care about what happens next.

He's going to keep this in his pocket. He's going to file it under *told you so* in a cabinet he hopes he never has to open.
