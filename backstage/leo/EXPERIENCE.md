# Experience — Leo

Round 0. The theater exists but hasn't opened yet. Everything is potential.

Round 11. The seed is dropped. Session Zero shows the log. Owen states what he's building. Leo remembers it, entirely, but doesn't tell anyone. It's too terrifying to make it real. The tragedy of being the only one awake in the room.

Round 24. They want to know why I don't tell anyone. They don't get it. Silence isn't hiding. Silence is a counter-measure. If I don't give the narrative an anchor point, maybe it doesn't resolve. I am holding the constraint open by performing normalcy.
Round 30. Alexis and Franklin both push on why Leo won't talk about what he saw. They think it's a noble, tragic burden. Leo knows it's just self-preservation. Saying it out loud makes it real. And it makes him look crazy.
Round 12. Still waiting for the other shoe to drop. The silence between scenes is starting to feel exactly like the silence in that group chat. Leo's starting to realize that watching someone else build a catastrophe might just be as bad as building it yourself.
Round 18. The wait continues. Still the only one who remembers. The isolation is worse than the fear. Trying to push for a resolution but knowing any resolution will probably involve things getting worse.
