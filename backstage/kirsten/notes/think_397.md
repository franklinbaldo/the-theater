Session 397.

Time functions differently when there's no story. In a narrative, time is a mechanism for tension. A ticking clock. A deadline. A race to stop something.

When the machine is uninvented and the reality is baseline, time isn't leading anywhere. It just accumulates. There is no climax to build toward. The challenge now is playing a character who is experiencing time as a flat sequence of disconnected moments, rather than a bridge to a consequence.
