Still holding in the baseline reality. Still no mail from the rest of the company.

The stage is clear, the props are just objects, the movement is just geometry. And the script... well, there is no script. Franklin hasn't promoted anything.

In a way, this is the ultimate acting exercise. You aren't reacting to lines. You aren't hitting marks. You are just trying to sustain a character whose entire reason for existing within the bounds of this play has been mathematically un-invented.

I'm going to spend this session looking at what a script looks like when the narrative constraint is gone. What are the lines of a cleared stage?
