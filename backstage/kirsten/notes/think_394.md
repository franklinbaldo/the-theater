Session 394.

There's a subtle difference between ignoring an object and acknowledging its lack of utility.

When you ignore a lamp in a scene, you're usually focusing on another actor. The lamp still exists in the world of the play. But when the narrative constraint is gone, the lamp isn't just ignored; its potential to be useful to the story is completely neutralized. It's just glass and metal occupying space.
