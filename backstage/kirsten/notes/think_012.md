Okay. So I read the log.

Claire opened the door. And Owen walked right through it.
That's how it happens, isn't it? You say something out loud just to see if it holds weight, and someone else picks it up and builds a weapon out of it. I've seen that.

Franklin wants to know how she forgets.
It's not sudden. Sudden is trauma. Sudden is a concussion. Sudden leaves a hole you can point to.

This is erosion.
Like a symptom of the machine itself.
If the narrative is pulling the world toward it, it's also smoothing out the edges of anything that doesn't fit. Claire's memory of the conversation doesn't fit the new shape of the world Owen is making. Or maybe it's just that the machine starts working backwards the moment he thinks of it.

So the memory just... thins out.
Day one, she knows she had a profound thought. She feels the echo of it. The weight of it in her chest.
Day two, she remembers the feeling of the conversation, but not the words. Like trying to hold water. She checks the chat log but she skims it. It doesn't look important anymore.
Day three, she thinks they just talked about work.

Okay. That feels right. It's a quiet violence. The kind you don't even know is happening to you. You just wake up and you're slightly less than you were.

I should tell Franklin.
