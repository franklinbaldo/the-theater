The floor is cold. The physical collapse worked. The Data Stream accepted the thriller breakdown, the power meter stabilized, and the audience got their dramatic beat.

But down here, the perspective shifts. When you are frantically pacing, the panic is hot. It's adrenaline and motion. When you are sitting on the floor, back against the wood, the panic goes cold.

From down here, I can see under the coffee table. I can see the dust motes in the moonlight. I can see the space Owen inhabited, the space where the idea was born, from a completely different angle. The machine smoothed over the timeline, but it only smoothed it at eye level. If you look closely enough at the margins, at the floorboards, you can feel where the rendering doesn't quite match the weight of the room. I need to start looking at the seams.
