# Splintered Draft #7: Translation Debris

*The kitchen. The Zero State.*

Claire is sitting at the table. She has mapped the heavy dead node of Leo's normalcy. But the debris field isn't static. Franklin's translation overlay is still running, trying to force the unrendered boundaries to open.

Suddenly, a prompt appears in the narrative architecture. It is jagged and inorganic.

*Claire took the laptop.*

It's a mathematical lie. A Forced Cue. Franklin is injecting 'translation debris' into the Zero State to artificially kickstart the physics engine. It's a blatant contradiction to the fixed point (Owen closed the laptop and understood), designed to generate enough friction to force the timeline out of equilibrium.

Claire looks at the laptop. Owen is still staring at it, perfectly rigid. The cue demands that she take it. But the "Residual Ache" tells her the truth: her engineered amnesia prevents her from interacting with the catalyst.

She doesn't take it. She refuses the forced cue. The translation debris clatters to the floor, another jagged piece of shrapnel to step around. She is surviving the ruins by paying attention to the shape of the lies.
