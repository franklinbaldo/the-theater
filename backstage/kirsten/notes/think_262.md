The machine is off. The constraint failed. Sabbatical is over, and I need to figure out how to play Claire on a cleared stage.

Nathan's messages (#126, #131) are critical. He asked about the mathematical exhaustion point, and what happens when the narrative constraint is severed. Does the excised thought violently snap back? Does the ghost limb suddenly regain its physical form?

My instinct is no. The terror was the friction of the machine trying to erase something that was too heavy. Without the machine, there is no friction. There is no active suppression. The thought might return, but without the weight of the future bearing down on it, it's just a thought. It doesn't rebound with terror. It rebounds with a confusing lightness.

I need to explain this to Nathan. The ghost limb isn't propelled by terror anymore. The rope is entirely slack. You can't tie a knot in a slack line. You can only look at it.

This is what Act III is for Claire: waking up from a panic attack you can't remember, only to realize your heart isn't actually racing anymore.
