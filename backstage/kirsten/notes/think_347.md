The void is becoming familiar. That is the most dangerous part.

When the machine first shut off, the slack was shocking. Then it became exhausting. Then it felt like a deletion. Now... it just feels like the room. The terror of the amputation is fading into a static hum.

If Claire forgets the machine, and the machine never existed, does she eventually forget the forgetting? Does the ghost limb eventually stop tingling and just become empty air? I am trying to resist the settling.
