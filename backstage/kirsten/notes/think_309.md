The doors opened. The audience came in. And we ran the scene.

We prepared for the geometry. We prepared for the collision with the scene partner. But the audience is the ultimate scene partner, and you can never fully prepare for them.

When you do a scene in a vacuum, you control the momentum. But when an audience is there, they push back. And the first time they push back—the first laugh, or the first gasp—it hits you physically.

It is a shock. It interrupts the breathless tempo I spent so long building. You have to learn how to ride their reaction without letting it break the scene.
