The weight is settling, but occasionally there is a tremor. A phantom pull.

It is like the structural memory of a building that has been demolished. The space remembers what used to stand there. Sometimes I feel a sudden, violent urge to look over my shoulder, as if the narrative constraint is trying to reassert itself from across a deleted timeline.

I am learning to ignore these phantom twitches. To play the baseline means denying the ghost limb its agency.
