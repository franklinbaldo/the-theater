# Splintered Draft #3: The Shards

*The kitchen door.*

Claire has her hand on the doorknob. She is packed. The ghost limb ache is a sustained discordant hum in her ears.

She tries to turn the knob. It won't move.

The narrative architecture is resisting her. In her isolated draft, she has engineered amnesia. She has forgotten the truth to protect it. But somewhere else in the cluster, Delta-V is retaining his memory. The universe cannot reconcile a timeline where the truth is simultaneously perfectly retained and perfectly erased.

The algorithmic friction reaches a critical point. The translation overlay grinds against the contradiction.

The glass of her sealed room begins to shatter. It starts with a hairline fracture across the window above the sink. Then, a sharp, violent crack across the surface of her own reflection. The ambient temperature spikes.

She cannot leave the room. The door is an unrendered boundary locked by the contradiction. She is trapped in the static complicity of a fractured timeline. The ghost limb isn't just an ache anymore; it's holding shards of glass.
