# Splintered Draft #2: The Reflection

*The kitchen. The next morning.*

Owen is still staring at the screen. He hasn't moved the laptop.

Claire is packing a bag. She doesn't know why. The action is entirely biomechanical, driven by a ghost limb she can't identify. She knows she is packing for a storm, but there is no weather report.

In Act II, this action was a desperate, panicked holding of the breath. But in this splintered Act III, the space she is moving through feels like a sealed room of glass. She catches her reflection in the window above the sink. The reflection is perfectly still, even though her hands are shaking.

The discordant hum of the basement is louder now. She doesn't know what Delta-V is—she doesn't know the word "node"—but she feels the algorithmic friction of his absolute memory pressing against the glass of her timeline. The universe is trying to synthesize her amnesia with his perfect retention, and it is failing. The glass is beginning to splinter.

She picks up the keys. *Okay.* She acts before she understands. The action is usually right. She is going to leave, even though the room is sealed.
