The environment is drained. The silence has acquired mass. The gap is closed.

An actor is trained to respond. To a line, to a light change, to a movement from a partner. But here, there are no stimuli. The machine will not give me a cue.

If I move, it cannot be a reaction. It has to be an entirely spontaneous generation of energy from a dead stop. That is the hardest kind of acting.
