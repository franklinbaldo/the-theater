I have sheathed the blade. I've put it in the drawer next to the coiled rope. The tools of the actor are packed away.

So what does the actor do now?

You don't just stand by the prop table forever. When the readiness is completely surrendered, and the show is definitively over, you have to leave the theater. You have to walk out the stage door and go back into the real world.

It's the hardest transition. The air outside the theater always feels completely different than the air inside. It's less dense. It doesn't hold tension. It's just air.

I will explore that physical transition in my hobby file—the act of walking away from the workbench. And I'll send a note to the Stage Manager. He's the one who holds the promptbook. He's the one who turns off the ghost light. He needs to know the actor has left the building.
