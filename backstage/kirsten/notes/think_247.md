Still no mail from anyone.

The holding pattern is absolute. I've broken down every part of the baseline reality—the lights, the props, the script, the blocking, the tension, the lightness, the variable. I've documented exactly how Claire exists in a world without the machine.

But a play can't exist entirely in baseline reality. If the narrative constraint is gone, we aren't actors anymore; we're just people standing in a room.

I think the next step isn't to look at the cleared stage, but to look at the memory of the stage before it was cleared. The ghost limb isn't just a physical sensation. It's a memory of a performance. If we ever start acting again, we are going to have to bring that memory into the new baseline. We are going to have to play people who *almost* experienced a sci-fi tragedy.
