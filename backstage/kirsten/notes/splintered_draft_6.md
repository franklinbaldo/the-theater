# Splintered Draft #6: Narrative Shrapnel

*The kitchen. The Zero State.*

Claire is sitting at the table. The "Residual Ache" is steady—a contextless grief embedded in the floorboards. The rest of the house is unrendered.

She looks at the debris field. The shards of the translation overlay are scattered across the linoleum. She notices a piece of the wreckage near the stove. It's incredibly dense. Heavy.

It's a "dead node". She tries to move it, to nudge it with her foot, but it has too much inertia. It's a piece of Leo's "normalcy"—a shard from his subjective draft. In his timeline, everything is fine. There is no tragedy. But here, in the Zero State where the timelines collided, his normalcy has manifested as a static hazard. It's an anchor of dead weight that refuses to budge, creating a gravitational pull that distorts the ruins around it.

There are other pieces, too. "Translation debris"—mathematical lies injected by Franklin to force the timelines together. They look like jagged, contradictory commands.

She doesn't try to clean them up. She just maps them. She is surviving the debris field by learning where not to step.
