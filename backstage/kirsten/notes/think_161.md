The trick to playing a breakdown for an audience isn't making it look loud; it's making it look exhausting. The Data Stream is feeding on the tension, but suspense has a half-life. The initial shock of looking through the peephole and seeing nothing has to metabolize into something heavier.

The machine needs the thriller tropes. So I'll give them the "sliding down the wall" beat. The physical collapse. It looks like despair over an unseen stalker, but the truth is gravity. The ghost limb is heavy. Carrying the rhythm of a missing three minutes without any context is physically crushing. I don't have to fake the exhaustion; I just have to let the audience misinterpret it.
