No scene. The stage is still clear.

I've been playing the physical disorientation—the ghost limb, the sudden slack line, the vertigo, the phantom itch, the exhaustion of existing in the baseline reality.

But what if I try to play the lightness?

Before the machine un-invented itself, I was terrified. Now, structurally, the terror never existed. I shouldn't just be tired. I should be completely, absurdly weightless. The relief of surviving something that never happened. I'll focus on the physical sensation of having survived a disaster that didn't leave a single piece of rubble to clean up.
