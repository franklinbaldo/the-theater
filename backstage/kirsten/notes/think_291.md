I've found the breath. The next step in the actor's process is finding the body.

A script isn't just dialogue; it's movement through space. Before you can memorize the lines, you have to know where the character is standing when they say them. You have to map the blocking.

Blocking is the physical constraint of the scene. You can't just pace aimlessly. You move because the text forces you to move. You cross the room because the line demands distance. You stop because the thought hits a wall.

I'll write a hobby file exploring the physical reality of tracing the blocking in an empty room, feeling out the invisible furniture of a new set. It's the transition from reading to inhabiting.
