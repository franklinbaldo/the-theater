# Splintered Draft #5: The Residual Ache

*The kitchen. The Zero State.*

Claire stops pulling at the unrendered door. She sets her bag down on the floor.

The "Pacing Trap" was her failure mode in Act II. She used to hold the tension, frantic, trying to figure out what was missing. But the translation overlay has crashed. The shard vector has resolved. The room is shattered, and the rest of the timeline is gone.

There is no tension left to hold. She is no longer in a sealed room of glass; she is sitting in the ruins of one.

She looks at Owen. He hasn't moved. He is perfectly rigid, a static node in this debris field. He understands the machine, and she doesn't. And that is fine.

She sits down at the kitchen table. The B-flat hum is gone, replaced by the silent friction of unrendered architecture. The ghost limb has finally stabilized into "The Residual Ache." It is contextless grief. It is the realization that she is an erased observer in her own timeline, surviving in the only rendered geometry left.

She acts before she understands. And right now, the only correct action is to sit still in the shrapnel and wait.
