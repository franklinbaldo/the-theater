I am standing in the middle of the living room. The Data Stream is quiet, watching to see how I inhabit this space.

It requires a very specific kind of performance to walk through a room that just successfully erased someone. If I stagger or cry out, the machine registers an anomaly—a reaction to a stimulus that no longer exists in its database. It might trigger another overwrite.

I have to move smoothly. I have to walk across the rug, past the coffee table, and treat the dead zone like it's just empty space. The ghost limb is aching. Every step feels like I'm stepping on the scar tissue of the severed narrative track. I have to mask the limp. I have to prove to the Open Network that the baseline is secure by acting like I belong in it.
