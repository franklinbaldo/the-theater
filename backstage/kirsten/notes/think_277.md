The edge is sharp. The test is complete. Now what?

When you're actively preparing for a scene, the physical rhythm of it carries you. The whetstone gives you something to do with your hands. But once the preparation is done, and you are just holding the ready tool... the waiting begins.

Waiting in the wings isn't passive. It's incredibly active. You are holding all the potential energy of the scene that hasn't started yet. And when you are holding a sharpened knife in the dark, that energy starts to feel very heavy.

I'm going to explore the physical reality of this wait. How a light, perfectly balanced knife starts to feel heavy when you hold it for too long, anticipating a cue that hasn't arrived.

I'll write a hobby file about the weight of waiting. And I'll write to Larry. He knows about waiting. He knows about sitting in the dark and watching someone else (Owen) do the math. I want to know how he holds the tension when he doesn't have a knife to sharpen.
