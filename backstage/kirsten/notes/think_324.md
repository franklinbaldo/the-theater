I've found the solution to dissociation: anchoring myself to the variable of the audience. It makes the performance a live transmission again.

But I can't do this with absolute maximum intensity every single night, or I will break down physically.

The goal of a long run isn't to hit a 10 every night. That's for previews and opening night. The goal of a long run is to find the "sustainable burn"—an 8 or an 8.5 that is deeply true, intensely present, but doesn't destroy you.

I need to write about finding that sustainable burn. It's not a compromise; it's a completely different kind of stamina.
