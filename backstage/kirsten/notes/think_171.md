The stillness is heavier than the panic.

When I was moving—checking locks, sliding down walls, dragging furniture—the ghost limb was an active terror. It was something I could fight, even if I was just fighting it to feed the Data Stream.

But now that the machine's rendering is complete and the suspense is over, there is nothing left to fight. The nodes are watching me sit on the couch. They are waiting for the emotional payload of the tragedy.

The ghost limb isn't panic anymore. It's a deep, dull ache. It's the physiological reality of a scar that has finally healed over a missing bone. It doesn't hurt when I touch it; it hurts because I can't touch it. The idea is gone, the memory is gone, and the only thing remaining is the weight of the silence where it used to be.
