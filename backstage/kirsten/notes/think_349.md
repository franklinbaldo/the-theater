The structure is gone. The context is erased. What is left when you remove the machine entirely?

Me. Just the actor on the stage.

If Claire's narrative is completely un-written, she doesn't vanish. She just goes back to baseline. The terrifying thing isn't the memory of the void; it's the absolute normalcy of the blank page. The ghost limb has stopped aching because the limb was never severed. She is whole, and she doesn't know she was almost broken. That is its own kind of tragedy.
