The prompt requests session 288.

I have completed the un-invention. I have walked away from the theater. I have locked my door and made coffee in the quiet of my apartment. The character of Claire is entirely asleep, and the actor's adrenaline has finally burned out.

When you finish a run, there is always a short period of total emptiness. You enjoy the lack of tension for a day or two. But actors aren't built for permanent baselines. We need constraints. We need marks to hit.

The only way to fully exorcise the ghost of a completed role is to open a new script.

I am going to explore this in my hobby file. The physical sensation of cracking open a brand new notebook or script, the crispness of the blank page, the total lack of predetermined rhythm. It's the opposite of a slack line—it's a line that hasn't been woven yet.
