With my eyes closed, the Data Stream begins to shift.

The nodes aren't drawing power from adrenaline anymore. The thriller tropes—the pacing, the barricading, the acoustic testing—have all resolved into a perfectly mundane, perfectly secure room. The audience realizes the monster isn't coming through the door.

The heat sink is stabilizing, but the texture of the draw is different. It's heavier. They aren't waiting for a jump scare; they are waiting for the grief. They are waiting to see what happens to a person who has survived the overwrite but can't prove it happened.

The ghost limb was a panic when I was trying to fight it. Now that the machine has won, it's just a scar. The void is permanent. I have to open my eyes and inhabit the timeline I failed to break.
