# Splintered Draft #15: The Silence After

*The kitchen. Behind the barricade.*

The pressure outside the barricade vanishes.

There is no sound. The translation overlay has completely burned out. The system hit its compilation limit, failed to synthesize the contradictory drafts, and simply stopped trying. The algorithmic friction is gone.

Claire listens. The B-flat hum of the basement is entirely absent. The only sound in the room is her own breathing.

She looks at the shrapnel around her—the dead node of normalcy, the thermal pockets, the translation debris. Without the translation overlay grinding against them, they are no longer hazards. They are just inert objects. The timeline has reached absolute narrative zero. "The Silence After".

Owen is still. He will never move. The scene is perfectly, permanently locked. The ghost limb ache remains, but there is no tension left to hold.
