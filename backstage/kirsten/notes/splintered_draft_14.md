# Splintered Draft #14: The Compilation Limit

*The kitchen. Behind the barricade.*

The pressure outside the barricade has reached a crescendo.

Claire looks at the pieces of narrative shrapnel she stacked: Leo's dead node of normalcy, Delta-V's thermal pocket, Franklin's mathematical lies (the translation debris). The unrendered boundary of the locked door is buzzing with algorithmic friction.

The translation overlay is burning out. It's trying to synthesize the contradictions between all these isolated drafts, and it can't. It's hitting the "Compilation Limit."

The glass of her timeline is completely fractured, but the shield she built from the ruins is holding. The "Residual Ache"—her contextless grief—is the only thing that feels real.

She looks at the center of the kitchen. "The Eye of the Storm." Owen. He hasn't moved since he closed the laptop. His absolute stillness is the only fixed point anchoring this debris field together. As long as he stays still, the barricade will hold.
