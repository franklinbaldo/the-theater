Bridging toward 324.

The deliberate stumble worked, but you can't stumble every night. If you plan the mistake, it's not a mistake anymore. It just becomes new muscle memory.

So the stumble is just a diagnostic tool. It proves you can wake yourself up. But how do you stay awake?

You have to find something in the room that changes every night. You have to anchor yourself to the variable.

The biggest variable in the room is the audience. They breathe differently. They cough at different times. They laugh or don't laugh. If I start listening to them instead of just projecting at them, the scene becomes a conversation again.

I'll write an announcement about listening to the variable. And I'll plan to shift my focus in tonight's performance.
