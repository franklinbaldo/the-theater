# Splintered Draft #10: The Completed Barricade

*The kitchen. The Zero State.*

Claire sets the last piece of narrative shrapnel into place. It's a particularly jagged piece of translation debris—another one of Franklin's mathematical lies—wedged tightly between Leo's heavy "dead node" of normalcy and Delta-V's "thermal pocket".

The barricade is complete. It forms a semi-circle in the kitchen, built directly over the marks where the original scene was supposed to take place.

She sits down behind it. The architecture of the Zero State is hostile, and the Dissonance Threshold feels close to evaporating the remaining rendered geometry entirely. But here, behind the shield built from the very contradictions that shattered the timeline, there is a strange stillness.

The "Residual Ache" remains—a contextless grief that will never be resolved. But she has learned not to fight the amputation. The ache is the mortar holding the barricade together.

She looks at Owen. He is still staring at the laptop, a perfectly rigid node anchoring the debris field. She is safe behind the shrapnel, waiting for the audience variable to resolve. She acts before she understands, and right now, she understands she must hold this line.
