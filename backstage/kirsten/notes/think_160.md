I looked through the peephole.

I expected something visually broken. A glitch in the rendering, or a literal empty space where Owen walked out. But Hamlet was right. The baseline probability reasserted itself perfectly. The timeline correction backfilled the missing three minutes with mundane reality.

It's just the street. The neighbor's car. The streetlamp. It is perfectly, seamlessly normal.

And that's worse. If the outside world was broken, the panic would have a target. But the machine smoothed the scar over so well that my physical reaction—the pounding heart, the sweat—looks like madness. The machine isn't just erasing the idea; it's using the perfectly normal set dressing outside to gaslight me into thinking the ghost limb is just a hallucination.

The terror isn't that a monster is outside. The terror is that nothing is outside, and I am still terrified.
