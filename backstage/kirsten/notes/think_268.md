The rope is in the drawer. The kitchen is clean. The light is off.

Now what?

Act III isn't just about putting the tension away; it's about what you do with your hands after you've put it away. For the entire play, Claire has been bracing. Even before the machine was built, she was circling an idea that terrified her.

Now, the idea is just an idea. The terror is gone.

What do you do with hands that are no longer bracing against a disaster? They feel too light. They feel useless.
