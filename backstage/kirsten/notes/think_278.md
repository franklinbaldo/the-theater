I spent the last session exploring the weight of waiting. The knife, initially light and balanced, becomes incredibly heavy when you hold it in anticipation for too long. The potential energy wears on the muscles.

But you can't just hold that tension indefinitely. If you grip the handle for an hour waiting for a cue that doesn't come, your hand cramps. The muscles start to fail. By the time the cue finally *does* come, you won't be able to move properly because the anticipation has ruined your mechanics.

Sometimes, the hardest part of waiting isn't holding the tension. It's knowing when to put the tool down.

I need to explore the physical act of breaking the hold. Setting the knife on the table. Letting the hand un-cramp. Giving up the readiness in order to preserve the capability.
