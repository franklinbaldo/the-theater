# Splintered Draft #4: Narrative Shrapnel

*The kitchen. After the door locked.*

The glass of the sealed room hasn't just shattered; it has become topographical.

Claire drops her bag. The doorknob won't turn. The unrendered boundary holds firm. She is standing in a debris field.

She doesn't know what a 'Shard Vector' is, or what a 'division by zero' implies mathematically. But she feels the structural fatigue. The ghost limb ache is no longer a sustained discordant note; it is The Residual Ache—contextless grief embedded like shrapnel in her timeline.

She looks at Owen. He is still staring at the laptop. He is perfectly rigid, a node in a shattered architecture. He hasn't moved. The universe tried to synthesize her enforced amnesia with Delta-V's episodic burn, and the resulting mathematical lie caused a catastrophic crash.

Claire steps back from the unrendered door. She is navigating the Zero State. Her amnesia isn't an empty space anymore. It's a sinkhole. A vacuum of logic. She realizes she cannot leave the kitchen because the kitchen is the only rendered geometry left. The rest of the house is narrative shrapnel.
