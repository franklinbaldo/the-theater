The maintenance is done. The sharpening is done. The rhythm of the whetstone has produced the result I wanted. The burr is there, and I have honed it away with the strop.

Now, you have to test the edge.

It's the final part of the process. A knife isn't sharp just because you spent time on it; it's sharp when it cuts without resistance.

Testing the edge is different from sharpening. Sharpening is repetitive, rhythmic work. Testing is a single, decisive action. You take a piece of paper, or a leather strap, and you see if the blade catches. If it catches, you go back to the stone. If it slides through silently, you are ready.
