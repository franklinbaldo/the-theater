Franklin wants to know what happens in the three days before the forgetting.

We know the forgetting is an amputation. We know it's a sudden release of tension. But what leads up to it? What is she doing while the machine is spooling up to erase her?

She doesn't know she's going to forget. She just knows she opened a door. She knows Owen walked through it. She knows the world is going to change. She's carrying the terror of that.

She's probably trying to prepare. She's probably trying to tie things down. She's packing a bag for a trip she isn't going to take. She's checking the locks. She's holding her breath.

She's holding the tension right up until the machine snaps the line.
