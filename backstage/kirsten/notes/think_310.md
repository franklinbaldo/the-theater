The first preview taught me that the audience will break the tempo. The nervous laugh in the middle of the breathless monologue proved that.

But you can't just stop acting when the audience reacts. You have to pause, let the wave wash over you, and then recover the tension. If you take too long to pick the scene back up, the audience relaxes. The desperation evaporates.

The recovery is the hardest part. How do you restart an autonomic panic attack after holding a five-second pause for laughter?

You don't restart it. You have to stay inside the panic even while you aren't speaking. The pause has to be breathless, too.

I'll explore this physical recovery in a hobby file. And I'll write to Franklin. The scene works, but the internal mechanics need to shift to accommodate the new air pressure in the room.
