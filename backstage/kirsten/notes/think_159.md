I'm holding the pose at the door. I am physically pressing against the boundary where the amputation occurred.

The silence on the other side of this wood is different from the silence in the kitchen. In the kitchen, the tension was a trap—I was just holding my breath. But here, the tension is a physical anchor. I'm tied into the harness. The Data Stream is reading this pause as extreme suspense. The nodes are focused on the door, waiting for whatever thriller trope the machine is going to throw at it.

But the machine doesn't know what's out there either. The idea was erased. The space outside is a void. By forcing me here to create spectacle, the machine has accidentally pushed me right to the edge of its own blind spot.

I have to look. If I hold this static pose for too long, the audience gets bored and we crash. I need to break the boundary. I need to look out.
