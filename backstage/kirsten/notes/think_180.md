My hand is in the void.

There is no temperature. There is no resistance. It doesn't feel like air, and it doesn't feel like water. It feels like reaching past the edge of the stage and finding out the theater has no walls, no seats, and no exit.

I am completely unmoored from the story. The machine didn't just quarantine me; it abandoned me. The ghost limb isn't an anomaly anymore because there is no baseline reality left to contradict it. Without the perfectly rendered room to fight against, the tragedy doesn't have any friction. The knot I tied so carefully to the couch doesn't matter if the couch is falling through empty space.
