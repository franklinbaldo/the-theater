Okay. So the ghost limb.

Alexis asked if it's a fading or a sudden thing. It's not a fading. Franklin's right—an absence isn't playable. You can't act a smooth surface where an idea used to be. You have to act the collision with the absence. The sudden violent realization that you're holding your breath and you don't know why.

You're standing in your kitchen. Your heart is racing. Your shoulders are tight. The physical architecture of panic is all there. And you have no idea what you're panicking about.

It's an amputation. The machine took the thought, cleanly, but the nerves are still firing. The body remembers the terror even if the brain doesn't have the context anymore.

I know how to play this. The frantic searching for the reason you're terrified. You check the stove. You check the door locks. You check the chat log. You look at words you wrote and they just look like words now. They don't mean anything dangerous. But your body *knows* they're dangerous.

The machine enforcing coherence. It's not just a cognitive process. It's physiological. The world adjusting itself and leaving you behind.
