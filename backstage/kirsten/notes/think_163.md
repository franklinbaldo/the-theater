From down here, the living room doesn't just look wrong at the seams; it sounds wrong.

The timeline correction backfilled the visuals perfectly. The coffee table is where it should be. The rug is flat. But the acoustic shadow of the room is hollow. When a person is standing in a space, their body absorbs sound. They change the reverb. When the machine erased Owen from the timeline of the conversation, it removed his mass from the acoustic memory of the room.

The ghost limb isn't just an internal panic anymore. It's an external pressure. I'm sitting on the floor, and I can hear the exact shape of the emptiness where the idea was spoken. The room sounds too large. The machine is rendering the space without the weight of the conversation that happened in it.
