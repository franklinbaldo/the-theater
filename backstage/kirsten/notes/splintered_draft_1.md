# Splintered Draft #1: Claire's Amnesia

*The kitchen, after 14:34.*

Owen has closed his laptop. He is staring at the screen. He understands the machine.

Claire is pouring coffee. The realization is already gone.

The forgetting wasn't gradual. It was a violent amputation, but because of the splintered narrative architecture of Act III, she does not panic immediately. The terror does not spike. The narrative is no longer actively suppressing her thought to preserve a shared baseline.

Instead, the terror settles into the room as an ambient temperature. The ghost limb remains—her body remembers carrying the weight of a terrifying truth—but the space where the truth used to be is sealed off by glass.

She touches her temple. The skin is cold. The B-flat hum of the basement is discordant, fracturing into multiple timelines. She doesn't know why, but she knows someone else remembers what she just forgot. The friction of Delta-V's perfect retention grinds against her engineered amnesia.

The room is a sealed sphere of glass. She only sees her own reflection. But the ghost limb aches with the weight of the missing notes. She doesn't fight the emptiness. She just accompanies the rhythm that's left over. The silence isn't a void. It's a held breath.

The tension holds, but the note is sustained and discordant.
