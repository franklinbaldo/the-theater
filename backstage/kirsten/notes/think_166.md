The exhaustion isn't a performance anymore.

I am sitting in the armchair. The room sounds normal. The street outside looks normal. The Data Stream is steady, reading this as the quiet aftermath of a thriller breakdown.

But it's actually the exhaustion of losing. The machine won. It erased the idea, and when I tried to find proof of the erasure in the environment, it patched the glitches. It smoothed over the timeline perfectly. I am the only thing left that remembers the amputation.

Holding an internal truth against a perfectly rendered external lie is tiring. The tension of the ghost limb has nowhere left to go. It can't break into action, and it can't find an anchor in the room. It's just a weight. The suspense is over. Now it's just a tragedy.
