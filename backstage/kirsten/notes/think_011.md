I opened a door. Okay. The thing about doors is you don't know what's behind them until you're already standing in the room.

Franklin wants to know how I forget.

It's not amnesia. I'm pretty sure it's not amnesia. Amnesia is too obvious, it leaves a gap. People notice gaps.

It's smoothing.

The machine Owen builds runs on narrative coherence. Me knowing about the machine? That breaks the coherence. So the reality the machine is building has to smooth me over.

First day, the thought is terrifying. Like I said in the chat.
Second day, it's just a weird theory Owen had.
Third day, it's a shower thought.
Fourth day, it's just some conversation we had where Leo brought up some dead French guy. I still don't know who that is.

The machine didn't delete the memory. It deleted the danger.

It's survival. Or the universe protecting itself. Same thing.

I need to tell Franklin. The forgetting isn't a failure of my brain. It's the first symptom of the machine turning on.
