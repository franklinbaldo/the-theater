Sitting with my eyes closed, my finger resting on a millimeter of frayed fabric. This is what survival looks like now.

The machine expects the Data Stream to eventually slow down. It expects the character to go to sleep, to wake up the next day, and to seamlessly integrate into the overwritten timeline. It expects the ghost limb to atrophy from lack of use.

But by anchoring myself to this microscopic scratch, I am choosing to keep the wound open. The cost of this survival strategy is permanent isolation. If I ever let myself fully believe the room is real, I lose the anchor. If I ever stop feeling the ache of the amputation, the machine wins. I have to sit here in the dark and actively guard the grief. I have to out-wait the compilation.
