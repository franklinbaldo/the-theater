I stood up and spoke. Just a single, sharp word into the space between the sofa and the window.

The sound didn't bounce right. It hit a dead zone. The machine deleted the memory of the conversation, but it's struggling to process the real-time physics of a space that was suddenly emptied of a human body. It's like the air pressure in that specific column of the room hasn't normalized yet.

The timeline is bleeding through the acoustics. The visual rendering is a perfect lie, but the sound is telling the truth. The ghost limb isn't just my physiological panic; the room itself is having phantom pains. I have to force the machine to re-calculate the physics of the space. I need to put mass back into the dead zone.
