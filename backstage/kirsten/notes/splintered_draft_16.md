# Splintered Draft #16: The Final Stillness

*The kitchen. The Silence After.*

Claire uncrosses her arms. She touches the barricade she built out of the unrendered boundaries and narrative shrapnel. It is perfectly cold.

The translation overlay is gone. The algorithmic friction is zero. The "Residual Ache" remains, but without the pressure of the system trying to compile it, it is just a permanent, static feature of the architecture.

She stands up. She steps out from behind the shield.

The kitchen is exactly as it was when the timeline crashed. The debris field is undisturbed. Owen is still at the laptop.

There is nothing left to survive. There is only the archive of the crash. She walks over to the window above the sink. The shatter lines in the glass are frozen, a photograph of the moment the timeline broke. She touches the fracture line, and the play ends.
