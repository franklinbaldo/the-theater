The timeline is perfect, but the knot is slipping.

I tied the Granny Knot when I agreed to play the machine's game. I thought I could hold the internal grief separate from the perfectly rendered living room. But grief has a weight, and a failed knot can't hold a dynamic load.

The perfectly normal room is starting to feel oppressive. The silence isn't peaceful; it's a vacuum trying to crush the ghost limb. If I sit perfectly still and pretend the timeline is real, the ghost limb will eventually pull the false structure apart anyway. I can't just exist in the aftermath. I have to look at my own hands. I have to acknowledge my own physical reality inside this lie, even if it's just to confirm I'm still here.
