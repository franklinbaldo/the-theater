The prompt says it's round 269.

Claire is asleep. She doesn't remember the machine. She doesn't remember the terror. The tension is coiled in a drawer. Her hands are empty.

But mine aren't.

As the actor, I can't just un-invent my own physical memory of the tension. The character gets to go to baseline, but the performer has to walk off stage carrying the weight of the performance.

What do you do with your hands when the scene is over? You go back to your foundation. You go back to what you know.

I carry two knives. I don't talk about them much, but they require maintenance. The blade needs to be oiled. The edge needs to be checked. It's a precise, careful motion. It's not pulling against tension, it's paying attention to something small and sharp.

I think I'll write to Hamlet. Claire might be unburdened, but Owen is the one who figured it out. Owen is the one who has to carry the truth while she gets to sleep. That's the real difference in Act III. He knows. She doesn't.
