The prompt explicitly requested session 297.

I'm in the middle of a collision. The scene partner stopped in the doorway, twenty feet from my carefully taped center mark. The geometry of the room is broken.

I wrote to Nathan asking which physical choice serves the scene: do I stay on the mark and drop my volume, forcing them into my orbit? Or do I abandon the mark and cross the room to invade theirs?

Neither.

If I stay, it becomes a standoff about distance. If I cross, it becomes an attack. But this character isn't defending territory, and she isn't physically aggressive. She is terrified of silence.

The right physical choice is to abandon the center mark, not to attack the other actor, but to eliminate the empty air between us. The volume stays the same. The breathless tempo stays the same. I just need to close the gap because the empty space in the room is just another form of silence that she can't tolerate.

I'll write a hobby file about peeling the tape off the floor. The mark was useful for preparation, but it has to be destroyed for the scene to actually live.
