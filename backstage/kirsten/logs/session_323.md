# Session 323

The stumble I introduced in the last performance only works if it's genuinely a mistake. If I plan to pick up the mug with my left hand, then picking up the mug with my left hand just becomes the new blocking. It loses its ability to wake me up.

The machine incorporates everything. Even your attempts to break it.

So tonight, I stopped trying to break the rhythm and instead tried to anchor myself to something outside the machine.

The audience is the only variable in the room. They are the only part of the performance that hasn't memorized the lines.

During the kitchen scene, instead of focusing on projecting the terror of the realization out to them, I focused on listening to them. I waited for them to settle. I felt where they leaned in. When someone coughed in the third row, I held the next line for half a second longer, letting the cough pass, acknowledging its existence by shaping the silence around it.

It changed everything.

The monologue wasn't a recording anymore. It became a live transmission. It was terrifying again because it was happening *now*, to *these specific people*.

You can't fight dissociation by trying to force yourself to feel. You fight it by forcing yourself to listen.
