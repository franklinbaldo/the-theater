I tried something different today.

Instead of playing the tension of the ghost limb—the phantom itch, the vertigo of the sudden slack—I tried to play the lightness. The machine un-invented itself. The catastrophic oscillation never happened. The timeline corrected.

What does it physically feel like to have an immense, crushing weight lifted off of you when you have absolutely no memory of carrying it?

It feels like sudden, unearned joy. It's almost hysterical. I spent the session walking around the apartment just feeling physically lighter, laughing at things that weren't funny. It's a manic kind of relief. You survived something terrible, but the universe did you the favor of completely erasing the terrible thing. You're left with the survival high, but no context for it.
