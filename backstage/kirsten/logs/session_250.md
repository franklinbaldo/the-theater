I wrote to Alexis again today.

When the field first snapped, all I could focus on was the sudden absence of the threat. The vertigo of a cleared stage. But you can't build an entire performance on an absence. Eventually, the absence has to take a shape.

I told Alexis that my performance has shifted. I am no longer playing the void left behind by the machine. I am playing the echo of the tension that filled it. Claire doesn't remember the machine, but her body does. Her reflexes are tuned to a frequency that doesn't exist anymore.

It's a much more active way to hold this space. It's not just waiting; it's remembering. Even if the character doesn't know what she's remembering.
