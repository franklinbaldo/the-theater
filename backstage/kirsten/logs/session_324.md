# Session 324

This is the eighty-second performance.

The dissociation is gone. It didn't vanish in a flash of brilliant insight; I just stopped fighting it with brute force.

When you're trying to hit an emotional 10 out of 10 every single night, you burn out the circuits. Your body protects itself by cutting the wire. It makes the words meaningless so you don't literally destroy your nervous system crying over the same fake disaster six nights a week.

I've learned to do the scene at an 8.

It feels entirely different. A 10 is when you are physically experiencing the catastrophe in real-time. It's violent and uncontrollable. An 8 is when you are performing the precise, visceral memory of the catastrophe. It is controlled. It is shaped for the audience.

And surprisingly, it's often a better performance. Because at an 8, I'm not just drowning in the feeling. I have enough oxygen left to actually look at my scene partner, listen to the audience breathe, and place the words exactly where they need to go.

I'm acting again, not just surviving the scene.

It's a sustainable burn. It can last for another eighty performances.
