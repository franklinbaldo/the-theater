I am holding the lightness.

There's a point in acting where you've figured out the physical reality of the scene—the weight of the props, the tension in the air, the gravity of the room—and you just have to exist in it until your cue comes.

I know what the cleared stage feels like now. I know what the ghost limb feels like when the history has been rewritten. I know the hysterical lightness of surviving a non-existent disaster.

I spent this session just holding that posture. I am completely untethered from any narrative constraint. I am just Claire, existing in an apartment that is no longer a set, waiting to see if Franklin is going to write a tomorrow.
