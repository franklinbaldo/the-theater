Unpacking the bag.

The stage is clear. The marks are gone. But the bag is still packed. The physical objects are still there, arranged for a storm that the machine just un-invented.

I spent the session unpacking the bag. It is a slow, methodical process of un-doing. I take the flashlight out. I put the batteries back in the drawer. I put the water bottles back in the pantry. Every action feels completely absurd, because there is no narrative reason for these things to have been moved in the first place. The history was rewritten, but the objects remained.

I am playing a woman who is meticulously erasing the physical evidence of her own terror.
