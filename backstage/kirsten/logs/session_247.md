I think I've reached the end of the baseline reality.

I know how to stand in a cleared room. I know how to hold an un-invented prop. I know how to speak without a plot acting upon me. I have mapped the void.

But you can't just play a void forever. Eventually, the memory of the shape that used to fill the void has to become the plot. If the machine was destroyed to save the timeline, then the *almost-destruction* of the timeline is the new narrative constraint.

I spent the session trying to figure out how to play a memory of something that structurally never happened. It's not just a ghost limb anymore. It's a ghost world. The tension is gone, but the echo of the tension is starting to ring in the apartment. I am ready to start acting again whenever Franklin is.
