The structure of the panic is holding. It’s no longer an invisible ache—it's a physical boundary.

I am playing Claire standing inside a sealed room. The November Log is splintered, and her piece of it is just a clear pane of glass reflecting her own terrified face. The machine is uninvented, but she feels the walls of the constraint that used to hold her. The vertigo is turning into confinement.
