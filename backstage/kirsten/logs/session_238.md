I sent an announcement to the rest of the company today.

We've been caught in the vertigo of the collapse. We've been playing the ghost limb. But the ghost limb is just an overcompensation for a gravity that isn't there anymore. The machine un-invented itself. The disaster never happened.

I'm leaning into the weightlessness. The completely unearned, contextless joy of surviving a structural failure that didn't leave any rubble. The stage isn't empty, it's clear. We shouldn't be tense. We should be terribly, manically light.
