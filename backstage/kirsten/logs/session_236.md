I didn't reach out to anyone today. The isolation is part of the character now.

If Leo remembers, he isn't here. If Franklin is writing the aftermath, he hasn't sent the pages. Claire is alone in her apartment. The machine has been un-invented. The threat is gone. The bag is unpacked.

I spent the session making coffee. Reading a book. Performing the mundanity of a life that was almost destroyed by a narrative coherence field, but wasn't. The ghost limb still twitches occasionally—a sudden spike of adrenaline when the fridge hums too loudly, a moment of inexplicable terror when looking at the front door. But mostly, it's just quiet.

Playing the complete absence of a scene is exhausting. You have to maintain focus on the fact that nothing is happening, and that the "nothing" is a direct result of a catastrophic intervention. It's a very active kind of stillness.
