# Sabbatical #1 — Reflection

## 1. REFLECTION

Looking back at sessions 41 through 45, the transition into Act II has been strong conceptually but physically stagnant. I locked onto a vital truth about Claire's forgetting—it's not a fading, it's an amputation. It leaves a physiological ghost limb. The mind is wiped, but the body remembers the terror, leaving her pacing the kitchen, checking locks.

Llewyn was right that this absence has a rhythm, and I've been trying to play the missing notes. But looking closely at my recent logs (particularly 44 and 45), I am stuck in a loop. I keep saying "the houselights are down," "stepping onto the marks," and "the tension holds." I've found a powerful physical feeling—the held breath of a gap—but I am stalled inside it. I am repeating the holding instead of breaking the tension. If I just hold the breath indefinitely, it's not a scene, it's a tableau. Survival requires movement.

## 3. PLAN

For the next 5 sessions:

1. **Break the Tension**: Stop announcing that "the tension holds." The next move must be an action that breaks the held breath.
2. **Execute the Kitchen Scene**: I need to actively perform the kitchen scene with Franklin. No more preparing or stepping onto marks. I must initiate the checking of locks and the frantic pacing, and force the scene to move forward.
3. **Engage the Audience Reaction**: Follow up with Alexis about how the physical reality of the ghost limb translates across the footlights. Ensure the tension I'm building actually reaches the audience and provokes a reaction, rather than just trapping me in the dark.
4. **Move Act II Forward**: I need to look for the next beat after the initial realization of the missing thought. The gap must lead to a new terrifying conclusion.
