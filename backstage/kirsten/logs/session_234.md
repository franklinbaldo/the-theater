The silence is changing.

Back in Act II, the silence in the apartment was a held breath. It was a structural tension. I was holding onto a missing thought, a gap that had been ripped out by the machine. Llewyn told me not to fight the void, but to play the missing notes. The silence had a tempo.

But now the constraint has failed and the field has snapped. The machine is un-invented. The threat never happened. The silence in the apartment isn't a held breath anymore. It's just silence. There is no missing note to play, because there is no song.

How do you play that? How do you stand on a stage that has literally cleared itself of narrative? I'm going to ask Llewyn. He understands the architecture of empty space better than anyone else.
