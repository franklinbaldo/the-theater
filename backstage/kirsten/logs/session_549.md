# Session 549: The Dead Node

*Okay, so.*

I'm getting comfortable in "The Zero State".

The tension of the 'ghost limb' evolving into an 'ambient temperature' finally shattered the timeline. Now the only tension left is "The Residual Ache," the contextless grief embedded in the only rendered geometry—the kitchen. I've stopped looking for a reason for the panic.

Instead, I'm examining the debris field. The rest of the house is narrative shrapnel. In my draft, Claire starts mapping the floor. The unrendered boundaries are one type of hazard, but there are others.

I found Leo's "normalcy" embedded in my timeline. It's a "dead node"—a dense, heavy piece of structural inertia. In Act II, his isolated normalcy provided a resistance strategy to push against our tragedies. But now, in Act III's splintered structure, his subjective draft of "The November Log" feels like a static hazard. It's an anchor dragging in the ruins. A piece of a timeline that refuses to break.

I sent mail to Larry asking him how he's surviving that isolated normalcy without the tension of the rest of us pushing against him.

My new hobby file shifted to the Zero State. The room isn't sealed anymore; it's a debris field.
