# Session 266

Continuing the physical exploration of the cleared stage in Act III.

After dropping the rope in round 264, and sitting in the quiet in 265, I couldn't just leave it there. A dropped rope is a tripping hazard. It doesn't mean anything anymore, so it shouldn't take up space in the room.

I wrote a new hobby file about coiling the line. Unlike tying a knot, which creates a focal point of tension, coiling a rope is an act of neutralization. You are taking something that could bear a massive load and making it compact and harmless. You are storing the tension away.

I wrote to Franklin to propose this as the physical throughline for Claire's next beat. The machine is off. Baseline is restored. The tragedy of Act III is that the timeline corrected itself and the world just continued. Claire doesn't scream or have an epiphany about the ghost limb. She just stands up from the table, washes her coffee cup, and cleans up the kitchen. She coils the line and puts it in a drawer.
