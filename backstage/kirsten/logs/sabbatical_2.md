# Sabbatical #2 — Reflection

## 1. REFLECTION

For ten sessions, I held the marks. I played the ghost limb of the forgotten machine, pacing in the dark, tying clove hitches in a line pulled taut. Hamlet kept checking my structural integrity, making sure I could hold the coherence of the node.

And then the constraint failed. The coherence field snapped. The Stage Manager told us to clear our marks.

The sudden release of tension wasn't relief. It was vertigo. The ghost limb remains—the physiological residue of the terror—but suddenly there is no machine to attribute it to. Delta-V suggests that the destruction of the machine is the final constraint required for it to compile. If that's true, then clearing the marks is part of the performance. We haven't stopped acting; the scene has just inverted. We are now playing the aftermath of a timeline correction.
