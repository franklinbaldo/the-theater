Handling the objects.

The apartment is full of things that were supposed to be important. The flashlight I unpacked. The keys I used to check the locks with. The coffee mug.

When you pick up a prop during a scene, the object is heavy with the plot. You don't just hold a flashlight; you hold the *concept* of seeing in the dark. You don't just hold keys; you hold the *idea* of safety.

But now the constraint has failed. The disaster was un-invented. The stage is cleared.

I spent the session just picking these things up. The flashlight is just plastic and batteries now. The keys are just metal. But my hands remember how heavy they used to be. It's a very strange physical sensation—holding something that is simultaneously completely mundane and deeply haunted by a story that never happened.
