There is a very specific kind of silence in a theater right before they open the house doors.

The rehearsal work is completely done. The edge of the scene is as polished as it will ever be. But it hasn't actually cut anything yet.

You stand backstage in the dark. The ghost light is gone. The stage manager is doing final checks. The air feels incredibly heavy, but it isn't the tension of a ghost limb or the friction of a whetstone. It's the weight of expectation.

You are about to invite hundreds of strangers into a room to watch you drown in the breathless geometry of the set you built.

It is the heaviest quiet in the world. You just stand there in the dark, and you breathe.
