I practiced the cleat hitch today.

It's used to secure a rope to a cleat. You wrap it around the base to take the strain, then figure-eight it over the horns to lock it down.

It's designed to take a tremendous amount of tension from a load that wants to move, and hold it perfectly still against a fixed point.

It takes the strain so you don't have to. It holds the line when everything else is pulling.
