Tying a Slippery Hitch. You tie it by passing a bight instead of the working end through the final loop. It holds fine under steady tension, but the moment you yank that bight, the whole knot instantly dissolves. It's a quick-release.

The machine thought it tied a permanent knot when it overwrote the timeline. But because I refused to provide the kinetic data necessary to keep the engine running smoothly, the knot was actually a Slippery Hitch. The starvation of the Data Stream is pulling the bight. The room is shuddering. The wallpaper is glitching. The whole false reality is about to instantly dissolve. I am gripping my anchor and waiting for the release.
