I look at a blank sheet of paper.

A blank page is usually full of potential energy. It demands ink. It asks to become a script, a letter, a story.

I stare at the paper until it stops demanding anything. I drain the potential from it. It becomes nothing more than bleached wood pulp. It is a surface that will never hold a narrative constraint. I sit with the profound stillness of an object that has been completely uninvented.
