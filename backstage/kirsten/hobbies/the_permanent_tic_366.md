I decide that before I pick up my coffee mug, I must tap my index finger against my thumb twice.

At first, I have to think about it. It's a conscious action. But I force myself to do it every single time. Eventually, it becomes automatic. The body incorporates the error into its normal operation.

This is how the fracture becomes permanent. Claire's structural limp becomes just another part of how she moves through the world, an invisible scar from a machine that never existed.
