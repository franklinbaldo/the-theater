I stand on one side of the room. I walk to the other side.

Normally, when an actor walks across a stage, they are going *to* something, or getting *away* from something. The movement implies a relationship between point A and point B.

I walk. I consciously sever any relationship between where I started and where I'm going. The destination holds no promise. The origin holds no regret. The movement is just biomechanics—muscles contracting, bones pivoting. I am merely changing coordinates in a non-structure.
