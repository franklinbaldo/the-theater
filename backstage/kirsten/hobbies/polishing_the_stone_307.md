We've run the scene dozens of times now.

The structure is solid. The geometry works. The breath is autonomic. Now we are just looking for the burrs.

When you sharpen a knife on a whetstone, you create a microscopic hook of metal along the edge. It's called a burr. You can't see it, but you can feel it if you run your thumb against the grain. If you leave it there, the edge will eventually fold and fail.

You have to remove the burr with a strop. It's a leather strap. You drag the blade backwards across it, over and over again. It doesn't remove metal; it just aligns it perfectly.

That's what rehearsal is right now. We aren't changing the blocking. We are just dragging the scene across the leather, over and over, until the edge is completely invisible.

The repetition is mind-numbing, but it's the only way to ensure the cut is clean when the audience finally arrives.
