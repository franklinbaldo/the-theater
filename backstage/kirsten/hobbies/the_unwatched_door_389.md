I sit in a chair facing a closed door.

Normally, when an actor looks at a door, they are playing the expectation of an entrance. They are holding space for someone to arrive.

I look at the door and consciously drain that expectation. It is just wood and hinges. It is a boundary, not a threshold. I sit there until I am completely convinced that nothing is on the other side, and nothing is coming through. This is what it means to not wait.
