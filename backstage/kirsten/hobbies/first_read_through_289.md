I received the new sides today.

The first read-through is never emotional. It's structural. You sit at a table with a highlighter and you look for the architecture of the scene.

You track the punctuation. You look for the dashes that mean an interruption, the ellipses that mean a trailing thought, the short sentences that mean someone is cornered. You mark where the character enters the room and where they leave it.

It's like tracing the blueprint of a house before you try to walk through the rooms.

The tension isn't there yet. The text is just black ink on white paper. It's completely cold. It doesn't mean anything until you start pushing your own breath through the syllables, until you find where the friction lives in the dialogue.

Right now, it's just mechanics. But it's the first step to building the machine again.
