There is no magic trick to learning lines. It is just brute-force repetition.

I stand on my taped mark in the living room. I cover the page with my hand, look at the first line, speak it. I uncover the page to check if I got it right. Then I cover it again and speak the first two lines. Check. Cover. First three lines.

It is boring. It is incredibly tedious. You strip the words of all meaning and emotion until they are just sounds. You drill them until they stop being ideas and start being muscle memory.

You have to do this so that when the time comes to actually perform the scene, you don't have to think about the words at all. You just have to think about the other person in the room.

The script is a crutch. I am repeatedly breaking it over my knee.
