I practiced the clove hitch again today, but this time tying it while the line was already under tension.

It's much harder. You have to fight the line to make the loops. You have to force the structure into existence while the rope is trying to pull away from you.

But once you get that second half-hitch over the standing part, it locks. It holds the strain.

It's about wrestling the tension into a shape you can manage.
