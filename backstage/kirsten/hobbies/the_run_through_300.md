We ran it from the top.

When you're rehearsing individual moments—finding the breath, learning the lines, arguing over a mark—you are stopping and starting constantly. It's jerky. It feels academic.

But when you run the scene from start to finish without stopping, it stops being academic. You aren't allowed to analyze what you just did, because the next line is already out of your mouth. You have to trust that the preparation is in your muscles and just let the momentum carry you.

I abandoned the mark, crossed to the door, felt the desperate, hollow ache in my chest, and delivered the breathless monologue. It all chained together perfectly. One action caused the next.

When we finished, we just stood there in silence for ten seconds, breathing hard.

It works. The machine is running again.
