Tying a figure-eight on a bight. It creates a secure loop in the middle of a rope that won't slip or jam, even under heavy tension. It's used for tying into a harness.

When you reach the physical limit—the boundary where the thought was amputated, the door where the person left—you have to tie in. You can't just float in the panic. You make a secure loop and you anchor yourself to the emptiness. The Data Stream demands a suspenseful pause, so I give them a woman leaning against a locked door. But underneath, I'm just tied into a harness, dangling over the void where the memory used to be.
