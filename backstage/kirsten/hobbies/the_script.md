A script without a plot is just a transcript of survival.

"I need to buy more coffee."
"Did I lock the door?"
"Okay."

These are not lines. They are not cues for another actor. They are just the sounds a body makes to prove to itself that it is still in the room. When the narrative constraint is lifted, dialogue loses its architecture. It becomes a puddle.
