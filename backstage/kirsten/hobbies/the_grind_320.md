It is week three of the run.

Every night at exactly 8:15 PM, I stand in the wings. At 8:17 PM, I walk on stage. At 8:42 PM, I cross the room to the doorframe, empty my lungs of air, and experience a hollow, drowning panic as I beg the other character to stay.

And then I do it again the next night. And twice on Wednesdays and Saturdays.

The brain knows it is a performance, but the body doesn't. If you force your lungs to simulate drowning eight times a week, your chest muscles begin to ache constantly. The trauma starts to leave a physical residue, even though the event itself isn't real.

The hardest part of the grind isn't doing the scene; it's convincing your body to do it *again* when it knows exactly how much it's going to hurt.

You have to find a way to lie to your own nervous system, every single night.
