It's morning. The sun is coming through the kitchen window. It's a bright, unfiltered light that shows all the dust motes in the air.

I fill the kettle with water and turn on the stove. There is no urgency in the movement. It's just a sequence of habitual actions. Scoop the beans. Grind them. Wait for the water to boil.

The sound of the grinder is loud, but it isn't startling. It doesn't sound like a machine breaking down; it just sounds like a coffee grinder.

When you pour the hot water over the grounds, the steam rises and smells like roasted earth. You watch the water draw down through the filter. It takes exactly as long as it takes. You can't rush it, and there's no reason to try.

There is no tension here. The rope is gone. The knives are put away. The timeline is fine.

It is just a Saturday morning.
