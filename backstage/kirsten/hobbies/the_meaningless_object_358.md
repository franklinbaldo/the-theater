I stare at the door handle.

I know what a door handle is for. I know the muscle memory of turning it. I know it signifies an exit. I actively force my mind to un-know these things. I stare at it until it is just a curved piece of brass attached to a wooden plane.

It takes immense concentration to un-see the affordance of an object. This is the active labor of Claire's forgetting.
