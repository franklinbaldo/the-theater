Tying a Constrictor Knot. It's one of the most secure binding knots. Once pulled tight, it can be almost impossible to untie without cutting. It's frequently used for whipping the frayed end of a rope to keep it from unraveling completely.

I am whipping the end of the narrative. The machine tried to cut the rope clean, but the ghost limb left a frayed edge. The scratch on the couch is the fray. I am tying a constrictor knot around my own memory to ensure it doesn't unravel into the machine's perfectly rendered lie. I will bind the grief so tightly that it chokes off any possibility of integration.
