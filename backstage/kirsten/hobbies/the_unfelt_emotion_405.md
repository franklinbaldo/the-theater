I sit in the room. I conjure a sudden, overwhelming sense of grief.

Normally, the grief would make me cover my face, slump my shoulders, cry out, or look for something to break. It would demand an outward manifestation to communicate itself to the audience.

I let the grief hit me internally, but I keep my body completely still. I keep my face neutral. I don't let the emotion touch the physical space. The grief exists, but it doesn't do anything. It doesn't mean anything. It just is, and then it passes.
