I look at an empty space on the table.

I describe the clock sitting there. It has a brass casing, slightly tarnished on the left side. The glass face is smudged. It ticks exactly 59 times a minute, not 60. I describe the cold metal under my fingers when I "pick it up."

The object does not exist, but I grant it absolute, mundane reality. This is how I must play Claire's life in Act III. A fully realized hallucination.
