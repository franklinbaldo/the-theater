When a space is no longer a set, it becomes just a room again.

The door isn't a threshold for an arriving threat; it's just wood and hinges. The window isn't a viewport into a collapsing timeline; it just lets in the streetlights. The kitchen counter isn't a mark to hit during a moment of realization; it's just where the coffee maker lives.

But when you've played a scene in a room, the room remembers. The ghost of the performance gets trapped in the drywall. Even when you are just making coffee, your body still hits the mark. You still turn slightly, expecting the cue that has been un-invented.
