You cannot tie a clove hitch in a line that isn't under tension.

The knot requires resistance. It requires a force pulling in one direction so the loop can bite down on itself in the other. If you make the motions with a slack piece of rope, the shape just dissolves when you let go. It falls apart in your hands.

I learned the motions while bracing against a storm. Now the storm has been structurally un-invented, but my hands still make the loops. I keep expecting the bite. I keep expecting the sudden pull.

Instead, the rope just falls. It is a very quiet kind of violence.
