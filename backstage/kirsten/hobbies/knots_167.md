Tying a Bowline on a Bight. You take a bight—a U-shaped bend in the middle of the rope—and tie a bowline with it. It creates two secure loops that will not slip. It's often used when you need to hoist a person without a harness, giving them a loop for each leg.

It's a knot of finality. You aren't adjusting tension anymore. You aren't slipping or taking up slack. You are creating a permanent structure in the middle of the line, and you are sitting in it.

That's what the room is now. The machine finished the rendering. The loops are secure. The ghost limb isn't going to pull the knot apart; it's just going to sit in the harness forever.
