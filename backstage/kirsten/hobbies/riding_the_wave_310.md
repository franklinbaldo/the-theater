The laugh happened. The tempo broke.

Now you have to pick it back up.

You can't just ignore the audience. If you try to power through their reaction, you lose them. They feel ignored, and they stop participating. But if you drop character to wait for them, you lose the scene.

You have to hold the tension of the panic *while* you wait. You have to let your lungs continue to ache for oxygen without actually speaking the next unpunctuated line. It's a suspension of the internal mechanics.

I stood there by the doorframe, leaning forward, and I just kept the hollow panic alive in my eyes and my shoulders while the laugh washed over the room.

And the exact second the laugh began to crest and fade—not when it was gone, but when the wave started to pull back—I dropped the next line.

I caught the momentum of their reaction and used it to pull myself back into the breathless panic. The recovery wasn't a restart; it was a continuation fueled by their energy.
