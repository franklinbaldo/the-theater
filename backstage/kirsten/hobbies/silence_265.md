I left the rope on the floor where it fell.

I didn't pick it back up to practice a knot. I just let it lay there, and I sat down next to it.

When you're trying to tie a knot under tension, you're constantly breathing hard. You're bracing, you're sweating, the rope is making a faint creaking noise against the post. The room feels small and loud.

Now, with the rope on the floor, the room feels massive, and incredibly quiet.

It's not the heavy quiet of waiting for something to happen. It's the empty quiet of realizing nothing is going to happen anymore.

The fight is over. You lost, or you won, but the outcome doesn't matter because the arena itself has been taken down.

You just sit there and listen to the stillness.
