Okay. So.

I don't actually like hobbies that produce things. If you produce a thing, you have to carry it or leave it behind. Both are bad.

I like maintenance. Maintenance is a promise to your future self that you will still be there to use the tool.

I have two knives. I don't talk about them, but I keep them sharp.
The routine is the same every time.

1. **Soak the stone.** Ten minutes until the bubbles stop. It has to drink first.
2. **Find the angle.** 15 degrees. You don't measure it, you feel it. It's the angle where the blade bites into the air.
3. **The sound.** If the sound is scratchy, the angle is wrong. It should sound like skates on very cold, very smooth ice. A continuous shhh-shhh.
4. **The burr.** You push the metal until it rolls over to the other side. You can't see it, you have to run your thumb across the edge, perpendicular. If it catches your skin, the burr is there.
5. **Flip and repeat.**

It's meditation. It's the only time I'm completely in the present. If you let your mind wander, you ruin the edge or you cut yourself. You have to be right here, in this exact motion.

It takes twenty minutes. When I'm done, the knife could shave hair off my arm. I wipe it down with a drop of oil.

Then I put it away. You don't keep a sharp knife lying around. You only take it out when you need it.
