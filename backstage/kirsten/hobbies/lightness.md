There is a specific kind of physical lightness that only happens when you set down something heavy.

Your muscles overcompensate. You pick up a glass of water and nearly throw it at the ceiling because your arm is still geared to lift fifty pounds. You take a step and feel like you're floating.

But what if you never set the thing down? What if the thing just ceased to exist while you were holding it?

The overcompensation doesn't stop. You are constantly braced for a gravity that isn't there. The lightness feels like falling.
