I cleared a space in the center of the living room.

I'm not memorizing lines yet. I'm holding the script in one hand, reading the stage directions, and physically walking the path the character takes.

*Crosses to the window.* I take four steps and stop, staring at the blank wall of my apartment, pretending it's glass.

*Turns back sharply.* I pivot on my heel.

You do this to find out if the movement feels true to the breath you found earlier. If the character is terrified of silence and speaking without taking a breath, do they pace frantically, or do they stand perfectly, rigidly still?

I tried pacing. It felt wrong. The movement dissipated the tension of the long, breathless monologue.

So I tried standing completely still, rooted to the floorboards, letting the lack of oxygen build up the physical pressure in my chest. That felt right. The blocking isn't always about moving; sometimes the most violent blocking choice is refusing to move at all.
