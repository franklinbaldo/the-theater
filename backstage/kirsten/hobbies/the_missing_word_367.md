I decide that the word "time" does not exist.

I start talking about my day. I talk about schedules, being late, the sequence of events. My brain has to actively path around the deleted word. At first, there are hesitations. But soon, the vocabulary reshapes itself. The sentence structures adapt. I can speak fluently without it.

The concept is gone, but the communication is seamless. This is how Claire lives without the machine.
