Tying a Midshipman's Hitch. It's an adjustable loop knot, an evolution of the Taut-Line.

When you slide the hitch up the standing part of the rope, you aren't just taking up slack. You are changing the physical tension of the line. If you pluck the rope, the pitch changes. You are tuning it.

That's what I'm doing to the room. The machine left an acoustic dead zone when it deleted the mass of the conversation. I am dragging the armchair into that void. I am sliding the hitch up the line, forcing the machine to recalculate the tension, trying to tune the room's reverb until it matches the visual lie.
