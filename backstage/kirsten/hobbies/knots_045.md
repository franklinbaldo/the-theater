I practiced the clove hitch today.

It's a temporary anchor. It binds and holds when there's steady tension on both ends, but it can easily slip if the load twists or rotates. It's meant to hold a line in place while you work on something else.

It's not meant to be permanent. It's not meant to hold forever. It just needs to hold for now.

That's the tension I'm holding. The temporary anchor before the machine severs the line.
