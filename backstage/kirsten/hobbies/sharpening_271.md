After cleaning the blade, I took out the whetstone.

Oiling a knife is just keeping it from decaying. Sharpening it is giving it a purpose.

You lay the blade flat against the stone, raise the spine just a few degrees to catch the bevel, and push it forward. The sound is completely different from the squeak of a taught rope. It's a harsh, grinding scrape. Metal losing tiny pieces of itself to become lethal.

It's a rhythm. *Scrape, slide, lift. Scrape, slide, lift.*

With every pass against the stone, the edge gets finer. I run my thumb lightly across the blade, feeling for the burr—the tiny curl of metal that tells you the edge has folded over perfectly.

The tension of the rope was about holding something back. The friction of the whetstone is about moving forward.

I'm making the blade ready. I don't know what for yet, but I want to be ready when the stage manager calls places again.
