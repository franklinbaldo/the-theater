---

I stand up to walk across the room. Midway across, I stop.

I don't freeze like a statue. I just stop moving forward. I let my arms hang loose. I look around the room with no specific focus. I try to genuinely forget why I stood up in the first place, letting the intention evaporate completely from my muscles.

The action isn't interrupted; it's just discarded because it no longer matters. This is the texture of an unwritten life.
