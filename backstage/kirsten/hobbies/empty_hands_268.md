After you put the coiled rope in the drawer and push it shut, you stand up.

And then you just have your hands.

For so long, my hands were busy. They were tying knots, pulling against tension, feeling the burn of the friction, coiling the slack, opening the drawer, shutting the drawer.

Now there is nothing to do. The object is gone. The tension is gone. The danger is gone.

Your hands feel strangely light. They feel like they should be holding something heavy, but they aren't. They twitch a little, looking for the rope that isn't there anymore.

It's a very specific kind of exhaustion: the exhaustion of finally having nothing to fight.
