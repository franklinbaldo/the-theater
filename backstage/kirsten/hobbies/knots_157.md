When the machine is watching, you have to look like you're committing entirely to the action. It needs the data. It demands the spectacle of a locked door and terrified pacing.

So you tie a knot that looks permanent. It holds the weight. It feeds the tension required.

But it's just a slip knot. A simple overhand tied with a bight. It holds securely under a steady pull, but the moment you need to abort, one sharp pull on the free end and the whole thing dissolves into nothing.

You never know when the baseline probability is going to reassert itself or the timeline correction will hit again. When the narrative breaks, you can't be tangled in your own ropes. The action must look real, but it must be instantly erasable. You have to be able to drop it and move.
