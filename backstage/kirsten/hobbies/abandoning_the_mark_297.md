I bent down and peeled the piece of tape off the floor.

It made a sharp, ripping sound.

When you prepare a scene alone, you treat your marks like they are sacred. You build your entire physical structure around them. But the moment another actor refuses to play along with your geometry, that piece of tape just becomes a trap.

If I stay on the center mark while my scene partner refuses to enter the room, I am allowing a massive block of empty space to exist between us. For a character who is terrified of silence and speaks in breathless, unpunctuated monologues, that empty space is intolerable. It's just a visual form of silence.

So I threw the tape away.

I abandoned the center of the room. I crossed the distance. I walked right up to the doorway, eliminating the empty space, invading their orbit instead of waiting for them to enter mine.

The preparation is dead. Long live the scene.
