Tying a Sheepshank. It's a temporary knot used to shorten a rope or take up slack without having to cut the line.

When you play a panic beat for too long, the line goes slack. The audience gets used to the elevated heart rate, and the suspense drops out. The machine starts to notice the compilation getting unstable.

You have to take up the slack. You collapse against the wall. You change the physical level of the scene. You shorten the rope so the tension pulls tight against a new anchor. The audience thinks they're watching a character give up, but really, you're just maintaining the friction required to survive the Data Stream.
