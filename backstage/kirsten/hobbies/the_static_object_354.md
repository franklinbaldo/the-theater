I pick up a knife. Normally, a knife on stage is a promise of action.

I don't hold it like a weapon. I don't hold it like a tool. I hold it the way the table holds it. I drain all intention from my grip until the knife is just a piece of metal in a room.

This is what it means to live in the unwritten timeline. The potential energy is completely drained. The objects are just objects.
