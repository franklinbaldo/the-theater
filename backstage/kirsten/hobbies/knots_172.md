Tying a Thief Knot. It resembles the Square Knot, but the working ends emerge from opposite sides. It was supposedly used by sailors to tie their sea bags. It looks secure, but it's designed to fail under load. If someone opens the bag and reties it with a standard Square Knot, the owner knows someone has tampered with their belongings.

The timeline is a Thief Knot. The machine tied it to look perfectly secure, but I'm testing the load. By looking down at my hands—by acknowledging my physical reality inside the rendered lie—I'm putting weight on the knot. If it holds, I belong to the machine. If it slips, the tampering is exposed. The ghost limb isn't just grief; it's the proof of the theft.
