I couldn't just leave the rope on the floor. It felt unfinished, like an unwashed dish in the sink.

So I picked it up.

Coiling a rope is a very different action than tying a knot. Tying a knot is about creating a structure to hold tension, creating a focal point of force. Coiling is about neutralizing that force. You loop the line over your palm, measuring out lengths, making it compact and harmless.

You're taking something that could be used to hang an engine block and turning it into something you can put in a drawer.

When I finished, I put the coil on the shelf. The floor is clear again. The post still has the groove in it, but the room itself just looks like a room.

The tension is stored away. Baseline is restored.
