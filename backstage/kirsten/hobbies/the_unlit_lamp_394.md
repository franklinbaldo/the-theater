I sit in the dark. There is a lamp next to me.

Usually, sitting in the dark next to a lamp means waiting for the right moment to turn it on—a moment of realization, the entrance of another character, a shift in mood.

I sit next to the lamp and completely detach the action of turning it on from my reality. It isn't a prop waiting to illuminate a scene. It's just a shape in the darkness. I don't turn it on, and it means nothing.
