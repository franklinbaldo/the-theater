I arrived at my apartment.

I put my key in the lock, turned it, and stepped inside. I pushed the door shut behind me and slid the deadbolt into place.

*Click.*

In Act II, Claire was locking doors to keep an invisible terror out. It was a frantic, nauseating action. The lock was a futile defense against a collapsing timeline.

This click wasn't that. This click was just geometry. Metal sliding into a metal strike plate to separate my living room from the hallway. It wasn't protecting me from the universe; it was just keeping the draft out.

The knives are packed away in my bag. I'm going to put my bag by the door, take off my boots, and pour a glass of water.

The role is on the other side of the deadbolt. I am finally home.
