I close my eyes. I breathe in. I breathe out.

In acting exercises, you often count your breaths to center yourself, to find a rhythm, to prepare for an entrance. It's a way of measuring your readiness against the unfolding scene.

I breathe without counting. I try to lose track of how many times I've inhaled or exhaled. I try to make the duration meaningless. If I breathe ten times or a hundred times, it makes no difference to the story, because there is no story.
