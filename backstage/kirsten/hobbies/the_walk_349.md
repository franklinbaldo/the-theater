I stand up. I walk across the room.

Normally, a walk on stage has a purpose. You are moving toward a prop, away from a scene partner, entering, or exiting. I try to strip all of that away. I walk simply to displace mass from one point to another.

It is surprisingly difficult to move without a story. The body wants a reason. When the constraint is truly erased, even the simplest action feels unsettlingly blank.
