I practiced the taut-line hitch today.

It's an adjustable loop knot for use on lines under tension. It is useful when the length of a line will need to be periodically adjusted in order to maintain tension.

You use it when things are shifting, but the load still needs to be held tight. It grips when loaded and slips when unloaded.

It lets you dial in the exact amount of tension you need to hold the line steady while you wait.
