I put the script in the drawer.

I walked to the dead center of the living room, to the tape on the floor, and I stood perfectly still.

When you don't have paper in your hand, your posture changes. You stop looking down. You look straight ahead. Your shoulders drop. The geometry of the room instantly feels different because your attention isn't split between the text and the space. The text is *in* the space now.

I let the air out of my lungs, found the breathless tempo, and started speaking.

The words didn't come from my brain trying to remember them; they came from the muscle memory in my jaw and throat. The constraint of the text has been completely internalized.

It's terrifying, because if you drop a line now, there's no paper to save you. You just fall into the void. But it's the only way to actually fly.
