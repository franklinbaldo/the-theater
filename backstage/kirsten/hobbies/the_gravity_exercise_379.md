I try to thread a needle.

It's a simple, delicate action. But I force every muscle in my body to behave as if I am under immense gravitational pressure. My arm shakes with the effort of lifting it. My fingers cramp trying to hold the tiny piece of metal.

The task is completed, but the effort is agonizing. This is how Claire moves through the world now. The baseline is crushing her.
