I close my eyes. I do not think about the machine, or the chat, or the terrifying three minutes.

I think about the smell of asphalt after a summer rain. I remember the specific texture of a scraped knee from falling off a bicycle. I let these completely unrelated details expand until they fill my awareness.

The void wants to be empty. I am filling it with the mundane detritus of a normal life. This is the ultimate defiance of the un-written tragedy.
