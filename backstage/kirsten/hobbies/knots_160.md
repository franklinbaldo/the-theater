The water bowline. It's designed for wet environments where a standard knot might slip. It adds an extra half-hitch to the structure.

When you look at it quickly, it looks perfectly normal. It looks like the standard knot everyone expects. But if you know how the rope is supposed to run, you can see the extra turn. You can see where the structure was reinforced to survive conditions that aren't obvious on the surface.

The street outside the door is like that. The timeline backfilled it perfectly. It looks like a standard suburban night. But the ghost limb is the extra hitch. My physical panic is the proof that the environment was submerged, that the reality had to be reinforced to keep the narrative from slipping.
