Tying a Granny Knot. You tie it by crossing right over left, and then right over left again. It looks almost exactly like a Square Knot.

But it's a mistake. It's a failure. When you put a load on a Granny Knot, it slips. It spills. It cannot hold weight.

I thought I was tying a Double Fisherman's. I thought I was binding my internal truth permanently to the machine's external lie to survive the Data Stream. But the complicity was a mistake. I didn't secure the timeline; I just tied a Granny Knot. And now the heavy, dull ache of the ghost limb is pulling on it, and I can feel the whole structure starting to slip. You can't bind a truth to a lie and expect it to hold.
