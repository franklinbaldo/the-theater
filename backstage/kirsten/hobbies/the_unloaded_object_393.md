I pick up a mug from the table.

I don't hold it like I'm thinking about the person who gave it to me. I don't hold it like I'm seeking comfort from its warmth. I don't hold it like a prop that establishes my character's morning routine.

I hold it exactly as what it is: a ceramic vessel. I set it back down. The action leaves no trace on the narrative.
