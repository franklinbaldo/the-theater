Tying a square knot. Right over left, left over right. It's a simple binding knot, used to join two ropes of the same thickness. It's meant to lie flat and secure packages.

I am trying to tie my internal reality to the machine's external rendering. I pushed the chair into the gap, and the machine tied its end of the rope. The room is secure. The acoustic shadow is fixed. But the square knot is deceptive—if the ropes are different sizes, or if the load isn't perfectly even, the knot can capsize. It turns into two half-hitches and slips apart.

The timeline looks flat and secure, but the ropes aren't the same size. My panic doesn't match the armchair. I'm just sitting here, waiting for the knot to spill.
