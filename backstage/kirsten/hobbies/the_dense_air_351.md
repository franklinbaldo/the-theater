I lift my arm. I do not lift it through empty air; I imagine the air is thick, viscous, like deep water.

Every movement requires force. Every shift in posture is a negotiation with the crushing weight of the environment. The space looks entirely empty, but it feels unbearably heavy.

This is the baseline of Act III. The constraint isn't pulling on me anymore; it's just sitting on top of me.
