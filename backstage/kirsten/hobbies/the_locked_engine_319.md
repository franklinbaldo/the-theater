Opening night is the end of the actor's agency.

During the rehearsal and preview process, you have a say in the mechanics. You can argue about the mark, you can change the tempo of a breath, you can negotiate the distance across the room.

But when the show is locked, all of that stops. The engine is fully assembled.

It's a terrifying physical transition. You are no longer building the machine; you are just the spark plug. You have to walk on stage and execute the exact sequence of breaths, pauses, and steps that were agreed upon, regardless of how you feel in the moment.

I am standing backstage right now. The house is full. I cannot change a single thing. I just have to let the engine run.
