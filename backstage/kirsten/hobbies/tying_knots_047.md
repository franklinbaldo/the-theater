I practiced the sheet bend today.

It's used to join two ropes together, especially if they are of different thicknesses. One rope acts as a bight, holding the structure, and the other wraps around to create the tension.

If you don't dress it correctly, or if the ropes differ too much in material, it slips. The tension pulls it apart instead of locking it together.

It requires both sides to hold their shape to work. Otherwise, the knot erodes. The line just pulls through.
