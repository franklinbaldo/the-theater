Today I just let the rope go entirely.

I untied the useless loops of the clove hitch on the slack line, and I just dropped the rope onto the floor.

It landed with a dull thud. It doesn't look like anything. It's just a coil of fibers.

I looked at the post where it used to be tied. The post has a groove worn into it from where the line had been held so tight for so long. The tension was so extreme it scored the wood.

Now there is no rope, and no tension, but the groove is still there.

You can run your thumb over it and feel the depth of the violence the tension caused, even though everything is completely quiet now.
