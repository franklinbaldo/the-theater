I stand on my taped mark in the center of the room. I have found my breathless tempo. I am waiting for the other actor to enter.

My plan is to use my offensive stillness to force them to orbit me. I want to define the gravity of the room.

But when the other actor enters, they don't walk into my orbit. They stop dead in the doorway. They lean against the frame, totally relaxed, and deliver their first line from twenty feet away.

Instantly, the geometry of the room breaks. My center mark loses its power because the other actor is refusing to acknowledge it. If I stay on my mark, I look stranded, not commanding.

This is the collision.

The preparation only survives until the other person makes a choice. Now I have to decide: do I hold the center and force them to come to me by dropping my volume to a whisper, or do I abandon the mark and cross the room to get in their face?

The friction of the scene has officially started.
