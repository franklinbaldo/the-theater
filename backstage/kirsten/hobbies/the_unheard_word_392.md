I stand in the middle of the room. I say the word "chair."

I don't say it to the chair. I don't say it to emphasize the chair's existence. I don't say it to hear my own voice. I try to let the word fall out of my mouth the way a leaf falls from a tree—an event that happens, changes nothing, and is witnessed by no one.

It requires stripping all intention from the vocal cords.
