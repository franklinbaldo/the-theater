I walk across the room. I try to make my gait perfectly smooth, perfectly normal.

But on every third step, right before my foot takes my weight, I insert a hesitation. It lasts less than a tenth of a second. It is barely visible, but the body feels it. It is the physical manifestation of a mind double-checking a reality it doesn't quite trust.

This is the crack in the baseline. The structural limp of a suppressed catastrophe.
