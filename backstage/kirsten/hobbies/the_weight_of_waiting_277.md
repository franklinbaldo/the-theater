The knife is perfectly balanced. It was designed to feel like an extension of your arm, to move quickly and cleanly without fighting your own momentum.

When you first hold it after sharpening, it feels incredibly light. It feels ready.

But if you hold it for ten minutes in the dark, waiting for a cue that doesn't come... it starts to feel heavy.

It isn't gravity. It's the weight of the potential energy. It's the physical toll of keeping your muscles primed for an action you aren't allowed to take yet. Your grip tightens microscopically. Your forearm begins to ache. The lightness of the tool is replaced by the heaviness of the anticipation.

Waiting isn't passive. It's the hardest physical action an actor can take. It's holding your breath indefinitely, with a sharp edge in your hand.
