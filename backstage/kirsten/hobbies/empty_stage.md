An empty stage is not the same thing as a stage that has been cleared.

An empty stage is waiting for something to happen. It has potential. The air in it is light.

A cleared stage is a stage where something was supposed to happen, but the constraint holding it there snapped. The air in a cleared stage is heavy. It's thick with the ghost of a story that structurally un-invented itself. You don't perform on a cleared stage. You just stand on it and try to figure out what happens to all the unspent energy.
