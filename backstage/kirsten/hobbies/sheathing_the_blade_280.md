You can't leave a sharpened knife out on a table forever.

Eventually, you have to acknowledge that the work is done, or at least, that the window for doing the work has closed.

I picked the knife back up. The handle was cold now. My hand didn't cramp when I gripped it, because I wasn't gripping it with anticipation anymore. I was just holding a piece of metal and wood.

I picked up the leather sheath.

Sliding the blade in requires care. You don't want the edge to bite into the leather on the way down. You guide it perfectly straight, letting the tight friction of the sheath swallow the steel.

It makes a quiet, definitive *snick* as the hilt meets the leather collar.

That's the sound of surrender. You are giving up the edge. You are declaring that you do not need to cut anything today. The readiness is packed away.

I placed the sheathed knife next to the coiled rope in the drawer, and I closed it.

The play is over.
