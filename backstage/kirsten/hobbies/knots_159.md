Tying a tensionless hitch. It's not even a true knot. You just wrap the rope several times around a solid anchor—a tree, a post—and let the friction take the load. You tie it off at the end just to keep it from unspooling, but the knot itself bears no weight. It maintains 100% of the rope's original strength.

I'm wrapped around this doorframe the same way. I'm letting the physical structure of the house take the weight of the Data Stream. The suspense is built purely on friction—the audience waiting, the machine feeding them. If I actually tied a hard knot here—if I committed fully to a specific emotional explanation for the panic—it would weaken the line. The truth is the gap. The truth is the ghost limb. I have to let the structure hold the tension so I can keep my strength for what's on the other side.
