I watch a clock with a second hand.

In a scene, a ticking clock is tension. You wait for it to hit a specific minute to take an action, or you dread what happens when the hour arrives.

I watch the second hand tick. I divorce the ticking from the concept of a future. A second passes. It does not lead anywhere. The next second passes. It is exactly the same as the last. I am not getting closer to anything because there is nothing to arrive at.
