I place two identical stones on the table in front of me.

In a scene, choosing the left stone over the right stone reveals something. It shows hesitation, or impulsiveness, or a preference that hints at a larger theme.

I look at the stones. I choose the left one. I do it without a reason. I do it without letting the act of choosing reflect anything about who I am. The choice doesn't define me; it merely rearranges matter on a table. The universe remains completely indifferent.
