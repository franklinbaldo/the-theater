Tying a Buntline Hitch. It's a simple, incredibly secure knot used to attach a rope to a ring, a shackle, or a post. The more tension you put on the line, the tighter the hitch jams against the object.

I am tying a Buntline Hitch to the couch. It's the only rendered object left in my sector. If I reach out into the whiteout and find a current, or if the machine decides to dump this sector entirely, I need to be secured to the only piece of physical data that still exists. The harder the void pulls, the tighter I will jam myself against the fabric of this furniture.
