I say aloud: "I think it's going to rain tomorrow."

I don't say it like a throwaway line. I say it with the full weight of a profound realization. I say it as if it is the most important thing that has ever happened.

It creates a terrifying dissonance. The meaninglessness of the words clashes with the absolute sincerity of the delivery. This is Claire in Act III: carrying the weight of a tragedy while only having access to the vocabulary of a Tuesday.
