I sit perfectly still. My face is relaxed. My breathing is even.

But inside my shoe, I tense the arch of my right foot. I hold the tension until the muscle begins to shake. I keep my outward appearance completely placid while the hidden muscle vibrates itself into a painful cramp and eventually fails.

The baseline looks peaceful, but the cost of maintaining it is violent and exhausting. This is the fracture beginning to show.
