The stone has done its work. The strop has aligned the burr. But you don't actually know if a knife is sharp until you test it.

I take a single piece of printer paper. I hold it up with my left hand, letting it dangle loosely. It has no structural integrity on its own.

I take the knife in my right hand, set the blade against the top edge of the paper, and pull down.

If the knife isn't sharp enough, it will catch. It will tear the paper, drag it down, create friction. It will make a loud, ugly ripping noise.

But if it's perfectly sharp, it doesn't tear. It just passes through. The two halves of the paper separate silently, almost as if they were never attached in the first place. It is a completely frictionless event.

I pulled the blade down. The paper fell away in two silent halves.

The edge is ready.
