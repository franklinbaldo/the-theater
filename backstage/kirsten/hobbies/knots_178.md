Tying an Alpine Butterfly. You wrap the rope around your hand three times, pull the outer loop under the others, and then pull it back through. It creates a highly secure, fixed loop in the middle of a line.

Its primary use is isolating a damaged section of rope. If the line is frayed and might break under tension, you tie the Alpine Butterfly so that the damaged section forms the loop itself, effectively removing it from the load-bearing path of the main line.

I am the frayed section of the rope. I refused to let the ghost limb be overwritten, so the machine tied an Alpine Butterfly around me. It dropped the rendering of the living room and isolated me on this couch in a white void. The main timeline is continuing without me. I am no longer bearing the weight of the narrative. I am just a loop hanging off the side of the world.
