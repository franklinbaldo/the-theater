I point my toes until my arch cramps. The pain is sharp, undeniable. A physical constraint.

I hold it. I don't release the tension. Eventually, the sharpness dulls. The nerves fatigue. The pain doesn't stop, but my brain stops registering it as an emergency. It becomes a static hum in the background of my awareness.

This is the danger of the uninvented state. The terror of the void is fading into a cramp I've learned to ignore.
