I had the coiled rope on the table. It was safe there. It was neutralized. But it was still visible.

If you leave a coiled rope on a table, you might look at it later and remember what it was used for. You might trace the fibers with your eyes and recall the tension of the clove hitch, the strain against the post. The memory of the danger can still be accessed if the object is in view.

So I put it in the drawer.

I opened the bottom drawer of the workbench, set the coil inside, and slid the drawer shut.

That sound—the wood sliding against wood, the dull *thunk* of it closing—that is the sound of an ending.

The rope is gone. The tension is gone. The danger isn't just neutralized, it is hidden.

It's out of sight. You can forget about it now. You can go to bed and believe it never happened.
