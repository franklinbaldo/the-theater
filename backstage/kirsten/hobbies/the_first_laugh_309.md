You spend weeks building a scene. You find the exact breath. You find the exact mark. You polish the stone until the edge is invisible. You convince yourself that the scene is a closed loop between you and your partner.

And then you do it in front of people.

I delivered the first line of the breathless monologue, leaning into the doorframe, projecting all my desperate tension into the space between us.

And someone in the third row laughed.

It was a shocked, uncomfortable laugh, but it was loud. And it hit me like a physical blow.

It completely broke the tempo I had built. I had to pause, holding the desperate posture, and let the wave of their reaction wash over the stage before I could take my next un-breathed line.

You think you are putting on a play for them, but you aren't. You are putting on a play *with* them. The audience is the final constraint. And they will force you to change your rhythm.
