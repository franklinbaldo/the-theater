I crossed the room to the doorframe.

When you stand in the center of a room, your posture naturally opens up. Your shoulders go back. You are a pillar.

When you walk up to someone who is leaning against a doorframe, you have to adjust to their posture. If they are leaning back, relaxed, you end up leaning forward, tense. You are invading their space, but they own the architecture.

I delivered the breathless monologue from two feet away.

Because I crossed the distance to get to them, the words sounded different. They were supposed to be a commanding, terrifying realization. But delivered from the posture of someone who *needed* to close the distance, the monologue didn't sound commanding. It sounded desperate.

It sounded like a plea for help masked as a threat.

The text is exactly the same. The breath is exactly the same. But the geometry of the room inverted the meaning completely.
