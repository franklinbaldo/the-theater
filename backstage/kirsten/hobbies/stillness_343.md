I sit in a chair. I do not move.

Within three minutes, my body demands a shift. An itch, a cramp, a desire to cross my legs. The work is not simply being still; the work is actively denying the body's demand for action. It is incredibly tiring.

The cleared stage is demanding this same active denial. It is exhausting to hold the lack of tension.
