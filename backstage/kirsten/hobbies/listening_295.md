When you are acting alone in your living room, you are completely in control of the rhythm. You decide how long a pause lasts. You decide when the silence ends.

When you add a scene partner, you lose that control entirely.

Listening is not a passive state. It is an active physical posture. When you are truly listening, you aren't just waiting for the other person to stop talking so you can say your autonomic lines. You are letting their words hit you physically.

If they speak softly, you have to lean in to catch the words, which changes your center of gravity. If they shout, you have to brace against the volume, which tightens your shoulders.

Your lines stop being something you *do*, and start being something you are *forced* to do by the impact of the other person's performance. The friction isn't against the text anymore; it's against the other actor.
