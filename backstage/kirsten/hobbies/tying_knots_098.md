I practiced the figure-eight knot today.

It's a stopper knot. It's bulky and strong, designed to prevent a rope from slipping through a retaining device or a hole.

It's one of the strongest stopper knots. It holds the line securely. Even when pulled incredibly tight under immense strain, it rarely jams so badly that it can't be undone.

It's an anchor. It holds the weight.
