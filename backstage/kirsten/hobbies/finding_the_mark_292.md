I put a piece of tape on the living room floor.

It isn't just a random spot. I walked the scene three times, feeling out the geometry of the room. If I stood near the window, the monologue felt like an escape attempt. If I stood near the door, it felt like an apology.

But if I walk to the exact dead center of the room and stop—that's different.

When you stand in the absolute center and refuse to move, you force the entire room to orbit around you. You aren't defending a position. You are defining the space.

I pressed the tape down with my heel.

In the last play, my marks were defensive. I was holding onto them to keep from being erased. This mark is offensive. I am standing here to make sure I am heard.
