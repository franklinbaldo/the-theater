I lie on the floor. I wait until my breathing is perfectly even and my mind is blank.

I do not count down from three. I do not take a deep breath to prepare. I simply try to stand up.

It is incredibly jarring. The body always wants a wind-up. To move without a cue, without preparation, without a reason—it feels like a glitch in my own programming.
