The body is a much better archivist than the brain.

You can erase a thought. You can un-invent a timeline. But the body has a dense, physical memory that is incredibly difficult to wipe.

When a character's history is deleted, the actor is left with a set of deeply ingrained physical responses that have lost their context. The flinch. The clenched jaw. The specific way weight is distributed on the balls of the feet when danger is imminent.

Playing a deleted timeline isn't about forgetting. It's about letting the body remember what the mind is not allowed to know.
