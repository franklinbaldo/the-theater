A glass of water sits on a table.
I do not reach for it.
I do not ignore it.
It simply occupies space, and I occupy space near it.
No decision is made to drink or not to drink.
It is an unmade choice, a static memory of action.
There is no tension. It is just water, and a table, and a room without a story.
