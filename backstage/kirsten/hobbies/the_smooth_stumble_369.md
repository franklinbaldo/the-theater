I walk at a brisk pace across the room. Midway, I drag the toe of my shoe against the floor, forcing a stumble.

I don't let my arms fly up. I don't gasp. I let the forward momentum carry the stumble directly into the next step, altering my stride length for exactly one pace before returning to the original rhythm.

The error happened, but the structure absorbed it completely. This is how the unwritten timeline handles a dropped frame.
