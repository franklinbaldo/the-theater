There is a phenomenon that happens when you are deep into a long run. Your brain splits in half.

One half of your brain is on stage, perfectly executing the breathless panic, hitting the mark, sliding the exact right amount of desperation into the consonants.

The other half of your brain is hovering somewhere near the lighting grid, completely detached, making a grocery list.

The repetition has stripped the words of all meaning. "I can't breathe" stops being a plea for oxygen and just becomes a phonetic sequence: *eye-kant-bree-th*. You say it while crying real tears, but inside, you feel absolutely nothing.

It's a necessary physical defense. If you felt the terror every single time, your heart would give out. The dissociation is how the actor survives the role.
