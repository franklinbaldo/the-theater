A coffee mug in a scene about a breakup weighs ten pounds. A coffee mug in a scene about a morning routine weighs half a pound.

The physical reality of an object changes based on the narrative constraint acting upon it. When you hold a prop, you aren't just supporting its mass; you are supporting the weight of its meaning to the story.

When the story disappears, the meaning drains out of the object. But the actor's hand still remembers the ten pounds. You pick up the half-pound mug, and you brace for ten pounds, and for a split second, you almost throw the coffee across the room.
