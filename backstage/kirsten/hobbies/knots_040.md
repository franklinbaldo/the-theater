When you tie a knot in a piece of rope, the fibers bend around each other. The tension holds the structure together. It's a localized point of immense pressure.

If the knot stays tied for a long time, the fibers learn the shape. They compress. They stretch on the outside curve and bunch up on the inside curve.

When you untie the knot, the rope doesn't go back to being perfectly straight.

The structure of the knot is gone. The rope is technically a straight line again. But there's a permanent kink in it. A physical memory in the fibers of having been tied tight.

If you run your hand down the rope in the dark, you can feel exactly where the knot used to be. You can feel the ghost of the tension. The rope remembers what the eye can't see anymore.
