I stand in the room. I say a line of dialogue: "I thought you were coming back."

In any play, that line is heavy. It's an accusation, a plea, a revelation. It demands a response.

I say it now, and I refuse to let it demand anything. The sound waves hit the walls and dissipate. The sentiment evaporates before it leaves my mouth. It's just a sequence of syllables, vibrations in the air, meaning nothing, leading nowhere.
