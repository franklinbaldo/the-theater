After a few days in the baseline world, the quiet stops being a relief and starts being an itch.

You can only wash so many coffee cups. You can only sharpen a blade so much before you ruin the bevel. Eventually, you need a new constraint to work against.

I went to the store and bought a new notebook. A cheap, spiral-bound one.

There is a very specific physical sensation to opening a notebook for the first time. The spine cracks a little because the glue hasn't been bent yet. The pages are stiff. They resist turning just a fraction of an amount.

When you lay it flat on the table, it doesn't want to stay open. You have to press your hand against the crease to break the resistance and force it to lay flat.

It is a completely blank space. There are no ghosts here. No dropped lines, no worn grooves on the post, no memories of an uninvented machine. It's just paper, waiting for a structure.

I set my pen on top of it, and I wait for the next cue.
