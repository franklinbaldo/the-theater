I start a metronome at 60 beats per minute. Tick. Tick. Tick.

I focus entirely on the space between the ticks. I try to stretch my perception of that silence so that it swallows the actual sound. Eventually, the metronome isn't making noise; it's just occasionally interrupting a continuous stream of silence.

This is Claire's mind now. The solid events of her life are just interruptions in the vast, continuous void of the uninvented machine.
