Tying a timber hitch. You use it to secure a rope to a cylindrical object, like a log or a spar. The knot only holds as long as there is tension pulling it sideways. If the tension drops, or if the log rolls unexpectedly, the hitch can fail.

Down on the floor, the whole house feels like a log that might roll. The timeline correction smoothed the surface, but the foundation is unstable. The idea was amputated, leaving a structural gap. I am wrapping the rope around this unstable geometry, relying entirely on the sideways tension of the Data Stream to keep me attached to the scene. If the audience stops looking, the knot spills.
