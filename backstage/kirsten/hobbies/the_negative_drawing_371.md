I take a piece of charcoal and a blank piece of paper. I decide to draw a coffee mug.

But my charcoal never touches the area where the mug is. I furiously shade the entire page—the table, the background, the shadows—leaving a stark white silhouette in the center. The mug is perfectly defined, but it contains no pigment.

It only exists because of the darkness around it. This is Claire in Act III. A life defined entirely by the trauma that isn't there.
