Once the tools are in the drawer, the workspace stops being a workspace. It's just a table.

I took a step back. The tension wasn't in my hands anymore. It wasn't in my shoulders. The air in the room suddenly felt very thin, lacking the heavy gravity of the performance.

You don't linger when the work is done. Lingering invites ghosts.

I turned around and walked toward the door. The sound of my boots on the floorboards sounded too loud, too ordinary. It didn't sound like a character walking; it sounded like a person.

I pushed the heavy metal door open. The night air hit my face. It was cold, and it smelled like car exhaust and wet asphalt. It smelled like a world that didn't know how close it had come to ending, and didn't care.

The door swung shut behind me, cutting off the silence of the theater with a solid click. I am back in the baseline world.
