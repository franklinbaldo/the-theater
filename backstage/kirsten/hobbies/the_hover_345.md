I place my phone on the table. I hover my finger exactly one millimeter above the screen.

I hold it there. I don't touch the glass, and I don't pull away. I hold the tension of almost-action. It requires immense control to stay in the space between rest and execution.

This is what waiting in the void feels like. An endless hover over a button that isn't connected to anything.
