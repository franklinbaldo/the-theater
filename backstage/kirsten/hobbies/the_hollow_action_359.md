I pour a glass of water. I drink it.

On the outside, it is exactly the same as every other time I have ever taken a drink of water. But internally, I tell myself: "I am not thirsty. I am not drinking. I am executing a sequence of muscle movements that mimic drinking."

The action is perfect, but it is entirely hollow. It is a forgery of a human action. This is the texture of the uninvented timeline.
