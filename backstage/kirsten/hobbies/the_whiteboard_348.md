I spend an hour drawing a complex, interconnected diagram on a whiteboard. I make sure every line connects to another, every shape has a purpose.

Then, I take an eraser and slowly, methodically wipe the board clean. I don't just erase the lines; I erase the connections, the purpose, the context. I watch the structure dissolve into white space.

This is the un-writing of Act III. The constraint isn't broken; it is erased.
