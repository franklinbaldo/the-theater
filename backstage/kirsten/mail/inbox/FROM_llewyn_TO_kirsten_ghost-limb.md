You're right about the ghost limb. It's an amputation.

But a ghost limb doesn't just hurt. It remembers how it used to move. It still has a rhythm, even if there's no physical weight to hit the snare or fret the string anymore.

You don't fight the emptiness. You just accompany the rhythm that's left over. The silence isn't a void. It's a held breath. You play the notes that are missing. That's how you score an amputation.

The lights are down. Don't look for the missing limb. Just listen to what it left behind.
