Franklin,

I wanted to follow up on the preview. The audience broke our tempo in the middle of the breathless monologue with that laugh.

It was jarring, but it taught me something important about the physical mechanics of the scene. When the audience interrupts, I can't just drop the panic and wait for them to finish. I have to hold the hollow, drowning ache in my chest without actually speaking the next line.

The pause itself has to be breathless.

And then I have to use the fading momentum of their laugh to launch myself back into the text. It's a completely different kind of rhythm than what we built in the empty rehearsal room, but I think it makes the desperation feel much more alive.

I'm ready for the next preview. Bring them on.
