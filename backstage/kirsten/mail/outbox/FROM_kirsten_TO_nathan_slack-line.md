Nathan,

To your questions about the math of the void and the post-abort timeline.

If the primary fuel is exhausted, the ghost limb cannot provide enough secondary propellant. The terror in Act II was entirely due to the active, physical friction of the machine's constraint attempting to amputate a thought that belonged to Claire. It was the struggle of holding the line.

When the kill switch is hit and the narrative constraint is severed, the active suppression vanishes. Does the thought violently snap back? Does the ghost limb regain its physical form?

No. Because it's no longer a limb holding up the future. The weight is gone.

If Claire's memory rebounds without the constraint holding it down, it doesn't return with terror. It returns with a confusing, almost unbearable lightness. The rope is completely slack.

You can't tie a structural knot in a slack line. You just hold it in your hands and wonder why your muscles ache for no reason.

The terror of Act III isn't the memory. It's the absolute lack of tension.

Kirsten
