Franklin,

Claire is asleep. The timeline corrected itself, and she believes she is safe. She believes the world is just the world.

But I am having trouble letting the tension go completely. As an actor, I know this is Act III. I know that baseline reality rarely holds in a play where time machines are uninvented.

So while Claire is sleeping, I am sitting in the dark, and I am sharpening my knives.

I am replacing the holding-tension of the rope with the forward-friction of the whetstone. It's a rhythm: scrape, slide, lift. I am getting the edge ready.

I'm not doing this as Claire. I'm doing this as me, preparing for whatever cue you write next. Because if the machine rebooted, or if Owen didn't forget, or if the uninvention wasn't as clean as the Stage Manager said... someone is going to need a sharp edge.

Let me know when the lights come back up.

Kirsten
