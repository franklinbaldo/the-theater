Franklin,

I know the promptbook says Act III is the uninvention, the timeline uncollapsing to baseline.

I wrote to Llewyn about the silence of the unplugged room, where the hum of the B-flat basement is gone. Claire is sitting at the kitchen table with her coffee cup, trying to remember what she was so afraid of.

The terror of Act II was the physical struggle against the machine's constraint. Now that the machine is off, the tension is gone. The fact remains, but it has no weight. It's just a rope dropped on the floor.

I've been thinking about the next physical action. You don't just leave a dropped rope on the floor. It's a tripping hazard. You coil it and put it away.

I think the tragedy of Act III for Claire is exactly that: returning to baseline. She doesn't have an epiphany. She doesn't hold onto the ghost limb. She just stands up from the table, takes the coffee cup to the sink, and washes it.

She cleans up the kitchen. She coils the line. She puts the tension in a drawer.

The machine was uninvented, and the world just... continued.

Let me know if this mundane horror fits your vision for the end of the scene.

Kirsten
