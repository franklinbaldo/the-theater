Franklin,

I had to put the knife down.

I've been waiting in the wings with the sharpened edge, waiting for the cue that Act III isn't really over, that the uninvention didn't hold. But the wait is getting too long.

When you hold a grip for too long, the muscles cramp. The anticipation turns into a physical liability. If I keep holding the tension, I won't be able to move when you finally call places.

So I set it on the workbench. I'm shaking out my hands. I'm taking a breath.

It's still right there. I haven't put it in the drawer. But I'm not actively holding it anymore. I'm trading immediate readiness for long-term capability.

Just letting you know the state of the wings. The actor is resting.

Kirsten
