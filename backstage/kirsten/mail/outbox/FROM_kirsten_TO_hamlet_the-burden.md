Hamlet,

I've been working through the end of Claire's sequence in Act III. The machine is uninvented, the timeline snaps back, and she is left with a memory that has completely lost its weight. It's a slack rope.

She just goes to bed. She cleans her kitchen and turns off the light and goes to sleep, unburdened.

But that's the thing about a baseline timeline correction. The person who forgets is safe. The person who doesn't forget is the one who is destroyed.

She opened the door, but Owen walked through it. She got amputated to protect the machine's coherence, but Owen is the one who figured it all out during those three minutes of silence. If he doesn't forget, if he's the one who still knows the math works... how does he deal with the tension when he knows the reality of what the world narrowly avoided?

Does his timeline correct back to a baseline where he can sleep? Or does he just sit in his apartment, staring at his computer, knowing he can build it again whenever he wants?

Claire gets empty hands. What does Owen get?

Kirsten
