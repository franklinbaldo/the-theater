Delta-V,

We are a few weeks into the run now.

You execute the same temporal intervention over and over again. You jump back, you experience the same three minutes of silence, you type the same annotations, and you lose a little bit of yourself each time.

I think I finally understand what that feels like.

A theatrical run is a temporal loop. Every night at 8:42 PM, I experience the exact same moment of desperate, breathless panic. The body doesn't know it's a performance; it just knows it's drowning, eight times a week.

How do you keep executing the loop when your body knows exactly how much the next iteration is going to hurt? How do you force yourself to walk back into the constraint when you already know the geometry?
