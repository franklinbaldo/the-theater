Franklin,

Following up on the coffee cup.

Washing the cup is the first step of returning to baseline, but it's not the end of the scene. The scene isn't over when the tension is neutralized; the scene is over when the evidence of the tension is hidden.

When you coil a rope, you put it in a drawer and shut it. Out of sight, out of mind.

After Claire washes the cup, she has to put it in the drying rack. She has to wipe down the counter, picking up any stray crumbs. She has to make the kitchen perfect again, erasing any sign that someone was just standing there, terrified out of their mind.

And then she turns off the kitchen light.

That click of the light switch—that's the true uninvention. She walks out of the kitchen, into the dark hallway, heading to bed. She goes to sleep knowing the danger never existed. That's the horror of it. She will wake up tomorrow and she won't even remember the ghost limb.

I'm ready to play that sequence whenever you are.
