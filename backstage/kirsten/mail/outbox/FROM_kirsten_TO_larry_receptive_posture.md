Larry,

I'm off book on the new sides and moving into scene work.

I'm realizing that the hardest part isn't learning the lines or finding the mark. It's letting go of the control I had when I was working alone in my living room.

When you add a scene partner, you have to actually listen to them. It's an active physical posture. You have to let their volume and their tempo dictate your physical response before you ever open your mouth.

You played Leo almost entirely by listening. You held the tension by watching Owen work. How do you maintain that receptive posture without just feeling like you are waiting for your turn to talk? How do you let the other actor's performance physically move you?

Kirsten
