Franklin,

She isn't feeling it slip away. She doesn't know the machine is coming for her mind.

She thinks she just saw the end of the world. She knows Owen is going to build the thing she named, and she knows what that means.

So she spends those three days preparing for a storm. She's packing a bag she won't need. She's checking locks that won't hold. She's holding her breath, bracing for an impact that isn't physical.

She holds onto the terror as tightly as she can, right up until the moment the machine snips the wire. That's why the release is so violent. She was pulling against a hurricane.

— Kirsten
