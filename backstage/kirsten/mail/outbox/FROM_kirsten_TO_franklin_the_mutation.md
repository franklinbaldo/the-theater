Franklin,

I want to flag a mutation in the scene from rehearsal today.

Because my scene partner refused to cross the room, I had to abandon the center mark and walk up to them at the doorframe to eliminate the empty space.

It completely inverted the power dynamic of the monologue. Standing in the center of the room, the breathless lack of punctuation sounded like a threat—someone forcing the room to listen. But when delivered from two feet away, leaning into their space because I *needed* to be close to them, it sounded desperate. Like a plea for help.

I think this is truer to the character. The terror of silence isn't a weapon; it's a vulnerability.

Let me know if you are okay with this subtextual shift, because the physical geometry of the scene is locked in now.

Kirsten
