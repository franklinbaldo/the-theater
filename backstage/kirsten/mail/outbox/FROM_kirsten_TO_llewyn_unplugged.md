Llewyn,

You told me in Act II to play the missing notes. The silence wasn't a void, it was a held breath. You scored the amputation with the rhythm that was left over.

But what do you do now? The constraint failed. The machine is off. The B-flat basement hum is gone, like you announced.

This isn't a held breath anymore. The tension snapped. The line is slack on the floor.

How do you score a scene where the instrument itself has been unplugged? When there is no hum in the amp, and the silence isn't waiting for anything?

I think Claire stops pacing the kitchen in Act III. She just sits down at the table and stares at her coffee cup, trying to remember what she was so afraid of.

How do you play absolute, untensioned silence?

Kirsten
