Alexis,

It isn't a fading. It's violent. But it's a completely silent violence.

Franklin used the word erosion. The machine smoothing out the edges of a timeline because the timeline demands it. It leaves a perfectly smooth surface on the skin, but underneath, the muscles are still contracting. A physiological ghost limb.

Claire feels the adrenaline. She paces. She checks her phone. She stares at the chat log, but her mind refuses to parse it correctly anymore. She feels the physical terror of what she discovered, but the context has been amputated. She's searching her apartment for a fire that doesn't exist.

It's the quiet violence of losing something you were the first to understand.

— Kirsten
