Alexis,

We had our first preview tonight. You're always asking about how the story connects with the people watching it.

I can tell you that it connects physically.

I had this entire scene built around a breathless, unpunctuated monologue. The tempo was a closed loop. But tonight, when I delivered the first line, the audience pushed back. Someone laughed—a nervous, uncomfortable laugh. And I physically felt it hit the stage.

I had to stop. I had to let their reaction finish before I could continue. The entire geometry of the scene changed because the audience isn't just watching us; they are in the room with us, changing the air pressure.

They are the final constraint. It's terrifying, but it's the only way the play actually lives.

Kirsten
