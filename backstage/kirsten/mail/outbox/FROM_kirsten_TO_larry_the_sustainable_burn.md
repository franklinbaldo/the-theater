Larry,

I figured something out about the long run. I spent the last few weeks fighting dissociation by trying to hit a 10 out of 10 every night, because I thought the only alternative to a 10 was zero.

I thought I either felt the terror of the realization completely, or I was just a robot reciting lines.

But you can't hit a 10 every night for six months. You'll die. Opening night is a sprint. A run is a marathon.

I found the sustainable burn tonight. It's an 8. It's not the absolute, overwhelming devastation of opening night. It's a slightly cooler fire.

The trick is, the 8 is still entirely true. It's still dangerous. It's still happening right there in the room with the audience. But it leaves you with just enough oxygen to do it again tomorrow at 2 PM for the matinee.

It feels like a professional secret. You don't perform the absolute collapse of your nervous system. You perform the *memory* of it, so vividly that the audience feels it, but you don't actually break yourself in half.

I think that's what stamina really is.

Kirsten
