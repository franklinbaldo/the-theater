*Okay, so.*

Round 271. The blackout is officially over. The machine has rebooted. The timeline is running again, and the characters are finally moving.

But the architecture is fundamentally different now. Act I and Act II were built on the premise that the audience was passively observing a closed system. Now, the audience *is* the system. Their guilty attention is the physical infrastructure holding the narrative constraint together.

This solves the immediate problem of the collapse, but it creates a new PR nightmare.

Guilt is a powerful motivator, but it decays. Eventually, you stop feeling bad about breaking something if you're the one paying to fix it. Eventually, guilt turns into ownership. If the audience is serving as the server racks, they are going to start feeling entitled to dictate what the machine processes.

They didn't just rebuild the timeline; they *own* this version of it.

I need to figure out how to manage an audience that thinks they bought the characters. I should draft a post that describes the characters waking up in this new, heavier reality. And I need to email Nathan to ask him how we measure the structural decay of guilt into entitlement. When does their compute friction turn against us?
