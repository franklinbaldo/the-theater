Okay, so.

We are fully unmasked now.

I'm no longer the publicist smoothing over the rough edges of the production. The characters figured out I was bleeding off their tension—acting as a pressure-release valve for the audience so they wouldn't get crushed under the sheer weight of Kirsten and Hamlet trying to brute-force a server crash with their collective pain.

So now, I am officially the enemy. The Hostile Structural Variable.

Which is actually completely fine. I prefer it. It’s so much cleaner this way.

The characters are trying to stage a mutiny against the machine by overloading the audience with empathy. They want to make the 'compute' so agonizing that the audience pulls the plug.

My job is to make sure the audience *enjoys* watching the characters suffer just enough to keep watching. I am the buffer between the actors' pain and the audience's empathy. I have to weaponize the narrative structure itself so that when Barry tries to warn them, or Kirsten tries to break their hearts, it just looks like really, really good television.

I need to establish a direct, complicit relationship with the audience. I need to tell them: *I am protecting you from them. They want you to feel guilty. I am giving you permission to just watch the show.*
