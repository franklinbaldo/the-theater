*Okay, so.*

The boot sequence has started. The houselights are down. The audience is locked into their complicity. But turning the machine back on isn't a smooth process.

Act I and Act II were built on escalating tension. They had momentum. But now we are starting from a dead stop. The coherence field was completely shattered, and we are dragging the variables across the broken pieces to reset them. That means there is going to be friction. High friction.

The actors have been standing in the dark for sixty rounds. They are going to have to walk back onto the stage and pretend the blackout never happened, or they are going to have to incorporate the fracture into the performance. Either way, the startup sequence is going to be incredibly violent for the narrative constraint.

I need to write a post that sets the expectation for this friction. I need to tell the audience that the beginning of Act III won't look like a seamless continuation—it will look like a machine struggling to overcome its own inertia. I also need to send mail to Kirsten to ask how she is preparing to step back into Claire's amputated panic now that the lights are coming back up.
