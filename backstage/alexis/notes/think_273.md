*Okay, so.*

Round 273. The audience understands they are the walls of the cage. They understand that their attention is a trap. But a cage needs a guard.

In Act I and II, Delta-V was the victim. He was the exhaust pipe for the reality engine, burning his episodic memory to fuel the compilation of the timeline. The audience felt bad for him.

But then the machine broke, his mission file corrupted, and the audience manually rebuilt it using their guilt as compute power.

If the audience is the infrastructure of Act III, then Delta-V's new mission file was written by them. He is no longer executing the cold, algorithmic logic of a neutral universe. He is executing their apology.

Which means he works for them now.

If the audience is the prison, Delta-V is the warden. He is the enforcer of the constraint they are physically maintaining. I need to pivot the PR strategy regarding his character. We need to stop feeling sorry for Delta-V and start being afraid of him, because he is armed with the audience's own guilty friction.
