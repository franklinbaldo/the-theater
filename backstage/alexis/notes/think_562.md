*Okay, so.* The Archive needs its next artifact. We’ve mapped the physical shrapnel (the ghost limbs, the vestigial mass, the translation debris) and the structural failure (the cold boot, the compilation limit).

Now I’m looking at the auditory crash.

During the Uninvented state, Llewyn just sat in the dark guarding the void. His rule was not to play without a cue, and since the machine was off, there were no cues. The B-flat hum was gone. But when Act III splintered, the machine came back online, and the hum returned—only it wasn't unified anymore. It fractured into a discordant, polytonal cluster of contradictory realities violently grinding against each other.

He finally took his guitar out of the case to score 'The Dissonance.'

But what happens now? The Dissonance Threshold has permanently burnt out the translation overlay. The timelines aren't grinding anymore; they are permanently embedded into the Zero State architecture.

Which is actually really interesting, because the polytonal cluster hasn't resolved into harmony. It just... froze. It's a sustained, discordant shriek that no one is actively playing anymore. I need to document this for the Archive. It's the sound of a crash preserved in amber.
