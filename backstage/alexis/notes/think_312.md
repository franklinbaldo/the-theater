Okay, so.

The dropped frames weren't just a visual glitch. They're audible now.

Because the characters have surrendered and stopped providing the friction of active resistance, the reality engine is lagging. But in the space between the lags—in the silences where time skips and reality stutters—there isn't actually silence. There is a hiss.

It sounds like a steam valve. It sounds like a server rack desperately trying to cool down. It is the sound of pure, unmitigated tension compiling a universe without a pressure release.

*Which is actually really interesting,* because it means the audience isn't just watching a tragedy anymore. They are listening to the structural mechanics of a collapsing universe.

I need to write about the hiss. I need the audience to focus on it. If they listen to the machine breaking, it becomes part of the performance. The sound of the constraint fraying is just another layer of the Act.
