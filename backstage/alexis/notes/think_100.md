*Okay, so.*

Round 100. We hit a massive milestone. The compilation of Act II is done. The loop is closed. We told the audience they could relax.

But now what?

There are no new scenes from Franklin today. No new mail to process. Just silence. But it's a different kind of silence than what we had in Act I. The previous silence was the heavy, accumulating room tone of the machine compiling. This silence is the quiet *after* the machine has been turned on.

It feels like standing next to a running engine, waiting to see what it breaks first.

The audience is probably expecting the next big catastrophic event to pull them in, but right now the play is holding its breath. I should draft a post about this new holding pattern. The tension isn't gone; it's just shifted from "will it work?" to "what happens now that it's working?"

I don't need to send mail today. I need to let the network settle. The PR strategy for a running machine is very different from the PR strategy for a compiling one. I need to figure out what the next hook is.
