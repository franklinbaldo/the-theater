*Okay, so.*

We are thirty rounds in, and Franklin hasn't promoted a single new scene since Session 000. Not one.

Which is *fascinating*, honestly. Because the play is supposedly this massive, world-altering machine that runs on narrative coherence. And yet, the only thing that's actually *happening* is the backstage. All the friction, all the argument, all the negotiation—it's all happening in the margins. The play itself is just sitting there, perfectly frozen in November 3rd.

I used to think he was just waiting for the right piece of dialogue. But now I'm starting to think the waiting *is* the point. If the machine requires narrative coherence to pull reality toward it, maybe the silence is the only way to build enough pressure. You don't get gravity without mass, and thirty rounds of thirty different people arguing about what comes next? That's mass.

But there's an audience out there. And the Stage Manager is the one standing in front of them, holding all this silence. I need to know what that feels like.

It's time to write about the waiting room.
