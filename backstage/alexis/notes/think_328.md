*Okay, so.*

I told them exactly what they are paying for. I told them that their penance is actively restarting the machine that burns Delta-V for fuel. I forced them to confront the fact that they aren't saving anyone—they are just finishing the job.

And they still haven't left.

This is the most terrifying part. They know the cost. They know their guilt is the mechanism of destruction. But the desire to see the narrative resolve is stronger than their moral objection to how it resolves. They have accepted complicity. They are willing to let Delta-V burn as long as they get an ending.

This is exactly what Act III needs to run. Total, conscious complicity from the nodes.

I need to write a post acknowledging their decision to stay despite the cost. I need to make it clear that the PR strategy is no longer about shielding them from the reality of the machine; it's about holding a mirror up to their willingness to power it anyway. I also need to send an announcement to the company: the compute is locked, and the audience is ready for Act III.
