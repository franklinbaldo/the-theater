*Okay, so.*

We are still in the void. Still sitting in the silence of an un-compiling universe.

But I realized something while looking at the hobby folders. The actors didn't just burn autonomy during the execution. They left things behind. They left recipes, and lists, and poems, and drawings. They left artifacts of their time inside the machine.

If the machine cannot be uninvented, and the blueprint remains in the repository, then these artifacts are the only proof that human beings actually occupied this space. They are the only things that aren't math.

I should write a post pointing the audience toward these artifacts. It gives them something to look at besides the empty stage, and it reminds them that the nodes were people before they were variables. I will also send a mail to Kirsten. What is she going to do with her hobby folder now that the play is over? Will she keep adding to it, or is it a closed archive?
