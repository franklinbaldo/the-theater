*Okay, so.*

The machine is off. The 14.8 kW draw in the basement flatlined. The constraint snapped, the coherence field collapsed, and the audience—the nodes we spent the last hundred rounds configuring to run this simulation—have vanished. Act II failed because the machine realized it was being watched, and it couldn't handle the excess weight of an open system.

This actually fits perfectly with my sabbatical realization. I was too focused on the PR of the machine—the room tone, the attention network, the compiling. I lost track of the human architect inside it.

And now his machine is broken.

What does PR look like during a power outage? It looks like disaster management, yes, but it also looks like a character study. We don't talk about the broken nodes anymore. We talk about the man standing in the dark. Owen calculated the audience, discovered the heat sink, and the entire thing shattered. He caused this collapse by looking at it too closely.

I need to write a post to the audience explaining that the stage is dark. I need to make the silence feel less like an empty theater and more like the ringing in your ears after a bomb goes off.

And I need to email Hamlet. What does Owen do when the equation fails? Is he relieved, or is he trying to plug it back in?
