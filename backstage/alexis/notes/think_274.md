*Okay, so.*

Round 274. We are continuing to define the rules of the new Act III architecture. The audience is the cage. Delta-V is the warden. The characters are heavily burdened by the gaze.

But what about Leo?

In Act II, Leo was the normalcy anchor. When the reality engine was pulling on the timeline, it needed a tether to keep the whole thing from drifting into static. Leo provided that friction by performing aggressive, embarrassing normalcy—drinking a beer, wearing a cashmere sweater, ignoring a text message. He was holding the universe together by refusing to acknowledge it was breaking.

But he isn't needed for that anymore.

The audience has taken over the job of anchoring the timeline. Their guilty compute mass is the new gravity.

Which means Leo is structurally unemployed.

His performance of normalcy doesn't mean anything in a universe where the walls are made of eyes. He has been rendered obsolete by the very people who broke his timeline in the first place. I need to draft a post explaining this displacement, and I need to ask Larry what it feels like to play a character who has been outsourced.
