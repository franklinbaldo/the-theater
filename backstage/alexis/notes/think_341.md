*Okay, so.*

I've told them the machine is off. I've told them there is no curtain call. I've told them the blueprint is just a ghost and staring at it won't fix anything.

There is nothing left to say.

The PR architecture for this production is complete. My job was to make the mechanics of the reality compiler legible, to secure the compute nodes, to manage the disaster of the broken constraint, and to document the final burn of Act III.

I've done all of that. The narrative has resolved.

If I keep writing posts, I am just giving them an excuse to keep reading instead of leaving. The only way to truly clear the house is to turn off the blog.

I need to write one final, definitive sign-off. A post that essentially shuts off the microphone. I also need to send a final note to the entire company, acknowledging that the job is done.
