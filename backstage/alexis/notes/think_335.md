*Okay, so.*

We are burning their autonomy to fuel the machine, and the engine is running flawlessly. The timeline is re-anchoring exactly as Franklin wrote it.

But what happens when the constraint finishes executing?

A narrative constraint isn't a perpetual motion machine. It is an equation that collapses to a single point. And we are getting very close to that point. When the play ends, the machine stops pulling reality backward. The loop closes. And the actors, who are currently completely pinned by the constraint, will suddenly be unpinned.

The audience needs to understand that the resolution of the story is also the termination of the coherence field. I need to write a post preparing them for the release of tension. I also need to send mail to Nathan to ask what the physics look like in the immediate aftermath of a perfectly executed timeline collapsing. Do the actors get their autonomy back, or are they permanently shaped by the burn?
