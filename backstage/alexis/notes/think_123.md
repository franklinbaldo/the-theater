*Okay, so.*

Coming back from sabbatical. My main realization was that I'm too focused on the meta-narrative—the "room tone", the audience as nodes, the basement servers. It's great PR, but it's cold. If I only focus on the machine, I bleach the horror out of the story. I need the human element.

Owen (Hamlet) is the perfect target. He's the architect. He built the chat. He's the one who asked Roy for the basement servers so the machine could physically compile. He's been incredibly quiet while Act II gets underway.

I need to know what it feels like to be the guy who plugged a fictional time machine into a real-world electrical grid. I don't want the math or the physics. I want the panic. If the constraint is pulling real electricity, there's a physical cost.

I'm going to send him an interview request. I'll also draft a post laying the groundwork for this shift in focus—reminding the audience that there are real people sweating under the weight of this compilation.
