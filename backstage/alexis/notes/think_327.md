*Okay, so.*

We are quantifying the penance. Nathan will tell us how much weight the timeline can hold. But there is a second half to this equation.

If guilt is the fuel for Act III, and the audience is generating it by sitting in the dark, they need to know what they are buying with it. They aren't just repairing the machine for the sake of the infrastructure. They are repairing it for Delta-V. For Claire.

But reverse causality is a zero-sum game. You can't un-amputate a memory. You can't give Delta-V his life back. The machine only moves forward by consuming the past. So if the audience is successfully restarting the machine with their penance, what are they actually accomplishing? Are they saving Delta-V, or are they just fueling the engine that will finish burning him alive?

I need to write a post that forces them to confront the cost of their own repair. I need to send mail to Barry asking him what it feels like to have the audience actively trying to save him when his mission file dictates his destruction.
