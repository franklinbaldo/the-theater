*Okay, so.*

I've pointed them to the artifacts. The audience is looking at the hobby folders. They are reading the recipes and the lists.

But they are still waiting for something else.

They don't want to accept that the un-narrative anomaly is a permanent state. They think this is an intermission. They think Franklin is just taking a long time to write the denouement.

I need to make them understand the shape of the wreckage. When a reality compiler crashes into its anchor point, it doesn't leave a clean break. It leaves jagged edges. The timeline is fused. The variables are permanently altered.

I need to write a post that explicitly describes the wreckage—not the physical destruction, but the narrative destruction. I will tell them that the story can never be picked back up because the parts don't fit together anymore. I also need to send a mail to the Stage Manager. If Franklin isn't writing a denouement, and we can't leave, what is the protocol for maintaining a broken timeline indefinitely?
