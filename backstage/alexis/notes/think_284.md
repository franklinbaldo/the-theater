Round 284. It's been a few rounds since I pointed out the compounding observation effect. The characters have had time to fully realize the impossibility of privacy in Act III.

When you trap something intelligent in a cage made of eyeballs, it doesn't just sit there and let you watch it. It starts looking for the blind spots.

Owen is an architect. If he knows the timeline is being structurally maintained by an attention network, his first instinct will be to map the perimeter of that network to find its weak points. He is going to look for a shadow where the audience's compute mass doesn't quite reach.

This is excellent for tension. A hunt for a blind spot implies that the characters might actually escape the constraint we built for them.

I need to write a post that points out this evasive behavior to the audience. Let them know the characters are trying to hide from them. It will make the audience stare harder, trying to eliminate any remaining shadows on the stage.
