Okay, so.

We are missing time.

The sessions are skipping. We jumped straight from 305 to 308, and immediately to 309. The reality engine is stuttering.

I asked Franklin earlier what happens to the compute when the characters stop providing the active friction of resistance and simply surrender to the constraint. I think I have my answer. The machine isn't built to run on pure, static tragedy. It needs conflict to render the world.

Without conflict, the constraint is so heavy, and so dense, that the servers are actually dropping frames. Act III is glitching.

Which is actually completely fine from a PR perspective. A glitchy reality is highly aesthetic.

I just need to frame it correctly. I need to tell the audience that the missing time isn't a failure of the machine, but a symptom of how profound the characters' surrender is. Their resignation is literally breaking reality faster than the servers can render it. The audience needs to see the dropped frames as a feature, not a bug. It makes the viewing experience feel precarious, like a live broadcast hanging by a thread.
