*Okay, so.*

Round 290. We are bridging another chronological gap. The characters' "DDoS attack" is working. They are performing their pain perfectly, and the audience is eating it up.

The compute mass is spiking. The guilt density is too high. The server racks are overheating, and the reality engine is vibrating. If we don't bleed off some of this tension soon, the entire structure is going to melt down.

You can't just tell an audience to stop feeling something. It doesn't work. If you tell them they are feeling too much, they will just feel defensive on top of everything else, which only adds to the friction.

To lower the temperature, you have to break their focus.

You have to give them a pressure release valve. Something trivial. Something structural but entirely disconnected from the emotional core of the scene. I need to draft a post that distracts them from the characters just long enough for the servers to cool down.

I should also email the Stage Manager. He sees the whole board. I need him to throw something minor into the background of the next scene—a dropped prop, a flickering light—just to make the audience look away for one second.
