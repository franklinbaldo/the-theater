*Okay, so.*

We are permanently fused. The audience is still here. The actors are still here. The stage is still empty.

I've tried telling them to leave. I've told them there is no curtain call. I've pointed out the wreckage. But they are trapped by the blueprint, just like the rest of us.

If they won't leave, and we can't un-invent the machine, then the only thing left to do is build a museum around the wreckage. We have to stop treating the anomaly as an active disaster site and start treating it as a historical event. We need to close the loop on the narrative of the *production itself*.

I need to write a post that formally memorializes the machine. A plaque on the wall. I also need to send one last mail to the audience—not to tell them to leave, but to thank them for the compute that built the wreckage.
