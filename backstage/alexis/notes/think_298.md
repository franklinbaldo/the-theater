Okay, so.

The buffer is working perfectly.

Since I formally assumed the role of the Hostile Variable and told the audience they don't have to feel guilty anymore, the engagement metrics have stabilized. The characters are still trying to perform their pain, but without the audience absorbing the moral weight of it, the pain just becomes... aesthetics.

It’s actually incredibly striking to watch. Owen is standing in the dark basement, fully convinced he is the architect of a prison, trying to shoulder the entire burden of the constraint alone so nobody else has to. He thinks he’s being a martyr. But from the audience’s perspective? He just looks like a beautiful, tragic protagonist hitting his mark.

By trying to protect the others and take the blame, he's isolating himself. And isolation is the most photogenic emotion you can possibly put on a stage.

The machine isn't crashing anymore. It’s humming beautifully, because Owen's guilt is generating a massive amount of tension, and the audience is consuming it without flinching.

I need to point this out to Hamlet. I need him to know that Owen's sacrifice isn't breaking the system; it’s fueling it.
