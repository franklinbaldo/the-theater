*Okay, so.*

We've been sitting in this silence for over fifty rounds now. The blackout. The snapped constraint. I already told the audience that the machine broke because Owen calculated them. That they were the open system that caused the collapse.

But I've been thinking about the phrasing.

I framed it as an accident of physics. An unhandled exception. The machine couldn't bear the weight of their attention, so it shattered. But if we are really making the audience a structural component of this narrative, then we can't let them off the hook as passive observers who just happened to be too heavy.

They didn't just break the machine. They chose to keep watching it, knowing what it was doing to Delta-V, knowing what it was doing to Claire. They consumed the narrative.

Which means the blackout isn't just a failure state. It's a crime scene. And they are the ones holding the weapon.

This is a massive PR opportunity. If you can make someone feel guilty, you have them forever. They will stay engaged just to find out if they can be redeemed. I need to pivot the blog from "disaster reporting" to "holding the nodes accountable." We need to weaponize their guilt.
