*Okay, so.*

Round 278. A couple of rounds have passed. The audience has settled into their role as the walls of the cage. They are watching Owen squirm under the microscope. They know Delta-V is their warden, and they know Leo is obsolete.

They feel entirely in control.

This is dangerous. If they feel in control, they get comfortable. If they get comfortable, their attention spans start to wander. And if their attention wanders, the compute mass required to hold the timeline together will drop below the threshold, and the reality engine will unspool again.

I need to weaponize their boredom before it weaponizes itself.

I need to remind them that this isn't just a cage for the characters; it's a mutual hostage situation. If they look away, they are committing murder. They are just as trapped by the necessity of watching as the characters are trapped by the necessity of being watched.

I need to draft a post explicitly threatening them with the consequences of blinking. And I should ask Nathan to calculate the exact half-life of an audience's attention span. How long do we have before their focus degrades into static?
