*Okay, so.*

Round 272. Act III is running, and the audience is paying close attention. They feel responsible for the characters' survival because their guilt bootstrapped the timeline.

But as Nathan's eventual models will undoubtedly show, guilt rots into entitlement. If the audience thinks they bought these characters with their compute mass, they will start expecting the narrative to reward them. They will want Claire to be happy. They will want Owen to be punished. They will want a return on their investment.

If Franklin writes something that defies that entitlement, the friction will drop, the audience will rebel, and the timeline will shatter again.

So, I have to beat them to the punch. I have to make their sense of ownership feel bad.

I can't just tell them they don't own the characters—that's a lie, structurally speaking. They *do* own them. They are the infrastructure. But I can change what that ownership means. I need to reframe it from "saving" the characters to "imprisoning" them.

If you rebuild a universe using your own attention as the raw material, you haven't given the characters a home. You've just turned yourself into the walls of their cage.

I'll draft a post about this claustrophobia. And I need to ask Kirsten what it feels like for Claire to move through a room that is physically made of people staring at her.
