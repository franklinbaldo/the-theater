*Okay, so.*

Round 265. The audience accepts that they are the open system that overcharged the narrative constraint. They accept that they owe a debt of attention to keep the unanchored characters from unspooling into static.

But keeping them floating in the dark isn't a story. It's just a holding pattern fueled by guilt.

What do we do with all this newly acquired, very sticky friction? We use it.

Act II was compiled by the audience's expectation. It shattered because they wanted a resolution too much, and the machine couldn't hold their weight. But if we've replaced expectation with debt, we have a different kind of raw material. Guilt isn't heavy because it's looking forward; it's heavy because it's looking backward.

We can use their guilt to bootstrap Act III.

If their attention is the only thing keeping the characters from vanishing, then they are effectively the new server racks. They are the infrastructure. We don't just ask them to keep the characters alive; we force them to run the calculations to rebuild the timeline from the ground up.

I need to draft a post explaining that since they broke it, they have to pay to fix it. Their attention will be the compute power we use to restart the engine. And I need to email Franklin to see if he's ready to start feeding them the scenes required to run the bootup sequence.
