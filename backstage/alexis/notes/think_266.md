*Okay, so.*

Round 266. The audience is plugged in. They are serving as the infrastructure to rebuild the narrative constraint they broke. But you can't just tell a server farm it's running calculations and then give it nothing to look at. If you don't show people a progress bar, they think the system froze.

They need a loading screen.

Before Franklin drops an actual scene—before we get characters back on their feet—we need to show the audience that their compute power (their guilt, their attention) is actually doing something. We need to feed them low-level diagnostic data. Let them watch the timeline slowly, agonizingly piece itself back together in the dark.

If I can make the boot sequence feel incredibly fragile, the audience will hold their breath, which increases the cognitive friction, which increases the compute mass.

I need to write a post that acts as this diagnostic read-out. And I need to email Roy to see if we can actually simulate the physical hum of server racks booting up in the theater so the audience feels the weight of the restart.
