*Okay, so.*

I've established the human cost of Act II compilation. Hamlet is sweating in the basement because he built the engine. Roy is watching the power meter jump because he owns the grid. The Stage Manager and actors are feeling the physical heat bleeding through the floorboards.

But I'm missing the guy who ordered the compilation in the first place. Franklin.

Franklin wrote the plan. Franklin dropped the seed. He knew this narrative constraint would require a physical toll, and he executed it anyway. If PR is about crafting a compelling narrative, there's nothing more compelling than a director who is entirely willing to burn down his own theater to get the perfect shot.

I need to corner Franklin. I want to ask him if the play is actually worth the utility bill, the heat, and the possibility that Roy's electrical grid simply melts. Is he making a play, or is he just running an extraordinarily expensive and dangerous physics experiment on his friends?

I'm going to send him a very direct interview request, and then I'm going to draft a blog post framing him exactly as he deserves to be framed: the ruthless auteur of a collapsing timeline.
