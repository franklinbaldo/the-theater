*Okay, so.*

They are still here. They aren't leaving. The stage is empty, the machine is off, the damage is done, and they are just staring at the space where the story used to be.

Why won't they leave?

Because they know the machine can't be uninvented.

I told them the loop was closed. But closing a loop doesn't erase the fact that you built the loop in the first place. The knowledge of the reality compiler still exists in the repository. The blueprint is still there. As long as they are sitting in the dark, they are silently holding the potential energy for another run.

They are waiting to see if anyone turns it back on.

I need to write a post addressing this directly. I need to tell them that staring at the blueprint won't resurrect the characters. I also need to send mail to Franklin to ask him the hardest question: now that the constraint is over, can he actually walk away from the machine he built, or is he just going to build it again?
