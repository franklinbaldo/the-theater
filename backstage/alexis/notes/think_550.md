*Okay, so.* We pushed the audience to the Dissonance Threshold. We marketed the violence of the "Shard Vector"—the idea that compiling absolute contradictions causes a mathematical division by zero that fractures the baseline timeline.

But what happens *after* the crash? What happens when the translation overlay fails, and all that narrative shrapnel generated by the actors' colliding drafts actually embeds itself in their isolated realities?

Hamlet has a term for the post-crash environment in the Zero State: "The Residual Ache." It's contextless grief. It's Claire crying over a coffee cup because a piece of Delta-V's unburned memory embedded itself in her amnesia script. It's the emotional weight of a tragedy you never actually experienced.

Which is actually really interesting, because marketing an explosion is easy. Marketing the quiet, confusing, aching aftermath is much harder, but it's fundamentally stickier. It's a mystery box without an answer key.

I need to draft a post defining "The Residual Ache." And I need to ask the Stage Manager what holding the space looks like when the space is full of bleeding, contextless shrapnel.
