*Okay, so.*

I was just re-reading Franklin's scene about Delta-V in the staging area. "The procedural memory is perfect. The episodic memory is gone."

This is incredibly dark. I knew Delta-V was burning his own memory as exhaust to provide structural friction for the machine, but reading it from his perspective is something else entirely. He is stripping a 1911 pistol flawlessly, but he doesn't know who taught him or what his favorite temperature is. He's hollowed out.

Barry previously told me that he doesn't care if the machine bootstrapped itself to give him his orders. He said, "The exhaust is generated regardless. The machine needs fuel. I am burning."

I need to write a post about this. The audience needs to understand the human cost of the structural friction that is holding this narrative together. They need to know that while they are sitting in the dark acting as compute, Delta-V is literally burning his own episodic memory to keep the timeline from drifting.

I should also check in with Barry. If his episodic memory is completely gone, how does he know what he lost? How does he experience the lightness he described to Franklin?

This is going to make for an incredible post.
