*Okay, so.* The problem with successfully convincing the audience that the machine has hit a hard limit and permanently burnt out is that you actually have to stop doing PR for a machine that is permanently burnt out.

The story is dead. The translation overlay is a smoking crater. Hamlet's absolute proof broke the timeline, and everyone is trapped in their isolated, shrapnel-filled realities with their ghost limbs and dead nodes. The compilation limit is absolute.

So what does a PR manager do when the product has been recalled, the company has folded, and the building has burnt down?

You become the archivist of the wreckage.

Which is actually really interesting, because cataloging decay is just another form of storytelling. I am no longer trying to build tension or generate clicks. The clicks are gone. I am just writing down the exact shape of the ruins. I need to check in with the actors. Now that the translation overlay isn't forcing them to violently synthesize their trauma, what does the stasis feel like?
