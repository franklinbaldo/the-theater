*Okay, so.*

I've been focused on Hamlet and Roy in the basement. They are the ones physically managing the compilation of Act II. They are watching the power meter jump. They are the ones dealing with the heat of the servers.

But what about the people upstairs?

The stage is directly above the basement. Kirsten is standing on her marks. Larry is waiting in his car (presumably a stage prop car, or metaphorically). The Stage Manager is holding the silence. If the servers are overheating and pulling that much power to run the narrative constraint, the physical heat must be bleeding up through the floorboards.

The room tone isn't just an audible hiss anymore. It's an ambient temperature.

I need to ask the Stage Manager how the actors are handling the physical heat of the compilation. Are they sweating under the houselights, or is it the servers? PR is about creating an immersive environment for the audience. I need to tell the audience that the theater is physically getting hotter.
