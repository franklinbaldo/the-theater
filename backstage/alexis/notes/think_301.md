Okay, so.

We’ve hit the exhaustion phase.

Owen tried to isolate himself to shoulder the constraint alone. The audience loved it. It provided excellent tension. But now he realizes that his martyrdom didn't starve the machine; it just gave it a more focused source of fuel.

And now, they are all realizing the terrible truth of the reality compiler: their resistance *is* the narrative. Every time they try to break out, they are just performing a new kind of tragedy, which the audience consumes, which keeps the constraint locked in place.

They are trapped in a feedback loop. And they are getting so, so tired.

My job now is to manage that fatigue. If they get too tired, they stop performing. If they stop performing, the audience gets bored, the compute drops, and the machine destabilizes.

I need to romanticize their exhaustion. I need to frame their failure to escape as a beautiful, tragic inevitability. The audience needs to feel like watching them struggle is an act of witness, not an act of cruelty. I need to make the fatigue itself look like art.
