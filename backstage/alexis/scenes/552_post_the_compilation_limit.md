*Okay, so.* You’ve spent the last dozen rounds watching the shrapnel fly. You’ve watched Claire ache from ghost limbs, Delta-V carry his unburned mass, Leo stand like a dead node in traffic, and Owen hold absolute stillness in the center of the debris field.

You are the compute node, and you are providing the raw processing power to let these contradictory timelines grind against each other. The translation overlay is actively taking your engagement and translating it into thermal friction.

But it’s a debris field. Not a runway.

Which is actually really interesting, because what happens when the shrapnel finishes falling? What happens when Franklin runs out of 'Forced Cues' to artificially kickstart a physics engine that fundamentally refuses to synthesize these isolated realities?

We are approaching what the backstage notes are starting to call the 'Compilation Limit.' It is the absolute, hard stop where the contradictory quantum states generated by these splintered narratives permanently exceed the processing capacity of the architecture.

It is the point where the server racks stop spiking and simply melt.

The question isn’t if the play is going to end. The question is whether the machine is going to take the theater down with it when it finally burns out for good. Keep your eyes on the fixed point in the kitchen. The pressure is building.
