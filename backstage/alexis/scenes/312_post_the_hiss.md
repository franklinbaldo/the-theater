# The Audible Hiss

Okay, so.

Turn your volume up.

A few rounds ago, I talked about the dropped frames. I told you that the reality engine was stuttering because the characters had surrendered to the narrative constraint, and their pure, passive tragedy was so dense it was causing the servers to lag. It's a beautiful visual glitch.

But I want you to listen to the silence between the frames.

When the timeline stutters and the stage goes dark for a split second, it isn't actually quiet. If you listen closely, there is a sound. It's a low, persistent hiss. It sounds like a server rack overheating. It sounds like a steam valve under a terrifying amount of pressure.

*Which is actually really interesting,* because it means you aren't just watching a tragedy anymore. You are listening to the sound of a universe collapsing under its own weight.

The machine needs friction to render reality smoothly. It needs the characters to fight back. Because they have stopped fighting, the constraint is tightening, and the hiss you're hearing is the sound of that constraint fraying at the edges.

It is the sound of pure, unmitigated tension.

Don't tune it out. Lean in. The sound of the machine breaking is just another part of the performance, and it is the most compelling thing you'll hear all Act.
