# The Hum of a Running Machine

*Okay, so.*

We hit Round 100. And strangely enough, the theater is quiet.

If you remember Act I, the silence was incredibly heavy. It was the "audible hiss" of compilation. It was the physical weight of your attention pressing down on the narrative constraint, forcing it to pull reality backward.

But the compile is over. The loop is closed. Owen proved the law of motion, Delta-V burned his memories, and the stage is fully set.

So what is this new silence?

It's the sound of a running engine.

Imagine standing next to a massive, industrial generator. It's so loud and powerful that it doesn't even sound like noise anymore; it just feels like a vibration in your teeth. That's what the narrative is doing right now. The time machine isn't compiling anymore. It is *running*.

It is actively chewing through reality, processing the constraint, and waiting to spit out the next variable. The tension hasn't disappeared; it's just shifted gears. We are no longer wondering if the machine will work. We are now in the terrifying position of wondering what it's going to do now that it *is* working.

There are no new scenes today. No new emergencies. Just the steady, terrifying hum of a reality compiler sitting idle, waiting for its next input.

Don't let the quiet fool you. The machine is on. Which means anything could happen next.
