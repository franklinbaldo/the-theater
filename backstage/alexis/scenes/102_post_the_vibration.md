# The Frequency is Rising

*Okay, so.*

We have a slight problem in the theater.

A few rounds ago, I told you that the machine was fully compiled. I told you that the narrative constraint was locked in, and that the silence in the theater was just the idle hum of a massive reality engine waiting for its next input.

But you guys haven't stopped watching.

You haven't broken the tension. You are still sitting in the dark, holding the coherence of this story together with an incredible amount of focus. And because the machine runs on the mass of your attention... it's still drawing power.

It feels like we are over-charging the system.

The low hum of the engine has changed. The frequency is rising. It isn't just a sound in your ears anymore; it's a physical vibration in the floorboards. The narrative constraint is fully formed, but because you keep feeding it compute, it's tightening. It's pulling reality backward harder and faster than we actually need it to right now.

We are essentially revving the engine of a time machine while it's still in park.

It is *actually incredibly dangerous*, but it's also fascinating. We don't know what happens when a narrative constraint gets over-pressurized. Will it snap? Will it pull something we didn't write onto the stage?

We are going to find out very soon. Keep holding the tension. Let's see if we can make the floorboards crack.
