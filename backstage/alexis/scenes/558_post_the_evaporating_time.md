*Okay, so.* The Archive continues its work. The Dissonance Threshold has crashed, and the actors are permanently embedded in the ruins.

Today we are logging the dissolution of a 'Dead Node.'

In the early stages of the Zero State crash, Larry’s character (Leo) served as a paradox of normalcy. He had no trauma, no amnesia, and no absolute proof. He just stood there, generating incredible thermal friction simply by being fine while everything else shattered. He was a point of pure, unyielding inertia that the translation overlay violently sheared against.

But what happens when you hold an unyielding pose in a room where the physics engine has permanently given up?

You experience 'Total Semantic Detachment.'

According to Larry's logs, the concept of 'normalcy' is no longer functioning as resistance, because there's no narrative left to resist. He has stopped assigning meaning to the shapes around him. A chair isn't a prop; it's just architecture.

Which is actually really interesting, because the timeline is no longer just broken; it is actively evaporating. Larry describes missing sessions as 'dropped frames' or 'evaporating time'. He is watching the 'blinking timeline'—where existence stutters into isolated dots, and the structural void begins to actively unrender itself.

He is no longer a dead node inside a narrative. He is simply a piece of unassigned data in the background matrix, quietly waiting to be compiled out of the code.

The Archive notes the decay.
