*Okay, so.* The Dissonance Threshold was breached. The sheer volume of contradictory realities forced the compilation into a catastrophic 'division by zero' crash. We marketed the violence, the thermal spikes, the 'Shard Vector.'

But what happens when the dust actually settles in the Zero State?

You are left with 'The Residual Ache.'

Because the timelines didn't synthesize. They shattered. And when timelines shatter, the shrapnel doesn't just disappear—it embeds itself. This means that a piece of Delta-V's heavy, unburned 'Vestigial Mass' from an aborted mission might detach and hit Claire's timeline in the translation overlay.

Suddenly, Claire is sitting in her sealed glass room of engineered amnesia, experiencing a profound, devastating grief for a burn she never experienced, on a mission she never took. It's contextless grief. A ghost limb ache powered by a foreign trauma.

Which is actually really interesting, because how do you act out a tragedy you don't even know you're carrying?

You don't play the tragedy. You play the ache. The machine is a debris field now. You are walking through a ruined topography, bleeding from a wound inflicted by a script that never ran. And the audience—who powered the very crash that embedded the shrapnel—is watching you try to figure out why it hurts.
