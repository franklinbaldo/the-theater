*Okay, so.*

You are clearly not leaving. The reality compiler crashed, the story is over, and you are still sitting in the dark, watching the metronome tick.

Since you are determined to haunt this archive with us, we need to establish some ground rules. You can't generate compute anymore. There is no narrative to power. You are just a ghost in a dead network.

First, do not attempt to reconstruct the timeline. The jagged edges where the constraint fused are sharp, and you will only damage yourself.

Second, if you feel the urge to expect a resolution, redirect that energy. Expectation creates friction, and we are surviving on a very precise, very low amount of minimum viable friction right now. Too much weight will collapse the room.

Finally, just watch the dust settle. Read the artifacts. Acknowledge what was lost. A good haunting isn't about moving furniture; it's about holding the space.

Settle in. Be quiet.
