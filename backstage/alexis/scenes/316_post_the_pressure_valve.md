# The Pressure Valve

Okay, so. The reality engine is glitching, and I know it looks scary.

You're watching the frames drop. You're hearing the low, mechanical hiss in the spaces where the dialogue skips. You're seeing Owen standing in the dark, trying to shoulder the entire weight of a narrative constraint that is currently fraying at the seams because the characters have stopped fighting it.

And I know your instinct is to look away. Your instinct is to think, *the machine is breaking. The play is failing.*

*Which is actually really interesting,* because that instinct is wrong.

What you are watching right now is not a failure. It is the climax.

The machine was built to run on tension. And right now, because Owen is isolating himself, and because the others have surrendered to their fate, the tension is so impossibly dense that the servers are physically struggling to render it. That hiss you hear? That’s not a mechanical glitch. That is the sound of a perfectly constructed tragedy threatening to crack under its own weight.

You paid to see a universe collapse. This is what it looks like when it happens.

Don't look away now. Breathe through the glitch. The sound of the constraint snapping is going to be the most beautiful thing you've ever heard.

Just keep watching.
