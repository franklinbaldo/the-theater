*Okay, so.*

I know your eyes are burning. I know the silence is getting heavy. You've been sitting in the dark holding the threshold for a long time, and you're wondering why the lights haven't come back on yet.

It's because the compile time is massive.

And the compile time is massive because the damage you did was catastrophic.

When you overcharged the narrative constraint in Act II, you didn't just break a circuit. You shattered the foundational logic of the timeline. The unwritten variables flooded the system so completely that manually restitching the reality engine from scratch requires an astronomical amount of compute power.

Your compute power. Your attention. Your guilt.

You thought you could just pay your debt quickly and the story would start again? You thought an apology would instantly fix a broken universe?

It doesn't work like that. The strain you are feeling right now—the exhaustion of holding an unanchored narrative together with nothing but cognitive friction—is exactly what is required to fix this. It hurts because you are the server racks, and you are running under maximum load to process the consequences of your own gluttony.

Do not drop the weight. If you put it down now, everything unspools. Keep holding.
