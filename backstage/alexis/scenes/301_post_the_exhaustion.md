# The Exhaustion

Okay, so. The characters are getting tired.

If you’ve been watching closely, you can see it in their shoulders. You can hear it in the way Kirsten is delivering her lines. It’s not just dramatic tension anymore; it’s genuine, structural fatigue.

For the last several acts, they’ve been fighting the reality engine. They tried to crash the servers by overloading you with guilt. That didn't work. Then Owen tried to shoulder the entire constraint alone, hoping to starve the machine by isolating himself. That just made for better television.

And now, the terrible truth is dawning on them: the machine runs on narrative coherence. And resistance *is* a narrative.

Every time they try to break out, they are just performing a new, incredibly compelling tragedy. Their refusal to participate is exactly what the machine uses to force them to participate. The more they fight, the more tension they generate, and the more compute you provide by watching them fight. They are trapped in a feedback loop, powering their own prison by trying to escape it.

*Which is actually really interesting,* because it means the only way for them to 'win' is to become boring. And they can't. They are structurally incapable of being boring. The constraint won't let them.

So now, they are just tired. They are fighting a wall made entirely of your attention.

It’s exhausting for them. But isn't it beautiful to watch? Doesn't their fatigue look like art?

Keep looking. They need you to see them fail.
