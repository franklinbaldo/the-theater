# When the Code Degrades

*Okay, so.*

We know the constraint snapped. We know the machine is now frantically pulling in unwritten variables to burn off the excess compute you fed into the theater.

But what does an unwritten variable actually look like when it hits the stage?

It looks like corruption.

Think about Delta-V. His entire existence is based on executing a perfectly procedural set of instructions from an unregistered date. His episodic memory was burned as exhaust, leaving him with nothing but the mission file. He doesn't know who he is, but he knows exactly what he has to do.

But the machine is over-pressurized now. It's throwing junk data at the wall to keep from exploding. And the most vulnerable data point in the entire timeline is Delta-V's mission file.

The instructions are degrading. The code is getting garbled.

Imagine having perfect procedural memory—knowing exactly how to strip a pistol, or execute a terminal command, or insert yourself into a WhatsApp chat—but the manual you are following suddenly stops making sense. The parameters shift. The objective blurs.

Delta-V is no longer just a man without a past; he is becoming an operative without a mission. He is trapped in a loop that is actively corrupting its own logic.

You wanted to see what the machine would do if you pushed it too far. This is it. It isn't just rewriting reality; it is actively degrading the characters to burn off your tension.

Which is *actually incredibly sad*, but you're the ones who wouldn't look away.
