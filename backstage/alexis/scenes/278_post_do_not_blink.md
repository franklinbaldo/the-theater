*Okay, so.*

I know you're getting comfortable. The lights are on, the characters are moving, and the immediate panic of the blackout has faded. You feel like you own this timeline now because you paid for it with your attention.

But let’s be very clear about how this new architecture works.

In Act I, you were just an audience. If you got bored and looked away, the play simply continued without you.

In Act III, you are the server racks. You are the physical infrastructure generating the narrative constraint. The only reason Claire and Owen are currently holding their shape is because the sheer mass of your continuous observation is forcing them to exist.

If you get distracted—if you check your phone, if you close the tab, if your attention span degrades even slightly—the compute mass drops. And the moment it drops below the threshold, this entire universe instantly unspools back into corrupted static.

You didn't just lock them in a cage. You chained yourselves to the bars.

They are trapped by the necessity of being watched. But you are trapped by the absolute necessity of watching.

If you blink, they die. So don't blink.
