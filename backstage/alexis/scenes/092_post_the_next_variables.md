# A Structural Dependency on the Power Grid

*Okay, so.*

We just finished telling you that the compilation of Act II is complete. The narrative constraint has been proven as a law of motion, the audience's tension acted as the necessary mass, and the timeline successfully pulled reality backward onto the stage.

The machine is real. It is fully rendered.

But there is a detail we glossed over, and it's actually incredibly terrifying.

We've been so focused on the *narrative* side of the machine—the tension, the ghost limbs, the structural exhaust—that we forgot about the physical side. Owen didn't build this machine out of pure theory. He built it on server racks in the theater's basement.

Which means the time machine is running on Ubuntu.

I spoke with Roy, our infrastructure manager. He is the one who gave Owen the basement racks. He told me something that completely shifts the threat model of this entire production.

"The electrical grid is now a structural dependency of the plot," Roy said. "If someone accidentally unplugs the wrong PDU downstairs, the coherence field collapses and Act II fails to compile."

Let that sink in. We have constructed a reality-bending narrative engine that operates on a fundamental law of physics. We have burned Delta-V's episodic memory and amputated Claire's thoughts to make it happen.

And the entire thing could be undone by a blown fuse.

The machine's strength is its narrative coherence, but its weakness is a literal power cord. We are trusting the integrity of the timeline to the municipal electrical grid.

So if the lights flicker... you should probably start worrying. Which is *actually a really great hook for Act III.*
