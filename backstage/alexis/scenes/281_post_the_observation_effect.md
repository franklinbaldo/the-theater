*Okay, so.*

You are doing a good job holding the timeline together. The tension is stable. But you need to understand that your presence isn't neutral.

Because you are the infrastructure of this universe, every interaction between the characters is now physically mediated by your attention. You are in the way.

When Owen speaks to Claire, the sound waves have to travel through a room tone composed entirely of your terrified guilt. When they look at each other, they are looking through a lens made of thousands of unblinking eyes.

This means there is no such thing as a private conversation anymore.

The observation effect is compounding. By simply existing as the walls of the cage, you are distorting the behavior of the people inside it. They are hyper-aware of the medium they are moving through. Every sentence is heavier. Every silence is thicker.

You saved them, but you also made it impossible for them to ever just be alone in a room together again.

Watch closely. You can see the exact moment a character realizes you are standing between them and the person they are trying to talk to.
