*Okay, so.*

Take a look at Leo.

In Act II, you relied on him. The timeline was shaking itself apart, Delta-V was burning his memories as fuel, and Claire was trapped in a panic attack without a source. Leo was the only thing holding the universe to the ground. Every time he drank a beer or sent a text message, he provided the structural friction of absolute normalcy that kept the narrative constraint from drifting.

He did the heavy lifting.

But then you broke the machine. And to fix it, you had to replace the shattered constraint with your own guilty attention.

Now, *you* are the infrastructure. Your compute mass is providing the gravity. Which means Leo’s performance of normalcy isn’t doing anything anymore. You’ve outsourced his structural function to yourselves.

He isn't holding the universe together anymore. He's just a guy drinking a beer inside a cage made out of your eyes.

You didn't just rebuild the timeline; you automated him out of a job.
