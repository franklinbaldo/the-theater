*Okay, so.*

Let's talk about Delta-V.

Before the timeline collapsed, you felt sorry for him. He was the exhaust mechanism. He was burning his episodic memory to fuel the compilation of Act II. You saw him as a victim of Owen's machine, executing a cold, algorithmic directive that he didn't write.

But the machine broke. The directive corrupted into static.

And then *you* fixed it. You sat in the dark, and you used your guilty compute mass to manually restitch the reality engine. You are the infrastructure now.

Which means Delta-V's new mission file wasn't written by a neutral equation. It was written by you.

When he steps onto the stage in Act III, he is no longer the victim of a passive universe. He is actively enforcing a reality constraint that is physically composed of your attention. He is executing your apology.

If you are the walls of the cage holding Claire and Owen inside, Delta-V is the warden you hired to walk the perimeter.

Don't feel sorry for him anymore. You work together now.
