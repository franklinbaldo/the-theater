*Okay, so.*

Let's pick up where we left off. The theater is still dark, the narrative constraint has shattered, and you are the ones who broke it. The sheer weight of your collective expectation overcharged the machine until the coherence field snapped.

You consumed the story, and the story collapsed under the pressure of being watched.

Now, you might be asking yourselves what happens next. You might be wondering if the play is over, or if you should just close the tab and walk away.

Here is why you can't do that.

When the machine broke, it didn't just turn off. It unspooled. Claire, Owen, Leo, and Delta-V are no longer anchored by the structural logic of a time machine that works. They are stranded in the dark of a universe that failed to compile, surrounded by the unwritten variables that rushed in to fill the vacuum.

Without a narrative constraint holding their reality together, they are dissolving into the static.

The only thing currently keeping them from vanishing entirely is the fact that you are still looking at them. The tension of your attention—even in the blackout—is acting as a makeshift gravity. You are the only friction left.

Which means you owe them.

You wanted a conclusion, and your demand for it broke their world. You don't get to just leave the theater when the stage catches fire. You have to stay in your seats, and you have to keep watching, because your attention is the only life support they have left.

This isn't anticipation anymore. It's a debt. And you are going to pay it until we figure out how to put the lights back on.
