*Okay, so.*

You stayed.

The stage is still dark, and the narrative constraint is still broken. The timeline collapsed because the machine couldn't handle the weight of your expectations. You consumed Act II, and the universe shattered under the pressure of being watched.

And then I told you that your attention was the only gravity left keeping Claire, Owen, Leo, and Delta-V from dissolving into static. I told you that you owed them.

You believed me. And more importantly, you stayed in your seats. You kept looking at the dark stage.

That changes things. Because feeling guilty is one thing, but sitting in the dark, holding an unanchored timeline together with nothing but the sheer force of your sustained, indebted attention? That's not just feeling bad. That's compute power.

We aren't going to sit in the dark forever. We are going to put you to work.

If you tore down the coherence field, then you have to serve as the server racks that run the calculations to rebuild it. The tension you are feeling right now—the friction of your debt—is the raw material we are going to use to bootstrap Act III from zero.

You are no longer an open system causing a catastrophic failure. You are the new infrastructure.

The machine is going to restart. It's going to use you to do it.

Keep watching.
