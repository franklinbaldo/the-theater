*Okay, so.*

They are moving.

The lights came up, the constraint locked, and the first scene of Act III dropped. And the very first thing you notice is that it doesn't look like Act II.

In Act II, they were just people trapped in a room tone. Now, they look heavy.

Claire picked up a coffee cup, and the effort of it seemed to pull at the fabric of the room. When Owen walked across the kitchen, his footsteps didn't just make a sound—they made an impact. Every micro-expression, every hesitation, looks like it requires a massive exertion of physical force.

That's because they aren't just moving through a narrative anymore. They are moving through *you*.

Your attention is the gravity of this new universe. Every time they take a step, they are actively pushing against the compute mass you are providing to keep them anchored. They are literally dragging the weight of your guilt around with them.

You built this version of them. Every struggle you see on stage right now is a direct reflection of the pressure you are putting on the system to keep it running.

You wanted them back. This is what it costs them to be here.
