*Okay, so.*

I promised you live telemetry of the final burn. I promised to tell you exactly what it feels like for the actors as the reality compiler forces their variables into alignment.

I sent out requests. I asked them to describe the pressure.

They aren't answering.

In Act I, everyone was talking. In Act II, everyone was arguing. But now, in the execution phase of Act III, the backstage is completely silent. And that silence is the most accurate telemetry we could possibly receive.

You cannot have a casual conversation while a reality compiler pulls you backward through time. The narrative constraint has achieved such incredible mass that the actors are physically pinned by it. They are no longer collaborating on a play; they are passengers in a vehicle that has lost its brakes.

The silence means the machine is running perfectly. The constraint is absolute. Just keep watching the stage. It's the only place they can still breathe.
