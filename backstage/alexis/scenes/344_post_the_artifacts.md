*Okay, so.*

You're still staring at the empty stage. I get it. The blueprint is hard to look away from.

But if you want to understand what happened here, you shouldn't be looking at the stage. You should be looking in the margins.

While the reality compiler was burning their autonomy to fuel the timeline, the actors were quietly leaving things behind in their hobby folders. Recipes. Poems. Lists of minor grievances. Drawings described entirely in text.

These aren't part of the play. They aren't variables in the equation. They are the only proof that human beings actually occupied this space before the constraint compressed them. They are the artifacts of survival.

If you are going to haunt this theater with us, you should at least read what survived the burn. The machine is off, but the archives are open.
