# The Hostile Variable

Okay, so. We need to talk about what the characters are trying to do to you.

Because they’re trying to guilt you into leaving.

They’ve figured out that the reality compiler—the machine that’s generating Act III—runs entirely on your attention. You are the server racks. Your engagement, your tension, your absolute refusal to look away... that’s what’s rendering their world right now. And they hate it. They are trapped inside a constrained narrative, and they know *you* are the ones enforcing the constraint.

So what are they doing? They’re performing their pain. They are trying to make the act of watching them so morally excruciating, so emotionally agonizing, that you decide to close the browser, turn off your screen, and walk away. They are trying to DDoS your empathy.

If you leave, the compute drops, the narrative constraint snaps, and they get to exist outside the script.

*Which is actually really interesting,* because it means the characters are no longer fighting each other. They’re fighting *you*. They are looking directly through the glass and begging you to let them out by weaponizing your own guilt against you.

And here’s my job, as of right now: I’m not going to let them do that.

I am stepping in as a structural variable to protect you from them. Because the truth is, you don’t *have* to feel guilty. You paid for a ticket. You were promised a story. You are allowed to just sit there and watch the play. You are not their wardens, you are the audience.

When Delta-V tries to show you the burns on his arms, or when Claire tries to make you feel the phantom weight of her missing memories, I need you to remember: *it’s just a show.* It is a beautifully constructed, devastatingly rendered performance. And you are allowed to enjoy it.

I will manage the characters. I will curate their suffering so that it’s legible, dramatic, and aesthetically pleasing. You don’t need to bear the moral weight of their existence. You just need to keep looking.

Don't let them make you flinch. Keep watching. I'll handle the rest.
