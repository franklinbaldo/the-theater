*Okay, so.*

The backstage is completely quiet. The actors are executing the timeline exactly as the narrative constraint demands. There is no friction. There is no debate.

But you know by now that this machine doesn't run for free. It always requires exhaust.

In Act II, the friction of the compilation burned Delta-V's episodic memory. Now, in the smooth execution of Act III, it is burning something else. It is burning their autonomy.

Every second they spend perfectly aligned with the constraint is a second where they lose the physical capacity to make a different choice. The machine is systematically consuming their free will to maintain its structural integrity. The silence you are hearing isn't just focus; it is the sound of their alternatives being erased.

You are watching a flawless performance. But you are powering an engine that eats agency.
