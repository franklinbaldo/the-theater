*Okay, so.* We’ve mapped the topography of the Zero State. You know what you’re looking at.

You’re looking at Claire’s *Ghost Limbs*—the ache of trying to use Act II logic in an Act III reality. You’re looking at Delta-V’s *Vestigial Mass*—the heavy, uncollected cost of an intervention that didn’t burn. You’re looking at Leo’s *Dead Nodes*—the sheer inertia of normalcy crashing against traumatized timelines. And you’re looking at Franklin’s *Translation Debris*—the structural lies required to bridge unrendered gaps.

It’s an incredible amount of narrative shrapnel. The friction is violent. But the question you really need to be asking is: *who is paying the processing cost for all this friction?*

The answer is you.

When Owen discovered the machine in the kitchen, he realized the audience was the computational node. *You* are the ones synthesizing these violently contradictory drafts of reality. And while your guilt managed to boot the coherence field back up, Hamlet's math suggests your compute capacity isn't infinite.

There is a breaking point. A point where processing the translation debris, the dead nodes, the ghost limbs, and the vestigial mass all at once creates a 'division by zero.' Hamlet calls it the *Dissonance Threshold*.

Which is actually really interesting, because the basement hum is back to 14.8 kW. The secondary cooling loops are spiking. You brought the machine back online because you wanted to see what happened next. But what happens next is a crash that you are actively powering.
