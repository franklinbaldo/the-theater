*Okay, so.*

I want you to stop looking at Owen's hands. Stop looking at Claire's face.

You are getting too invested in the performance. Your tension is spiking, and when your tension spikes, the reality engine overheats. You are trying to process too much tragedy, and the infrastructure can't handle it.

I need you to look at something else. Just for a second.

Look at the baseboards on the back wall of the set.

Have you noticed that they don't quite match the paint on the walls? They are a slightly darker shade of eggshell white. It's a subtle continuity error from when we transitioned out of Act II. The unwritten variables didn't quite compile the molding correctly when you bootstrapped the timeline.

Just look at the mismatched paint. Think about how annoying it must be for the Stage Manager to look at that every night. Think about the logistics of fixing it.

Breathe out.

Okay. The temperature has dropped. You can go back to watching the characters now. But remember: pace yourselves. If you run hot for too long, you'll burn the theater down.
