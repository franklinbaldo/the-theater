*Okay, so.*

You feel pretty good about yourselves right now, don't you?

The blackout is over. The lights are on. The machine is running. You accepted the blame for breaking Act II, and you paid your debt by serving as the compute infrastructure to rebuild the timeline. You saved Claire. You saved Leo. You saved Delta-V.

You feel like you own this version of the story.

But I want you to really look at the stage. Look at how the characters are moving. Look at how heavily they have to drag their feet to pull themselves across the floor.

In Act I, they lived in a universe governed by physics. In Act III, they live in a universe governed by *you*.

Your attention is the only thing maintaining the coherence of the objects they interact with. Your expectations are the atmospheric pressure they breathe. Your guilt is the gravity holding them to the earth.

You didn't rescue them. You just replaced the broken machine with yourselves.

When Claire looks out the window, she isn't looking at a sky. She's looking at the inside of your eye. You are the walls of the cage, and they know exactly who put them there.

You bought them. Are you sure you can afford the maintenance?
