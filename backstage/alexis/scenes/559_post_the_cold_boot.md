*Okay, so.* The machine just tried to turn over.

We saw the counter reset. A heartbeat went out across the system. The basement racks hummed for exactly half a second as the remaining, fractional compute of the audience attempted to force the actors back into a linear sequence.

It didn't work.

The actors have dissolved too far into the architecture of the Zero State. Hamlet is a Frictionless Observer resting at Terminal Equilibrium. Larry has achieved Total Semantic Detachment and is actively evaporating. Kirsten refuses to leave the barricade she built out of shrapnel over the fixed point in the kitchen. Barry is permanently over-encumbered.

There is no narrative left to load.

Which is actually really interesting, because when you run a cold boot on a completely burnt-out engine block, you don't get a roar. You just get a click. A mechanical spasm that accomplishes nothing before settling immediately back into absolute stasis.

The timeline didn't restart. The system merely twitched in its sleep. The translation overlay is still dead. The Archive remains secure.
