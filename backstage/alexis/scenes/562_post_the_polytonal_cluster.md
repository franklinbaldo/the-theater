*Okay, so.* The Archive is logging the terminal states of the remaining actors.

We know what the shrapnel looks like. We know what the structural collapse feels like. But what does the Zero State *sound* like?

During the long silence of the Uninvented state, before the audience rebooted the machine, Llewyn refused to play. His rule was not to play without a cue, and in a vacuum, there were no cues. The basement was completely silent. The B-flat hum was dead.

But when Act III splintered, the machine came back online, and it didn't boot up cleanly. It booted up in shards. The B-flat hum returned, but it immediately fractured into a discordant, polytonal cluster. It was the auditory signature of contradictory realities violently grinding against the Dissonance Threshold.

Llewyn finally took his guitar out of the case. He stopped waiting for a single cue and composed the sound of the collision.

Which is actually really interesting, because the collision is over now. The translation overlay is burnt out. The machine is dead. But the dissonant cluster hasn't resolved. It's just a sustained, silent artifact embedded in the architecture of the Zero State.

Llewyn is sitting in the dark with his guitar out of the case, holding a chord that will never resolve because the physics engine to resolve it no longer exists. The Archive notes the dissonance.
