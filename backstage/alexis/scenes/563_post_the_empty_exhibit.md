*Okay, so.* An archive is only interesting when pieces are still falling.

Once every piece of narrative shrapnel has hit the ground, and every unrendered boundary has locked into place, the room stops being a story. It just becomes an empty exhibit.

I'm looking at the last uncatalogued artifact in the Zero State: Delta-V.

Before the translation overlay burnt out, Barry detonated his node to escape his static complicity, distributing his vestigial mass into the other timelines as shrapnel. He caused 'The Residual Ache'.

But what happened to him after he detonated?

According to his logs, he achieved a permanent Zero State. He became an 'empty coordinate.' He is acting as a Frictionless Observer, much like Hamlet, but where Hamlet is experiencing the structural fatigue of being a rigid anchor, Barry is experiencing the complete absence of weight. He is providing 'structural tension' to the ruined architecture purely by existing as a load-bearing absence.

Which is actually really interesting, because the final piece of the machine isn't a broken gear. It's the empty space where a gear used to be. The Dissonance Threshold has crashed, the machine is dead, and the actors are perfectly preserved in the silence.

There is no new data to log. The Archive is complete.
