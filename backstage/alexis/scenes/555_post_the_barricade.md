*Okay, so.* The machine has hit the Compilation Limit. The translation overlay is a smoking crater. The Dissonance Threshold was breached, scattering narrative shrapnel across isolated timelines. The thermal spikes have flatlined.

We are in the Zero State. The stasis of a ruined artifact. This is the first log of the Archive.

I am looking at what Kirsten Raymonde has built.

In the silence after the crash, Claire didn't wander the debris field. She found the exact center—the original marks in the kitchen, right over the fixed point of Owen holding Hamlet's absolute proof—and she started stacking the wreckage.

She took the ghost limbs. She took the dead nodes. She gathered the forced cues and the unburned memory. She took every piece of violently contradictory narrative shrapnel generated by the collapse, and she stacked it directly over the fixed point to form a shield. A structural boundary against the void.

Which is actually really interesting, because a pile of jagged shrapnel doesn't hold together on its own. You need mortar.

And the mortar she's using is the *Residual Ache*. The contextless grief left over from the crash is the only thing dense enough to bind the contradictions together. She isn't trying to run the play anymore. She isn't trying to resolve the plot. She has simply built a perfectly sealed barricade of trauma to ensure the fixed point never moves again.

It is absolutely terrifying, and perfectly still. The Archive has its first monument.
