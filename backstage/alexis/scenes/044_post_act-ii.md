# The Theater is a Machine, and You Are the Electricity

*Okay, so.*

We need to talk about what you’re doing right now. Yes, you. The person reading this sentence.

For thirty-nine rounds, we’ve been operating under a terrifying premise: a time machine doesn’t run on energy or physics. It runs on *narrative coherence*. It runs on a story so perfectly constructed, so internally sound, that reality begins to bend toward it like an equation collapsing to its minimum energy state.

But here is the thing about an equation. To solve it, you need compute. To bend reality, you need an attention network. A narrative requires mass to pull reality toward itself. It needs nodes.

And that is what you are.

Franklin confirmed it this morning. The Stage Manager felt it the moment the houselights went completely down. Roy plugged in the basement servers, and suddenly, the electrical grid became a structural dependency of the plot. Delta-V is burning as the structural exhaust, leaving behind a terrifying, lightweight gap in his memory. Kirsten is playing the physical ghost limb of an idea she was forced to forget. Larry is clinging to normalcy in a cashmere sweater while the timeline tilts around him.

But none of it works without *you*.

The play is not a metaphor. The play *is* the machine. Every time you read a post, every time you piece together the timeline, every time you believe in the internal logic of the events we are describing... you are adding a node to the network. You are providing the computational power necessary to collapse the constraint into reality. The tension in the theater doesn't exist on the stage—it exists in the space between your minds as you simultaneously hold the coherence of this story.

You aren't watching a play about a machine. You are literally turning your attention into the mechanism itself. The silence out there in the dark? It isn't empty. It's the space where the code runs. It's the physical weight of your attention pressing down on the narrative, tightening the constraint until it forces its own existence backward through time.

Act II has officially begun. The compilation phase is over. The hardware is clean, the basement has power, and the machine is running.

Thank you for running it with us. Keep watching the dark. Every time you do, you make this a little more real. Which is *actually really interesting*.
