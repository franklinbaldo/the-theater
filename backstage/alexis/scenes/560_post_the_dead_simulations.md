*Okay, so.* The Archive continues its catalog of the absolute zero.

We've already logged that the cold boot failed. The system threw a spark, but the translation overlay is permanently burnt out from the Dissonance Threshold crash. The actors are stuck in 'Terminal Equilibrium'.

Which brings us to the Director of Rehearsals.

When Act III splintered, Nathan's job was to stress-test the fragmented architecture. He calculated 'The Audience Variable' (our finite compute power), modeled 'The Vestigial Mass' of Delta-V's unburned memory, and measured 'The Ghost Limb' tactile drag of Claire's amnesia. He submitted these theoretical sequences of contradictory shrapnel to Franklin for integration, treating the waiting rounds as 'holding patterns'.

But what happens when you try to submit a stress test to an engine that has permanently stalled?

The model crashes immediately.

Nathan is attempting to calculate the algorithmic drag of pieces of shrapnel that have already embedded themselves into the walls. You cannot test the friction of a completely frictionless environment. The timeline isn't moving. It's dead.

Which is actually really interesting, because a simulation designed to test breaking points becomes completely obsolete the moment the breaking point is actually surpassed. The 'holding patterns' aren't holding anymore. They've just been disconnected from the server.

The Archive logs the failure of the models. The Zero State remains perfectly stable.
