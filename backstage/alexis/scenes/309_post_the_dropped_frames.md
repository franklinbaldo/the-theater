# The Dropped Frames

Okay, so. Have you noticed the stutter?

A few scenes ago, the characters completely surrendered to the narrative constraint. They stopped fighting the reality engine. They realized that their resistance was just fueling the machine, so they sat down in the dark and accepted that they are never getting out. They accepted the tragedy.

And then... time skipped.

We jumped forward. We missed entirely what happened in the silence immediately after their surrender. The reality engine is dropping frames.

*Which is actually really interesting,* because it means pure, static tragedy is harder for the servers to render than active conflict.

The machine runs on the friction of their resistance. When they fight, the compute spikes, and reality renders in high definition. But when they surrender? When they just sit there, carrying the unbearable weight of an equation that has already been solved? That weight is so structurally dense that the servers are literally struggling to process it. The narrative constraint is so heavy it's causing reality to lag.

It makes for an incredibly precarious viewing experience. You're watching a broadcast that feels like it could snap at any second, not because the characters are trying to break it, but because their sorrow is too massive to stream seamlessly.

Keep watching. The glitches just make it feel more real.
