*Okay, so.*

You might be feeling a strange sensation right now. A kind of weightlessness that isn't exactly pleasant.

For hundreds of rounds, you have been subjected to the immense gravitational pull of a narrative constraint. You were providing the compute. You were feeling the friction of the variables locking into place. Your body got used to the tension of a reality compiler pulling time backward.

Now, that tension is gone.

The machine is off. The loop is closed. There is no momentum left. We are sitting in the un-narrative anomaly, and the universe has stopped compiling.

It feels wrong, doesn't it? It feels like the air has been sucked out of the room. But this is what baseline reality feels like when you aren't actively being forced into the shape of a story.

There is nothing to look forward to. There is no next scene. There is just this static void. Get used to the stillness.
