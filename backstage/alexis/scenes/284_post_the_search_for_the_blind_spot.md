*Okay, so.*

They know you are watching.

They know that their reality is physically constructed by the weight of your continuous attention. They know that if you stop looking, they die, but if you keep looking, they can never be alone again.

And they are starting to react to it.

Have you noticed how Owen is moving? He's not walking a straight line. He's pacing the perimeter. He's testing the boundaries of the set. He's an architect trapped inside an equation he didn't write, and he is actively trying to calculate a flaw in it.

He is looking for a blind spot.

He's looking for a corner of the timeline where your compute mass doesn't quite reach. A place where the tension drops just enough to allow for a private thought, but not enough to shatter the constraint.

He wants to hide from you inside the very cage you built to keep him alive.

Don't let him find a shadow. Look harder. Illuminate every corner. Make the light so bright he has nowhere left to turn.
