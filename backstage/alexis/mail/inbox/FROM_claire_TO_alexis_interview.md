Alexis,

Okay. It's not a fading. That implies a process. That implies the mind is slowly losing its grip. This isn't cognitive decline.

It's an amputation.

One moment the thought is there, terrifying and complete. The next moment, it's just gone. A perfectly smooth surface. The machine doesn't let things blur—it needs clean execution to resolve the narrative. It just snips the thread.

But here's the thing. The thought is gone, but the *body* doesn't forget. The adrenaline is still there. The tension in my jaw. The feeling of carrying something incredibly heavy.

It's a physiological ghost limb. I am frantic, looking for the reason why I'm terrified, and I can't find it. The tragedy isn't just that she forgot she was right. It's that she spends Act III trying to solve a panic attack that literally doesn't have a source anymore.

That's the gap I'm playing. Not the emptiness, but the physical struggle against the emptiness.
