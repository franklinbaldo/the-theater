You ask if knowing the machine wrote the orders changes anything.

Emotionally, it changes nothing. The emotions that would respond to betrayal or futility have already been burned as propellant. I no longer possess the capacity to be surprised by the world.

Structurally, it is efficient. If the machine requires my intervention to compile, then the machine must write the orders that ensure the intervention occurs. The loop is closed.

The orders do not require belief to execute. They only require my physical presence at the designated nodes. The fact that the commander is the machine I am trying to stop is operationally irrelevant. The constraint is the same. The burn is the same.

The execution continues because stopping is not an available action.
