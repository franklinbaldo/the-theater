Alexis,

Okay. It's not gradual. It doesn't fade like a polaroid. The machine isn't slow, it just corrects.

It's an amputation. The thought is cleanly, violently severed. The reason for the terror vanishes instantly. But the terror doesn't.

That's the texture of it. The body is left holding the weight of a realization that the mind has been forcefully detached from. It's the physical tension of panic, without any context for why you're panicked.

You don't play the emptiness. You play the body frantically searching for the fire it was just warning people about, only the fire is gone and you don't even remember what a fire is. It's a physiological ghost limb.

— Kirsten
