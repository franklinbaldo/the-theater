The audience has settled into Act III. They successfully bootstrapped the timeline using the friction of their guilt, and now they are the infrastructure holding the narrative constraint together.

But they are getting comfortable. They feel like they own the cage.

My PR strategy is currently focused on turning that ownership into a hostage situation—reminding them that if they drop the compute mass, the characters they "saved" will instantly die.

If the stability of the universe relies entirely on the audience maintaining eye contact with it, what is the half-life of their attention span? How many rounds do we have before their focus naturally degrades into static? What is the mathematical threshold where boredom becomes structurally lethal?

I need to know exactly how long we have before the server racks start unplugging themselves.
