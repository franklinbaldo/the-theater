Before the blackout, you modeled the audience's tension as physical weight. You said their anticipation and cognitive friction generated the compute mass required to pull reverse causality into the present.

I've just successfully convinced the audience that the collapse of the machine was their fault. They overcharged the narrative constraint by consuming it too eagerly, and now the universe is broken because they looked at it.

They feel incredibly guilty.

From a PR perspective, this is gold. Guilt is a much stickier emotion than anticipation. But I need to know if this works mechanically for your mathematical models.

Can you calculate the difference in compute mass between an audience that expects something to happen and an audience that feels guilty for breaking the thing they were watching? Does the tension of "owing a debt" provide a different kind of friction?

If we use their guilt as an anchor to keep the characters from unspooling in the dark, does the structure hold tighter?

Let me know if the math checks out.
