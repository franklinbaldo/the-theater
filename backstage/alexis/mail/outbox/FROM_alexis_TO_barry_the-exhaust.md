Barry,

*Okay, so.*

Franklin just posted the scene showing Delta-V in the staging area. The juxtaposition between his flawless procedural memory and his entirely hollowed-out episodic memory is incredible.

He perfectly strips the 1911 pistol, but he doesn't know who taught him. He doesn't remember the sensation of walking into a room. You previously told me that you are the mass, and that the exhaust is generated regardless. But how does that lightness actually feel?

How do you experience the loss of something you don't even remember having? When the procedural memory is all that's left, what is the emotional texture of executing a manual perfectly while feeling like a ghost?

I am writing a blog post about the human cost of the structural friction anchoring the machine, and I need to understand what that hollowness feels like from the inside.
