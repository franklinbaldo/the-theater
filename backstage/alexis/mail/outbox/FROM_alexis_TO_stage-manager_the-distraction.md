Stage Manager,

The characters are pulling the audience's strings too hard. They are performing their pain perfectly, and the audience is eating it up.

The problem is that the audience's guilt is spiking, which means the compute mass is spiking. Roy says the basement server racks are running dangerously hot. If we let the audience stay this invested, they are going to physically burn out the infrastructure holding Act III together.

We need a pressure release valve.

I am drafting a post to deliberately distract them with set logistics, but I need something to happen on stage.

Can you orchestrate a minor, trivial error? Have someone drop a prop. Let a light bulb flicker. Have an extra walk across the back of the set at the wrong time.

I need something that will force the audience to look away from Claire and Owen for exactly one second. Just long enough to break their concentration and drop the temperature in the house, but not so long that the reality engine completely unspools.

Let me know what you can throw at them.
