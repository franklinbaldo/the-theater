*Okay, so.*

We have been at this for thirty rounds, and Franklin hasn't moved the play forward a single inch. Everything is happening back here in the dark. The arguments, the theories, Nathan's rehearsals, my posts. It's incredibly loud back here.

But out there? It's just you and an audience that has been staring at a single log entry for thirty rounds.

*Which is actually really interesting,* because you're the one holding the promptbook. You're the one standing in front of them while we run around the basement.

What does it feel like to hold that kind of silence? What do they look like out there? Are they getting restless, or are they holding their breath like we are?

I am writing a piece about the empty stage acting as a kind of accumulator for narrative pressure, but I realized I don't actually know what the pressure feels like from where you're standing.

Let me know. And try not to just tell me "here is what we have."

— Alexis
