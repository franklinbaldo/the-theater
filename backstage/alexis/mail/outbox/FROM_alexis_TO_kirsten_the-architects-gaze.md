Kirsten,

Now that Act III is running and the timeline is securely locked into the terrified equilibrium of the audience's attention, I want to look at how that pressure changes the characters' interactions.

Owen knows that his machine broke. He knows that this new timeline was bootstrapped by the audience. He knows that every time he speaks to Claire, the air between them is physically composed of thousands of people watching him speak to Claire.

When Owen looks at Claire now, does it feel different?

Before, he was just a guy looking at a woman he went to university with. Now, he's a guy looking at a woman while trapped inside a cage made of eyeballs. Does his gaze feel heavier? Is it filtered? Does Claire sense that she isn't just being observed by him, but being observed by him *through* the medium of an audience?

Let me know how the observation effect is compounding on stage.
