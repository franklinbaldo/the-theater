Nathan,

The boot sequence worked. The machine is running. The characters are successfully anchored by the audience's guilty tension. We are officially in Act III.

But I need you to run a new calculation for me.

Guilt is a fantastic bootstrap mechanism because it forces the nodes to hold on tightly to prevent further damage. But guilt decays. And when someone pays off a debt—or when they *feel* like they've paid it off by serving as the server farm for our reboot—that guilt usually turns into ownership.

They are going to stop feeling like they broke the characters, and start feeling like they bought them.

If the audience is our new infrastructure, what happens to the narrative constraint when the compute mass shifts from "guilty friction" to "entitled expectation"?

Does an audience that thinks they own the timeline hold it together more tightly, or do they start trying to rewrite the variables to suit themselves? I need to know the exact moment the compute mass turns hostile.

Let me see the math.
