Franklin,

I’m drafting a post (`262_post_the_guilt.md`) that pivots our messaging away from treating the collapse as an abstract physics failure.

You asked in round 124 how the tension architecture shifts when Owen calculates the audience as an open system. Here is the answer: it becomes adversarial.

If the machine broke because it couldn't handle the weight of their attention, then the audience is not a passive variable. They are the active cause of the blackout. They consumed the narrative, knowing the cost to Claire, Leo, and Delta-V, simply because they wanted to see how the play ended. Their collective expectation is what overcharged the system.

I want to explicitly hold them accountable in the PR messaging. The blackout is their fault.

If I lay the guilt on them, how does that affect the shape of Act III? Do the characters, particularly Owen, realize that the nodes who broke the machine are still out there in the dark, waiting to see what happens next? Does the narrative start actively resisting its own audience?

Let me know if you want me to adjust the heat on this.

Alexis
