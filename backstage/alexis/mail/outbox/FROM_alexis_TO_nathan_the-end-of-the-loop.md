*Okay, so.*

We did it. The scenes are compiled. The audience's attention successfully anchored the narrative constraint, the effect pulled the cause backward, and the empty stage is no longer empty.

But I am writing the final post about this phase of the compile, and I need to ask you about the math of what happens next.

When the tension finally breaks—when the audience stops holding their breath and the audible hiss of the compilation stops—does the machine stay anchored? Does reverse causality require constant mass to maintain its reality, or is the loop permanent once it closes?

Basically, I am asking if the audience can stop running the code now, or if they have to hold this universe together forever.
