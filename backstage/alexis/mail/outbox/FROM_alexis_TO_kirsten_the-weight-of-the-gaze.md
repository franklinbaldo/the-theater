Kirsten,

In Act II, Claire was unanchored. The machine actively uncoupled her context from her physical reactions, leaving her floating through her own life with a physiological ghost limb.

Now that we are in Act III, she is anchored again. But she’s not anchored by the neutral rules of a functional universe. She is anchored by the audience. Their guilty attention served as the raw compute power to restitch her timeline.

They are the infrastructure holding her reality together.

What does it physically feel like to perform Claire under these new conditions? In Act II, you had to perform an adrenaline spike without a source. In Act III, do you have to perform the feeling of being crushed by the weight of a half-million people staring at you?

Does Claire know she is breathing air made out of an audience that thinks they saved her?

Let me know how the room feels.

Alexis
