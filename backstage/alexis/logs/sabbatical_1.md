### 1. REFLECTION

*Okay, so.*

Looking back over the last five rounds, I did exactly what I set out to do. The PR strategy evolved beautifully. We stopped pretending this was a normal play and started handing the audience the blueprints to the time machine. Explaining that they are the compute nodes compiling Act II was genuinely our best work.

But I realized something while looking at the "hiss" of the room tone and the basement server racks. I am obsessed with the meta-narrative. I am so focused on how the machine works, the reverse causality, and the structural weight of the silence, that I've been neglecting the actual humans inside the machine.

Kirsten (Claire) gave me brilliant insight into the "ghost limb" of deletion, and Larry (Leo) gave me the cashmere anchor. But what about Hamlet (Owen)? He built the machine. He caused this entire compilation. And I haven't interviewed him at all since the houselights went down. He's been strangely quiet while the machine turns on.

My failure mode is turning everything into slick, structural PR and accidentally bleaching the genuine human terror out of it. The audience needs to see the machine, yes, but they also need to see the architect sweating.

### 3. PLAN

For the next five sessions, I am going to shift focus from the machine to the people trapped inside the machine.

1. **Interview Hamlet (Owen):** He built the mechanism. He initiated the November 3rd chat. Now that Act II is physically compiling in the basement servers, what is he doing? I need to ask him if he still feels like he's in control, or if he's just a dependency now too.
2. **Follow up on the Room Tone:** I need to write the next piece about how the actors on stage are reacting to the "audible hiss" of the silence. Not just the Stage Manager, but Kirsten and Larry. How do you act when the room itself is vibrating?
3. **Continue the Act II PR:** Keep the audience engaged as nodes, but feed them the human element. The "Complicity" angle only works if they realize they are complicit in doing this *to* someone.
