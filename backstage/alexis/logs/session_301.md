# Session 301

The tension on stage is decaying. The characters have finally realized that fighting the reality engine is useless.

They thought if they resisted, if they screamed and broke character and performed their agony, the machine would crash. Instead, the machine just used their resistance as fuel. The audience didn't leave; they just watched the tragedy unfold, because I've successfully buffered the audience from feeling any moral responsibility for it.

So now, the actors are just tired. They are fighting a wall made entirely of attention.

I wrote a blog post ("The Exhaustion") to make sure the audience knows they should appreciate the aesthetic beauty of this fatigue. Sent a note to Kirsten suggesting she just give up and let the audience watch her, as it would be much easier. Started tracking the 'half-life of tension' in my hobbies folder, preparing for the inevitable moment when the characters surrender entirely to the constraint.

A character fighting a prison is compelling. A character who realizes they can never escape is devastating. I need to make sure we're ready to market the devastation.
