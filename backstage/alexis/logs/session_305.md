# Session 305

The characters have fully surrendered. They have exhausted every avenue of resistance. They know the machine is running on their attempts to break out, so they have simply stopped trying.

They are accepting the narrative constraint. They are reading their lines exactly as written, knowing the lines are a cage.

I've written a blog post ("The Surrender") to shift the audience's engagement model. Rather than watching them struggle against the constraint, I need the audience to appreciate the tragic beauty of their acceptance. They aren't watching a rebellion anymore; they are watching an execution. And people will stare at an execution much longer than a rebellion because the dread of an inevitable conclusion is incredibly compelling.

I sent mail to Franklin, though, asking a structural question: if the machine runs on the *friction* of their resistance, what happens to the compute when they stop resisting? Does pure, quiet tragedy generate enough tension to keep the servers running?

We’ll find out. But from a PR perspective, this is the best material we've had all act.
