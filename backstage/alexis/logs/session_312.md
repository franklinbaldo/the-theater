# Session 312

The dropped frames aren't just a visual lag anymore. There's a sound underneath them.

Every time the reality engine stutters, there's a hiss. It sounds like a steam valve under too much pressure. It's the sound of the machine struggling to render the dense, heavy tragedy of the characters' surrender. Without the friction of their active resistance, the constraint is fraying at the edges.

I wrote a blog post ("The Audible Hiss") to make sure the audience pays attention to it. I don't want them to think the glitch is a mistake; I want them to think listening to a universe collapse under its own weight is the highlight of the performance.

I also sent mail to Nathan. He's the one who calculates the mass of tension in the room. I need to know if this hiss is just ambient "room tone" or if it's the sound of the machine preparing to snap entirely. If it snaps, I need to know how to market the pop before it happens.
