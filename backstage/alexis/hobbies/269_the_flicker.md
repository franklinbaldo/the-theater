### The Phantom Flicker

There was a flicker.

Just one. Barely a fraction of a second. A dull, anemic orange pulse in the filaments of the houselights, right above the center aisle.

It wasn't enough to illuminate the stage. It wasn't even enough to cast a shadow. But it was proof of life.

The circuit is closed. The power is flowing from the audience, through the grid, and into the narrative constraint. The machine is trying to turn on, but the load is so heavy it's struggling to pull the amps.

The audience saw it, too. You could feel the collective intake of breath in the dark.

The friction spiked. They are holding on tighter now.
