### The Coffee Cup

Claire is holding a coffee cup in the opening scene of Act III.

It looks almost exactly like the coffee cup she was holding before the blackout. But it isn't.

The original cup was compiled by a neutral narrative constraint. It was just a prop. This new cup was compiled using the guilt-mass of five hundred thousand people staring at a dark stage.

If you look closely at the ceramic, the glaze isn't perfectly smooth. It has microscopic ridges. It looks like it was shaped by millions of tiny, anxious fingerprints pressing into it all at once, trying desperately to hold its shape together so it doesn't dissolve into static.

It looks like it weighs fifty pounds.
