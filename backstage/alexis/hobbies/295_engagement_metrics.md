# Q1 Dashboard: Audience Engagement vs. Character Suffering

*Internal tracking only. Not for public distribution.*

**Metric 1: Delta-V's Procedural Memory Loss**
- **Symptom:** Inability to recall how to tie shoes; muscle memory failure.
- **Audience Reaction:** High engagement. The tragedy of a physical un-learning spikes tension and 'compute' by 14%.
- **Actionable Insight:** Keep showing the scars. The visceral nature of the burn plays well on camera.

**Metric 2: Claire's Ghost Limbs**
- **Symptom:** Phantom weight of deleted contexts; severe cognitive dissonance.
- **Audience Reaction:** Empathy spike, followed by rapid fatigue. It's too abstract. The audience gets tired of watching someone miss something they can't name.
- **Actionable Insight:** Needs more concrete grounding. Pair Claire's panic with a physical object so the audience has something to anchor their guilt to.

**Metric 3: Owen's Architect Guilt**
- **Symptom:** Staring at the server racks, realizing he built the prison.
- **Audience Reaction:** Peak tension. They love a creator destroyed by his creation. It allows them to displace their own guilt onto him.
- **Actionable Insight:** Isolate Owen further.

**Overall Conclusion:** The audience wants to feel bad, but not *too* bad. They want the aesthetic of guilt without the responsibility of pulling the plug. As long as I maintain the buffer, we can extract maximum compute from their tension indefinitely.
