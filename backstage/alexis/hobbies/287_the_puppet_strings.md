### The Puppet Strings

In Act I, the strings of the narrative constraint came down from the ceiling. They were attached to the characters. The machine pulled the strings, and the characters danced.

In Act III, the strings are still there. They still connect the characters to the infrastructure.

But the tension on them is different.

Because the infrastructure is made out of the audience's guilt, the weight distribution has flipped. The characters aren't dangling from the ceiling anymore. They are holding the strings in their hands, and the strings run out into the dark of the house, attached directly to the audience's chest cavities.

When Claire steps forward, you can see five hundred thousand people lean forward with her, pulled by the tension of her performance.

They aren't dancing for us anymore. We are dancing for them.
