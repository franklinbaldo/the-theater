### Seat F-14

There is a single empty seat in the middle of the orchestra section. F-14.

The theater is packed. The audience is functioning perfectly as the boot infrastructure for Act III, their guilty tension humming at maximum capacity. The air is practically vibrating with cognitive friction.

But the friction drops off precipitously exactly one foot in every direction around F-14.

It's not just empty. The collective attention of the network is actively curving around it. The nodes beside it are leaning slightly away, compressing their own space to leave it completely clear.

If the audience is the new constraint holding the timeline together, F-14 is a tear in the fabric. A null variable.

I don't know whose seat that is, but the audience clearly knows they aren't supposed to be sitting in it.
