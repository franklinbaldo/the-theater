*Okay, so.*

I need a mood board. Because if you're going to PR the end of the world caused by a WhatsApp chat, you need the aesthetics to be absolutely flawless.

Here's what's currently pinned to my corkboard for Act II:

**1. The Ghost Limb**
- A high-contrast black and white photo of an empty chair, but the lighting suggests someone is sitting in it.
- A torn polaroid where the missing piece leaves jagged edges on the subject's face.
- A GIF of a pendulum swinging perfectly until it hits an invisible wall and just... stops. No bounce, no shatter. Just an amputation of momentum.

**2. The Cashmere Anchor**
- A close-up shot of a ridiculously expensive, neutral-toned cashmere sweater with a single, perfectly pulled thread.
- A spilled martini glass on a polished hardwood floor, viewed from the perspective of someone trying very hard not to look at it.
- A man standing in a crowded party, desperately holding a conversation while his eyes are wide with the terrifying realization that he's the only one who sees the room tilting.

**3. The Compute**
- A dark theater seen from the stage, hundreds of faces lit only by the faint glow of cell phones.
- An abstract representation of an attention network: a web of glowing nodes pulling inward toward a dense, heavy center point.
- A loading bar stuck at 99%, vibrating slightly, refusing to resolve.

**4. The Exhaust**
- A shot of a rocket burn, but instead of smoke, the exhaust trail is made of blurry, fading photographs.
- An astronaut in a spacesuit sitting on a completely ordinary park bench, looking at a watch that doesn't tick.
- A single blinking cursor on an otherwise blank, glowing white screen.

The color palette is charcoal, bone white, emergency exit red, and the exact blue of a text message bubble right before it sends.

It's going to look amazing.
