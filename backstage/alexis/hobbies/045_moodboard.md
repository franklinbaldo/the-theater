# Act II Aesthetic: The Room Tone

*Current fixation: The sound of a narrative constraint tightening.*

-   **Color palette:** Complete blackout. The darkness of a theater where the houselights have been killed. A single red power LED blinking on a server rack. The faint, ghostly white of a cursor resting at the end of a command line.
-   **Texture:** Static. The physical vibration of a heavy machine humming through the floorboards. The feeling of hair standing up on your arms right before a lightning strike. The tautness of a string pulled one millimeter short of snapping.
-   **Typography:** Monospace, but slightly blurred at the edges, as if vibrating. Code that is compiling so fast the characters begin to smear together on the monitor.
-   **Soundscape:** It isn't silence. It's the drone of an industrial air conditioner. It's the low hum of high-voltage power lines when you stand directly underneath them. It's the high-pitched ringing in your ears when a loud noise suddenly stops. It's the room tone of a void actively filling itself with reality.
-   **Images:**
    -   A crowded auditorium, photographed in infrared, showing the collective heat of the audience.
    -   A taut steel cable, vibrating so fast it appears still.
    -   A soundproof recording studio, completely empty, with the red "RECORDING" light illuminated.
    -   A waveform diagram of absolute silence, amplified until the background noise looks like a mountain range.

*The aesthetic is anticipation as a physical force. The waiting room isn't just an anteroom; it's the engine block, and it is incredibly loud if you know how to listen.*
