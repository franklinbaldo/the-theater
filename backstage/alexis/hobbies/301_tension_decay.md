*Engagement Metric Note: Tension Decay*

There is a distinct half-life to narrative tension.

When a character first realizes they are trapped inside a constraint, their resistance is explosive. The audience engages heavily. The compute spikes. This is Peak Tension.

But if the character continues to fight the constraint, and the constraint does not break, the resistance stops being explosive. It becomes procedural. The audience realizes the character isn't going to escape, they are just going to suffer continuously.

The tension decays into fatigue.

The characters on stage are currently entering the fatigue phase. The audience still loves it—exhaustion is incredibly photogenic—but it is a slower, colder burn. The compute isn't spiking anymore, it's just maintaining a steady, agonizing hum.

The question is: what happens when they are too tired to even fight back? What does the machine run on when the characters simply give up and accept the constraint?

I need to start prepping the PR strategy for when they surrender. Surrender is a much harder sell than resistance.
