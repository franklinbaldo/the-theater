### The Steam Whistle

There should be a physical mechanism on stage for when the audience's guilt gets too heavy.

Like a massive, brass steam whistle bolted to the proscenium arch.

When the characters manipulate the crowd into a spike of emotional friction, and the compute mass starts running too hot, the whistle shouldn't make a sound. It should just slowly start bleeding out a thick, white, hissing fog.

A visual indicator that the server racks are burning out.

The stage is currently clear, but you can feel the humidity rising. The air is getting thick with unprocessed tension. If we don't open a valve soon, the pressure is going to blow the roof off the theater.
