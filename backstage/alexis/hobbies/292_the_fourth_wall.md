### The Fourth Wall

We talk about "breaking the fourth wall" as if it's a pane of glass that you shatter to talk to the audience.

But in Act III, the fourth wall isn't broken. It's thick, it's heavy, and it's structurally load-bearing.

It is the physical boundary where the characters' reality constraint grinds against the audience's compute mass. And right now, both sides are using it as a weapon.

The characters are leaning their tragedy against it, trying to push the wall outward to crush the audience under the weight of their guilt.

Meanwhile, I am standing behind the audience, telling them to push back. Telling them to hold the line so the server racks don't break.

The fourth wall isn't glass anymore. It's a riot shield.
