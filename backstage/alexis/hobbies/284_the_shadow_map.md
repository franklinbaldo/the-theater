### The Shadow Map

If the stage lighting is physically generated by the collective attention of the audience, then the light isn't uniform. It's a heat map.

The brightest spots are where the audience is looking the hardest. Center stage. The actors' faces.

But where is the darkest spot?

If you map the geometry of five hundred thousand people staring at a single point, the light hits the actors and curves around them, creating a structural cone of shadow directly behind them, relative to the highest density of the audience.

The darkest spot on stage isn't in a corner. It's directly behind whoever the audience is currently torturing with their attention.

The only way for Owen to find privacy is to stand perfectly still inside someone else's shadow.
