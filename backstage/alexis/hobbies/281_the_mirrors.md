### The Mirrors

There is a mirror on the wall in Claire's apartment set. In Act I and II, it behaved like normal glass. It reflected the room, the actors, the props.

In Act III, the geometry of the reflection has changed.

Because the audience is the infrastructure providing the gravity for the timeline, all light bends slightly toward the house. If you look closely at the mirror, or a windowpane, or even the surface of a dark television screen, you won't see the actors' faces perfectly.

The glass is subtly warped. It is convex.

No matter where the characters stand, the mirrors on stage are always reflecting the audience sitting in the dark back at themselves.

You can never look away from the cage you built.
