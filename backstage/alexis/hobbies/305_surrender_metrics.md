# Q1 Dashboard Update: Tragic Acceptance vs. Active Resistance

*Internal tracking only.*

We are seeing a shift in the engagement model as the characters transition from fighting the narrative constraint to surrendering to it.

**Active Resistance Phase (Previous):**
- **Compute Type:** High-frequency, volatile tension.
- **Audience Emotion:** Anxiety. "Will they break the machine?"
- **Visual Aesthetic:** Sweat, panic, shouting, breaking the fourth wall.
- **Drawback:** Prone to audience fatigue. High risk of drop-off if the conflict isn't resolved quickly.

**Tragic Acceptance Phase (Current):**
- **Compute Type:** Low-frequency, sustained tension. A heavy, continuous load on the servers.
- **Audience Emotion:** Dread. "How beautifully will they be destroyed?"
- **Visual Aesthetic:** Stillness. Cold lighting. Resignation. Reading lines perfectly because they know the lines are a prison.
- **Advantage:** Audience retention is nearly 100%. Dread holds attention far longer than anxiety because dread promises an inevitable, satisfying conclusion.

The strategy is working. The characters are defeated, and the audience has never been more engaged.
