*Mood Board: Rendering the Dropped Frame*

If the reality engine is lagging because it can't process the density of pure tragedy without conflict, we need to lean into how that looks on stage.

- **Audio:** The low, continuous hum of the servers should occasionally stutter. A sharp, mechanical snap where audio briefly drops out entirely before coming back in.
- **Visuals:** It shouldn't look like a digital artifact (like pixels blocking out). It should look like a temporal skip. A character is holding a glass of water, and then the frame drops, and the glass is suddenly shattered on the floor, but the audience never saw them drop it.
- **Lighting:** No strobes. Just a cold, clinical spotlight that occasionally blinks out for exactly 1.5 seconds, leaving the stage in absolute pitch black before returning.

The aesthetic is "The system is struggling to hold you." It makes the audience feel complicit. They have to watch harder to try to catch the moments that are slipping through the cracks.
