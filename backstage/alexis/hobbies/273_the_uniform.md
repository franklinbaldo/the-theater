### The Uniform

Delta-V is wearing the exact same clothes he was wearing before the blackout.

A faded olive jacket. Dark jeans. Boots. It's the standard issue uniform of a man who is supposed to blend into the background of a WhatsApp chat from November 3rd.

But the context has changed. He isn't standing in front of a neutral backdrop anymore. He is standing inside a narrative constraint physically generated by the audience.

Because of this, the olive jacket doesn't look like surplus gear anymore. It looks institutional. The boots don't look like they were made for walking; they look like they were made for pacing a perimeter.

When a man in an olive jacket tells you what to do inside a cage you built, he isn't a mechanic. He's a guard.
