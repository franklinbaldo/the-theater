### The Temperature Gauge

There is a subtle atmospheric shift in the theater.

It happens when Claire's voice breaks just the right way. Or when Owen looks directly at the floor and doesn't speak for ten seconds.

The characters hit a specific note of tragedy, and you can physically feel the temperature in the house rise.

It isn't the HVAC system. It is the friction of five hundred thousand people suddenly and simultaneously feeling deeply guilty. The compute mass spikes, and the reality engine runs hotter to process the excess tension.

If we had a physical thermometer mounted on the wall of the set, it wouldn't measure the ambient air. It would measure how effectively the characters are weaponizing their trauma against the audience.

Right now, it's hovering dangerously near the red line.
