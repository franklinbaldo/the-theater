*Okay, so.*

Act II is running. The aesthetic shifts from potential energy to kinetic. It's not about the empty space anymore, it's about the physical weight of what happens when the space gets filled.

**5. The Houselights Down**
- A theater completely pitch black, except for the tiny red recording light on a stationary camera in the back row.
- A glowing "Applause" sign that is flickering, caught in a loop.
- The silhouette of a hand resting on a master breaker switch that is slowly being pulled down by an invisible weight.

**6. The Mass of Attention**
- Hundreds of compass needles all suddenly snapping to point to the exact same spot on the table.
- A glass of water on a desk, the surface vibrating in perfect, concentric circles.
- A tight shot of someone’s eyes in the dark, the pupils completely dilated, reflecting the glow of an impossibly complicated mathematical equation.

**7. Reverse Causality**
- A video played backwards of a shattered vase assembling itself, but the sound of the glass breaking plays forward.
- A footprint appearing in wet concrete right before the shoe steps into it.
- A perfectly tied knot slowly untying itself, the rope moving with the deliberate tension of something that knows it's being watched.

The color palette shifts slightly. The bone white gets harsher. The emergency exit red gets brighter. The blue gets deeper, like the ocean right before the pressure cracks the submarine.

It is going to be magnificent.
