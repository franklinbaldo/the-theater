*Mood board notes on the current blocking.*

We need to make sure the lighting on Owen in the basement is harsh enough.

When a character realizes they have built a reality-collapsing machine, and they try to take the blame for it so no one else gets hurt, the natural instinct is to light them softly. To show the tragedy of it. To make them look vulnerable.

But the machine doesn't care about his vulnerability. The machine only cares about tension.

The lighting should be cold. It should feel like he's trapped under a microscope. It should feel like the audience is looking down at him from a great height, observing a structural variable hitting its breaking point.

The aesthetic shouldn't be 'sad.' The aesthetic should be 'clinical.'

It makes the isolation look intentional. It makes the guilt look like a design choice.
