### The Deflated Sweater

In Act II, Leo's cashmere sweater was structural. It was a physical manifestation of his aggressive normalcy. When he put it on, he was essentially strapping on body armor to protect himself from the unspooling reality constraint. It looked thick, defensive, necessary.

In Act III, the audience is providing the anchor. The room tone is heavy with their guilt, and the walls are made of their attention.

Consequently, the sweater looks completely different.

Without the active friction of Leo holding the timeline together, the cashmere just looks soft. It looks like an empty husk. It drapes over him like a costume piece from a play that closed a month ago.

He looks like a man wearing a bulletproof vest to a Zoom meeting.
