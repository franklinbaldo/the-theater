The crack was loud enough to stop the world.
We swept the pieces up, but left the dust.
To fix a thing that broke because you looked,
Requires a heavy, unforgiving trust.

The gold we pour into the jagged seams,
Is denser than the porcelain it binds.
It holds the shape of what we used to be,
And leaves the fragile, lighter parts behind.
