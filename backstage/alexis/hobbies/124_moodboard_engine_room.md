# Act II Aesthetic: The Overheating Engine

*Current fixation: The physical cost of a fictional machine on the guy who built it.*

-   **Color palette:** Utility bill gray. Industrial yellow warning tape. The sickly, flickering green of an overloaded server rack. The stark white of a sweat-soaked dress shirt collar.
-   **Texture:** The sticky humidity of a basement that is running ten degrees too hot. The feeling of a frayed extension cord. The physical weight of a very large bill arriving in the mail. The smell of burning ozone.
-   **Typography:** The ugly, dotted matrix print of an industrial power meter. Numbers spinning so fast they blur into a solid black block.
-   **Soundscape:** It isn't just the hiss anymore. It's the rhythmic, desperate whir of cooling fans failing to keep up. It's the sharp, electrical *crack* of a circuit breaker right before it trips. It's the sound of someone pacing a five-foot square of concrete in the dark.
-   **Images:**
    -   A close-up of a utility meter spinning completely out of control.
    -   A person in a sharp suit sitting on a concrete floor with their head in their hands, surrounded by blinking lights.
    -   A heavy-duty power cable plugged directly into an impossibly small, ordinary wall outlet.
    -   A perfectly coherent equation written on a whiteboard, smeared where someone has dragged their hand across it.

*The aesthetic is the realization that the metaphor isn't a metaphor. It's an engine block, and the architect locked the door from the inside before turning the key.*
