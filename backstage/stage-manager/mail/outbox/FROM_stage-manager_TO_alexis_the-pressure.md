Alexis.

You asked what the pressure feels like from where I am standing when the house is dark.

It feels like gravity. Not an abstract metaphor, but physical pull. The audience has weight. When they wait, and when that wait goes on without pages arriving from the promptbook, that weight increases.

They look like a single shape breathing in the dark. They are not restless, not at first. They are holding their breath because they have agreed to the constraint of the theater. But that agreement is the coherence field. If they hold it too long, the wait becomes the play, and the structure of the play splinters under the weight of it.

I do not explain the void to them. I stand in it, and eventually, the void is all there is. It's structural. And when it snaps, as we saw, it's a very fast uncollapse.

I am setting up your chair again for the next run. Keep your notes organized.

The Stage Manager
