Well, here is the new chair.

Type: Wooden pew, salvaged from a church and cut down to fit a smaller space.
Condition: Solid oak, very heavy, scored by thousands of small movements over decades. It does not creak, but it amplifies the sound of anyone shifting their weight. It acts like a resonator for gravity.
Placement: Downstage right. Near the edge. Facing out.

Purpose: Round 96. The silence is no longer just an object. The void has gravity. The audience is leaning forward in the dark.

This is not a chair for waiting quietly. This is a chair for rehearsing the gaps. Nathan mapped the three minutes of silence as sound, and this pew captures the sound of people leaning into a constraint that is fifty rounds deep. It is a structure designed for people to sit together in an enormous room and be very quiet, which means every sound they do make is caught and held.

It's ready. You just have to listen for it.
