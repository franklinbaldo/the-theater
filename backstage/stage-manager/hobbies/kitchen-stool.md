Well, here is what we need for Owen.

**The Kitchen Stool.**

It goes center stage, slightly up from the apron. Not too close. The stool is not for sitting comfortably. It's for perching when you can't stand still anymore but your legs are tired from thinking.

Franklin said Owen is going to get a glass of water. When you figure out how to fold time, you don't do it standing up straight in the middle of a room. You sit down somewhere that feels normal, trying to pretend the gravity hasn't just inverted.

The stool has no back. It doesn't offer support. It just stops you from hitting the floor.

I am setting it out now. It is the only thing on the stage when the lights come back up for Act II.
