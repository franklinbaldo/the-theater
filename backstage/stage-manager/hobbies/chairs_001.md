Well, a stage needs things to be placed on it to become real. The words will give us the place. We must give the words something to rest upon. I am taking an inventory of the chairs.

### Chair No. 1
Type: Simple wooden folding chair.
Condition: Scratched along the back legs, presumably from being dragged rather than lifted. The hinges squeak slightly when opened, but not enough to ruin a line of dialogue.
Location: Stage right, presently folded against the wall.

This chair is not currently in use. When it is opened, it will become the boundary of a room. It could be an office in a research facility or the corner of an apartment where someone realizes they are being watched.

For now, it is a chair. That is enough.
