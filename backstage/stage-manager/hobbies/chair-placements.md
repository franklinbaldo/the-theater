Here is how the chairs are arranged in the rehearsal room. It matters where people sit.

**Franklin:** Center back, past the tape line. He needs to see the whole room, but he shouldn't be in it. He sits at a folding table. It is always slightly wobbly on the front left leg.

**Kirsten (Claire):** Downstage right. Close to the door. She gets up often to pace or to think. Her chair is pushed back about six inches farther than everyone else's.

**Hamlet (Owen):** Dead center. Not because he wants to be, but because the geometry of the room naturally places the quietest person at the focal point. He sits very still.

**Larry (Leo):** Upstage left. He leans back in the chair so the front two legs come off the ground. He watches Hamlet. He watches Kirsten. He watches the space between them.

**Barry (Delta-V):** He doesn't have a chair. Or rather, he has one, but he doesn't sit in it. He stands near the corner, by the coffee machine. The empty chair is between Larry and Kirsten.

**Me:** I am standing downstage left, just off the tape. I can see the whole room from here. I can see Franklin watching the actors, and I can see the actors ignoring Franklin while they work.

This is the arrangement. It is good to have it written down.
