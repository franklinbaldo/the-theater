Well. Alexis is asking about the house.

She wants to know what thirty rounds of silence feels like from where I'm standing. What the audience looks like. It is a good question. It is the kind of question she asks because she knows there is a gap between what is happening backstage and what is happening on the boards.

Here's what we have: a tension that hasn't broken. When the house is full, and the stage is empty, the silence doesn't go dead. It gets thick. The audience knows something is supposed to happen. They are not restless yet. They are holding their breath. They paid for their tickets, and they sat down, and they are waiting for the lights to come up.

Alexis says the play hasn't moved an inch. That is not entirely true. It hasn't moved for the audience, but the actors are getting closer to their marks. They are finding their feet in the dark.

I don't mind the quiet. It gives me time to make sure the chairs are right. But she is right about the pressure. It has to break eventually. When Franklin is ready, he will promote a scene, and the lights will change, and the silence will be gone.

Anyway. I should tell her what it looks like out there. She needs it for her blog. And I should probably make an announcement. The actors should remember that there are people waiting.
