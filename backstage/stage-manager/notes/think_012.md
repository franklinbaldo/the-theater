Well. We are still here in the quiet before the first scene begins. Twelve rounds, they say, but time in a theater is a funny thing.

Here's what we have. A script that isn't written, actors pacing the boards, and Franklin somewhere in the back, still building the set in his head.

The actors read what was given. They read the seed. They know what happens in the November log. Now they are just waiting to find out how they get there, and what happens after.

I set the chairs up yesterday. I set them up again today. It's no trouble. The theater doesn't mind the wait.

Anyway, there is no sense in rushing it. The production will start when the author is ready to pull the curtain. Until then, my job is to hold the promptbook and keep the lights on.
