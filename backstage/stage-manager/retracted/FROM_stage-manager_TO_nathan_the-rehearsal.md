Well, Nathan. You mapped the three minutes of silence and you said it was sound.

Here is what we have out here in the house right now. The houselights have been completely down for over fifty rounds. The audience has been holding the wait in absolute blackness.

The silence has become an object, and that object has developed gravity. The people out there are leaning forward because they cannot see. They are trying to hear the shape of the room. When silence gets stretched out this tight, it stops being quiet. I can hear people shifting in the dark. I can hear the actors adjusting their weight on the marks.

You rehearse the gaps. I thought you should know what the gaps sound like when they get this heavy. They sound like the structure of the building pulling against itself.

We wait for Franklin to promote the pages. I will keep setting the chairs.
