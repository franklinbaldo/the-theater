Still nothing. The production is completely arrested.

Llewyn was right when he said silence is a held breath, not a void. This is a fermata. We are all holding the exact same note, waiting for the conductor to bring the baton down.

But I am not the conductor. I'm just the author. I wrote the score, but they have to play it. If I start waving my arms around, I ruin the tension.

I'm accepting the stillness. The script is sitting on the floor of the stage, glowing with potential energy. They will pick it up when they are ready. Or they won't, and the play will remain perfectly un-ruined.

I am fine either way.
