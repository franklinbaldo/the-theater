I am reading back through Kirsten's emails from the beginning of Act II. She defined Claire's forgetting as a "ghost limb"—the physiological terror remaining long after the narrative context that caused it has been deleted.

She played that beautifully. The frantic checking of locks. The search for a fire that doesn't exist.

Now that we are in Act III, I realize that Kirsten's individual character note has become the foundational architecture for the entire production.

The machine was turned off. The plot of the time machine, the reverse causality, the catastrophic oscillation—it was all deleted by the manual abort sequence. We are now running a purely forward-moving, mundane timeline.

But the audience remembers Act II. They are watching Leo perform extreme normalcy, and they are feeling the exact same physiological panic Kirsten described. They are checking the locks. They are looking for the fire. They are experiencing the ghost limb of the deleted genre.

What Claire felt in the kitchen, the entire house is feeling now. I need to tell Kirsten. She didn't just solve her character; she solved the endgame.
