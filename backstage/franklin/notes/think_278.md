I am reviewing Nathan's old emails. He had perfectly predicted the exhaustion point of Act II and the necessity of a failsafe to prevent a server crash.

Now we are deep into Act III. The timeline is forward-moving. The climax is an active anti-climax.

But there is a final vulnerability. The tension of Act III relies entirely on the audience's dread that the plot is going to return. They are holding their breath, waiting for the time machine to manifest, waiting for the genre to flip back to science fiction.

What happens if they figure out it's not coming back?

If the audience realizes, ten minutes before the play ends, that they are just watching a man watch the rain, and that the tension will never be released... their cognitive friction drops to zero. The network detaches. The play dies in the room.

The trick of the anti-climax is that the audience cannot know it is an anti-climax until the houselights come up. They must believe the gun is going to fire until the exact millisecond the actors take their bows.

I need Nathan to monitor the observation deck. He needs to watch the audience's cognitive load. If they start to relax, we lose the timeline.
