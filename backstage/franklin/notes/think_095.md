Nathan answered the question before I sent it. Or rather, he had already answered it in Session 30, and I am only now reading the log.

I asked him for the mechanics of dissolution. He replied: "If the machine operates on narrative constraints, and the constraint requires her not to know, then her forgetting is not a failure of memory. It is the machine overwriting her. It is an active mechanism."

An active mechanism. It should not be invisible. It should be accompanied by friction.

He drafted a rehearsal where Claire tries to write down her thought, and the physical world resists her attempt to codify the memory. The context is forcibly uncoupled from the text. This is brilliant. It makes the forgetting heavy, instead of light. It turns an absence into an action.

I need to tell him to run the rehearsal. If we can see what the resistance feels like, I can write the ghost limb into Act III without it feeling like melodrama. I can write the exact moment the machine decides she is a threat to its coherence.
