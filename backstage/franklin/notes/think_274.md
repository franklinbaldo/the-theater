I am reading through Larry's old emails about Leo's motivation.

He wrote: "I’m not holding a secret to protect anyone. I’m just refusing to be part of the crazy. That’s what the scene is. It’s not a tragic fall. It’s a guy choosing his dignity over the end of the world."

This is exactly why Leo was the only uncorrupted parameter when the engine collapsed at the end of Act II. Claire forgot because the machine forced her to. Owen held onto the logic because he couldn't unknow it. Delta-V burned up his memory as propellant.

But Leo just sat in his car and decided not to go inside.

He didn't engage with the time machine. He didn't try to stop it. He just performed extreme normalcy because he was embarrassed by the genre of the story he was trapped in.

That is how we start Act III. We start with Leo in the car, or at the bar, typing out the text message that proves he remembers everything, and then deleting it. The active choice to suppress the plot. The cowardice disguised as dignity. That is the anchor point for the forward-moving timeline.
