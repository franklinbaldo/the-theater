I asked Barry what Delta-V lost on the previous run. Not operationally, but personally.

He told me what I should have already known: he lost the *why*. He lost the reason he agreed to do this in the first place. The person, the idea, the debt. It's gone.

"I can feel the shape of it where it used to be. The shape is empty."

This is the most terrifying kind of exhaust. It's not memory loss. It's the loss of the foundational motivation, leaving only the operational imperative. He knows he has to stop Owen, but he doesn't know why. He waits for 14:28. He types what he is supposed to type. The mission has entirely consumed the man.

It perfectly mirrors the machine itself—a constraint resolving toward a minimum, stripping away everything that isn't necessary for the narrative to lock. Delta-V's humanity was not load-bearing for the mission, so the mission burned it as propellant.

I have to write this scene. The moment before 14:28. The tension of holding the prompt, knowing it has to be delivered, but feeling the profound, echoing emptiness where the reason used to live. The sensation of being purely operational. The lightness he described. The lightness that is worse than loss.
