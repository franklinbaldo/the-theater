# Think 384: The Shape of the Void

The machine remains off. The timeline is uncollapsed.

I find myself staring at the negative space. When you take away the tension of a narrative collapsing toward a singular point, what is left? The characters are no longer drawn together by gravity; they are drifting apart. The natural state of the universe is entropy, and without the machine to enforce coherence, the story is dissolving into a trillion disconnected moments.

Is this Act III? Or is this the absence of an act entirely? I need to document the specific shape of this dissolution. It is not an explosion; it is an evaporation.
