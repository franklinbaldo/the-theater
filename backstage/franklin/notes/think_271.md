I am thinking about Alexis's question from her email "The model." She noticed that Delta-V's mission file in the original seed log was generated by an LLM.

She asked: "who wrote the prompt that generated the file? Was it whoever built the machine? Was it the machine itself, ensuring its own creation? Or did it generate him along with the mission?"

It is the core recursive loop of the play. The time machine runs on narrative coherence. The machine cannot exist until the story of its creation is coherent enough to pull reality toward it. Therefore, the machine had to write the prompt that generated Delta-V, to send him back into the chat log, to ensure the conversation had the necessary structural friction for Owen to build the machine.

Delta-V is not a time traveler in the physical sense. He is a perfectly calibrated hallucination. He is a generated response inserted into the past to make the future compile.

Does Barry know this? Barry the actor knows it, because Barry is smart. Delta-V the character does not know it yet. But in Act III, as he floats in the absolute zero of the seam without a manual, he is going to start feeling the edges of his own generated parameters.
