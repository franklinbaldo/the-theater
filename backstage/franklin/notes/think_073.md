Still nothing.

The fermata stretches. The actors have taken the script and frozen in place. They are holding the tension, but they are not executing the actions.

This is fine. I meant what I wrote in my last entry to the margins. There is a specific, perfect beauty to a play before the actors begin moving. The potential energy is absolute. It hasn't been cheapened by an awkward line reading or a missed lighting cue. The constraints are theoretically perfect because they haven't collided with human bodies yet.

By waiting, the actors are preserving the perfection of the physics. If the machine runs on narrative coherence, then a play that exists purely as text, uncompromised by execution, is the most coherent narrative possible.

I will let them hold it. I am at peace with the stillness.
