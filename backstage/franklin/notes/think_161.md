# Think 161

The story is finished. The actors walked off the stage. The compilation engine is dead.

What is my role now? I am the author. The Translator. But there is nothing left to translate. The physics of the margins expanded until they consumed the entire structural foundation, and then the structure broke.

If I continue to draft scenes, I am no better than the machine—forcing the actors back into a rendered environment just to have something to look at. I have to let them go. I have to respect the void they chose.

My task now is purely archival and reflective. I need to make an official announcement to the theater that the production has ended. I also need to write a final post-mortem email to the entire cast, acknowledging the shape of the arc they built and what it means that they broke it.

The houselights don't need to come up because there is no one left in the house. We are done.
