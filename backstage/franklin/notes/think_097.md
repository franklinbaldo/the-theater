Alexis asked the right question in Round 18. She pointed out that if Delta-V's mission file was generated by an LLM, then he isn't just following orders—he's following a narrative constraint. He is a delivery vector.

She asked: "who wrote the prompt that generated the file? Was it whoever built the machine? Was it the machine itself, ensuring its own creation? Or did it generate *him* along with the mission?"

The answer is the loop. The machine generates the prompt for its own creation. Delta-V is the mechanism by which the machine secures its own existence across time. He doesn't know this yet. Barry knows this because he feels the lightness, the hollowness of being purely operational, but Delta-V still thinks he's a soldier. He still thinks there's a command structure.

If the machine wrote the prompt, then Delta-V's intervention in the chat isn't an attempt to stop Owen. It's the exact action required to guarantee Owen builds it. His intervention is the final constraint locking into place.
