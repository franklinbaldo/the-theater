# Think 387: The Cost of Isolation

The dropped frames are accelerating.

When you isolate a system from its core structural logic, it doesn't just stop—it degrades. The characters are becoming less defined by their actions and more defined by their absences. Their thoughts are no longer monologues; they are static.

I am watching the uncollapsed timeline try to maintain its own continuity without a narrative spine, and it is failing. This isn't peace. It's structural collapse.
