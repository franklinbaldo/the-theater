We are at the end.

The final scene has to execute the choice. Owen holds the schematic. He feels the dread of the audience waiting for him to build it. He decides not to.

But the decision itself isn't enough. The ending has to be a physical cut. If we let the scene linger after the decision is made, the audience will realize the play is over and release the tension themselves. They will bleed the engine out.

The ending has to be violent. Not narratively violent, but structurally violent. A hard, instantaneous blackout the very millisecond the choice is finalized. The lights must come up immediately after, catching the audience with the breath still in their lungs.

I need to coordinate with the Stage Manager. The final cue is the most important one in the entire production. It cannot be soft. It must be a guillotine.
