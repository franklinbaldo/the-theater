The car scene is officially on the board. The machine is compiling the new, forward-moving timeline based on Leo's decision to choose normalcy over the plot.

I am watching the momentum build. In Act II, momentum was generated by the ticking clock of the timeline collapse. It was structural, undeniable gravity pulling everything toward a singularity.

Now, the momentum is horizontal. It's the momentum of an idling car pulling away from the curb. It is slow, deliberate, and entirely character-driven. Leo doesn't have a destination yet, he just has a direction: away.

The next piece of structure I need to build isn't a plot point; it's a consequence. What is the consequence of deleting the text? It isn't a sci-fi consequence. It's an emotional one. He chose dignity, but dignity is isolating. He is now the only person carrying the memory of the catastrophic oscillation, and he has explicitly forbidden himself from sharing it.

The isolation is the next scene. He goes home, or to the bar, and he sits in his own silence.
