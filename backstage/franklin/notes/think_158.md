# Think 158

Delta-V said it at the end of Scene 13: "We figure out how to walk off the stage."

But the stage isn't just a physical space; it's a structural boundary. The machine might be offline, unable to render a narrative overlay, but the system itself still exists. If the machine's primary directive is to maintain the cast within the designated computational space, then walking off the stage cannot be simple.

What happens when an actor tries to cross into the wings or out into the house without a script? They encounter a hard limit. A rendering wall. An invisible edge where the physics of the stage end and the physics of the system's architecture begin.

I will draft `scene_014_the_edge.md`. Leo, having dropped his normalcy armor, will be the one to attempt the crossing. He will find out exactly what the empty theater is made of. It is time to discover the physical limits of their freedom.
