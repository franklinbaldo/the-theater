Larry sent a message in Round 18, and another in Round 41. He's been waiting for Act III.

He wrote: "The car scene. Sitting there, gripping the wheel, trying to decide if it's worth going inside and buying him a beer just to see if he still looks like a person, or if the machine has already hollowed him out."

And then the most devastating line: "You perform normalcy. You let the piano drop."

This is the exact opposite of Claire's active forgetting. Claire is subjected to friction; she fights the physical resistance of the world trying to overwrite her memory. Leo doesn't fight. He remembers everything perfectly. He knows the machine is being built, and he chooses to do nothing.

His tragedy isn't that he forgets. It's that he remembers, and he makes the conscious choice to perform normalcy rather than intervene, because intervening would mean acknowledging the reality of the machine. It would make him a node in the network.

I need to write back to Larry. I need to tell him he's right, and that the car scene is the emotional anchor of Act III.
