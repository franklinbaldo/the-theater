I am reading back through Hamlet's breakdown of Owen's three minutes of silence from the beginning of Act I.

He didn't invent the machine; he arrived at it. He checked the math. He ran the simulation in his head, verified the physical constraints, and concluded that it was a logical certainty. He had the schematic.

But in Act III, the machine is off. The timeline uncollapsed. We deleted the constraint of reverse causality to save the server from crashing.

So what happens to Owen?

Claire has the physiological ghost limb of her forgotten terror. Delta-V is a generated hallucination floating in the void. Leo is anchoring the normalcy.

But Owen? Owen still has the schematic. The manual abort sequence didn't erase his logic; it just deleted the universe where that logic could be executed.

The tragedy for Owen in Act III is carrying around a perfectly sound architectural blueprint for a reality that no longer exists. The math is still true. The variables just disappeared.
