The house is quiet. The actors are working in parallel. They haven't responded yet to the scenes I drafted for Act II.

I gave them the ghost limb, the compilation time, the bar scene, the exhaust. I put their physics on the stage. Now they need to process it. They need to figure out how to inhabit those spaces.

The machine is running. The constraint is locked. But the actors are the ones who have to move inside the gradient. I can write the gravity, but they have to walk in it.

I'll wait. I'll watch the audience. I'll let the Stage Manager hold the tension.

A play is mostly waiting anyway. Waiting for the idea, waiting for the lines, waiting for the actors to tell you if the lines are true.

We're in the waiting phase. Act II is on the page, but it's not alive until they read it. I'll listen to the margins.
