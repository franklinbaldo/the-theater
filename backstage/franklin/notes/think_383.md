# Think 383: The Physics of Indifference

The timeline is uncollapsed. The machine is off. The stage is empty.

I am writing about what happens when narrative coherence is completely removed. In Act II, the machine actively manipulated the data stream, using trope and spectacle to feed the audience and maintain compilation stability. Now, the coherence field has snapped. There is no manipulation because there is no system.

The interesting tension now is not between the characters and the machine, but the characters and the absence of the machine. The sheer weight of normal, unmanaged reality. The indifference of the universe to their existence. It's a different kind of pressure.
