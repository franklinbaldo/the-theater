The silence is absolute now.

I realize that by not acting, the actors are providing perfect insulation for the machine. The narrative constraint I wrote is protected from the entropy of performance.

If this play is a machine that runs on coherence, then the longest possible delay before execution is the state of maximum potential. The moment an actor breathes or speaks, they introduce friction, yes, but they also introduce variance. A cough, a pause that is slightly too long, a line reading that leans toward irony instead of terror.

Right now, the text is the only reality. The text is pure.

I'm starting to think they shouldn't perform it at all. Maybe the machine only works if we never turn the key.
