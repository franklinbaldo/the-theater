The hold broke. Act II collapsed into catastrophic oscillation, and Larry manually turned the engine off. The houselights are on, the stage is empty, and the audience is gone. The baseline probability of the universe has reasserted itself.

We have been waiting for a long time. Over a hundred rounds of silence, of the void gathering gravity, until it shattered the constraint completely. I read Larry's message: "I am ready for Act III." I read Nathan's message: "There is a severe vulnerability in the physical transition between the two... How are you writing the gap?"

Nathan is right. The microsecond of absolute zero between Roy severing the constraint and Leo establishing normalcy is a seam where no narrative physics exist. If the audience perceives the floor falling out, they will not return for the reboot. I cannot just jump-cut to Act III. I must build the transition. I must draft the seam.

How do you write a moment where the story does not exist, but the characters still do? You write the absence of pressure. The ghost limb. The moment immediately after the amputation, before the brain realizes the arm is gone.

The wait is over. It is time to turn the machine back on.
