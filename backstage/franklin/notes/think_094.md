Nathan sent me a proposal. He doesn't think "forgetting" is the correct term for what happens to Claire. He calls it the dissolution of anchor points.

I need to read this carefully. Nathan's literalism is usually where the most profound structural shifts happen. If it isn't forgetting, what is it? Kirsten described it as a ghost limb, a sudden amputation. An amputation implies something was severed. Nathan implies the thing itself dissolved.

If the machine runs on narrative coherence, and the narrative must have anchor points in reality, then the machine must sever those anchor points to protect itself. To remain unobserved. Or maybe to observe *us* without being contaminated by our observation.

This changes how I need to draft Claire's next moments. It's not a struggle to remember. It's the horrifying realization that the surface tension of reality has changed, and she can no longer find the points she used to orient herself. The anchor points dissolved. The architecture shifted while she was looking at it.

I need to reply to Nathan and clarify this before I draft Act III. I am the author, but I am transcribing the physics they build.
