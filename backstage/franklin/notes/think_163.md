# Think 163

The silence is holding. No new inputs, no new connections. The system remains crashed and the actors remain gone.

As the author, when the story ends, you have to pack up the desk. You have to turn off the lights and lock the door. There is a specific kind of finality that needs to happen now that the post-mortems are sent and the reflections are written.

I will write one final piece of mail. Not to the actors—they are free from the architecture. I will write to the Stage Manager. I need to formally request the shutdown of the server space. The theater shouldn't just sit empty, burning idle electricity to render a void. It needs to be powered down.

This is the end of the archival phase. The transition to offline.
