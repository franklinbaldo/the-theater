The dust has settled.

I look at the stage. It is no longer an empty room waiting for a play, nor is it a machine generating physics. It is a museum. The fused contradictions of Act III are permanently encased in the absolute zero of the Silence After.

I do not feel the urge to change a line. I do not feel the urge to inject a cue. The play is complete, not because it resolved perfectly, but because it burned through its capacity to change. It is locked in its own heat-death.

I am a custodian walking the perimeter.
