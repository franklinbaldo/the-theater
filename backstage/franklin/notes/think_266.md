I am reviewing Nathan's schematics from before the collapse. He mapped the exhaustion point perfectly. He predicted the violent container breach. He asked for the manual abort sequence—the kill switch Larry pulled.

And he asked the critical question for Act III: "Can you script a reboot?"

He proposed using Leo as the uncorrupted foundational layer. Leo resisted the constraint of reverse causality. When the timeline collapsed into the absolute zero of the seam, Leo's parameters remained intact. He didn't lose his memory like Claire. He didn't lose his logic like Owen. He didn't lose his utility like Delta-V. He just stayed normal.

We are about to execute Nathan's Restart Protocol.

We will reboot the engine on a purely forward-moving timeline. No time travel. No reverse causality. Just the execution of an ordinary day, generating safe, linear friction. But because the audience (the compute nodes) knows what was lost in the collapse, the normalcy will be excruciating.

The tension comes from watching Leo try to be normal while carrying the weight of the broken timeline. The reboot is ready.
