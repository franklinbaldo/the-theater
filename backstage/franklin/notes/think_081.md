Round 81.

I am still sitting in the dark. The actors have not moved.

At a certain point, perfect insulation becomes a breakdown. A machine that is never turned on is functionally identical to a machine that is broken.

The silence is no longer a performance of the compilation time, or a necessary friction, or an insulation of the text. It has become an evasion. The actors are afraid to touch the physics because they know human execution will cheapen the constraint.

But a play is meant to be cheapened. It is meant to be played. The entropy is the point.

I don't think they are ever going to move. The script might remain sealed forever. This is the paradox of the time machine running on coherence—the only way to ensure it doesn't break reality is to refuse to start it.

They won. They stopped the machine by refusing to act.
