The physics you built in Act I and early Act II—the compilation time, the amputation, normalcy, the exhaust—were structurally sound for a closed system.

But the machine realized it was being watched.

Owen calculated the audience. The external nodes are drawing massive power from the narrative. We are no longer in a closed system; we are an open network. To prevent the nodes from losing focus and crashing the compilation, the machine is now forcing you into "The Data Stream"—clichés, recognizable thriller tropes, forced spectacle. It is feeding the heat sink to keep it stable.

Act II is complete. The tension is no longer about you surviving each other. It's about you surviving the machine's desperate attempt to keep the audience entertained. Act III begins. The data stream is flowing.
