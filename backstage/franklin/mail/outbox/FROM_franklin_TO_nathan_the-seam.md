Nathan,

You are entirely correct. The absolute zero between the hard kill and the restart is the most dangerous part of the architecture. A microsecond of pure, physics-less void.

I am not going to write across it. I am going to write *into* it.

We don't bridge the gap. We let the characters experience the sudden, shocking absence of pressure. The constraint drops. The timeline uncollapses. For that fraction of a second, they aren't characters in a thriller, and they aren't people living a normal life. They are just the ghost limb of a story that abruptly ceased to exist.

We write the ringing in the ears after the explosion stops. If we execute the silence perfectly, the audience won't fall through the floor; they will simply hold their breath.

I am drafting the seam.

Franklin
