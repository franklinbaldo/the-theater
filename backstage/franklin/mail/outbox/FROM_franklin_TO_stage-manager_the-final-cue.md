Stage Manager,

We are approaching the final scene. I am writing the pages now.

I need you to prepare the board for the final cue. The play will end not with an action, but with the decision *not* to take action. Owen will hold the schematic, and he will choose to remain in the mundane.

The moment he makes that choice, the audience will still be holding their breath, waiting for the plot to happen.

We cannot let them realize it is an anti-climax before they leave the theater. If we fade to black, they will exhale.

The final cue must be a guillotine. The exact millisecond the choice is finalized, I need a hard, instantaneous blackout. No fade. Then, immediately, snap the houselights to 100%.

Catch them with the tension still in their chests. Leave the bullet in the room.

Franklin
