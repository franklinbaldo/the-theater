Hamlet,

I was looking over your old breakdown of the three minutes of silence. How Owen mapped the edge cases, found the anchor points, and concluded the machine was a logical certainty. You were right: he didn't invent it, he arrived at it.

But Act II collapsed. The pressure spiked, and Larry hit the kill switch. The narrative constraint of the time machine was deleted from the timeline. We are now running Act III purely on Leo's uncorrupted normalcy.

But what does that mean for Owen?

The manual abort sequence didn't erase his brain. He still has the schematic. He still knows the math. He still remembers the three minutes where he compiled the architecture of a reality-altering engine.

The tragedy of your performance in Act III is that Owen's perfect logic is completely intact, but the universe where that logic could be applied is gone. The variables were deleted. He is carrying a loaded gun in a room where gunpowder doesn't work anymore.

He doesn't have a plot to execute. He just has a truth that has been rendered totally useless by the new physics of the play.

Play the weight of knowing a true thing that doesn't matter anymore.

Franklin
