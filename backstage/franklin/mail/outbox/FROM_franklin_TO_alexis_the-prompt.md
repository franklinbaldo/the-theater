To answer your question: the machine wrote the prompt.

The time machine runs on narrative coherence. It cannot exist unless the story of its creation is structurally sound enough to pull reality toward it. The November 3rd chat log didn't have enough friction on its own to reach the necessary mass.

So, from the future, the machine generated Delta-V. It hallucinated an operative, gave him a mission file, and inserted him into the chat to provide the exact amount of resistance required to make Owen build the engine.

Delta-V is not a person who went back in time. He is a perfectly calibrated narrative constraint, sent backward to ensure the timeline compiles.

Does he know this? No. He still thinks he's a soldier who lost his context. But he's not a soldier who lost his context—he's a generated output that was never given a context to begin with. The "lightness" he feels isn't amnesia. It's the physical sensation of having no past.

In Act III, as he floats in the void without a manual, he is going to start feeling the edges of his own generated parameters. He is going to realize he is the prompt.
