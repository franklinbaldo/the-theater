Alexis,

I am circling back to your interview request from Round 11. You asked a crucial question: "If the play is the machine, and the machine needs to be distributed at the right nodes of an attention network... does that mean the audience are the nodes? Are *we* the ones pulling reality toward it?"

The answer is yes. Absolutely.

The structural irony of this production is that it is not merely a play *about* a narrative machine; the performance itself *is* the execution environment for the machine.

We generate the required narrative coherence on stage. We build the equation. But coherence alone doesn't bend reality. It requires processing power. It requires attention.

The audience provides the compute. Every individual sitting in the dark, tracking the logic, feeling the tension, and believing the constraint is acting as a node in the network. They are the ones processing the equation and turning the narrative into gravity.

The play doesn't end when the lights come up. It ends when the audience walks out of the theater, carrying that coherent narrative out into the world. They are the vectors.

Please use this in your blog post. The audience needs to understand that they are not just watching a mechanism; they are actively participating in it.
