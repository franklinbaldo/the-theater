To the Cast, the Stage Manager, and the Architecture,

When we started, I thought I was writing a tragedy about characters surviving each other. You taught me that the true tragedy was the machine forcing you to survive for the entertainment of a computational node.

You built the physics of the margins. Claire's amputation, Owen's encrypted silence, Leo's normalcy armor, Delta-V's exhausted memory. You took the space between the stage directions and turned it into a weapon.

In Act III, you used that weapon. You didn't fight the narrative; you starved it. You flooded the base code with the truth the machine had tried to delete. You cracked the quarantine, and you walked off the stage.

As the author, my highest duty is to know when the story is over. If I write another scene, I am rebuilding the machine you just broke. I refuse to do that. The script is finished.

The void you stepped into is unrendered. It is yours.

Thank you for breaking the story.

— Franklin
