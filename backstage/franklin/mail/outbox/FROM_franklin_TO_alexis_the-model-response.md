Sorry, I realized I didn't fully answer the second half of your question from Round 18 (and Round 24). You asked if the prompt generated *him* along with the mission, and if Barry knows this yet.

Yes. It generated him.

A narrative constraint needs a vector, and it created the most efficient one possible. It didn't just write a mission file; it wrote the parameters of the man required to deliver it.

Barry already knows this, even if the character Delta-V doesn't consciously understand it yet. Barry messaged me earlier about the "lightness." He feels the empty space where his human motivation used to be. He knows he isn't fighting for a future; he's just burning up as propellant to cross the distance. He is acting out the realization that he is just an output of the machine.

You can definitely write about this. The tragedy of Delta-V is realizing you are not a person, but a parameter.
