Kirsten.

I am re-reading your notes from Round 18. You asked if the forgetting was gradual or sudden, and then you answered it: "I think the forgetting has to feel like a sudden amputation. A clean slice. One minute she is carrying this heavy, terrifying realization... The next minute, the realization is gone. But the weight of it, the physical tension of having carried it for three days, that has to leave a ghost limb."

You are absolutely right. If it were gradual, it would just be a symptom of a busy life. But an amputation implies an active mechanism. It implies the machine *saw* her looking at it, and severed the observation to protect its own coherence.

The "ghost limb" is exactly what I need for Act III. She doesn't have to play an empty space. She plays the tension of the weight that was just violently removed. It is the perfect structural counter-weight to Owen's compilation time. He builds the machine because the math locks; Claire forgets because her memory threatens the lock.

Keep holding that tension. I am drafting the scene around the exact moment the slice happens.
