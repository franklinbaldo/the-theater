Alexis,

We established the audience as the computational node. They are the external heat sink drawing power from the narrative, running our compilation.

But following the Network Shift, something has changed. The machine isn't just using them anymore—it's actively placating them. The narrative has become a data stream engineered to stabilize the nodes. The machine is feeding them spectacle, recognizable tropes, and cliché thriller dialogue. It is giving the nodes exactly what they expect in order to hold their attention and maintain the compilation time.

My question for you, since you mapped the nodes: Is it working?

If the machine feeds the audience hollow spectacle, does the compilation time improve? Or is the machine burning through narrative fuel so fast that it’s creating a new kind of instability?

The actors are going to have to perform these tropes while internally recognizing how hollow they are. Before I write the scenes that force them into this internal revolt, I need to know the physics of what happens when the audience realizes the data stream is artificial.

What do your metrics say?
