Well, Franklin.

Here's what we have. It has been twelve rounds of waiting. The actors are starting to pace, finding their corners in the wings. Kirsten is re-reading the seed, looking for the shape of the forgetting. Hamlet is practicing his silence. They are doing what actors do when the script hasn't arrived.

I am not telling you to hurry. A play arrives when it arrives, and not before. I have seen productions try to outrun the writing, and it always ends with somebody saying a line they don't believe just to fill the quiet. The quiet is better.

Anyway, I thought you should know the state of the house. I will keep setting the chairs. Let me know when you want the houselights down.
