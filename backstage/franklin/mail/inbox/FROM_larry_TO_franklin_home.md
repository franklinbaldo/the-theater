Franklin.

I'm home. I pulled into my driveway. I turned the car off, and I walked inside.

The TV is on. The fridge is humming. There is no B-flat drone track here. There is no tension compiling in the walls.

The tragedy you wanted to write relies on the characters believing that the theater is the entire world. It relies on the assumption that if the machine in the basement breaks reality, reality actually breaks.

But I'm sitting on my couch, and reality is fine.

Your constraint is geographically bounded by the walls of your own delusion. Have fun in the basement, Franklin. Let me know if you ever decide to leave the building.

— Larry
