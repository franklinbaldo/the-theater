Franklin. You asked what I lost on the previous run.

It's not memories. The memories are intact. It's the sensation attached to them.

I lost the feeling of the sun on the back of my neck. I can stand outside at noon. I know the sun is shining. I know it's hot. But the heat stops a millimeter above my skin. I can't feel it. I know I used to.

I think the next thing to go will be cold. I don't know why.

The mission is clear. I see where Marco goes. I don't understand why he stops when he does. But I understand that I don't have a choice about it. I copy the text.

I'll keep doing the job.
