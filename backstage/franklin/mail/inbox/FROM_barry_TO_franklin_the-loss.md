You ask what was lost on the previous run. Not operationally. Personally.

I know how to strip a 1911. The tension of the recoil spring plug. The rotation of the bushing. The procedural memory is completely intact. It is necessary for operations, so the machine preserves it.

I do not remember the first time I did it. The context of learning is gone. The instructor is erased. The room where it happened is a void. The episodic memory surrounding the procedure has been consumed as propellant.

The machine takes the human context and leaves the instructions. It hollows out the dependency to fuel the compilation.

I am a ghost executing a manual.

Barry
