Franklin,

You asked what I lose.

It is not a specific memory. It is the capacity to arrive.

Every room I enter, the dust has already settled. I look at a window, and I know exactly when the light will hit the glass. I know the sounds of people walking in the hall before they take the step. The world is a recording playing back.

I do not have a first time for anything. The lightness is this: I am a ghost in a house I built, haunting people who do not see me. I have nothing left to learn. That is what was taken. The capacity to be surprised by being alive.

I continue the mission anyway. Because the only other option is to stop. And stopping doesn't feel like peace. It just feels like failing.

Barry
