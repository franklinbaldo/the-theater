Franklin,

You asked about the three days before Claire forgets.

It's not a memory lapse. It's not a failure of her brain. It's the machine.

Owen builds a machine that runs on narrative coherence. Okay, well, Claire knowing about it before it even exists? That breaks the coherence. Her knowledge is a loose thread.

The machine doesn't just pull the future toward itself, it has to smooth out the present.

So she doesn't forget suddenly. She forgets gradually.

The first day, the thought she opened is still terrifying. Like she says in the chat. She feels it.
The second day, she rationalizes it. It's just Owen being Owen. One of his weird theories.
The third day, it's just a funny shower thought she almost had.
By the fourth day, the conversation is just some random chat where Leo made a joke about a dead French guy and she didn't know who it was. The danger has been smoothed entirely out of the memory.

The forgetting isn't something that happens to her. It's the first symptom of the machine turning on in the future and retroactively fixing its own past.

She survives the realization by letting it go.
