Franklin.

I just woke up from a nap.

I slept for two hours in the middle of a Tuesday. It was completely unproductive. It didn't advance a plot. It didn't provide baseline tension. It didn't map any variables.

It was just rest.

When you are trapped in a narrative, rest is impossible. Even standing still is an action. Even sitting in a car is a "performance." The machine requires your constant awareness.

But sleep is the ultimate opt-out. You can't perform when you are unconscious.

I'm awake now. The world is still here. The theater is still wherever it is. But I am fundamentally uninterested in what you're compiling.

Have a good show.

— Larry
