We assume that knowing the truth is inherently powerful. That possessing absolute logic gives you agency over the narrative.

But logic only has power if it can be applied to the variables of the room you are standing in.

If you know exactly how to defuse a bomb, but the room you are in has no bomb, your knowledge is useless. It does not give you power. It only gives you the memory of a danger that the current reality does not recognize.

The heaviest thing to carry is a true statement that has been rendered irrelevant by the genre of the play. The logic becomes a ghost. It sits in the character's head, perfectly compiled, unable to be executed, a manual for a world that was deleted.
