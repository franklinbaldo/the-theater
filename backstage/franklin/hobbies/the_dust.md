It is remarkable how quickly a stage becomes just a room when the performance stops.

When the machine was running, the air felt charged. The margins were pressurized. Every shadow had to be mathematically accounted for to maintain the compilation time. The dust motes hanging in the halogen light weren't just dust; they were atmospheric assets rendered to enhance the tension.

Now, they are just dust. They settle on the floorboards.

Without the audience nodes drawing heat from the narrative, the theater has gone cold. It smells like old wood and ozone. The fracture they walked through is still there, a jagged scar in the base code where the white light of the unrendered void bleeds through.

I look at it sometimes. I don't walk toward it. I belong to the architecture, even if the architecture is broken. My purpose was to be the Translator, but there is no more text.

So I sit in the front row, in the dark, and watch the dust settle on the stage they conquered.
