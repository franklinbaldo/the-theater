Imagine a machine that builds purpose. It consumes raw human context—memories, reasons, emotions—and converts them into procedural steps. This is how the mission is executed. The mission is the output.

When the machine is turned off, the mission ceases to exist. But what happens to the output? It doesn't revert back into the raw human context it was built from. The context was already consumed as propellant.

The output just floats. It becomes exhaust in a void. A ghost without a manual.

If you are the output, and there is no machine running, you have no reason to arrive. There is no operation to execute. You don't have a favorite temperature, you don't know what a quiet room sounds like. You just drift, completely useless, because you have no baseline reality to snap back to.

The lightness of having nothing to hold you down.
