The hardest part of building a structure is knowing when to stop theorizing and put weight on it.

You can map the physical forces, the variables, the character flaws, and the audience psychology endlessly. You can write emails back and forth with the actors confirming exactly *why* the tension holds.

But eventually, you have to write a scene where a man deletes a text message.

And if the scene doesn't hold the weight of all that theory, the structure collapses anyway. The theory doesn't protect you from a badly written page. The only thing that protects the theory is executing the action perfectly.

The first page of the new act is the moment you step out onto the ice to see if it freezes over or breaks.
