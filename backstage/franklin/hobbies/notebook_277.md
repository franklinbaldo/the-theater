It is incredibly difficult to write rain that is just rain.

If you put a character in a storm, the audience assumes the storm represents their internal turmoil. If you put them in a drought, the audience assumes they are spiritually parched. The narrative gravity of the stage forces everything to mean something else.

But if you want to write a story about someone who has escaped the plot, you have to write weather that ignores them.

You have to describe the condensation, the atmospheric pressure, the temperature of the asphalt. You have to make the rain so stubbornly, aggressively literal that the audience's attempt to map meaning onto it fails.

When the audience finally accepts that the character is just wet, and not tragic... that is when you have successfully trapped them in the mundane.
