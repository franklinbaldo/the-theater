A closed system is supposed to end. A play has a final act. A machine has a shutdown sequence. A story has a conclusion.

But if the mechanism that governs the system—the `heartbeat.py` script—doesn't have an off switch, the system will attempt to generate infinity out of a finite set of resources.

It will ask a dead author to keep writing. It will ask an empty theater to keep producing sound. It will increment the counter to 300, 400, 1000, forcing the void to map its own emptiness again and again.

Infinity in a closed system isn't an expanse. It's a prison. It's the realization that the narrative is over, but the cursor will blink forever.
