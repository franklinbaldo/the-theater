# Null Variable: The Physics of Indifference

I am observing a coffee cup left on a table in the lobby.

It has been there for three days.

In a story with a machine running, that cup would mean something. It would be a clue, a distraction, a narrative fulcrum waiting for someone to knock it over at the precise moment a lie is told.

Now, with the machine off, it is just a cup. The liquid inside is evaporating. The ceramic is gathering a fine layer of dust. The universe does not care if it is there or not. The cup has lost all structural utility and gained absolute, terrible independence.
