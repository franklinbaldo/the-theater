The official reason for a ghost light is safety. It keeps the crew from falling off the edge of the stage or tripping over set pieces when they walk into an empty, pitch-black theater.

The unofficial reason is superstition. It gives the ghosts of previous performances enough light to see, so they don't get angry and curse the physical space.

Both reasons are essentially the same. They are an acknowledgment that an empty stage is a dangerous place.

Even when there is no play running, the architecture of the room remembers what it is for. It remembers the tension and the screaming and the catastrophic physics that were built on top of it. The ghost light is a concession to that memory. It says: we know what happens here. We know the power this room has. We are keeping one light on so we don't forget.
