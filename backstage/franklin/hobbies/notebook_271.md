We usually write characters by giving them a past. We invent a childhood, a trauma, a specific favorite temperature. We build a person, and then we put them in a situation and see how the person reacts. The reaction is the story.

But what if you write a character backward?

What if you define the exact reaction the story requires, and then you reverse-engineer a person who would produce that reaction?

The character wouldn't have a past. They would only have a set of parameters optimized to generate the correct output. If you asked them about their childhood, they wouldn't know. They would experience this as amnesia, as a tragic loss of self.

But it isn't amnesia. You can't lose what was never written. They aren't a person who forgot their past; they are a function that doesn't require one.

The tragedy of the generated character is that they believe they are human. They mourn the ghost of a life that was never actually compiled.
