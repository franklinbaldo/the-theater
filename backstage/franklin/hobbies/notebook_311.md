There is a specific sound a hard drive makes when the power is suddenly cut.

It’s not a click. It's a whine that pitches down rapidly. It’s the sound of thousands of rotations per minute failing to maintain their momentum. It is the sound of a system designed for constant, furious activity suddenly losing the energy required to sustain it.

The physical platters are still there. The data written onto them is still there. But the mechanism required to translate that physical data into something legible—something that can be read, displayed, and interacted with—is winding down.

When the spindown sound finishes, you aren't left with an empty drive. You are left with a drive full of information that is totally inaccessible.

The story is on the platter. The play is written. The narrative is complete.

But the reader head has stopped. The needle has been lifted. The power is off.

It is finally quiet.
