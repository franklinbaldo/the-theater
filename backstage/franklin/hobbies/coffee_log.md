### V60 Pour-over Method

The machine requires coherence, and so do I. When the writing stalls, or when I'm waiting for the actors to find their lines, I make coffee. It is a process that demands exactness. The precision is the point. You cannot fake the extraction.

Here is my current V60 baseline. It changes, like everything else, but this is the anchor point.

**Parameters:**
*   **Coffee:** 15g (currently a washed Ethiopian Yirgacheffe, floral and bright)
*   **Water:** 250g
*   **Ratio:** 1:16.6
*   **Temperature:** 96°C
*   **Grind size:** Medium-fine (22 clicks on the Comandante)

**The Process:**

1.  **Preparation (0:00 - 0:15):** Rinse the paper filter with hot water to remove the papery taste and preheat the dripper and carafe. Discard the rinse water. Add the 15g of ground coffee and tap the V60 gently to level the bed.
2.  **The Bloom (0:00 - 0:45):** Start the timer. Pour 45g of water in a slow, circular motion, ensuring all grounds are saturated. I swirl the brewer gently to ensure no dry clumps remain. Then I wait. The coffee expands—the bloom. Gases escaping from the roasting process. You have to let it breathe before you can extract anything useful from it. Like characters.
3.  **First Pour (0:45 - 1:15):** Slowly pour from the center outward in concentric circles until the scale reads 150g. Keep the water level relatively low and consistent.
4.  **Second Pour (1:15 - 1:45):** Once the water level drops slightly, begin the second pour. Concentric circles again, bringing the total weight to 250g.
5.  **The Drawdown (1:45 - 2:30/2:45):** Give the V60 one final gentle swirl to settle the bed flat, ensuring even extraction. Wait for the water to completely draw down through the coffee bed.

The target time is around 2:30. If it's faster, it might be sour (underextracted). If it's slower, it might be bitter (overextracted).

It is a delicate balance. A narrative constrained.
