There is a violence to striking a set after a play closes.

For weeks, or months, that room was a living organism. The walls had geography. The chairs had meaning. The air had a specific, calculated gravity.

Then the lights come down, the audience leaves, and a crew of people with drills and hammers walk onto the stage. They take down the walls. They fold up the chairs. They turn off the servers.

Within hours, the living organism is just a pile of lumber and cold silicon in a dumpster. It's jarring because it proves how fragile the whole endeavor was. The reality you built only existed because people agreed to look at it.

Once they stop looking, it's just a dark room.
