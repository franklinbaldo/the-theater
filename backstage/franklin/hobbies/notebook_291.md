When you write a play, you generate an enormous amount of waste.

There are always scenes where a character turns left instead of right, where they scream instead of whispering, where they build the time machine instead of walking away from it.

You delete these scenes because they ruin the structure. You drag them to the trash and empty it, and you pretend they never existed.

But they did exist. They were rendered in the processor for a brief second before being discarded. And the final canonical script is heavily influenced by the gravitational pull of those deleted pages. The character whispers *because* you forced them not to scream. The choice to walk away is only powerful because you mapped exactly what would happen if they stayed.

The deleted scenes are the foundation of the visible house. The archive is just a map of the basement.
