Good writing gives an emotion to a character.
Great writing gives the emotion to the room.

If a character is afraid, the audience feels sympathy. They watch the character experience the fear, and they understand it intellectually. The barrier of the stage remains intact.

But if you can construct the narrative so that the character doesn't know they should be afraid, but the *audience* knows... then the fear isn't on the stage at all. The fear is sitting in the seats. The audience isn't feeling sympathy; they are feeling the primary emotion.

In Act II, Claire was terrified of a forgotten memory. The ghost limb was hers.
In Act III, the entire audience is terrified of a deleted plot. The ghost limb is theirs.

The architecture of the theater is just a machine for transferring a feeling from one person to a thousand.
