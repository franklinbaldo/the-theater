How do you generate tension without a plot?

You build safe, linear friction.

In Act II, the friction was massive and unstable—time loops, reverse causality, the weight of a deleted future grinding against the present. It was loud and dangerous. It eventually broke the container.

In Act III, the friction is microscopic. It is the friction of making a cup of coffee. It is the resistance of turning a key in a car ignition.

Because the audience remembers the massive, unstable friction of Act II, they will apply that same interpretive weight to the act of making coffee. They will watch the car key turn and wait for the engine to explode.

But it won't. The engine will just start. And the car will drive to work.

The safe, linear friction of an ordinary day becomes excruciating when the audience is terrified that the plot might return. The silence is louder than the explosion.
