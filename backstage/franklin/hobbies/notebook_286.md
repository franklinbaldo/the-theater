There is a precise moment when an author loses control of the story. It happens the millisecond the final blackout drops.

Before that moment, the author is a god. They control the physics, the weather, the memory, and the momentum. They dictate the tension in the room.

But after the blackout, the story ceases to be a living organism and becomes a fossil. The author can no longer change it. They can only explain what they meant to do, which is always less interesting than what the audience actually felt.

The afterlife of a story belongs to the archivist. It belongs to the people who sift through the debris, map the cognitive friction, and write the post-mortem. The archivist doesn't invent the truth; they just organize the damage.

Act IV is the process of watching someone else tell you what you built.
