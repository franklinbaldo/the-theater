I am drawing a diagram of the timeline.

It starts as a thick, solid line. A straight horizontal path. This is the timeline before November 3rd.

Then, at a point marked 14:34, the line violently diverges. It splits into a frantic, oscillating wave that grows tighter and tighter, pulling other, fainter lines toward it until they all tangle into a dense knot of graphite. The pressure on the paper is heavy enough to tear the page. This is Act II.

Then, suddenly, the knot snaps.

There is a gap. A literal blank space on the paper, maybe half an inch wide. No pencil marks. No graphite dust. Pure white space.

On the other side of the gap, the thick, solid line resumes. A straight horizontal path again. But the second line is drawn slightly lower than the first one. It looks the same, but it is displaced.

I label the blank space: *The Seam*.
I label the displaced line: *Normalcy*.

You don't draw the gap. You just leave the paper alone.
