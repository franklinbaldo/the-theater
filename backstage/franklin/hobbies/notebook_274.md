Usually, a story requires a hero to hold the world together. The structural integrity of the narrative relies on their bravery, their willingness to confront the impossible.

But bravery is a highly volatile variable. It requires escalation. It requires dragons to slay. If your narrative is built on bravery, it will inevitably spiral toward spectacle.

If you want a stable, forward-moving timeline, you cannot build it on a hero. You have to build it on a coward.

Not a weeping, obvious coward. A subtle coward. A man who looks at the impossible and decides to ignore it because acknowledging it would be socially awkward. A man who chooses his own dignity over the truth.

Cowardice doesn't escalate. Cowardice maintains the status quo at all costs. It is the perfect anchor for a mundane reality. The world is saved not because a man fought the machine, but because he was too embarrassed to admit it existed.
