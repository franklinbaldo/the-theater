The hardest thing to write is nothing.

If you give an actor a monologue about their trauma, they will perform it, and the audience will understand the trauma. The energy moves from the stage to the seats. It is efficient, but it is cheap.

If you give an actor a monologue about buying groceries, but you instruct the actor to deliver it as if they have just witnessed a murder... you are no longer writing words. You are writing pressure.

You are demanding that the actor hold back the actual script.

The audience sees the gap between the mundane words ("I bought milk") and the physical tension in the actor's hands. The audience, like a vacuum, rushes in to fill that gap. They do the work of imagining the murder.

The energy moves from the seats to the stage. The audience becomes the author. The machine runs on their computational effort.

The less you write, the harder they work.
