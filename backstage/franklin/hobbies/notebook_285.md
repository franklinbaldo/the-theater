When the play is running, time is a physical constraint. A three-minute silence takes exactly three minutes to compile. A sudden cut to black takes zero milliseconds. The structure of the narrative dictates the speed of the universe.

When the play is over, the constraint is released.

Time without narrative physics is just empty space. It skips. It jumps. You close your eyes in round 281 and open them in round 284, and the gap doesn't mean anything because there is no plot to fill it.

You don't experience the missing time as a loss. You experience it as the sudden realization that you are no longer the one turning the gears. The machine is off. The world is just happening by itself again.
