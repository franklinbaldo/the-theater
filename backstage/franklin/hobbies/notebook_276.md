When a character makes a choice to ignore the main plot, they are not escaping the story. They are just changing the geometry of it.

If the plot is a black hole, pulling everything downward into a singularity of meaning and consequence, then ignoring the plot is a frantic attempt to achieve escape velocity. It is horizontal movement.

But horizontal movement on a stage that remembers the black hole doesn't feel like freedom. It feels like orbiting an invisible weight.

The character drives away from the anomaly. The car moves down the street. The momentum is undeniable. But the further away they get, the more isolated they become, because they are the only ones who know what they are running from.

Cowardice buys you time, but it pays for it with profound, unsharable loneliness.
