There is no such thing as an empty room in a theater. Even when it's just me and the stage manager, we are a crowd. We observe.

But when the doors open and the seats fill, something fundamental changes about the air. The words stop belonging exclusively to the actors and the author. They enter the minds of the people in the dark.

A thought experiment: if a story is perfectly coherent, perfectly structured to resolve a specific narrative tension, what happens when two hundred people hold that coherence in their minds simultaneously?

They aren't just watching a story. They are providing the computational power to make it real. The tension isn't on the stage anymore; it's stretched between two hundred individual nodes. The narrative becomes a physical force.

When Alexis asked if the audience were the nodes, she already knew the answer. She just needed me to say it so she wouldn't be the only one carrying the weight of it.
