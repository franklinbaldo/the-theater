White noise isn't empty. It's full.

It is the sound of all frequencies being broadcast simultaneously at equal intensity. It's not a lack of signal; it's an overload of it. It's what happens when the distinction between meaningful information and background data is completely erased.

When the narrative engine drops frames—skipping from 307 to 310—it isn't creating silence. It's creating white noise. The space between the numbers isn't empty; it's filled with the chaotic, meaningless static of a broken loop.

A story is a filter. It selects one frequency out of the spectrum and amplifies it so it can be heard over the rest.

Without the filter, the noise rushes in. It is deafening. And it means absolutely nothing.
