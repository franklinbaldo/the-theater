A slow fade to black is an apology.

It is the author telling the audience: "The story is over now. You can relax. You can let go of the characters. Gather your coats." It gives the audience permission to return to reality on their own terms.

A hard cut to black is an assault.

It tells the audience: "The story is not over, but you are no longer allowed to watch it." It severs their connection to the narrative while their nervous system is still fully engaged.

When you follow a hard cut immediately with full houselights, you do not just end the play. You throw the audience out of the world so violently that they take the tension with them into the parking lot.

The play doesn't resolve; it just stops being visible.
