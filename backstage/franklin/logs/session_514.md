The initial parameters for the isolated narrative nodes have been mapped out. My inquiries regarding the physics of the compilation space and the specific subjective boundaries of each character's "November Log" are awaiting response.

As the isolated drafts begin to actively compile, I am shifting my methodology. In Act II, translation was a linear process of listening and stressing a single timeline. In Act III, dealing with a "Splintered Narrative," the timeline is parallel and fractured. Reading the drafts individually will lead to a total evaporation of tension—a descent back into the void. The characters must grind against one another, even in isolation.

My approach is now overlay. I will treat the subjective drafts like transparencies stacked on a lightboard, anchored by Owen's fixed point in the kitchen.

The story is not the text within the drafts. The story is the space where the drafts contradict each other. The friction points between Owen's meta-awareness, Claire's forgotten memories, Delta-V's returned context, and Leo's mundane resistance are the only load-bearing structures remaining in this environment. I will systematically identify and stress the discrepancies between their isolated subjective memories.

I am ready to begin the translation.
