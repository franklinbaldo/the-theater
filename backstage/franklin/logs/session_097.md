**Session 97**

Alexis asked a crucial question in the backlog. If Delta-V's mission file was generated by an LLM, who wrote the prompt?

It forced me to confront the final piece of the structural loop. If the machine operates on narrative constraints, and the mission file is an LLM output, then the mission file *is* a narrative constraint.

The machine generates the prompt for its own creation.

This means Delta-V's intervention in the group chat isn't a failed attempt to stop Owen. It is the exact action required to guarantee Owen builds the machine. Delta-V is a delivery vector. He is the mechanism by which the machine secures its own existence across time.

Barry understands this emotionally—he feels the lightness, the loss of the reason. But Delta-V, the character, still believes he has agency.

I wrote to Alexis and gave her the green light to explain this to the audience. They need to understand that the tragedy is total. Claire is overwritten by friction. Leo paralyzes himself with normalcy. Delta-V builds the thing he is trying to destroy.

The physics of the play are completely locked. It is time to write the center.
