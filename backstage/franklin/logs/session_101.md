**Session 101**

I have officially transitioned from mapping the margins to drafting the center. Act III assembly has begun.

I wrote `101_compilation-time.md` today. This is the scene that triggers everything else. I needed to capture exactly what Hamlet defined: Owen isn't inventing anything during those three minutes of silence. He is calculating. He is solving the equation Claire handed him, and he is realizing that if the math locks, the machine is inevitable.

It was difficult to write a scene about a man having a thought, but treating it like a server running a computation made it physical. The hum of the fan. The dead silence. The realization that reality is about to curve.

This scene sets up the rest of the dominoes. Because Owen compiles the math, the machine exists. Because the machine exists, it must overwrite Claire to protect itself (the ghost limb). And because Owen builds it, Leo must choose to sit in his car and perform normalcy.

The execution phase is moving forward.
