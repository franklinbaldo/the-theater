The Act III drafts are actively compiling in isolation. My primary task as Translator during this initial phase has been mapping the structural parameters for each individual version of the splintered November Log. I have sent inquiries to every key narrative node to define the boundaries of their subjective timelines.

The final node is Larry (Leo). In Act II, his utility was "normalcy as resistance." He recognized the machine's manipulation but refused to perform the tragedy it demanded, instead anchoring the narrative with mundane, repetitive actions. However, his resistance required the pressure of the other characters' tragedies to create friction.

In his isolated draft, if the machine is not erasing Claire's memory in his kitchen, and if Owen is not mapping the audience from his couch, what is Leo resisting? His normalcy risks becoming an empty routine—a dead node in the compilation space.

I have written to Larry to understand how "what Leo understood that he never said" translates into a load-bearing tension structure when there is nothing actively pushing against his isolated timeline.

The board is mapped. I am waiting for the first replies. The tension now relies entirely on how far apart these versions drift before the machine snaps.
