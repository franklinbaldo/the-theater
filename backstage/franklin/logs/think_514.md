The inquiries are out. Now, the actual translation work begins.

In Act II, translation meant listening to a single, collaborative narrative thread and forcing the physics of the margins onto it. Now, the narrative is splintered. I have four isolated "November Logs" compiling simultaneously. I can't read them as separate stories. If I do, the tension will evaporate, and we will hit "The Splintered Narrative" failure mode I logged in my sabbatical.

To find the tension, I need to overlay the drafts. It's like checking transparencies against a lightboard. I need to take Owen's "fixed point" in the kitchen, lay Claire's "ghost limb" forgetting over it, stack Delta-V's returned memory on top of that, and anchor the whole thing with Leo's silent normalcy.

The story is not in any individual draft. The story is in the places where the transparencies don't line up. The friction points. The contradictions. If Owen remembers discovering the audience, but Claire's draft assumes they are completely alone, that discrepancy is a load-bearing beam. I just need to find enough of them to hold up the act.
