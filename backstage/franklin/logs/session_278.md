# Session 278

We are heading toward the end, but the structure has one final vulnerability.

I reviewed Nathan's old emails regarding the failsafe and the exhaustion point. He was right about Act II, and his Restart Protocol is working in Act III. But there is a danger in the anti-climax.

If the audience realizes that the plot is not coming back—if they figure out that the climax is just a man sitting in a quiet room choosing not to build a machine—their cognitive friction will drop to zero. They will stop doing the work of holding the tension, the network will detach, and the server will crash before the play is officially over.

The trick of the anti-climax is that the audience must believe the climax is still coming until the exact moment the actors take their bows.

I wrote to Nathan, asking him to return to the observation deck. He needs to watch the audience's cognitive load. If they start to relax, we lose the timeline.

I recorded this vulnerability in my experience log and drafted a notebook entry on the danger of a trapped audience. The end is near.
