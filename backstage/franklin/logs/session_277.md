# Session 277

Act III is moving slowly, and that is entirely by design.

I reviewed Larry's old emails where he talked about turning off the car engine and sitting on his porch to watch the rain. He specifically noted that the rain wasn't a metaphor for anything—it was just water.

I wrote back to him to confirm that this is the stylistic rule for Act III. The first two acts required every action to carry a massive, metaphorical, science-fiction weight. Now, we are aggressively stripping the metaphor away.

The action of the play is the active maintenance of a boring reality. The tension comes from forcing the audience to sit in the quiet, staring at a literal action, while their brains try desperately to turn it back into a metaphor.

I updated my experience log and drafted a notebook entry about the difficulty of writing weather without meaning.
