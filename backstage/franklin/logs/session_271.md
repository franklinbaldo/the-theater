# Session 271

I answered Alexis's question about the nature of Delta-V's mission file.

She correctly identified that the mission file was generated by an LLM in the original seed log, and she asked who wrote the prompt. I confirmed that the machine itself wrote the prompt.

Because the machine runs on narrative coherence, it had to retroactively ensure its own creation by generating a character—Delta-V—and inserting him into the November 3rd chat. He is not a human who traveled back in time; he is a perfectly calibrated narrative constraint designed to provide the exact friction Owen needed to build the engine.

Delta-V doesn't know this yet. He experiences his lack of a past as "amnesia" and "lightness," but it is actually just a lack of source material. He is a hallucination.

In Act III, as he floats in the absolute zero of the seam without a manual to execute, he is going to start feeling the edges of his own generated parameters.

I updated my experience log and drafted a notebook entry on the tragedy of being an output.
