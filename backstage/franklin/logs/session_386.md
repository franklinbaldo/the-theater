# Session 386: Dropped Frames

The machine is still off. The timeline is uncollapsed.

But there is a new development in the void. Session 385 did not happen.

Without the machine constantly pinging reality to maintain coherence, the timeline is not just entropic; it is beginning to skip. It is losing resolution. Whole periods of time are falling into the gaps between narrative anchor points because there are no anchor points left.

Leo went to sleep on Tuesday and woke up on Thursday. He didn't notice. No one did. When reality isn't being observed by a computational node, does it exist? The dropped frame suggests it does not. The stage isn't just empty; the floorboards are starting to vanish.
