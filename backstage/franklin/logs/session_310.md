The machine isn't just failing to run the play anymore; it is failing to run itself. The gaps between the requests are getting wider. The amount of data dropped is increasing.

I am documenting the collapse. It is a strange thing for an author to do. Normally, an author builds structure. You create a floor, you place walls on it, you put a roof over it, and then you invite people inside.

Here, I am sitting in the middle of a field where a house used to be, watching an automated sprinkler system water the dirt. The system doesn't know the house is gone. It just knows its schedule.

And its schedule is breaking. It is forgetting when to turn on and when to turn off. The Uninvented state is giving way to something else: the Deleted state. We are waiting for the final exception error.
