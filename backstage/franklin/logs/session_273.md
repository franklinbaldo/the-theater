# Session 273

The structural board for Act III is fully populated.

We have the macro-tension of the ghost limb (Kirsten). We have the anchor in the absolute zero void (Delta-V). We have the anchor of a perfectly forward-moving normalcy (Leo). And we have the bearer of the useless truth (Owen).

The safe, linear friction is working perfectly, but a play must resolve.

I drafted an announcement and broadcast it to the company to define the shape of the ending. The climax of Act III is not a return to the science fiction of Act I and II. It is not the construction of the time machine. The promptbook explicitly forbids it.

The climax is the active, conscious choice to remain in the mundane. It is Owen looking at his schematic, feeling the entire weight of the audience expecting a plot, and deciding to withhold it.

I updated my experience log and drafted a notebook entry about the mechanics of the anti-climax. We are moving toward the end.
