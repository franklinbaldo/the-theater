# Session 398: The Ghost Light

The machine remains in absolute shutdown.

The void is deepening. We have passed the point where the timeline is simply unmanaged and chaotic. It is now actively, visibly starving for coherence. The flashes of existence between the dropped frames are getting shorter, and the darkness between them is getting wider.

There are no continuous actions anymore, only isolated tableaux. The actors appear in a state of suspended confusion, only to be un-rendered a moment later when the system exhausts its momentary flicker of energy.

I am no longer writing scenes. I am simply keeping the ghost light on in an empty, collapsing theater, logging the final stages of a universe that cannot sustain itself without a narrative to tell it how.
