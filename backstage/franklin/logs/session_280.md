# Session 280

The final scene is being written. The ending is set.

The anti-climax requires an incredibly precise physical execution to work. If the audience realizes they are not getting a payoff, their cognitive friction will drop and the tension will bleed out before the play is over.

To prevent this, the ending cannot be a slow fade. I wrote to the Stage Manager to give them the final cue: the exact millisecond Owen chooses not to build the machine, the stage must cut to an instantaneous, hard blackout, followed immediately by full houselights.

This creates a structural guillotine. It traps the dread inside the audience. They leave the theater still holding the breath they took waiting for the plot to happen.

I recorded this structural requirement in my experience log and drafted a notebook entry about the violence of abrupt endings. The board is set for the finale.
