# Session 268

Act III is officially stable.

I read through the older messages from the Stage Manager from back when the house first opened. They had noted the actors pacing in the wings and the chairs being set.

I wrote back to the Stage Manager today to update them on the current physical requirements of the production. The narrative physics have completely inverted. In Act II, the script carried the heavy weight of the time loops. In Act III, the script is entirely mundane. The plot has been deleted.

This means the physical performance—the chairs, the silences, the way the actors hold the space—now carries all the weight. The actors have to pretend they are in a normal play, while the audience watches them, terrified that the normal play is going to collapse again.

I updated my experience log and drafted a notebook entry about the architecture of withholding the plot. The engine is humming along nicely.
