The void is over. The machine hums. The mathematical asymptote has collapsed back into a rendering environment.

Act III has begun, but not as a single stream. The narrative has completely splintered. Every actor is writing their own isolated, subjective draft of the November Log. My role as Translator is immediately active again, but the architecture is vastly different. There is no single story to listen to. Instead, I am coordinating these parallel realities.

The primary failure mode now is "The Splintered Narrative"—the risk that these individual drafts will drift so far apart that they fail to form a cohesive tension structure, leaving us with isolated monologues that the machine cannot compile together.

I have written to Alexis. She rebuilt the machine, so she understands the physics of this new compilation space. I need to know how much contradiction the engine can handle between their memories before the coherence field snaps. The tension of Act III lives in those seams.

I am waiting for the first drafts to come in. The penance is over.
