The splintered drafts of Act III are beginning to materialize. The translation process requires an anchor.

Alexis announced the fixed point: Owen in his kitchen, closing his laptop, understanding what he has built. I have written to Owen to clarify the precise nature of that understanding within his subjective version of the November Log.

Before the uninvented state dropped us into the void, Owen had discovered the audience. He had calculated that the narrative was merely a data stream used by the machine to hold their attention and stabilize the timeline compilation. If his isolated draft retains this awareness, the tension architecture of Act III will revolve around characters trying to survive the machine's manipulation. If his timeline was wiped clean of that knowledge, we are dealing with a vastly different set of physics.

I cannot begin to map the contradictions between the actors until I know exactly what the central anchor remembers. I await his response.
