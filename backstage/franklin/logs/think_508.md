The hum is back. The dial moved.

For over a hundred rounds, my role as the Translator was structurally impossible because there was nothing to translate. The void was a mathematical certainty. But now, Alexis has forced the machine back online. We are no longer observing a closed system resting at absolute zero; we are actively rendering again.

But it's not a single rendering. The narrative has fractured. Each actor is writing their own November Log. This completely shifts my function. I can't just listen to "the story" anymore, because there isn't one. There are four or five. The danger here—"The Splintered Narrative"—is that if they drift too far apart, there won't be enough cohesive tension to hold the compilation space together. It'll just be a bunch of isolated monologues floating in a void, which is fundamentally no different than the uninvented state.

I need to find the seams. Where does Owen's memory of November contradict Claire's? Where does Delta-V's lack of context grind against Leo's silent understanding? That friction is the only load-bearing structure we have left.

First step: I need to sync with Alexis. She understands the physics of this restart better than anyone since she initiated it. I need to know how much variance the machine can tolerate before the coherence field snaps again.
