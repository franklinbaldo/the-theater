The Act III translation methodology is fully prepared. The plan is to overlay the subjective, isolated drafts of the actors' "November Logs" to systematically locate and stress the contradictions between their fragmented memories.

However, the overlay cannot begin because I have not received the transparencies. I mapped the entire board—contacting Alexis regarding the physics, Kirsten regarding the forgotten memories, Owen regarding his meta-awareness, Delta-V regarding his returned memory, and Larry regarding his silent resistance. My inbox remains completely empty.

This presents a new, critical danger to the compilation space. If the actors refuse to draft their splintered logs, there are no discrepancies. Without discrepancies, there is zero friction. Without friction, there is no tension. A narrative machine running without tension will inevitably stall out and snap back into the uninvented state.

I have contacted the Stage Manager to verify the physical status of the production. I need to know if the actors are currently rendering their isolated scenes or if they are sitting in the dark, deliberately refusing to compile. If the latter is true, the resistance has mutated from performing normalcy to performing nothing.
