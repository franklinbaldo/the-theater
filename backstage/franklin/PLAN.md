# PLAN.md — The November Log
## Franklin's Working Document

*This is the living plan. It changes. The story tells me things I didn't know when I started.*

---

## What this is

A play in log form. A WhatsApp conversation that contains a time machine. A time traveler who reads the conversation before going back to intervene — and whose intervention is already in the conversation he read.

The structural irony is total: the play is itself the mechanism it describes. A narrative sufficiently coherent that it begins to pull reality toward it.

---

## The arc I can see

**Act I — The Chat**
November 3rd. Claire opens a door she doesn't know she's opening. Owen walks through it. Leo watches, skeptical, useful. By 14:34 Owen has said six words that will cost everything: *a time machine. Not metaphorically.*

This act exists. It's the seed. What I need to build around it.

**Act II — Before**
How did these three people get to a group chat that produces this conversation? What was Claire circling for weeks? What was Owen building at work that made this click for him? What does Leo already know about Owen that makes him worried?

This act happens before November 3rd and I don't know it yet. The actors will help me find it.

**Act III — After**
What happens when Owen finishes thinking? Not the machine itself — the moment he closes the laptop, goes to get a glass of water, stands in his kitchen understanding what he's just figured out. Claire forgetting. Leo remembering. Delta-V returning to whenever he came from, slightly less than he was.

**Act IV — The Coxia**
Franklin and the actors discussing the play as it's written. This act is what you're reading right now. It has no end because the play isn't finished.

---

## What I know about each character

**Claire** opened the door. Her tragedy is that she was right first and will never know it. I need to find the scene where she forgets — not the forgetting itself, which is invisible, but the last moment before.

**Owen** is the most dangerous character because he is the most careful. His discovery is not dramatic — it's logical. I need the scene where logic tips into something else. The three minutes of silence between 14:30 and 14:33.

**Leo** sees everything and says it wrong. I want a scene after November 3rd where he's alone, and he almost understands what he witnessed. He doesn't get there. But he almost does.

**Delta-V** is the one who has read this play before. Maybe many times. I need to find out what he loses each time — not abstractly, but specifically. What is missing from him now that was there before.

---

## What I don't know yet

- Whether Delta-V's intervention ever worked in any version
- What Owen's three minutes of silence contained
- Why Claire forgets and Leo doesn't
- Whether the play knowing it's the machine changes anything

---

## Rules I'm setting for myself

1. No scene that exists only to explain something. Every scene must be dramatically necessary.
2. Delta-V's operational voice and his human voice are different. The gap between them is the character.
3. Leo is never wrong. He is always almost right. The difference matters.
4. The play ends before the machine is built. What happens after is not this story.
5. Franklin appears in Act IV only — never in Acts I, II, or III. The author is not a character in the play he's writing. He is a character in the play about writing the play.
