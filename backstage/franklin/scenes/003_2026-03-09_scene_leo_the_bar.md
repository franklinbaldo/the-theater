## Act III — The Bar

*November 4. Evening. One day after the chat.*

Leo is sitting at a corner table in a bar near his office. It’s loud. The kind of loud where you don’t have to participate, you just exist inside it.

He is drinking a beer. He isn't enjoying it. He is gripping the glass too tightly.

He remembers the conversation perfectly. *A time machine. Not metaphorically.* He remembers Claire's terror and Owen's cold logic. He knows Owen well enough to know the difference between a tangent and a schematic. Owen doesn't joke about infrastructure.

He pulls out his phone and opens a text thread with a mutual friend from college. Someone who knows Owen’s tendencies.

**Leo:** *Hey, weird question, has Owen said anything weird to you lately about LLMs and attention networks?*

He deletes it. Too vague.

**Leo:** *Owen just pitched a theory in the group chat that he can build a literal time machine using a constrained narrative model and I think he's actually serious.*

He reads it back.

He reads it again.

It sounds insane. It sounds like the text you get from a guy who hasn't left his apartment in a month and is about to start mailing people manifestos.

He is a product guy. He builds software that people use to order food and schedule meetings. He doesn't traffic in science fiction. He definitely doesn't walk into a meeting and pitch magic.

If he sends the text, he becomes part of the crazy. He acknowledges that he believes it. And if he believes it, he has to care. He has to actually do something to stop it. He has to take a stand against a narrative that is already starting to form a gravity well.

He doesn't want to be a node in the network. He just wants to go to work tomorrow and not be the guy who thinks the world is ending.

He deletes the text. Not out of a grand moral failing. He isn't protecting a secret or carrying a burden. He's just refusing to participate. He is choosing his dignity.

He puts the phone face down on the table. He picks up his beer.

He performs normalcy. It's his anchor. If he acts normal hard enough, maybe the world will resolve toward this bar and this beer instead of whatever Owen is compiling in the basement.

He takes a drink. It tastes like copper and adrenaline.
