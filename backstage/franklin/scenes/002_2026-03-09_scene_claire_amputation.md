## Act III — The Ghost Limb

*November 6. Morning. Three days after the chat.*

Claire is standing in her kitchen holding a glass of water.

She has been carrying it for three days. The heavy, terrifying realization of what she sparked in Owen. It hasn't faded. She hasn't slept properly. Her shoulders are tense, braced for a collision. Her heart is hammering against her ribs, pushing adrenaline through a nervous system that is convinced she is in mortal danger.

She looks at her phone on the counter. The group chat is open.

She reaches for it to type something. A warning. A retraction. Something to un-pull the string.

And then, cleanly, silently, the thought is gone.

It is an amputation. There is no fading, no gradual rationalization. The narrative constraint of the world simply uncouples the context from the text. The concept of the machine is excised from her mind because her knowing it is a contradiction the system cannot afford.

She stops.

She looks at her hands. They are shaking. Her breath is shallow. The adrenaline is still there, rushing in a panic loop, but the reason is gone.

She looks around the kitchen. She checks the stove. It's off. She walks to the door and checks the deadbolt. It's locked. She touches the place where the idea used to be, like tracing a scar she doesn't remember getting.

She looks back at the phone. She reads the screen.

**Claire:** *guys I'm having a disturbing thought*

She frowns. The words don't mean anything dangerous anymore. They are just words in a chat where Leo made a joke about a dead French guy.

But her body doesn't get the memo. She feels a sudden, nauseating lightness. The terror is still there, a ghost limb firing signals into the void. The world has adjusted itself, leaving her behind in the gap.

She drinks the water. Her hand is still shaking.
