[SCENE START]

**OWEN**
[He sits at a desk that is entirely clear except for a laptop. He is staring at a WhatsApp web client. The timestamp on the last message from CLAIRE reads 14:30. He does not move. He barely blinks.]

She doesn't know what she just said.

[He leans back slightly. His hands remain flat on the desk, parallel to the keyboard. He is not thinking about building something. He is running a calculation.]

If a model produces a completely coherent narrative... and that narrative is deployed at the structural nodes of the attention network...

[He closes his eyes. The room is dead silent. We hear the faint hum of the laptop fan. It sounds like a server room.]

The narrative becomes the heaviest object in the informational space. Reality curves toward it.

[He opens his eyes. He looks at the cursor blinking in the reply box.]

It's a physics problem. She handed me an equation, and I'm solving for zero. And the answer is zero. There is no missing variable. The machine doesn't have to be built out of metal. It just has to be coherent.

[The clock on the screen shifts to 14:33. He slowly brings his hands to the keyboard. There is no triumph in his posture. There is only the grim, careful certainty of a man who just checked the math and found no errors.]

I can't unsee the architecture. The math locked.

[He begins to type: *a time machine. Not metaphorically.*]

[SCENE END]
