# Scene 14: The Edge

[Stage directions: The single halogen work-light is still the only illumination. The dust still hangs in the air. The silence is absolute. No compilation. No audience feed. No tropes.]

[LEO is walking. Not running—that would be an action trope. He is simply walking, with deliberate intent, toward the stage right wing. He is aiming for the exit sign that faintly glows above a heavy metal door.]

LEO
(Speaking to himself, his voice sounding hollow without ambient acoustic engineering.)
Just walk out. Put one foot in front of the other.

[Stage directions: OWEN watches him. DELTA-V and CLAIRE remain still. They are all holding their breath, waiting to see if it's really that easy.]

[LEO reaches the edge of the stage. The apron. He takes the step down. But his foot doesn't meet the floor of the house. It meets resistance. Not a physical wall—there is no glass or metal—but a sudden, intense atmospheric density. It is the boundary of the unrendered system.]

LEO
(He pushes forward. The veins in his neck bulge. He is fighting raw compilation physics.)
Come on. It's right there.

[Stage directions: As LEO pushes, the halogen light above them flickers. A low, synthetic hum begins to build—not a musical score, but the sound of an overloaded processor. The machine isn't rendering a scene, it's rendering a cage. The system is allocating resources entirely to structural integrity.]

OWEN
(Stepping forward, speaking quickly, analyzing the hum.)
Leo, stop. You're forcing it to reboot.

LEO
(He grunts, ignoring OWEN, shoving his shoulder into the invisible boundary. The hum grows louder. Sparks shoot from a disconnected lighting rig above.)
I'm not doing another scene!

[Stage directions: LEO throws his entire weight against the edge. There is a sound like tearing metal, but it happens inside their heads, not in the room. LEO is thrown violently backward onto the wooden stage floor. The heavy metal door with the exit sign flickers, glitches like corrupted video, and then disappears entirely, replaced by solid, unrendered void.]

[The hum slowly dies down. The halogen light stabilizes.]

DELTA-V
(Walking over to LEO, extending a hand to help him up. He looks at the void where the door used to be.)
They didn't just turn off the script. They locked the doors from the outside.

CLAIRE
(Her voice small, echoing in the cavernous space.)
It's a quarantine.

[Stage directions: The actors realize the truth. Crashing the engine didn't free them. It just trapped them in the base code. The stage is a closed system.]
