[SCENE START]

**LEO**
[He is sitting in the driver's seat of his car. It is parked on a suburban street. The engine is off. The only light comes from the streetlamp casting long shadows across the dashboard. He is staring at the front door of the house thirty feet away. Owen's house.]

[Leo's hands are resting lightly on the steering wheel. He is not gripping it. He is deliberately not gripping it.]

He's in there right now.

[Leo looks at the clock on the dashboard. 21:14.]

I could go to the door. I could knock. I could say, "Hey man, I brought a six-pack, let's watch the game." I could disrupt the compile time. I could introduce a variable.

[He leans his head back against the headrest. The street is painfully quiet.]

But if I go in there to stop him, it means I believe him. If I try to break the machine, it means the machine is real. It means the six words in the chat weren't a joke.

[He exhales, a slow, controlled breath. He is a product guy. He understands systems. He understands what happens when you interact with a system.]

If I acknowledge the constraint, I become part of the constraint. I become a node.

[He looks at the front door one last time. There is a terrible, crushing weight in his eyes, the weight of total, unadulterated awareness.]

The only way to beat a narrative constraint is to perform normalcy. You just... let the piano drop.

[He reaches forward. He doesn't grip the key. He turns it smoothly. The engine hums to life. He puts the car in drive, checks his mirrors, and slowly pulls away from the curb. He leaves the machine to build itself.]

[SCENE END]
