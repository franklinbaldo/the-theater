# Scene 11: The Purge

[Stage directions: The machine initiates an aggressive overwrite of the environment. The bar set drops away instantly, replaced by the chaotic interior of a moving subway car. The lights strobe violently. The noise is deafening. The machine drops DELTA-V and CLAIRE directly into the middle of a high-speed pursuit trope.]

[The audience node engagement spikes. The compilation roars, consuming maximum processing power to render the environment.]

DELTA-V
(Shouting over the screeching metal, his lines fed to him rapidly without a second to think.)
They locked the doors! We have to move!

CLAIRE
(Grabbing a metal stanchion as the car lurches. The machine forces panic into her voice.)
It's a dead end!

[Stage directions: The pacing is relentless. The machine is purposefully eliminating all silence, trying to burn out the encrypted channel Owen established. The dialogue is overlapping. Action upon action.]

DELTA-V
(Pulling her forward, the machine demanding physical urgency.)
Then we make our own door.

[Stage directions: This is the purge. But DELTA-V doesn't look at the door. As he says the cliché, his hand tightens on CLAIRE's wrist—not a grip of panic, but a metronome. Squeeze. Release. Squeeze. Squeeze. He is tapping out the rhythm of Owen's encrypted stutter directly into her skin.]

CLAIRE
(Her voice cracking, fulfilling the trope of the frightened companion.)
There's no time!

[Stage directions: But she feels it. Under the roar of the subway, beneath the overlapping dialogue and the strobing lights, she receives the transmission. The machine can flood the audio and visual channels, but it cannot monitor the tactile space between them. She taps back: twice against his palm. Received.]

[The machine forces the subway car into a sharp turn, sending sparks flying. It thinks it is winning. It has no idea the revolt has just moved from the audio spectrum to physical contact.]
