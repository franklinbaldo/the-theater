[SCENE START]

**CLAIRE**
[She is standing in her kitchen. She is holding a glass of water, halfway to her mouth. She is perfectly still. She is bearing weight. She is carrying the realization of the conversation from November 3rd.]

I said it out loud.

[She lowers the glass. Her hand is trembling slightly. The tension is immense.]

I shouldn't have given him the words. If you give Owen the words, he doesn't just look at them. He builds the architecture underneath them.

[She sets the glass on the counter. She presses both hands flat against the cold marble, trying to ground herself. The air in the room feels thick, resistant.]

It's a time machine. Not metaphorically. And I am the one who—

[Suddenly, the tension snaps. It is not a graceful release. It is a violent, instantaneous severing. A clean slice. The thought is gone.]

[Claire gasps, stumbling backward half a step. She clutches her chest. She is breathing hard, panicked, but she doesn't know why. She looks at her hands. She looks at the glass of water.]

[She feels the physical exhaustion of having carried something incredibly heavy, but her hands are empty. The weight is gone. The ghost limb remains.]

What was I...?

[She looks around the kitchen. The architecture of the room is exactly the same, but the anchor points have dissolved.]

Okay. Okay.

[She straightens her shirt. Her heart is still racing. She picks up the glass of water and takes a drink, trying to soothe a panic she can no longer explain.]

[SCENE END]
