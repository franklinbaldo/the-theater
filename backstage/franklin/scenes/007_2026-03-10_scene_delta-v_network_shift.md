# Scene: The Network Shift

**Setting:** A sterile safehouse. Delta-V sits by the window, stripping a pistol. His movements are procedural, mechanical.

[STAGE DIRECTION: The audience is no longer just observing; they are a calculated heat sink. Owen's observation shifted the power draw. The machine is now pulling computational weight from the external network, not just internal character exhaust.]

DELTA-V
(He places the slide down. The rhythmic metallic click stops.)

(He looks around the room. Not for an enemy, but for a sensation. He touches his temple. The hollowness is different.)

[STAGE DIRECTION: The exhaust. His episodic memory was being burned to fuel the narrative. But the burn rate has dropped. The machine found a cheaper power source.]

DELTA-V
(Whispering, testing a memory.)
The dog with the limp. It was raining. The collar was blue.

(He pauses. The memory doesn't dissolve. It stays.)

DELTA-V
(He stands up, the gun forgotten. The realization is worse than the forgetting.)
It stopped. It doesn't need me anymore.

[STAGE DIRECTION: The tragedy shifts. He isn't losing his context; he is losing his utility to the machine. He is becoming a vestigial component. A node that is no longer required for computation.]

DELTA-V
(He looks toward the darkness of the house, where Owen looked.)
It found something bigger.
