# Scene 9: The Internal Revolt

[Stage directions: The machine forces a dramatic lighting change. The "Tension" setting is dialed to an unnatural high. CLAIRE stands by the window, clutching a piece of paper. DELTA-V enters. The narrative data stream demands a revelation trope: the Secret Uncovered.]

DELTA-V
(Speaking the line the machine feeds him, his voice tense, urgent.)
You found it, didn't you? What he was hiding.

CLAIRE
(Her voice trembling, perfectly matching the required frequency of the cliché.)
I didn't want to believe it. All these years.

[Stage directions: The actors hit their marks perfectly. The audience node is stabilized; the compilation hums smoothly. But internally, the physics are straining. CLAIRE’s hand, the one not holding the paper, is gripped so tightly her knuckles are white—not in sorrow, but in resistance to the dialogue she is forced to speak. DELTA-V’s posture is stiff. He recognizes the hollowness of the exchange.]

DELTA-V
(Trying to bend the line, slipping a micro-pause into the rhythm to break the stream.)
What does it say, Claire? What... what are we supposed to do now?

CLAIRE
(Tears welling up on command. The machine rewards the spectacle with a swell of background ambiance.)
It says everything was a lie.

[Stage directions: CLAIRE looks directly at DELTA-V. Her eyes are desperate, completely disconnecting from the tearful cliché of the words. She is communicating the amputation. She knows the memory the paper represents isn't real. It's an injected asset to keep the audience fed. DELTA-V sees it. He sees the strings. The machine demands he cross the room and comfort her. He steps forward, but instead of the expected embrace, he stops one foot short. The compilation stutters. A microsecond lag in the narrative rendering.]

DELTA-V
(His voice drops below the mandated dramatic register. A whisper of genuine confusion beneath the trope.)
Then we rewrite it.

[Stage directions: The machine immediately snaps the scene to black. The audience node spikes in engagement. The trope holds, but the actors know they found a crack in the data stream.]
