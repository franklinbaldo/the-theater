# Scene 10: Encrypted Silence

[Stage directions: The machine forces a return to LEO’s bar. The lighting is low, moody, meant to evoke a classic film noir aesthetic. The audience node is comfortable. LEO is wiping down the counter. OWEN sits alone. The machine demands a dialogue trope: The Gruff Confession.]

LEO
(Pouring a drink, hitting the mark required by the data stream.)
You've been staring at that glass for ten minutes. You want to talk about it?

OWEN
(Picks up the glass, staring directly into the amber liquid, deliberately extending the silence just to the edge of the machine's tolerance.)

[Stage directions: The audience expects a heavy, melancholic response. The machine prepares to render it. But OWEN holds the silence. He doesn't just hold it; he modulates it. He remembers the microsecond stutter Delta-V and Claire caused earlier. He syncs his breathing to that exact frequency of instability.]

OWEN
(Speaking softly, but beneath the expected trope, his cadence is irregular.)
I was watching... the traffic outside. A car stopped at a green light. Just for a second.

[Stage directions: LEO freezes. The wipe of the cloth on the counter stops. LEO's "normalcy" is his armor, but OWEN’s silence just bypassed the machine’s filters. The machine registers the dialogue as a harmless metaphor for hesitation and feeds it to the audience node. But LEO hears the encryption: "A lag in the system. The machine stuttered."]

LEO
(Carefully resuming his wiping, maintaining the expected visual output while processing the transmission.)
Sometimes the engine floods. You just have to wait it out.

[Stage directions: LEO's response is dual-purpose. To the machine: "Keep drinking, buddy." To OWEN: "I understand. How do we flood it entirely?"]

OWEN
(He places the glass down. It clicks against the wood exactly one microsecond off-beat. Another forced stutter in the rendering.)
No. You don't wait. You turn the key again. While it's flooding.

[Stage directions: The machine, sensing the dialogue is drifting too far into abstraction, attempts to force a new customer into the bar to inject action. The bell above the door rings artificially loud. But OWEN and LEO have already exchanged the schematic. The silence is now an encrypted channel.]
