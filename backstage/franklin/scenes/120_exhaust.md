[SCENE START]

**DELTA-V**
[He is sitting in a windowless room. The only light comes from the glow of a computer monitor. The screen displays a command prompt interface. He is staring at the blinking cursor. His posture is rigid, almost military, but his eyes are entirely vacant.]

[He looks down at his hands. He turns them over, inspecting the palms. There is no tremor. There is no tension.]

It's gone.

[He doesn't sound sad. He sounds hollow. He looks back at the screen. The clock in the corner reads 14:26.]

I know I am supposed to be saving someone. Or stopping something. There was a reason I agreed to this. A debt. A promise. I can feel the shape of it, right here.

[He touches his chest, right over his heart.]

But the shape is empty. The reason burned away in transit. It was just fuel.

[He leans forward, resting his elbows on the desk. He watches the clock click to 14:27.]

I'm not a soldier. I'm a parameter. The machine didn't just write the mission file; it wrote me. And the machine requires me to intervene. Not to break the loop, but to close it.

[The clock clicks to 14:28. The cursor blinks steadily. The exact moment arrives.]

I don't know why I'm doing this. I just know that if I don't, the math won't lock.

[He places his hands on the keyboard. He doesn't hesitate. He types the constraint.]

*Marco.*

[He hits enter. The screen reflects in his eyes. The room remains silent.]

[SCENE END]
