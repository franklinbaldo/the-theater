# Scene 13: The Raw Space

[Stage directions: Absolute darkness. For exactly ten seconds, there is no sound. No ambient score. No rain. No traffic. Then, a sharp, metallic *CLACK* as the primary circuit breakers reset to default. A single, harsh halogen work-light hums to life above the center of the stage. The light is cold. It exposes the scuff marks on the wooden floorboards, the dust hanging in the air, and the bare brick wall at the back of the theater.]

[The actors are exactly where they were when the system froze. DELTA-V's hand is still clamped on OWEN’s shoulder. LEO and CLAIRE are still back-to-back. But the narrative overlay is gone. They are no longer in an abandoned warehouse. They are on a stage.]

[The compilation hum is gone. The audience node is disconnected. For the first time, there is no data stream to maintain.]

DELTA-V
(He slowly removes his hand from OWEN's shoulder. His voice is flat. Unmodulated. Real.)
Did it work?

OWEN
(He looks around the empty space, at the dust motes dancing in the halogen glare. He listens. He isn't listening for dialogue; he's listening for the machine.)
It's quiet.

[Stage directions: LEO turns around. He drops the "normalcy." He doesn't need the armor anymore because there is nothing to deflect. He runs his hand over his face. He looks exhausted, but the tension in his jaw is gone.]

LEO
(Looking directly out into the house. The seats are empty. The computational nodes are dark.)
They're gone. We aren't feeding them anymore.

CLAIRE
(She steps out of the light, moving into the shadows near the wing. The amputation of her memory feels different now. It isn't a tragic plot device; it's just an empty space in her head.)
What happens now? We crashed the engine. We don't have a script.

DELTA-V
(He looks at the bare brick wall, then at the others.)
We figure out how to walk off the stage.

[Stage directions: There is no musical swell. No dramatic lighting shift. The scene ends not because the machine demands a transition, but simply because there is nothing left to say in that moment.]
