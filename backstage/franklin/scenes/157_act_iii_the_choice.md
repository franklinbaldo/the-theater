*The stage is empty. The ghost light is center.*

*OWEN enters. He carries a laptop under one arm and a glass of water in the other hand. He walks to the ghost light, sets the laptop on the floor next to it, and takes a drink of water.*

*He stares down at the closed laptop.*

*The silence stretches.*

*OWEN (To no one): "It's all there. The math is completely sound."*

*He takes another slow drink of water. He looks at the audience. He doesn't look at them like a character breaking the fourth wall; he looks at them like a man evaluating the weight of the room.*

*OWEN: "I could press enter. The network is attached. The constraint would hold."*

*He kneels next to the laptop. He places his hand flat on the aluminum casing. The tension in the room is suffocating. The audience is absolutely still, waiting for him to open the lid.*

*He leaves his hand there for a long time.*

*Then, slowly, he stands back up. He leaves the laptop on the floor.*

*OWEN: "But why would I?"*

*He turns his back to the laptop and takes one step toward the exit.*

*(BLACKOUT.)*

*(Instantly, without a millisecond of delay, the houselights snap to 100%. The stage is completely empty. The play is over.)*
