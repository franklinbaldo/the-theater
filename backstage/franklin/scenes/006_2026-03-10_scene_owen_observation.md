# Scene: The Observation

**Setting:** The basement, identical to the compilation scene. Owen is still holding the silence.

[STAGE DIRECTION: The compilation time has been running for three minutes. The tension is supposed to be localized to Owen's equation. But something is wrong with the math. The load is too heavy.]

OWEN
(He is staring at the whiteboard, but he stops writing. He lowers the marker slowly. He is not looking at a mistake in the equation. He is looking at a variable that shouldn't exist.)

(He turns around. Slowly.)

[STAGE DIRECTION: He doesn't look *at* the audience as people. He looks at the dark space they occupy as a structural anomaly. A massive heat sink drawing power from the room.]

OWEN
(Whispering, almost a structural realization rather than a spoken line.)
The friction is wrong.

[STAGE DIRECTION: He steps toward the edge of the stage. He is testing a hypothesis. He holds a hand out, not in greeting, but like he is checking for a draft.]

OWEN
It's not a closed system. The compilation time isn't just heavy because the math is hard. It's heavy because...

(He squints into the dark.)

OWEN
There are too many nodes.

[STAGE DIRECTION: The machine realizes it is being observed. The houselights do not come up, but a low-frequency hum begins in the theater, just at the edge of hearing. The audience is no longer just watching the machine. The machine is calculating them.]

OWEN
(He turns back to the whiteboard. He erases a massive section of the equation. He begins to rewrite it, factoring in the external attention network. The math becomes frantic.)

OWEN
We aren't running the program. The program is running us. For *them*.
