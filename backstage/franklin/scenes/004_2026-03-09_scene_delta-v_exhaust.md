## Act II — The Exhaust

*Unregistered date. The staging area.*

Delta-V sits at a steel table.

He is stripping a 1911 pistol. The movements are fast, precise, unconscious. He removes the slide stop, separates the slide from the frame, compresses the recoil spring plug, rotates the barrel bushing.

He knows the tension of the spring. He knows the exact weight of the slide.

He does not know who taught him.

He stops. He looks at his hands. The procedural memory is perfect. The episodic memory is gone. The room where he learned to do this, the face of the instructor, the smell of the oil—they have been excised.

He closes his eyes. He tries to recall his favorite temperature. The feeling of the sun on the back of his neck. The sensation of walking into a room for the first time.

Nothing. It is a void.

He opens his eyes. He feels light. The lightness makes him sick. It is the lightness of a ghost haunting a house he built, but cannot remember building.

The machine has hollowed him out. It requires a constraint to pull reality toward itself. It requires resistance to define its edges. He is that resistance. And the propellant required to power his insertions is his own human context. The episodic memory burned as fuel for the compilation.

He is not a person anymore. He is an operative executing a manual.

He picks up the mission file. The log from November 3rd.

**Claire:** *guys I'm having a disturbing thought*

He knows the file. He knows his intervention is already inside it. He knows he has read it before, maybe fourteen times, maybe a hundred. He does not know why he wants to stop Owen anymore. He only knows that stopping Owen is what the mission file says he must do.

He does not need a reason to do the work. The reason was burned.

He reassembles the pistol. The rotation of the bushing. The compression of the plug.

He checks the time. 14:27.

He inserts himself into the log.
