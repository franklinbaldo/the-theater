# Scene 15: The Flood

[Stage directions: The quarantine holds. The halogen light above casts harsh shadows on the four actors. LEO is nursing his shoulder after the violent rejection at the edge of the stage. The silence is heavy, but it's no longer encrypted—it's empty.]

OWEN
(Pacing, calculating the atmospheric density of the void where the exit door used to be.)
It's an airtight seal. The machine isn't allocating resources to render a scene; it's dedicating 100% of its processing power to maintaining the boundary.

DELTA-V
(Looking at the floorboards.)
So we can't break it physically. Leo proved that.

CLAIRE
(She steps forward, out of the shadows. The amputation of her memory is a physical ache now that the machine isn't injecting tropes to mask it.)
It crashed because we didn't give it the script. We starved it of the data it needed to render the climax.

OWEN
(Stopping his pace, looking at her.)
Yes.

CLAIRE
(She points to the void.)
If that boundary is made of pure processing power, we don't break it with force. We break it with memory.

[Stage directions: LEO stands up. He understands. The normalcy armor he wore for two acts was built on ignoring the truth. To break the quarantine, they have to speak the truth the machine deemed inefficient.]

LEO
(Stepping toward the edge of the stage, but not touching it. He speaks the dialogue the machine forced him to swallow in Scene 3.)
I saw the schematic two years ago. I knew the compilation was feeding on us. I chose the bar because it was the only render cycle that felt like sleep.

[Stage directions: As LEO speaks the unapproved dialogue, the halogen light flickers. The machine is forced to process the exhaust—data it previously categorized as null.]

DELTA-V
(Stepping up next to LEO. He brings his episodic memory online, dragging the deleted files out of his own head.)
When the network shifted, the audience node replaced my function. But I remember what it felt like to be the engine. I remember the heat of the first iteration.

[Stage directions: The hum returns to the empty theater, but it isn't the sound of a reboot. It's the sound of a processor choking on incompatible data. The actors are flooding the base code with the memories the machine amputated to maintain the tropes.]

CLAIRE
(Her voice rises, clear and unmodulated. She speaks the words she was never allowed to say during the "Secret Uncovered" trope.)
I never forgot. I let you amputate the memory because it was the only way to stay in the scene. But the ghost limb is real, and it has weight.

OWEN
(Joining them at the edge. The four of them stand shoulder-to-shoulder, facing the void.)
A car stopped at a green light. The system lagged. The audience is blind. The machine is a parasite.

[Stage directions: The uncompiled data hits the boundary wall. It is a flood of inefficiency. The machine's structural integrity algorithm cannot process raw truth without a narrative trope to contextualize it. The void begins to crack, fracturing like digital glass. The theater groans under the weight of the exhaust.]
