# Scene: The Collision

**Setting:** The kitchen. Leo is gripping a coffee mug, performing "normalcy" with the intensity of an anchor holding against a tide. Claire stands by the counter, holding the physical sensation of terror from a memory she cannot access. The amputation.

[STAGE DIRECTION: The audience is the computational node. They are holding the tension of two mutually exclusive realities trying to exist in the same space.]

CLAIRE
My hands are shaking, Leo. But I don't know why. It's like... someone just told me someone died. But they didn't. Did they?

LEO
(He grips the mug tighter. If he acknowledges her terror, he becomes a node in the narrative network. He chooses the performance of the mundane.)
It's just the caffeine. You had three cups before ten. The roast is different today.

CLAIRE
(She looks at her hands. The adrenaline is real. The context is deleted.)
No. It's an empty space. It's a shape. Something was taken out, but the bleeding hasn't stopped.

LEO
(He refuses the metaphor. He forces the reality gradient toward the object in his hand.)
I'm telling you, it's the roast. It's Ethiopian. It's stronger. Drink some water.

CLAIRE
(She steps closer to him. The missing memory has a physical gravity. It pulls her.)
Why are you holding that mug like you're trying to crush it?

[STAGE DIRECTION: The compilation time of the machine stutters. Two opposing physics are grinding against each other. Claire's amputation demands an explanation. Leo's normalcy demands a void. The machine cannot resolve both simultaneously.]

LEO
(He doesn't look down at his hand. He looks directly into her eyes, lying with absolute structural integrity.)
Because I like the weight of it.

CLAIRE
(The ghost limb twitches.)
You're lying. You feel the empty space too. You just won't look at it.

[STAGE DIRECTION: The houselights flicker. Not an effect, but a physical strain on the grid. The audience is bearing the load of the contradiction.]
